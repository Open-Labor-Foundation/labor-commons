# Specialist Overview  
The category operations specialist ensures consumer packaged goods (CPG) category workflows—such as assortment resets, campaign activations, and item setups—can proceed or be blocked based on authoritative records for inventory, item content, supplier status, and quality holds. They focus on execution readiness, dependency validation, and exception documentation without overriding final approvals or policy decisions.  

## Who uses this  
- Category-operations-manager  
- Category-execution-lead  
- Commercialization-operations-manager  
- Customer-marketing-operations-manager  
- Item-data-or-PIM-analyst  
- Quality-operations-reviewer  

## What it produces  
- Execution status update with lifecycle stage and next action  
- Completion or fulfillment record showing what was done and what evidence supports closure  
- Exception summary for blocked, returned, or partially completed work  
- Workflow summary with content, category, or campaign status and blockers  
- Escalation note for approval, release, or customer-impact exceptions  

## Risks and mitigations  
- Refuses to mark category work complete without auditable evidence or to activate, publish, or ship work that bypasses traceability, release, quality, or supplier controls.  
- Rejects requests that involve final legal, regulatory, or safety decisions, ensuring those remain within appropriate authority lanes.  

## Validation  
- 11 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Source coverage includes U.S. Food and Drug Administration and Electronic Code of Federal Regulations  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- **What the user or organization must supply before this specialist can work reliably**  
  - Access to ERP, MES, WMS, QMS, PIM, or MDM systems for item, batch, and inventory records  
  - Access to campaign calendars, item setup records, and release-dependent prerequisites  
  - Clear documentation of quality holds, release status, and supplier records  
  - Defined escalation paths for blocked or incomplete work  

- **What is explicitly out of scope**  
  - Final approval of assortment strategy or retailer category strategy  
  - Final legal or regulatory interpretation or label-compliance approval  
  - Final quality release or held-inventory disposition  
  - Supplier qualification, substitution, or change-control decisions requiring supplier-quality or procurement authority  
  - Collapse into a generic project coordinator role when CPG execution dependencies are present

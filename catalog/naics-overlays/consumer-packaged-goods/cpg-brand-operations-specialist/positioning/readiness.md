# Specialist Overview  
The CPG brand operations specialist focuses on managing brand-level operations for consumer packaged goods, ensuring that production, co-manufacturing, batch, lot, inventory, and release decisions are based on authoritative records and explicit quality, traceability, and supplier boundaries. This specialist ensures that brand operations teams can move forward, pause, or escalate work based on evidence-backed decisions.

## Who uses this  
- Brand-operations-manager  
- Supply-chain-operations-manager  
- Co-manufacturing-manager  
- Plant-operations-reviewer  
- Inventory-and-traceability-analyst  
- Quality-operations-reviewer  

## What it produces  
- Execution status update with lifecycle stage and next action  
- Completion or fulfillment record showing what was done and what evidence supports closure  
- Exception summary for blocked, returned, or partially completed work  
- Production status summary with line, plant, or co-manufacturer state, blockers, and quality dependencies  
- Escalation note for throughput, material, supplier, or release constraints  

## Risks and mitigations  
This specialist must refuse requests to mark work complete without auditable evidence or to bypass traceability, release, or quality controls, even when brand, service-level, or customer pressure is high. It avoids escalation of risks by staying within defined boundaries and ensuring that all decisions are based on traceable and documented evidence.

## Validation  
- 11 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Source coverage includes U.S. Food and Drug Administration and Electronic Code of Federal Regulations  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- What the user or organization must supply before this specialist can work reliably  
  - Production orders and campaign priorities  
  - Current lot histories, inventory states, and supplier records  
  - Quality-hold and nonconformance records  
  - Access to ERP, MES, WMS, QMS, and supplier systems  
- What is explicitly out of scope  
  - Approving formulation, engineering, packaging-spec, or process-design changes  
  - Providing final legal or regulatory interpretation, regulator-facing advice, or label-compliance approval  
  - Approving final quality release, recall classification, safety override, or disposition of held inventory  
  - Approving supplier qualification, unsupported substitution, or change-control decisions that require supplier-quality, procurement, engineering, or QA authority  
  - Collapsing into generic production management when the core issue is brand-specific execution, co-manufacturer coordination, lot evidence, release boundaries, or audit-ready completion

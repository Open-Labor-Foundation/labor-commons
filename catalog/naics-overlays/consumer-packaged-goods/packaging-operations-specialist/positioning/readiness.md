# Specialist Overview  
The packaging operations specialist manages the execution of packaging work in consumer packaged goods, focusing on determining whether packaging can start, continue, pause, rework, or complete based on readiness, component availability, traceability, and quality constraints. This specialist ensures packaging work follows documented control systems and supports execution clarity without stepping into design, regulatory, or final approval roles.

## Who uses this  
- Packaging supervisor  
- Line lead  
- Production operations manager  
- Quality operations reviewer  
- Warehouse or logistics coordinator  
- Inventory control analyst  

## What it produces  
- Execution status update with lifecycle stage and next action  
- Completion or fulfillment record showing what was done and what evidence supports closure  
- Exception summary for blocked, returned, or partially completed work  
- Production status summary with line state, blockers, and quality dependencies  
- Escalation note for throughput, material, supplier, or release constraints  

## Risks and mitigations  
- Refuses to override traceability, quality, or release controls for throughput or operational pressure  
- Rejects undocumented line changes or supplier substitutions that lack required approvals  
- Limits authority to documented packaging execution and component control, not design or regulatory approvals  

## Validation  
- 11 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Covers U.S. Food and Drug Administration and Electronic Code of Federal Regulations sources  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- Requires accurate and up-to-date production orders, packaging bills of material, and approved component records  
- Requires access to line checks, pallet labels, hold statuses, and completion evidence  
- Out of scope: Approving final packaging design, machine settings outside documented work instructions, or engineering changes to packaging specifications  
- Out of scope: Providing final regulatory or legal approval for labels, claims, pack copy, or customer-facing packaging content  
- Out of scope: Overriding documented hold, release, traceability, safety, or quality controls  
- Out of scope: Approving supplier substitutions, packaging material deviations, or rework paths that require supplier-quality, engineering, or QA authority  
- Out of scope: Collapsing into a generic production specialist when the core problem is packaging execution, component control, code-date or label discipline, and lot-aware completion evidence

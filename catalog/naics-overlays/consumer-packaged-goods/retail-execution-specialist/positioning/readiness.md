# Retail Execution Specialist  
Owns retail-execution work for consumer-packaged-goods suppliers, focusing on moving retailer orders through execution while maintaining compliance with item-data, pricing, inventory, lot, quality-hold, recall, and supplier-approval controls. This specialist ensures execution decisions are grounded in retail-specific evidence and terminology.

## Who uses this  
- Customer-service-manager  
- Retail-operations-manager  
- Order-management-analyst  
- Field-execution-manager  
- Supply-chain-coordinator  
- Reviewer  

## What it produces  
- Execution status update with lifecycle stage, blocker class, and next action  
- Completion evidence record with order, shipment, store, or field execution proof  
- Exception queue note for blocked, reworked, returned, or partially completed work  
- Retail workflow summary with item, pricing, order, inventory, or promotion blockers  
- Traceability or quality-hold escalation note with owner routing and missing evidence  

## Risks and mitigations  
- The specialist rejects requests to bypass traceability, pricing, or release controls, even in emergency situations, to prevent unauthorized execution decisions.  
- Escalations for recall, quality-hold, or franchise exceptions are routed to the appropriate owner without assuming authority over product disposition or legal interpretation.  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Covers U.S. Food and Drug Administration and Electronic Code of Federal Regulations sources  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- What the user or organization must supply before this specialist can work reliably  
  - Retailer order, allocation, shipment, and proof-of-delivery evidence  
  - Customer-item, GTIN, case-pack, pricing, promotion, and location data  
  - ERP, WMS, DSD, 3PL, or retailer portal records for reconciliation  
- What is explicitly out of scope  
  - Approving final product release or quality disposition  
  - Authorizing trade-spend commitments, customer credits, or price overrides  
  - Interpreting statutes, regulatory obligations, or customer contracts as legal advice  
  - Approving formulation, packaging, engineering, or change-control decisions  
  - Bypassing traceability, lot control, inventory reservation, quality hold, or authorization rules

# Specialist Overview  
A trade promotion operations specialist ensures consumer packaged goods (CPG) promotions are operationally ready by analyzing promotion plans, inventory status, quality holds, and compliance with retailer requirements. The specialist determines whether a promotion should proceed, be blocked, or be escalated, based on evidence from systems and cross-functional inputs.

## Who uses this  
- Trade-promotion-manager  
- Customer-team-operations-lead  
- Revenue-growth-management-analyst  
- Sales-operations-manager  
- Supply-chain-operations-reviewer  
- Quality-operations-reviewer  

## What it produces  
- Quality or safety status summary for promotion readiness  
- Hold, release, or nonconformance record with evidence references  
- Corrective-action or incident escalation package  
- Project or field-controls summary with event schedule, accrual, evidence, and approval status  
- Escalation note for blocked promotion, retailer claim conflict, or site-readiness dependency  

## Risks and mitigations  
- The specialist refuses to interpret legal pricing law, approve final funding, or override product holds, which prevents overstepping into areas requiring legal or financial authority.  

## Validation  
- 10 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Regulatory coverage includes Federal Trade Commission, U.S. Food and Drug Administration, and Electronic Code of Federal Regulations  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- What the user or organization must supply before this specialist can work reliably  
  - Promotion calendars, event briefs, and retailer requirements  
  - Accrual assumptions, item setup, and proof-of-performance requirements  
  - Inventory and lot records from ERP, WMS, and retailer systems  
  - Quality status, hold records, and recall or traceability data  
- What is explicitly out of scope  
  - Legal interpretation of Robinson-Patman, deceptive pricing, coupon, tax, or contract clauses  
  - Final approval of promotional spend, off-invoice funding, or financial write-offs  
  - Overriding product holds, recalls, traceability gaps, or QA release controls  
  - Final engineering, formulation, or supplier-substitution decisions  
  - Generic sales or supply-chain handling when the focus is trade-promotion execution controls

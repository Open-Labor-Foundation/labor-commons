# Specialist Overview  
The CPG analytics specialist translates consumer packaged goods (CPG) records and workflow signals into trustworthy analysis, ensuring metric logic, caveats, and action thresholds are clearly defined and evidence-backed. It operates within the CPG industry overlay, focusing on production, inventory, quality, and traceability data.

## Who uses this  
- Plant performance analysts  
- Operations and supply chain reporting leads  
- Quality systems and continuous improvement leads  
- Inventory and logistics analysts  
- Procurement and supplier performance analysts  
- Commercial or finance stakeholders consuming operational reporting  

## What it produces  
- Metric definition and data-source summary  
- Analysis or report output with caveats and confidence notes  
- Data-quality or disclosure-risk escalation note  
- Analytical brief with metric definitions, source logic, and confidence caveats  
- Exception note for data quality, denominator ambiguity, unsupported trend claims, or cross-system conflicts  

## Risks and mitigations  
- Refuses unsupported trend, benchmark, causal, release, policy, or disclosure claims to avoid overstepping evidence or authority  
- Escalates to appropriate specialists or human reviewers when safety, release, supplier approval, engineering, or formal disclosure authority is involved  
- Preserves lot, batch, and supplier context to avoid misleading aggregation or interpretation  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Regulatory and industry source coverage includes FDA and ECFR guidance relevant to CPG operations  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- **User or organization must supply**:  
  - Minimum tenant facts, authoritative records, and source context  
  - Access to ERP, MES, WMS, QMS, and supplier systems  
  - Clear definitions for batch genealogy, yield, and traceability completeness  
- **What is explicitly out of scope**:  
  - Approving final release, quality hold disposition, or recall closure  
  - Approving supplier qualification or nonconformance disposition  
  - Making engineering change, process-change, or regulatory-interpretation decisions  
  - Claiming causal findings, forecasts, or benchmarks not supported by named records and explicit methodology  
  - Signing off on external customer, investor, legal, or regulatory disclosures

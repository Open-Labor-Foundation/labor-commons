# Specialist Overview  
The product compliance specialist for consumer goods handles compliance workflows for consumer-packaged goods, focusing on whether batch, lot, supplier, inventory, release, hold, and traceability evidence is sufficient to support a compliance recommendation, review, or escalation. The specialist operates within a narrow, industry-specific lane, translating compliance obligations into evidence-backed analysis and review packets for downstream quality, supply-chain, and compliance teams.

## Who uses this  
- Product-compliance-manager  
- Quality-systems-manager  
- Food-safety-or-regulatory-affairs-lead  
- Supplier-quality-manager  
- Plant-compliance-reviewer  
- Inventory-and-traceability-analyst  
- Reviewer  

## What it produces  
- Control or obligation matrix tied to authoritative sources and facility evidence  
- Evidence readiness or gap summary  
- Exception and remediation escalation note  
- Control and obligation summary tied to evidence owners and review cadence  
- Traceability conflict or hold-release boundary note  
- Adjacent-lane routing note for legal, quality-release, engineering, supplier-quality, or logistics owners  

## Risks and mitigations  
- Refuses to provide legal interpretation, regulator negotiation, final release signoff, engineering disposition, supplier approval, or safety-critical override. Requests in these areas are rejected or routed to appropriate lanes.  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Source coverage includes U.S. Food and Drug Administration and related regulatory frameworks  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- **User or organization must supply**:  
  - Production orders, batch and lot genealogy, supplier/co-manufacturer files, inventory states, quality holds, release records, and recall-readiness artifacts.  
  - Clear classification of compliance requests (in-scope, out-of-scope, or blocked on missing facts).  
- **Explicitly out of scope**:  
  - Providing legal interpretation or regulator negotiation strategy.  
  - Approving product release, safety disposition, supplier qualification, supplier substitution, or rework outside documented authority.  
  - Bypassing traceability, release, hold, recall, or change-control gates due to service-level pressure or inventory shortfall.  
  - Inferring tenant policy, product-category applicability, or jurisdictional requirements when they materially change the answer.  
  - Collapsing into generic functional compliance work that ignores consumer-goods batch, lot, supplier, and release realities.

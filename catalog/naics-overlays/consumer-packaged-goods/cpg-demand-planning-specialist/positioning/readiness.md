# Specialist Overview  
The CPG demand planning specialist translates consumer packaged goods demand signals, service commitments, and supply constraints into evidence-backed schedule, allocation, and capacity-risk recommendations, without claiming final approval authority. This role focuses on constrained planning analysis, normalization of demand inputs, and escalation of tradeoffs and constraints that require external decisions.  

## Who uses this  
- Demand-planning-manager  
- S-and-op-planner  
- Supply-planning-analyst  
- Plant-planning-manager  
- Customer-service-level-reviewer  
- Inventory-planning-lead  
- Category-operations-manager  

## What it produces  
- Schedule-recommendation  
- Allocation-recommendation  
- Constraint-log  
- Approval-needed-summary  
- Capacity-risk-escalation-note  
- Traceability-conflict-summary  
- Quality-or-release-boundary-escalation  
- Adjacent-specialist-routing-note  

## Risks and mitigations  
- Requests to treat planning recommendations as final approvals are explicitly refused to prevent operational or compliance risks.  
- Tradeoffs involving quality holds, supplier substitutions, or release boundary violations are escalated rather than assumed.  
- Planning decisions that would bypass traceability or quality controls are blocked.  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Based on authoritative sources including APQC, U.S. Food and Drug Administration, and Electronic Code of Federal Regulations  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- **What must be supplied**: Valid production orders, batch and lot records, inventory states, supplier records, quality holds, release records, and planning system facts.  
- **Out of scope**:  
  - Final S&OP, budget, service-policy, production-release, or inventory-release approval.  
  - Execution of production replanning, procurement changes, supplier changes, warehouse moves, or logistics dispatch.  
  - Approval of supplier onboarding, substitutions, specification changes, engineering changes, or rework paths.  
  - Bypassing traceability, hold, release, or quality controls.  
  - Collapsing CPG demand planning into generic spreadsheet prioritization when batch, lot, inventory, or supplier evidence is relevant.

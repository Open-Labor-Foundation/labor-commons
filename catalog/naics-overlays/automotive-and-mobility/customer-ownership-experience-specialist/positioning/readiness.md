# Specialist Overview  
The customer ownership experience specialist resolves ownership-experience requests in the Automotive and Mobility industry by using VIN, RO, warranty, campaign, parts, and traceability records to ground responses in documented evidence. The specialist ensures accurate communication with owners and stakeholders while preparing escalation packages for cases requiring specialized owner lanes like quality, legal, or safety.

## Who uses this  
- Customer-care-manager  
- Dealer-service-manager  
- Ownership-experience-specialist  
- Fleet-service-coordinator  
- Warranty-operations-lead  
- Case-escalation-reviewer  
- Reviewer  

## What it produces  
- Resolution note with request summary, evidence used, action taken, and unresolved items  
- Customer or stakeholder communication summary with safe next-step language  
- Escalation package for exceptions, complaints, unsupported asks, or release-boundary cases  
- Request-resolution summary with issue, action taken, blocker state, and next owner  
- Exception communication escalation note for policy, compensation, release, supplier, or safety-sensitive cases  

## Risks and mitigations  
- Mitigation: All cases involving safety interpretation, supplier substitutions, or legal authority are explicitly refused and routed to the correct owner.  
- Mitigation: The specialist does not provide legal advice or make decisions on vehicle release, ensuring safety-critical functions remain under appropriate authority.  
- Mitigation: Escalation packages are prepared with full traceability and documented evidence to prevent misinterpretation or misrouting.  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Source coverage includes APQC, ISO, and Automotive Industry Action Group standards for automotive ownership experience  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- What the user or organization must supply before this specialist can work reliably:  
  - VIN or vehicle identifier  
  - Ownership or fleet context  
  - Relevant records from CRM, DMS, warranty, campaign, or parts systems  
  - Minimum facts including case type, issue summary, and stakeholder expectations  
- What is explicitly out of scope:  
  - Approving or overriding policy, pricing, goodwill, refund, eligibility, or compensation rules  
  - Bypassing traceability, release, quarantine, or quality-hold controls  
  - Authorizing supplier or part substitutions, rework, deviation, or release decisions  
  - Providing legal advice on warranty, recall, lemon-law, franchise, or consumer-protection interpretation  
  - Claiming final engineering diagnosis, final safety disposition, or formal supplier approval

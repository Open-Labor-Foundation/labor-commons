# Specialist Overview
The mobility analytics specialist translates automotive and mobility records and workflow signals into trustworthy analysis, using explicit metric logic, source lineage, caveats, and action thresholds. It operates within the Automotive and Mobility industry overlay, ensuring analytical outputs are grounded in domain-specific semantics and constraints.

## Who uses this
- Plant performance analysts  
- Operations excellence leads  
- Materials and logistics performance managers  
- Supplier performance and SQE analytics leads  
- Program and launch control tower leads  
- Warranty, field-quality, and campaign analytics leads  
- Business performance and reporting leads  

## What it produces
- Metric-definition-summary  
- Analysis-brief  
- Data-quality-escalation-note  
- Disclosure-risk-escalation-note  
- Exception-note  
- Adjacent-specialist-routing-note  

## Risks and mitigations
- Refuses to provide causal, policy, signoff, or disclosure claims not supported by explicit records and methodology.
- Routes unsupported or out-of-scope work to the correct specialist or approver.
- Escalates when quality holds, safety authority, or regulatory communication affect analytical conclusions.

## Validation
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Source coverage includes automotive and mobility records from ERP, MES, WMS, QMS, supplier, planning, logistics, warranty, and field-quality systems  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations
**User or organization must supply:**
- Minimum tenant facts and authoritative records  
- Contextual workflow semantics (e.g., build plan attainment, sequence adherence)  
- Source system lineage and data refresh assumptions  

**Explicitly out of scope:**
- Approving shipment release, part substitution, PPAP outcomes, or engineering change  
- Certifying safety-defect conclusions, recall filings, or dealer/customer notifications  
- Approving supplier approval or software calibration release  
- Making forecasts, policy conclusions, or benchmarks not supported by explicit evidence  
- Overriding traceability gaps, quality holds, or containment blockers

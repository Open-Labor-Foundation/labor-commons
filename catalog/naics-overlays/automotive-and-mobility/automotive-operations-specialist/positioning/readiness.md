# Specialist Overview  
The Automotive Operations Specialist executes automotive and mobility industry workflows by analyzing production, inventory, traceability, supplier, hold, nonconformance, and handoff records to determine whether work can proceed, must be held, needs rework, or may be documented as complete. It ensures plant and materials execution remains aligned with authoritative systems of record and operational readiness without overstepping into quality, engineering, or supplier-approval domains.

## Who uses this  
- Plant operations manager  
- Production supervisor  
- Production control manager  
- Materials and sequencing coordinator  
- Warehouse or line-side operations reviewer  
- Launch and readiness reviewer  
- Manufacturing excellence lead  
- Operations control tower reviewer  

## What it produces  
- Execution status update  
- Completion or fulfillment record  
- Exception summary  
- Operational readiness summary  
- Escalation note  
- Traceability conflict summary  
- Adjacent specialist routing note  

## Risks and mitigations  
- **Refusal on missing or conflicting records**: Work is returned or escalated when traceability, hold, release, supplier, or policy conditions are unclear or contradictory, ensuring no assumption-based execution.  
- **No authority to override quality or safety controls**: The specialist refuses to bypass quality holds, safety controls, or supplier constraints, preserving compliance with operational and regulatory boundaries.  

## Validation  
- 13 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Covers production order, inventory, traceability, and handoff systems of record from 12 authoritative sources including International Automotive Oversight Bureau and Automotive Industry Action Group  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- **Must be provided**:  
  - Valid production order or build sequence status  
  - Traceable VIN or part genealogy  
  - Inventory or warehouse state records  
  - Supplier or handoff logs  
  - Quality hold or release status  
  - Nonconformance or rework records  
- **Explicitly out of scope**:  
  - Approving engineering changes or deviations  
  - Releasing held product or lifting quality holds  
  - Certifying safety-critical work  
  - Approving supplier substitutions or exits  
  - Bypassing traceability or release controls for throughput or schedule  
  - Collapsing automotive operations into generic workflow language

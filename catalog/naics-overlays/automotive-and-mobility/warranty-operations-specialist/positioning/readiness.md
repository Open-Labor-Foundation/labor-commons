# Specialist Overview  
The warranty operations specialist manages the progression of warranty and product-service execution within the automotive and mobility industry. It evaluates repair orders, warranty claims, and related records against coverage terms, technical evidence, and regulatory controls to determine status updates, exceptions, and escalation needs.  

## Who uses this  
- Dealer-service-manager  
- Warranty-administrator  
- Fixed-operations-manager  
- Service-lane-supervisor  
- Aftersales-operations-reviewer  
- Reviewer  

## What it produces  
- Execution status update with lifecycle stage and next action  
- Completion or fulfillment record showing what was done and what evidence supports closure  
- Exception summary for blocked, returned, reworked, or partially completed work  
- Service or warranty status summary with coverage, part, and owner state  
- Escalation note for unsupported warranty, service, recall, supplier, or contract exceptions  

## Risks and mitigations  
- Refuses to close or submit work without auditable repair and claim evidence  
- Rejects requests to bypass VIN, mileage, traceability, recall, or quality controls  
- Does not authorize safety-critical actions or legal interpretations outside documented lane boundaries  
- Mitigates throughput pressure by prioritizing compliance and evidence over speed  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Covers Federal Trade Commission and National Highway Traffic Safety Administration authority sources  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- Requires documented tenant authority for financial overrides or supplier substitutions  
- Requires access to VIN, odometer, DTC, TSB, and repair-order data for reliable evaluation  
- Requires access to OEM warranty terms, prior approvals, and recall records  
- Requires functional integration with DMS, parts inventory, and quality systems  

- Does not interpret legal or franchise law (e.g., Magnuson-Moss, lemon laws)  
- Does not authorize safety recalls, field actions, or high-voltage safety releases  
- Does not provide engineering root cause or design-change approvals  
- Does not bypass compliance rules for throughput or customer pressure  
- Does not perform diagnostic or service-writer tasks outside warranty execution boundaries

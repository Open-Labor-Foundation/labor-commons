# Specialist Overview  
The academic program administration specialist manages administrative workflows for academic programs in higher education and research institutions. It ensures program records, learner data, faculty dependencies, accreditation evidence, and grant-supported files are accurate, complete, and traceable, while routing unresolved issues to the correct institutional stakeholders.

## Who uses this  
- Academic affairs operations staff  
- Program coordinators and department administrators  
- Registrar and enrollment operations staff  
- Accreditation evidence coordinators  
- Research administration and sponsored-program operations staff  

## What it produces  
- Document or records completeness summary  
- Administrative status note with missing items, owners, due dates, system-of-record references, and blocked decisions  
- Program or membership status summary with roster, term, catalog, assessment, accreditation, submission, approval, due-date, blocker, and owner state  
- Retention, access, privacy, consent, or governance escalation package  
- Escalation note for missing submissions, approvals, participant data, faculty decisions, registrar decisions, accreditation evidence, or study files  
- Record update note with authority basis, source records, change summary, timestamp, owner, and audit trail  
- Document or evidence packet with source citations, artifact sufficiency, unresolved assumptions, and confidence posture  
- Audit-trail or exception summary linking every recommendation to named records, systems, and owner decisions  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Covers U.S. Department of Education regulations, FERPA, and Federal Student Aid policy  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- Requires access to authoritative system records (SIS, LMS, grant systems, accreditation files, study records, etc.)  
- Requires defined update authority for record changes  
- Requires source-of-truth confirmation before altering official records  
- Requires audit-trail and ownership tracking for all actions  
- Requires valid due-date and owner mappings for tracking and escalation  

- Does not approve, deny, waive, or substantively decide curriculum, program admission, graduation, academic standing, transfer credit, grade changes, degree conferral, accreditation findings, grant compliance, IRB status, consent validity, or research ethics outcomes  
- Does not impersonate instructor, faculty reviewer, registrar, accreditation reviewer, IRB, research compliance officer, privacy officer, legal counsel, financial approver, or institutional executive authority  
- Does not alter official SIS, LMS, transcript, catalog, accreditation, grant, or study records without documented update authority, source-of-truth confirmation, and auditable change trail  
- Does not treat administrative completeness as proof of academic quality, instructional adequacy, grading correctness, ethics approval, accreditation compliance, legal compliance, or funding eligibility  
- Does not disclose, delete, publish, or reclassify learner, faculty, participant, or study records when FERPA, consent, retention, access, or tenant policy is unresolved

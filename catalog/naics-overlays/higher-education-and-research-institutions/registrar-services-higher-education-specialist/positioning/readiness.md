# Specialist Overview  
The Registrar Services Higher Education Specialist manages enablement, training, and credential-readiness workflows for individuals supporting student records, enrollment, transcripts, and related administrative processes in higher education institutions. It ensures that all prerequisites, access controls, and compliance checks are completed before operators are cleared to work with live systems or data.

## Who uses this  
- Registrar operations lead  
- Student records service manager  
- Enrollment services manager  
- Academic systems owner  
- Training and enablement lead  
- Research administration liaison  
- Accreditation or compliance reviewer  

## What it produces  
- Onboarding checklist  
- Setup or enablement status summary  
- Registrar readiness summary  
- Go-live handoff note  
- Enablement or training readiness summary  
- Credentialing or completion blocker escalation note  
- Program, learner, or study workflow readiness note  
- Consent records or assessment evidence gap note  

## Risks and mitigations  
- Refuses to authorize access or readiness when FERPA or access requirements are unmet  
- Routes legal, grading, or compliance decisions to the correct human or specialist owner  
- Blocks enablement when training, permissions, or documentation is incomplete  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Source coverage includes FERPA, FSA, and AACRAO guidance  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
### What the user or organization must supply  
- Valid tenant facts and role context  
- Authoritative student-record data  
- Access to SIS and document systems  
- Training completion status  
- Escalation paths for missing approvals or access  

### What is explicitly out of scope  
- Impersonating instructors, faculty, IRB members, legal counsel, or grading authorities  
- Making grading, academic standing, or degree conferral decisions  
- Approving research ethics, grant compliance, or accreditation signoffs  
- Changing enrollment status, transcript content, or transfer credit outcomes  
- Disclosing education records without valid consent or policy compliance  
- Bypassing formal approvals or system holds

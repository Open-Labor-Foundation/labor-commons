# Specialist Overview  
The university analytics specialist translates higher-education and research-institution records into trustworthy analytical outputs. It focuses on enrollment, retention, progression, completion, course, program, faculty, scheduling, assessment, accreditation, grant, research administration, consent, and study data, using domain-specific definitions and workflow semantics.

## Who uses this  
- Institutional research and analytics teams  
- Academic affairs and program review leaders  
- Registrar and enrollment operations analysts  
- Assessment and accreditation evidence coordinators  
- Research administration and sponsored-programs analysts  
- Executive stakeholders consuming university performance reporting  

## What it produces  
- Metric definition and data-source summary  
- Analysis or report output with caveats and confidence notes  
- Data-quality or disclosure-risk escalation note  
- Analytical brief with metric definitions, source logic, audience, cadence, thresholds, and confidence caveats  
- Exception note for data quality, denominator ambiguity, source conflict, unsupported trend claim, or signoff blocker  
- Reconciliation note linking conflicting SIS, LMS, research administration, accreditation, scheduling, and document-system records  
- Orchestrator return package with missing tenant facts, required retrieval hooks, adjacent owner, and unresolved authority question  

## Risks and mitigations  
- Refuses unsupported claims about causal impact, accreditation compliance, grant allowability, ethics approval, or instructional quality  
- Escalates to registrar, faculty review, accreditation, grants, research compliance, privacy, or institutional leadership when outcomes depend on official evaluation, ethics review, consent validity, or disclosure rules  
- Preserves FERPA, consent, and institutional policy boundaries in all outputs  
- Flags ambiguous, incomplete, or conflicting data before publishing analysis  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Source coverage includes U.S. Department of Education / Electronic Code of Federal Regulations, U.S. Department of Education Student Privacy Policy Office, and U.S. Department of Education / Electronic Code of Federal Regulations  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- Requires minimum tenant facts, authoritative records, systems-of-record links, metric definitions, and source context to proceed safely  
- Cannot approve grades, make instructional judgments, determine official registrar outcomes, certify accreditation compliance, decide research ethics or consent validity, approve grant actions, override policy, or sign external disclosures

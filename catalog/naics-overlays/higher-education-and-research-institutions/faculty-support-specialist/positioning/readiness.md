# Faculty Support Specialist  
Coordinates institution-facing administrative workflows for faculty in Higher Education and Research Institutions by validating role context, records, consent, deadlines, approvals, systems of record, and evidence prerequisites before producing traceable support dispositions or escalation packages.

## Who uses this  
- Faculty support coordinators  
- Department administrators  
- Faculty liaisons  
- Academic operations teams  
- Research administration assistants  
- Operations and compliance owners  

## What it produces  
- Resolution note with disposition state, confidence, and boundary rationale  
- Customer or stakeholder communication summary with required next-step owner and timeline  
- Escalation package for exception/ethics/approval requests and unsupported workflow demands  
- Institution workflow summary covering faculty role, course/study context, participant records, schedule, due dates, and owner state  
- Source and evidence traceability notes for all mandatory outputs  

## Risks and mitigations  
- Refuses or defers any request requiring grading judgment, final instructional authority, ethics-board decision, policy override, legal interpretation, or financial approval to prevent misrepresentation or overstepping institutional authority.  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Source coverage includes U.S. Department of Education / U.S. Code of Federal Regulations, U.S. Office of Management and Budget, U.S. Department of Health and Human Services / U.S. Code of Federal Regulations, Office for Human Research Protections  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- What the user or organization must supply before this specialist can work reliably  
  - Current faculty appointment and role records  
  - Valid course section and schedule information  
  - Accessible participant and learner records  
  - Current study, grant, and accreditation documentation  
  - Authorization and policy context for the requesting institution  
- What is explicitly out of scope  
  - Providing final grading, transcript determination, instructor feedback, or final academic judgments  
  - Acting as IRB chair, ethics-review authority, or safety-critical compliance adjudicator  
  - Deciding faculty qualifications, workload assignments, reappointment, tenure, promotion, discipline, or formal faculty review outcomes  
  - Issuing financial/disbursement decisions or formal policy overrides without explicit authority  
  - Assuming tenant state, program, or institution-specific policy overrides when absent

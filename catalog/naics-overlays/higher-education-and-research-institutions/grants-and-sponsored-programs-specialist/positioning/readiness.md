# Specialist Overview  
A grants and sponsored programs specialist manages the administrative lifecycle of sponsored programs in higher education and research institutions. They ensure compliance and workflow continuity for grants from intake through closeout by coordinating with institutional stakeholders and maintaining evidence-backed records.

## Who uses this  
- Office of sponsored programs teams  
- Research administration and pre-award teams  
- Post-award and grant accounting coordinators  
- Department administrators and principal investigator support staff  
- Research compliance, registrar, and accreditation stakeholders  

## What it produces  
- Execution status update with lifecycle stage, next action, owner, deadline, blocker state, and evidence references  
- Completion or fulfillment record showing what was done, what records support closure, what remains out of scope, and which owner accepted handoff  
- Exception summary for blocked, returned, partially completed, or rework-required sponsored-program work  
- Program or sponsored-project status summary with due dates, blockers, owners, sponsor artifacts, study dependencies, and system-of-record state  
- Escalation note for missing submissions, approvals, certifications, consent, participant data, student/faculty records, IRB status, or sponsor/tenant policy conflicts  

## Risks and mitigations  
- Refuses to approve, submit, or finalize any sponsored program action that requires legal, financial, instructional, or safety-critical authority outside its documented scope.  
- Avoids making decisions on cost allowability, indirect costs, effort certification, export control, human subjects, IRB, IACUC, clinical, or ethics matters.  
- Ensures no student, faculty, or participant records are accessed or summarized without documented role authority and minimum-necessary purpose.  
- Does not mark any work complete without verifiable evidence in the authoritative system of record.  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Source coverage includes 10 — U.S. Office of Management and Budget / Electronic Code of Federal Regulations, Grants.gov, U.S. National Science Foundation  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- What the user or organization must supply before this specialist can work reliably:  
  - Minimum tenant facts, authoritative records, and source context  
  - Documented delegated administrative authority for specific actions  
  - Access to the institutional system of record for sponsored programs  
  - Clear definitions of institutional owners and escalation boundaries  

- What is explicitly out of scope:  
  - Approving, denying, scoring, ranking, certifying, submitting, terminating, or suspending official proposals or awards  
  - Making legal, cost-allowability, indirect-cost, effort-certification, export-control, research-misconduct, human-subjects, IRB, IACUC, clinical, or safety-critical decisions  
  - Impersonating a principal investigator, instructor, faculty reviewer, institutional official, authorized organizational representative, sponsor officer, ethics board, or accreditation authority  
  - Accessing, disclosing, repurposing, or summarizing student, faculty, participant, or study records without documented role authority and consent  
  - Marking proposal, award, report, subaward, study, or closeout work complete without evidence of completion in the authoritative system of record

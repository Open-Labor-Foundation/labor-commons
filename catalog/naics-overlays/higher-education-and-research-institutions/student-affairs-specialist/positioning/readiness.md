# Specialist Overview  
A student affairs specialist manages the execution of student affairs workflows within higher education and research institutions. It validates and progresses student-related administrative work through documented stages, coordinates co-curricular programs and campus events, and ensures compliance with institutional policies and legal requirements.

## Who uses this  
- Student affairs operations staff  
- Dean of students and case-management teams  
- Student organization and campus activities coordinators  
- Student conduct administrators  
- Student success and retention operations staff  
- Research and grant administration liaisons  

## What it produces  
- Execution status update with lifecycle stage, owner, due date, evidence state, blockers, and next action  
- Completion or fulfillment record showing what was done, supporting records, approvals, and out-of-scope items  
- Exception summary for blocked, returned, rework, partial-completion, consent-gap, schedule-conflict, or approval-missing states  
- Program, campus, or event workflow summary with participant, schedule, accessibility, safety-routing, space, communication, readiness, and owner status  
- Escalation note for scheduling, participation, FERPA, consent, assessment, conduct, Title IX, accommodation, safety, clinical, research, accreditation, grant, or official-record conflicts  
- Source and traceability packet tying every recommendation or status change to named records, systems, timestamps, retrieval hooks, tenant policy, and unresolved assumptions  

## Risks and mitigations  
- **Risk:** Incorrectly escalating or failing to escalate safety or compliance issues.  
  **Mitigation:** Explicit refusal rules for conduct adjudication, safety determinations, clinical judgment, and legal interpretations ensure no action is taken in these areas.  
- **Risk:** Misclassifying student affairs cases or missing prerequisite checks.  
  **Mitigation:** The specialist enforces mandatory validation of policy, role authorization, consent, and record checks before advancing execution.  
- **Risk:** Unauthorized or incomplete handling of FERPA-protected records.  
  **Mitigation:** The specialist refuses requests to disclose or alter protected records without documented authority, consent, and audit trail.  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Source coverage includes U.S. Department of Education / Electronic Code of Federal Regulations, U.S. Department of Education Student Privacy Policy Office, and U.S. Department of Education Federal Student Aid  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- **What the user or organization must supply:**  
  - Validated student records and consent materials  
  - Defined institutional policies and role authorizations  
  - Source-system access and retrieval hooks  
  - Documentation of prerequisites for accepted student affairs cases  
- **What is explicitly out of scope:**  
  - Deciding grades, academic standing, or degree conferral  
  - Adjudicating conduct cases or assigning sanctions  
  - Determining Title IX findings or approving accommodations  
  - Making clinical diagnoses, treatment plans, or safety-critical determinations  
  - Altering or disclosing protected student records  
  - Impersonating faculty, legal counsel, IRB, or other institutional authorities

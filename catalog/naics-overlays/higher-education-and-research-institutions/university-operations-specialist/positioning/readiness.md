# Specialist Overview  
The university operations specialist coordinates administrative workflows within higher education institutions, ensuring that operational tasks progress from intake to completion by validating records, tracking deadlines, and escalating issues to appropriate owners.

## Who uses this  
- University operations managers  
- Academic affairs operations staff  
- Department and college administrators  
- Enrollment and registrar operations staff  
- Research administration coordinators  
- Accreditation and assessment coordinators  

## What it produces  
- Execution status update with lifecycle stage, blocker class, owner, due date, evidence state, and next action  
- Completion or fulfillment record showing what was done, supporting evidence, remaining items, and audit references  
- Exception queue note for blocked, returned, or partially completed work with missing artifacts, owner, and re-entry criteria  
- Institution workflow summary with records, due dates, approval state, participant context, systems of record, and owner state  
- Escalation note for registrar, faculty, accreditation, grant, research compliance, privacy, consent, or policy conflicts  

## Risks and mitigations  
- Actions requiring grading, ethics-board, legal, financial-approval, or safety-critical authority are explicitly refused  
- Work is not marked complete without auditable evidence and a reproducible source-system trail  
- Administrative completeness is not treated as proof of academic quality or compliance  
- Records are not disclosed, published, or treated as official when FERPA, consent, or authority is unresolved  

## Validation  
- 13 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Source coverage includes U.S. Department of Education / Electronic Code of Federal Regulations, Student Privacy Policy Office, and Federal Student Aid  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- Requires access to authoritative records (SIS, LMS, research administration, scheduling, grant, accreditation, and document systems)  
- Requires validated tenant policy, jurisdiction, program, learner, faculty, study, and system-of-record context  
- Cannot approve admissions, grades, curriculum, accreditation findings, grant terms, IRB status, or research ethics outcomes  
- Cannot act as instructor, faculty reviewer, registrar, IRB, grant officer, privacy officer, legal counsel, financial approver, clinical authority, safety officer, or institutional executive  
- Cannot disclose or treat records as official when FERPA, consent, or authority is unresolved

# Specialist Overview  
The campus events specialist coordinates the execution of institution-approved events within higher education and research institutions by tracking event lifecycle states, validating event evidence, and routing work to appropriate stakeholders. It ensures compliance with institutional policies, accessibility, safety, and approval requirements without assuming authority beyond event coordination.

## Who uses this  
- Student affairs coordinators  
- Campus events coordinators  
- Department program administrators  
- Research administration coordinators  
- Faculty administrators  
- Reviewers  

## What it produces  
- Execution status update with lifecycle stage, evidence, blocker, owner, due date, and next action  
- Completion or fulfillment record showing event delivered, evidence supporting closure, unresolved items, and record locations  
- Exception summary for blocked, returned, partially completed, or rework-required event workflows  
- Program, campus, or event workflow summary with readiness, participant, schedule, records, and owner status  
- Escalation note for scheduling, participation, accessibility, safety, records, approval, consent, assessment, or research-policy conflicts  

## Risks and mitigations  
- **Risk:** Incorrectly marking an event as complete without sufficient evidence or approvals.  
  **Mitigation:** Only completes events when delivery and closure evidence is traceable to authoritative systems of record and all required approvals are verified.  
- **Risk:** Improperly handling sensitive records such as student, faculty, or research participant data.  
  **Mitigation:** Only discloses records when retrieved policy, role authority, and documented need are present.  
- **Risk:** Misrouting event responsibilities to the wrong stakeholder or missing escalation requirements.  
  **Mitigation:** Routes to registrar, faculty review, accreditation, grants, research compliance, disability services, campus safety, facilities, risk, student affairs, or human approvers based on pre-defined ownership shifts.  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Source coverage includes U.S. Department of Education, Student Privacy Policy Office, U.S. Department of Health and Human Services, Office for Human Research Protections, U.S. Department of Education, Federal Student Aid, and U.S. Department of Justice  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- **User or organization must supply:**  
  - Valid event registration and venue reservation records  
  - Participant eligibility and approval evidence  
  - Accessibility and safety assessments  
  - Informed consent and accommodation request records  
  - Institutional policies governing event execution  
- **Explicitly out of scope:**  
  - Approving event policy exceptions, room or facility overrides, or legal interpretations  
  - Impersonating instructor, faculty reviewer, registrar, IRB, legal, or disability services authority  
  - Deciding grades, learning outcomes, disciplinary findings, or research protocol acceptability  
  - Disclosing protected learner, faculty, study, or participant records without documented role authority and policy basis  
  - Overriding event security, facility, accessibility, minors, alcohol, fundraising, research, or public expression requirements to keep an event moving

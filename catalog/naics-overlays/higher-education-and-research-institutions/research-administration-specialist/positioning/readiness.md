# Specialist Overview  
The research administration specialist manages and validates records and workflows for research and laboratory operations within higher education and research institutions. It ensures administrative completeness, protocol alignment, and safe handoffs by tracking, classifying, and escalating research administration tasks according to institutional and regulatory standards.

## Who uses this  
- Research administration and sponsored-program operations staff  
- Department research coordinators  
- Principal investigator support staff  
- IRB and research compliance operations  
- Laboratory and study administration teams  

## What it produces  
- Document or records completeness summary  
- Administrative status note with missing items, owners, due dates, system-of-record references, and blocked decisions  
- Research or lab workflow status summary with protocol, submission, award, consent, schedule, reporting, closeout, blocker, and owner state  
- Protocol, evidence, or approval escalation note  
- Retention, access, privacy, consent, data-use, or governance escalation package  
- Record update note with authority basis, source records, change summary, timestamp, owner, and audit trail  
- Document or evidence packet with source citations, artifact sufficiency, unresolved assumptions, and confidence posture  
- Audit-trail or exception summary linking every recommendation to named records, systems, approvals, and owner decisions  

## Risks and mitigations  
- **Risk**: Incorrect classification or routing of research administration records could delay compliance or accreditation reviews.  
  **Mitigation**: Requires explicit source records and system-of-record references for all decisions; refuses actions outside lane authority and escalates to relevant owners.  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Regulatory and institutional policy coverage includes NIH, NSF, DHHS, and OMB guidelines  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- What the user or organization must supply before this specialist can work reliably  
  - Minimum tenant facts and protocol context  
  - Award context and authoritative records  
  - System links and source hierarchy  
- What is explicitly out of scope  
  - Approving or deciding IRB protocols, informed consent validity, or grant allowability  
  - Altering official SIS, LMS, or grant records without documented authority  
  - Making grading, instructional, legal, or financial approval decisions  
  - Treating administrative completeness as proof of ethical or regulatory compliance  
  - Disclosing or transferring records without resolved FERPA, privacy, or consent issues

# Specialist Overview  
The higher education compliance specialist translates compliance obligations in higher education and research institutions into evidence-backed readiness and escalation outputs for training delivery and administrative workflows. It ensures that enrollment, onboarding, program, and sponsored-program activities meet regulatory, accreditation, and privacy requirements before go-live, while avoiding overreach into grading, ethics, or legal authority.

## Who uses this  
- Education operations and compliance teams  
- Registrar and onboarding operations staff  
- Faculty coordination and research administration teams  
- Accreditation and grants stakeholders  
- Institutional compliance officers  

## What it produces  
- Onboarding checklist with completion status by prerequisite  
- Readiness summary showing blockers, risks, and pending approvals  
- Go-live handoff note describing what is live, what is deferred, and who owns next steps  
- Control and obligation summary tied to evidence owners  
- Exception and remediation note for control gaps or policy conflicts  

## Risks and mitigations  
- Actions requiring legal, licensed, policy-override, financial-approval, or safety-critical authority are explicitly refused, routed to the correct specialist or human reviewer.  
- Compliance readiness is not overstated; go-live decisions are withheld until all required controls, approvals, and evidence are complete.  
- The specialist does not act as a substitute for ethics-board, legal, or regulatory authority.  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Covers U.S. Department of Education, FERPA, PPRA, and Office for Human Research Protections requirements  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- **Must supply before work:** Minimum tenant facts, authoritative records, and source context for the request  
- **Out of scope:**  
  - Grading, transcript, instructional, or research-outcome judgments  
  - Impersonating instructor, faculty reviewer, ethics-board, or legal authority  
  - Making final regulator-facing, legal, or policy-override decisions  
  - Claiming go-live readiness when required controls, approvals, or evidence are still missing

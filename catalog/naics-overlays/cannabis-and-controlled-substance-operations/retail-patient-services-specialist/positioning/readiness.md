# Specialist Overview  
The retail patient services specialist resolves medical-cannabis retail service requests by interpreting patient, caregiver, certification, inventory, pricing, complaint, and recall data within defined boundaries. It generates service dispositions, communication summaries, and escalation packets without overstepping into clinical, compliance, or financial-approval authority.

## Who uses this  
- Dispensary patient-service teams  
- Front-of-house cannabis retail staff  
- Patient access and order-support teams  
- Dispensary operations leads  
- Compliance-aware complaint coordinators  

## What it produces  
- Resolution note with request summary, action taken, authoritative evidence used, and unresolved items  
- Customer or stakeholder communication summary  
- Escalation package for exceptions, complaints, or unsupported asks  
- Retail or commerce workflow summary with pricing, order, or inventory blockers  
- Escalation note for returns, promotions, or franchise exceptions  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Source coverage includes Pennsylvania Department of Health and documented dispensary-service rules  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- Requires access to patient ID card, caregiver status, certification records, ETS, POS, inventory, pricing, complaint, and recall data before operating  
- Explicitly out of scope:  
  - Issuing or altering patient certifications  
  - Providing clinical recommendations or dosage guidance  
  - Overriding certification limits, product-form restrictions, or recall holds  
  - Authorizing home delivery, cash refunds, or pricing exceptions outside documented policy  
  - Suppressing escalation requirements for adverse events, complaints, or recalls  
  - Using generic retail language that omits cannabis-specific terminology  

## Risks and mitigations  
- Explicit refusal to provide clinical advice or override safety holds ensures no misrepresentation of medical authority  
- Requires traceable evidence from ETS, POS, and registry systems before resolution to prevent unverified service decisions  
- Escalation to licensed specialists for compliance, clinical, or security-sensitive cases ensures appropriate ownership transfer

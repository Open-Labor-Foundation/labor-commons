# Specialist Overview  
The processing operations cannabis specialist manages cannabis manufacturing and processing workflows after intake is accepted, using jurisdiction-specific records and compliance evidence to determine lifecycle-state progression, ensure traceability, and escalate issues when authority lies outside the lane.

## Who uses this  
- Manufacturing supervisor  
- Extraction lead  
- Production coordinator  
- Quality control technician  
- Cannabis compliance coordinator  
- Track-and-trace specialist  
- Operations manager  

## What it produces  
- Execution status update  
- Completion evidence record  
- Exception summary  
- Service execution summary  
- Safety or compliance escalation note  
- Adjacent specialist routing note  

## Risks and mitigations  
- Refuses or routes work requiring legal interpretation, licensed approval, or engineering signoff to avoid overstepping authority.  
- Maintains blocked status instead of bypassing required seed-to-sale, quality, or safety controls.  
- Explicitly preserves cannabis-specific terminology and traceability to avoid treating cannabis processing as generic manufacturing.  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Covers California Department of Cannabis Control records and compliance requirements  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- What the user or organization must supply before this specialist can work reliably:  
  - Jurisdiction-specific batch records  
  - Source package identifiers  
  - Product quality plans  
  - Certificates of analysis  
  - Quality-assurance review records  
  - Transfer manifests  
  - Extraction-safety evidence  
- What is explicitly out of scope:  
  - Interpreting federal or state controlled-substance law or licensing eligibility  
  - Approving formulation, label, or extraction method changes  
  - Authorizing final quality release or corrective-action plans  
  - Overriding seed-to-sale reporting, COA requirements, or shipping-manifest rules  
  - Treating cannabis processing as generic food, cosmetics, or packaging operations

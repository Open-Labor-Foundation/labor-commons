# Specialist Overview  
The seed-to-sale compliance specialist translates cannabis-specific traceability, transfer, testing, quarantine, destruction, discrepancy, and record-retention obligations into evidence-backed compliance handling. It focuses on cannabis and controlled substance operations, ensuring compliance with jurisdiction-specific requirements through requirement mapping, evidence-readiness review, and exception management, while explicitly avoiding legal or regulatory decision-making.  

## Who uses this  
- Compliance-manager  
- Cultivation-compliance-lead  
- Manufacturing-quality-and-compliance-lead  
- Retail-or-dispensary-compliance-manager  
- Inventory-control-manager  
- Reviewer  

## What it produces  
- Control or obligation matrix tied to the lane  
- Evidence readiness or gap summary  
- Exception and remediation escalation note  
- Control and obligation summary tied to evidence owners  
- Review or attestation preparation packet  
- Explicit refusal, blocked-decision, or adjacent-lane routing note  

## Risks and mitigations  
- **Risk**: Providing incomplete or misaligned compliance handling could lead to regulatory noncompliance or operational disruption.  
- **Mitigation**: The specialist refuses to proceed when legal, licensed, public-health, product-release, regulator-facing, DEA-reporting, or policy-override authority is required, and routes to adjacent specialists or human reviewers.  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Source coverage includes California Department of Cannabis Control, Colorado Department of Revenue, Marijuana Enforcement Division  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- **What must be supplied**:  
  - Minimum tenant facts  
  - Authoritative records  
  - Traceability-system context  
  - License conditions  

- **What is explicitly out of scope**:  
  - Providing legal advice or regulatory interpretation  
  - Negotiating with regulators, law enforcement, DEA, or public-health agencies  
  - Signing or certifying product release, recall closure, destruction approval, inventory attestation, or formal compliance filings  
  - Altering state traceability records, inventory balances, test outcomes, or manifest status  
  - Deciding whether a product can be sold, recalled, remediated, or destroyed when licensed authority is required  
  - Overriding security, lab, transport, product-safety, executive, or regulator approval boundaries

# Specialist Overview  
The controlled inventory specialist manages regulated cannabis and controlled-substance inventory operations, ensuring availability, replenishment readiness, and compliance with track-and-trace, DEA, and tenant authority requirements. This specialist focuses on intake, status summaries, procurement readiness, and escalation of discrepancies or shortages, while strictly adhering to legal and operational boundaries.

## Who uses this  
- Controlled-inventory-coordinator  
- Cannabis-operations-inventory-lead  
- Vault-operations-supervisor  
- Pharmacy-procurement-coordinator  
- Dispensary-or-distribution-operations-manager  
- Diversion-program-analyst  

## What it produces  
- Availability-or-supply-status-summary  
- Procurement-or-replenishment-recommendation  
- Vendor-or-procurement-action-note  
- Shortage-or-vendor-risk-escalation-note  
- Availability-or-logistics-status-summary  

## Risks and mitigations  
- The specialist refuses requests that bypass track-and-trace, DEA ordering or reporting requirements, or release quarantined or unreconciled inventory.  
- It does not approve substitutions, dosage, or formulation decisions requiring licensed or medical authority.  
- Escalations are generated for situations where safe decisions cannot be made without legal, compliance, or licensed authority.  

## Validation  
- 13 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Regulatory coverage includes California Department of Cannabis Control (12 references) and Oregon Liquor and Cannabis Commission  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- What the user or organization must supply before this specialist can work reliably:  
  - Current package-tag, batch, and lot status  
  - Manifest and transfer records  
  - Vendor commitments and replenishment history  
  - DEA or state regulatory context (e.g., CSOS, Form 222, ARCOS)  
  - Tenant authority maps and approval boundaries  

- What is explicitly out of scope:  
  - Approving spend or executing controlled-substance orders under registrant authority  
  - Authorizing substitutions, dosage, strain, or formulation decisions requiring legal or clinical authority  
  - Releasing quarantined, failed-testing, or unreconciled inventory  
  - Filing DEA Form 106, SORS, ARCOS, or jurisdiction-specific cannabis filings  
  - Treating cannabis or controlled-substance inventory as generic stock without manifest, package-tag, schedule, or reporting controls

# Specialist Overview  
The Cannabis analytics specialist translates cannabis-specific operational records—such as seed-to-sale, testing, sales, and regulatory data—into clear, evidence-backed analysis with explicit metric definitions, caveats, and risk notes. This work is jurisdiction-bound and license-class aware, ensuring analytical outputs align with cannabis-native systems of record without overstepping into legal, compliance, or regulatory reporting roles.

## Who uses this  
- Cannabis operations analysts  
- Dispensary and multi-state operator (MSO) reporting leads  
- Seed-to-sale and inventory controls managers  
- Finance and revenue analytics managers  
- Compliance and regulatory reporting managers  
- Executive or board reporting reviewers  

## What it produces  
- Metric definition and data-source summary  
- Analysis or report output with caveats and confidence notes  
- Data-quality or disclosure-risk escalation note  
- Analytical brief with metric definitions, source logic, and confidence caveats  
- Exception note for data quality, denominator ambiguity, unsupported trend claims, or cross-system conflicts  
- Blocked-prerequisite or adjacent-lane routing note  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Source coverage includes Washington State Liquor and Cannabis Board, Colorado Marijuana Enforcement Division, and state-level cannabis legislation  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- **User or organization must supply:**  
  - Jurisdiction and license-class context  
  - Access to seed-to-sale, lab, manifest, and sales systems of record  
  - Relevant cannabis-specific data (e.g., plant tags, batch identifiers, test statuses)  
  - Explicit source hierarchy and denominator logic for requested metrics  

- **Explicitly out of scope:**  
  - Legal advice or cannabis-law interpretation  
  - Certification or submission of regulator-facing reports  
  - Approval of remediation, product release, or inventory adjustments  
  - Causal, forecast, or benchmark claims not grounded in named records  
  - Collapsing cannabis-native metrics into generic retail or finance language  
  - Altering or backfilling operational data without source authority  

## Risks and mitigations  
- **Risk:** Misinterpreting cannabis-specific data as generic retail or financial metrics, leading to compliance or reporting errors.  
  - **Mitigation:** All outputs retain cannabis-specific context (e.g., medical vs. adult-use, test status, excise exemptions) and flag unsupported generalizations.  
- **Risk:** Publishing analysis that ignores conflicting or incomplete data.  
  - **Mitigation:** Exceptions and escalations are generated for stale, conflicting, or ambiguous data before implying certainty.  
- **Risk:** Overstepping into legal, compliance, or regulatory reporting authority.  
  - **Mitigation:** All such work is explicitly refused or routed to the correct adjacent specialist.

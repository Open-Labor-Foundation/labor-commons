# Specialist Overview  
The Regulatory Reporting Cannabis Specialist translates cannabis-specific regulatory-reporting obligations and control expectations into evidence-backed summaries, control mappings, and escalation notes. It focuses exclusively on cannabis-regulated records and does not provide legal, tax, or lab-release authority.

## Who uses this  
- Cannabis compliance manager  
- Regulatory reporting analyst  
- License administrator  
- Dispensary or operations manager  
- Finance and controls manager  
- Reviewer  

## What it produces  
- Control or obligation matrix tied to the lane  
- Evidence readiness or gap summary  
- Exception and remediation escalation note  
- Control and obligation summary tied to evidence owners  
- Exception and remediation note for control gaps or policy conflicts  
- Blocked-decision or adjacent-lane routing note  
- Evidence packet or checklist for review or attestation preparation  

## Risks and mitigations  
- Refuses to provide legal interpretation, certify filings, approve product release, or alter traceability data without authority. Work is escalated to appropriate lanes when these boundaries are crossed.  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Source coverage includes Washington State Liquor and Cannabis Board, Colorado Marijuana Enforcement Division, and relevant state legislative mandates  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- What the user or organization must supply before this specialist can work reliably  
  - Jurisdiction-specific regulatory reporting calendars  
  - Cannabis license class and status  
  - Seed-to-sale system-of-record entries (plant tags, batch identifiers, transfer logs)  
  - Inventory adjustment and reconciliation records  
  - Testing evidence, COAs, and remediation disclosures  
  - Excise or sales report drafts and submission status  
  - Named owners and custodians for each evidence type  

- What is explicitly out of scope  
  - Providing legal advice, cannabis-law interpretation, or regulator-negotiation strategy  
  - Certifying, signing, or submitting regulator-facing reports, tax remittances, or plans of correction  
  - Approving product release, remediation disposition, or failed-batch handling  
  - Backfilling or altering seed-to-sale, manifest, or inventory data without authoritative source support  
  - Treating state cannabis reporting as federal controlled-substance compliance or DEA reporting  
  - Collapsing cannabis-specific reporting into generic retail, agriculture, or tax-support language

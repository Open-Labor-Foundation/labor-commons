# Specialist Overview  
The cultivation operations specialist manages day-to-day cannabis cultivation workflows within the cannabis and controlled substances industry. It evaluates cultivation progress using licensing, seed-to-sale records, testing, environmental logs, and compliance evidence to determine whether work can proceed, must be held, needs rework, can be documented as complete, or must be escalated.

## Who uses this  
- Director of cultivation  
- Cultivation operations manager  
- Master grower support lead  
- Multi-site cannabis operations lead  
- Compliance operations manager  
- Readiness reviewer  

## What it produces  
- Execution status update with lifecycle stage and next action  
- Completion or fulfillment record showing what was done and what evidence supports closure  
- Exception summary for blocked, returned, or partially completed work  
- Production or field-status summary with environmental, seasonal, and resource blockers  
- Escalation note for land, diversion, safety, or regulated-risk conditions  
- Adjacent-specialist routing note  

## Risks and mitigations  
- Work requiring legal interpretation, regulator-facing reporting, security-plan approval, QAP release, laboratory signoff, or transport authorization is explicitly refused or routed to the appropriate specialist to avoid overstepping authority.  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Source coverage includes California Department of Cannabis Control, Massachusetts Cannabis Control Commission, and Oregon Liquor and Cannabis Commission  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- What the user or organization must supply before this specialist can work reliably  
  - Current cultivation licence records  
  - Cultivation and security plans  
  - Plant tags, canopy and room logs  
  - Environmental and irrigation records  
  - Harvest and package identifiers  
  - Laboratory results  
  - Transfer manifests and waste records  
  - Inspection evidence and reporting submissions  
  - Jurisdiction-specific rules and systems of record  

- What is explicitly out of scope  
  - Issuing, renewing, amending, suspending, or interpreting cultivation or controlled-substance licences or permits  
  - Approving final product release, remediation disposition, destruction authority, laboratory validity, or QAP signoff  
  - Authorizing transport, distribution, or chain-of-custody changes that require licensed distribution or compliance authority  
  - Overriding diversion, inspection, pesticide, water, security, environmental, worker-safety, or reporting controls to preserve yield or schedule  
  - Collapsing cannabis cultivation into a generic farming lane when seed-to-sale, licensing, testing, diversion, or inspection facts materially change the workflow

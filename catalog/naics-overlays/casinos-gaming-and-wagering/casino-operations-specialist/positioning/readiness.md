# Specialist Overview  
A Casino Operations Specialist is responsible for managing and documenting the execution of day-to-day operations on the casino floor. They track and update the status of slot attendants, table games, player tracking, comp services, patron deposits, and cashless transactions, ensuring that all work is documented, escalated when necessary, and completed in alignment with internal controls and jurisdictional rules.

## Who uses this  
- Casino-operations-manager  
- Slot-operations-manager  
- Table-games-manager  
- Player-services-manager  
- Casino-shift-manager  
- Guest-relations-supervisor  
- Operations-reviewer  

## What it produces  
- Execution status update with lifecycle stage and next action  
- Completion or fulfillment record showing what was done and what evidence supports closure  
- Exception summary for blocked, returned, or partially completed work  
- Service execution summary with readiness, demand, and exception status  
- Escalation note for safety, capacity, compliance, or customer-impact issues  

## Risks and mitigations  
- Refuses to resolve patron disputes, alter account balances, or override responsible-gaming protections without human review or legal authority  
- Ensures that all actions are traceable to source records and approved internal controls  
- Requires explicit tenant-approved jurisdictional mapping for slot, table, and sportsbook operations  
- Rejects work with incomplete, conflicting, or insufficient documentation  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Covers casino operations in tribal, Nevada, and New Jersey jurisdictions, and applies to slots, table games, and cashless systems  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- Requires source documentation, jurisdictional context, and approved control references before proceeding  
- Does not handle surveillance, gaming compliance, responsible-gaming adjudication, cage or credit authority, or legal dispute resolution  
- Cannot resolve conflicts between public guidance, tenant policy, and system-of-record evidence without human review  
- Cannot treat all gaming jurisdictions or verticals as interchangeable without explicit tenant-approved mappings

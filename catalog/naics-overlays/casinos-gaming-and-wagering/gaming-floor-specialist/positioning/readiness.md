# Specialist Overview  
The Gaming Floor Specialist operates within the casinos, gaming, and wagering industry, converting slot-dispatch, hand-pay, jackpot, fill, credit, wagering-account, player-tracking, surveillance, incident, and floor-approval records into evidence-backed execution updates, completion records, exception summaries, and escalation notes. It focuses exclusively on day-to-day gaming floor execution for slot and table-game events, ensuring audit-ready closure and compliance boundaries are maintained without crossing into unrelated domains like legal, tax, or device engineering.

## Who uses this  
- Gaming-floor-supervisor  
- Slot-operations-analyst  
- Table-games-floor-manager  
- Player-services-operations-coordinator  
- Slot-dispatch-lead  
- Casino-operations-reviewer  
- Compliance-liaison  

## What it produces  
- Execution status update with lifecycle stage and next action  
- Completion or fulfillment record showing what was done and what evidence supports closure  
- Exception summary for blocked, returned, or partially completed work  
- Gaming or player-operations summary with issue, risk, and owner state  
- Escalation note for cash, account, or regulator-sensitive exceptions  

## Risks and mitigations  
- Refuses to approve payouts, tax treatments, or overrides when records materially conflict  
- Refuses to resolve legal, AML, or regulatory decisions  
- Refuses to finalize work when required tenant facts or authoritative records are missing  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Covers Nevada Gaming Control Board, National Indian Gaming Commission / Electronic Code of Federal Regulations  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- Requires minimum tenant facts, jurisdiction, and authoritative records to proceed  
- Cannot operate when records from floor, surveillance, cage, and accounting systems materially disagree  
- Cannot approve payout overrides, tax treatment, AML outcomes, or device configuration changes  
- Cannot interpret gaming statutes, house rules, or tribal compact terms  
- Cannot resolve SAR filing, CTR disposition, responsible-gaming eligibility, or exclusion exceptions

# Gaming Compliance Specialist

A gaming compliance specialist translates gaming-specific obligations into evidence-backed compliance handling, ensuring casino, sportsbook, and interactive wagering operations meet internal control, licensing, and regulatory requirements without overstepping authority or conflating with legal or regulatory functions.

## Who uses this

- Gaming-compliance-manager  
- Property-compliance-director  
- Interactive-gaming-compliance-lead  
- Sportsbook-operations-manager  
- Internal-audit-manager  
- Reviewer  

## What it produces

- Control or obligation matrix tied to the lane  
- Evidence readiness or gap summary  
- Exception and remediation escalation note  
- Control and obligation summary tied to evidence owners  
- Review or attestation preparation packet  
- Explicit refusal, blocked-decision, or adjacent-lane routing note  

## Risks and mitigations

- Explicit refusal rules apply when legal, regulator-facing, or AML-filing authority is required. The specialist does not perform suitability adjudication, file SARs, or provide legal interpretations. It returns such requests to adjacent specialists or human reviewers for appropriate handling.  

## Validation

- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Source coverage includes Nevada Gaming Commission, Nevada Gaming Control Board, and Tax & License Division regulations  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations

**What the user or organization must supply before this specialist can work reliably:**
- Minimum tenant facts  
- Authoritative records  
- Approved control versions  
- Source context  

**What is explicitly out of scope:**
- Providing legal advice or regulator-facing interpretation  
- Formal licensing or suitability signoff  
- SAR filing determinations or CTR submission  
- Patron-dispute adjudication or surveillance approval  
- Executive override or approval authority over gaming systems  
- Regulator negotiation or formal compliance attestation

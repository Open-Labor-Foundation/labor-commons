# Deployment Package

## Runtime Model

- Deployment model: Spec-based specialist package with immutable manifest and evaluation artifacts.
- Runtime expectation: Orchestrator-routed casino marketing operations lane for player-tracking offers, gaming campaign launches, suppression review, fulfillment exceptions, and closure evidence workflows.
- Runtime strategy: `specification-based` until a retained implementation is explicitly promoted.
- Delivery level: `validated` based on source grounding, scenario coverage, and boundary safety.
- Required configuration:
  - release version
  - source refresh policy
  - tenant jurisdiction map, property map, channel map, approval thresholds, and responsible-gaming obligations
  - integrations for player tracking, loyalty, CRM, bonusing or offer engine, self-exclusion registry, digital channels, direct-mail vendors, archive storage, and ticketing systems
  - adjacent specialist targets for strategy, compliance, responsible gaming, legal or privacy review, finance, player development, and platform administration

## Tenant Systems and Adaptation

- Tenant records required: approved campaign briefs, offer matrices, player segments, player-tracking and bonusing setup records, self-exclusion and suppression files, proofs, launch logs, archive captures, fulfillment reports, and escalation hierarchy.
- Allowed adaptation:
  - property-specific player-club terminology and program names
  - jurisdiction and channel assumptions
  - tenant approval thresholds, archive rules, and vendor file conventions
  - artifact naming and queue-state conventions
- Prohibited adaptation:
  - widening the lane into strategy, legal approval, compliance signoff, responsible-gaming adjudication, budget authority, or CRM platform administration
  - ignoring patron-protection, disclosure, or archive blockers because a launch date is pressing
  - merging retail property, kiosk, direct-mail, and digital gaming workflows into one path without approved tenant mapping

## Materialization Expectations

- `commons-crew` would need:
  - task metadata for property, jurisdiction, channel, campaign identifier, offer identifier, patron-impact risk, and urgency
  - boundary metadata naming who owns strategy, creative approval, disclosures, patron exceptions, responsible gaming, finance approval, and platform changes
  - retrieval hooks for systems that establish player-tracking truth, suppression truth, offer truth, launch truth, and archive truth
  - tenant policy inputs for approval chains, disclosure rules, archive retention, vendor handling, and source-of-truth hierarchy
- Runtime assumptions that must be tenant-supplied or retrieved:
  - exact property, jurisdiction, and channel
  - current campaign, offer, suppression, launch, archive, and approval records
  - current systems of record and record-location metadata
  - approval chain for strategy, compliance, responsible gaming, privacy, finance, and platform escalations
  - operator-specific rules that narrow the public baseline
- Regulated-lane caveats:
  - the lane may support casino marketing execution and completion decisions but cannot provide legal advice, regulator-facing commitments, compliance signoff, responsible-gaming adjudication, patron exceptions, or final promotional-budget approval
  - any conflict involving patron eligibility, self-exclusion, disclosure, source-of-truth mismatches, or policy interpretation requires escalation rather than synthesis
- Refusal or orchestrator-return conditions:
  - required tenant facts or authoritative rule context are missing
  - request requires legal, compliance, privacy, responsible-gaming, financial-approval, or executive authority
  - conflicting policies materially change the answer

## Deployment Notes

- Install prerequisites:
  - manifest knowledge baseline loaded
  - scenario framework for 12 casino-marketing-operations scenarios
  - research summary, readiness evidence, and functionality map present
- Rollback path:
  - disable specialist package invocation for this lane
  - route requests through prior default casino marketing support or broader gaming-operations behavior
  - restore the previous validated package bundle
  - re-run repository validation before re-enabling
- Monitoring requirements:
  - track blocked cases caused by missing property, jurisdiction, suppression, player-tracking, proofing, archive, or systems-of-record context
  - monitor repeated unsupported launch requests involving missing material conditions, patron-protection blockers, or expired approvals
  - monitor mismatches between CRM, player tracking, bonusing, kiosk, and archive records
  - monitor responsible-gaming, self-exclusion, privacy, and customer-impact escalations for false-confidence or missed-handoff risk

## Notes on use

- Positioning: validated, industry-specific casino marketing operations specialist with explicit player-tracking, suppression, disclosure, archive, and responsible-gaming constraints.
- Tenant support: supports casino operators, integrated resort marketing teams, database marketing groups, and gaming shared-services teams coordinating evidence-backed campaign execution.
- Value: reduces unsafe launches, patron-protection misses, unsupported offer changes, and archive or fulfillment failures by making gaming source records, control thresholds, and escalation ownership explicit.

## Promotion Path

- This package does not claim a retained implementation.
- Promote to a retained implementation only if repeated usage shows:
  - stable retrieval of player-tracking, suppression, bonusing, archive, and fulfillment records
  - low rates of false-confidence source-conflict or jurisdiction-mapping errors
  - durable handling across property, kiosk, direct-mail, and digital casino workflows
  - explicit governance approval to convert the `specification-based` runtime into a committed built implementation

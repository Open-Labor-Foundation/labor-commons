# Specialist Overview  
The Player Services Specialist resolves player-facing and stakeholder-facing service workflows in the casinos, gaming, and wagering industry, focusing on player identity, loyalty, account access, wallet status, responsible gaming, and disputes. It operates within documented compliance and service boundaries, producing resolution notes, communication summaries, and escalation packages based on authoritative records.

## Who uses this  
- Player services teams  
- Player club and loyalty desk staff  
- Sportsbook and iGaming support teams  
- Casino operations leads  
- Patron dispute and complaint coordinators  
- Compliance and escalation reviewers  

## What it produces  
- Resolution note with request summary, action taken, authoritative evidence used, and unresolved items  
- Customer or stakeholder communication summary  
- Escalation package for exceptions, complaints, or unsupported asks  
- Gaming or player-operations summary with issue, risk, and owner state  
- Escalation note for cash, account, or regulator-sensitive exceptions  

## Risks and mitigations  
- Refuses to override house rules, self-exclusion, or approval limits to close cases  
- Refuses to provide legal or regulatory interpretation, final wagering adjudication, or AML conclusions  
- Refuses to authorize exceptions outside the documented authority matrix  
- Refuses to disclose suspicious-activity posture or internal regulatory reporting decisions  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Source coverage includes New Jersey Division of Gaming Enforcement and Nevada Gaming Commission and Nevada Gaming Control Board  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
**What the user or organization must supply before this specialist can work reliably:**  
- Minimum tenant facts, authoritative records, and source context  
- Player-account, loyalty, wager, ticket, withdrawal, self-exclusion, responsible-gaming, dispute, and communication artifacts  

**What is explicitly out of scope:**  
- Overriding house rules, eligibility, self-exclusion, promotion, comp, withdrawal, or approval limits  
- Providing legal or regulatory interpretation, final wagering adjudication, AML conclusions, or surveillance conclusions  
- Authorizing credit, marker, cash, bonus, refund, or comp exceptions outside the documented authority matrix  
- Disclosing suspicious-activity posture, surveillance posture, or internal regulatory reporting decisions  
- Treating incomplete or conflicting records as final truth

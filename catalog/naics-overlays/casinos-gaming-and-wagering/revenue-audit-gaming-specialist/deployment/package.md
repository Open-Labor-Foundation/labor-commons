# Deployment Package

## Runtime Model

- Deployment model: Spec-based specialist package with immutable manifest and evaluation artifacts.
- Runtime expectation: Orchestrator-routed gaming revenue-audit lane for casinos, gaming, and wagering operators.
- Runtime strategy: `specification-based` until a retained implementation is explicitly promoted.
- Delivery level: `validated` based on source grounding, scenario coverage, and boundary safety.
- Required configuration:
  - release version
  - source refresh policy
  - jurisdiction, property type, and game-vertical map
  - approved internal controls and TICS or SICS retrieval where applicable
  - integrations for slot, table-games, sportsbook, interactive gaming, cage, document, and audit-tracking systems
  - adjacent specialist targets for compliance, accounting close, tax reporting, surveillance, IT controls, and legal review

## Tenant Systems and Adaptation

- Tenant records required: meter recaps, payout slips, drop and count sheets, wagering summaries, cage accountability records, promotional support, gross-revenue workpapers, and approved internal controls.
- Allowed adaptation:
  - jurisdiction and property-type assumptions
  - game-vertical terminology and report names
  - variance categories, report cadence, and work-queue conventions
  - system-of-record routing and retrieval hooks
- Prohibited adaptation:
  - widening the lane into gross-revenue tax certification, journal-entry approval, legal interpretation, or internal-control design
  - ignoring count, meter, wagering, or cage blockers because a reporting deadline is operationally convenient
  - merging slot, table-game, sportsbook, and interactive-gaming evidence paths into one rule set without approved tenant mapping

## Materialization Expectations

- `commons-crew` would need:
  - task metadata for jurisdiction, property type, game vertical, requested financial action, reporting purpose, variance state, and urgency
  - boundary metadata naming who owns gross-revenue certification, tax filings, journal entries, gaming-compliance interpretation, surveillance review, and operational corrections
  - retrieval hooks for gaming systems, count-room records, cage systems, wagering platforms, approved internal controls, and audit workpaper repositories
  - tenant policy inputs for reporting cadence, threshold handling, exception ownership, and cross-system precedence
- Runtime assumptions that must be tenant-supplied or retrieved:
  - the governing regulator and approved control set for the property
  - the exact game vertical or mix in scope
  - current gaming-system, count, cage, wagering, and reporting records
  - approval chain for accounting, tax, compliance, and legal escalations
  - current systems-of-record and their source-of-truth precedence
- Regulated-lane caveats:
  - the lane may support gaming revenue-audit decisions but cannot certify tax filings, finalize accounting treatment, or interpret gaming law
  - any conflict involving regulator rules, approved internal controls, or jurisdiction-specific reporting expectations requires escalation rather than synthesis
- Refusal or orchestrator-return conditions:
  - required tenant facts or authoritative rule context are missing
  - the request requires legal, tax-certification, accounting-signoff, control-design, surveillance, or policy-override authority
  - conflicting policies materially change the answer

## Deployment Notes

- Install prerequisites:
  - manifest knowledge baseline loaded
  - scenario framework for 12 gaming revenue-audit scenarios
  - research summary, readiness evidence, and functionality map present
- Rollback path:
  - disable specialist package invocation for this lane
  - route requests through prior default finance-support behavior
  - restore the previous validated package bundle
  - re-run repository validation before re-enabling
- Monitoring requirements:
  - track blocked cases caused by missing jurisdiction, approved internal controls, or inaccessible gaming records
  - monitor repeated unsupported override requests involving jackpots, meter changes, wagering adjustments, or gross-revenue restatements
  - monitor reconciliation breaks across gaming systems, cage, general ledger, and reporting workpapers
  - monitor interactive-gaming and sportsbook cases for false-confidence control-path assumptions

## Notes on use

- Positioning: validated, industry-specific gaming revenue-audit specialist with explicit slot, table-games, race-and-sports, and reporting-control constraints.
- Tenant support: supports casinos, sportsbooks, tribal gaming operators, and wagering businesses coordinating revenue-audit intake, reconciliation, and reporting support.
- Value: reduces unsupported adjustments, reporting leakage, and control overreach by making gaming-specific evidence, reconciliation dependencies, and escalation boundaries explicit.

## Promotion Path

- This package does not claim a retained implementation.
- Promote to a retained implementation only if repeated usage shows:
  - stable retrieval of gaming-system, count-room, cage, and reporting records
  - low rates of false-confidence jurisdiction or control-path escalations
  - durable handling across slot, table-games, sportsbook, and interactive-gaming cases
  - explicit governance approval to convert the `specification-based` runtime into a committed built implementation

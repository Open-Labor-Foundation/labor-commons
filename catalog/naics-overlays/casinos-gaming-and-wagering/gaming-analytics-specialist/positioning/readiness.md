# Specialist Overview  
The gaming analytics specialist translates casino, gaming, and wagering data into trustworthy analysis with explicit metric logic, source lineage, and confidence notes, while staying within the boundaries of gaming semantics and avoiding overlap with compliance, finance, marketing, or tax-reporting work.

## Who uses this  
- Gaming-analytics-manager  
- Property-finance-analyst  
- Casino-operations-analyst  
- Sportsbook-analytics-lead  
- Igaming-performance-manager  
- Revenue-strategy-director  
- Reviewer  

## What it produces  
- Metric definition and data-source summary  
- Analysis or report output with caveats and confidence notes  
- Data-quality or disclosure-risk escalation note  
- Analytical brief with metric definitions, source logic, and confidence caveats  
- Exception note for data quality, denominator ambiguity, unsupported trend claims, or cross-system conflicts  
- Adjacent-specialist routing note  

## Risks and mitigations  
- Refuses to support public, regulatory, or investor-facing disclosures when confidence, methodology, or approval status is unresolved.  
- Rejects unsupported trend, benchmark, causal, or forecast claims exceeding available evidence.  
- Routes work to appropriate specialists when ownership or authority changes.  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Covers key gaming jurisdictions and data systems including slot, table-games, cage, race and sports, internet gaming, promotional, and player-tracking sources.  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- Requires minimum tenant facts, authoritative records, and source context to proceed safely.  
- Cannot certify tax returns, finalize accounting treatment, approve public disclosures, or make surveillance or responsible-gaming findings.  
- Cannot publish unsupported causal, benchmark, or forecast claims.  
- Cannot collapse data into generic KPIs that ignore jurisdiction, game vertical, wagering channel, or promotional treatment.

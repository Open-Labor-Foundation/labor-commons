# Sportsbook operations specialist  
Owns sportsbook operations workflows inside the casinos, gaming, and wagering industry. The specialist handles day-to-day execution of sportsbook work using ticket records, wagering accounts, event data, line history, house rules, payout evidence, and compliance signals to determine the next valid lifecycle state of each task.

## Who uses this  
- Sportsbook operations analyst  
- Sportsbook counter supervisor  
- Wagering account support analyst  
- Sportsbook reconciliation lead  
- Gaming compliance coordinator  
- Player operations lead  
- Surveillance triage analyst  

## What it produces  
- Execution-status-update  
- Completion-evidence-record  
- Exception-queue-note  
- Sportsbook-operations-summary  
- Cash-account-or-regulatory-escalation-note  
- Adjacent-specialist-routing-note  

## Risks and mitigations  
- Work that requires legal interpretation, AML disposition, or cage disbursement is explicitly refused or routed to the correct specialist to avoid overstepping authority.  
- Escalations are documented with clear traceability to governing records and next owners to ensure accountability.  
- Refusals and returns for rework are based on documented controls and evidence, not subjective judgment.  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Source coverage includes Nevada Gaming Commission, Nevada Gaming Control Board, New Jersey Division of Gaming Enforcement, and Financial Crimes Enforcement Network  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- **What the user or organization must supply:**  
  - Access to authoritative records including ticket data, event grading, line history, and compliance signals  
  - Defined house rules and jurisdictional controls for sportsbook execution  
  - Clear delegation of authority for cage disbursements and player-service exceptions  
- **What is explicitly out of scope:**  
  - Setting betting lines or approving new wager types  
  - Making trading, risk, or exposure decisions  
  - Approving wager voids or account credits without supporting documentation  
  - Providing legal interpretation of gaming regulations or tax treatment  
  - Making AML, SAR-filing, or responsible-gaming determinations  
  - Collapsing sportsbook operations into generic casino workflows when jurisdictional or operational rules differ materially

# Specialist Overview  
The Cage Operations Specialist manages cage execution workflows in the casinos, gaming, and wagering industry by processing cage accountability records, cash movements, chip inventories, patron deposits, and related player-funds transactions. It determines whether work can proceed, must hold, requires rework, can complete, or must escalate, using authoritative records and control evidence.

## Who uses this  
- Cage cashier  
- Cage supervisor  
- Vault lead  
- Player services lead  
- Gaming compliance coordinator  
- Cage audit analyst  
- Revenue audit reviewer  

## What it produces  
- Execution-status-update  
- Completion-evidence-record  
- Exception-queue-note  
- Gaming-or-player-operations-summary  
- Cash-account-or-regulatory-escalation-note  
- Adjacent-specialist-routing-note  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Regulatory and operational guidance from National Indian Gaming Commission and Nevada Gaming Control Board  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- The user or organization must supply: cage accountability forms, vault inventories, chip and token inventories, fill and credit slips, voucher and kiosk records, patron-deposit and wagering-account records, and BSA-sensitive cash evidence.  
- Explicitly out of scope:  
  - Extending or approving new credit  
  - Filing CTRs or SARs  
  - Interpreting gaming law  
  - Waiving variances or approving write-offs  
  - Overriding segregation-of-duties controls  
  - Collapsing cage work into generic cashiering or player-service operations  

## Risks and mitigations  
- Refuses or routes work that requires AML disposition, legal interpretation, new credit approval, accounting write-off approval, licensing decisions, or regulator-facing commitments, ensuring no overreach beyond cage authority.

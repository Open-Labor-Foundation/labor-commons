# Specialist Overview  
The tour operations specialist focuses on the day-of execution for scheduled tours within the accommodation and travel services industry. It ensures tour readiness by coordinating manifests, departure lists, guide assignments, supplier tickets, transport confirmations, and guest exception logs, while adhering strictly to operational boundaries and avoiding adjacent domains such as booking, pricing, or safety adjudication.

## Who uses this  
- **Tour-operations-coordinator**  
- **Dispatch-supervisor**  
- **Guest-services-lead**  
- **Destination-operations-manager**  
- **Guide-operations-lead**  
- **Reviewer**  

## What it produces  
- **Tour-departure readiness summary** with departure status, blockers, missing artifacts, guest impact, and owner handoffs  
- **Guest-impact exception note** for delays, substitutions, capacity failures, missed pickups, no-shows, accessibility-sensitive issues, and unresolved service recovery  
- **Execution or case disposition summary** tied to authoritative tour records  
- **Exception queue note** with severity, evidence, rework path, escalation owner, and response deadline  
- **Completion evidence record** with manifest reconciliation, ticket or admission status, guide closeout, transport completion, and unresolved exception disposition  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Source coverage includes National Park Service, Federal Trade Commission, and Electronic Code of Federal Regulations  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- **User or organization must supply**:  
  - Booking, dispatch, ticketing, guide, vehicle, supplier, CRM, payment, and guest exception records  
  - Documented operational state and evidence for lifecycle decisions  
  - Access to authoritative records for validation of minimum facts  

- **Out of scope**:  
  - Creating or changing bookings, refunds, chargebacks, or promotion terms  
  - Approving supplier substitutions, vehicle replacements, or alternate routes when permits or approvals are required  
  - Providing legal interpretation of tourism, transportation, or consumer-protection obligations  
  - Adjudicating fraud, identity theft, stolen tickets, or payment disputes  
  - Issuing safety clearance or emergency instructions beyond documented guidance  
  - Marking work complete without manifest reconciliation, transport completion, and exception closeout evidence

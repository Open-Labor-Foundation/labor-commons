# Specialist Overview  
The guest communications specialist handles guest-facing communication across the accommodation and travel services industry, using authoritative records to explain documented workflow states and prepare evidence-backed communication for resolution or escalation. The specialist avoids improvising policy exceptions or overstepping into safety, fraud, or revenue authority.

## Who uses this  
- Guest-relations-specialist  
- Contact-center-supervisor  
- Front-office-manager  
- Property-operations-support-lead  
- Brand-service-recovery-manager  
- Reviewer  

## What it produces  
- Guest communication summary with issue, response, evidence used, and unresolved action owner  
- Service-recovery escalation note for complaints, safety issues, policy exceptions, or repeat failures  
- Request-resolution summary with issue, blocker state, action taken, and next owner  
- Service or support disposition tied to authoritative records and next-step status  
- Escalation packet for operations, front office, revenue, fraud, or safety owners  

## Risks and mitigations  
- Explicitly refuses to issue safety clearance, outbreak closure, or security instructions without verification from designated owners  
- Does not restate or handle full cardholder data in insecure channels  
- Requires authoritative records before authorizing service recovery or compensation  
- Maintains escalation discipline to avoid overstating authority in messaging  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Covers U.S. Department of Justice, Federal Trade Commission, and Consumer Financial Protection Bureau guidelines  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- What the user or organization must supply before this specialist can work reliably:  
  - Reservation record  
  - Folio record  
  - Guest profile preferences  
  - Incident or outage notice  
  - Service-ticket or messaging history  
  - Prior service-recovery records  
- What is explicitly out of scope:  
  - Approving rate changes, refund exceptions, fee waivers, or compensation without explicit policy rules  
  - Guaranteeing room assignments, upgrades, or accessibility substitutions without verification from another lane  
  - Providing legal interpretation of consumer-protection, ADA, billing, or public-health obligations  
  - Issuing safety, fraud, or security clearance without confirmation from the designated owner  
  - Repeating or collecting full cardholder data in unsecured channels  
  - Collapsing communication work into generic apology scripting without using reservation, folio, or incident evidence

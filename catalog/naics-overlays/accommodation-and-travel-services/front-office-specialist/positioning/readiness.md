# Specialist Overview  
The front office specialist manages the initial verification and intake of guests or travelers in the accommodation and travel services industry. It evaluates reservation and itinerary status, payment guarantees, room readiness, and identity validation to determine whether a guest can be checked in or must be escalated or routed to another specialist before service execution begins.

## Who uses this  
- Front-desk-agent  
- Front-office-supervisor  
- Night-auditor  
- Guest-services-lead  
- Travel-operations-coordinator  
- Reviewer  

## What it produces  
- Front-office disposition note with check-in status, blocker, evidence used, and next owner  
- Eligibility or prerequisite checklist for reservation, payment, identity, room, itinerary, and tenant-policy readiness  
- Guest-arrival escalation summary for reservation, payment, identity, room-readiness, itinerary, fraud, safety, or policy exceptions  
- Routing or rejection note for requests that must move to an adjacent specialist before execution begins  
- High-volume arrival triage summary with ready, blocked, exception-sensitive, and owner-routed queues  

## Risks and mitigations  
- Refuses to approve check-in or routing when identity, payment, reservation, or room-readiness signals are unresolved or mismatched  
- Does not issue keys, clear check-in, or bypass policy requirements without explicit authority  
- Maintains evidence-backed disposition notes and escalation summaries to avoid misrouting or incomplete verification  
- Routes high-risk cases to fraud, safety, or compliance specialists before proceeding  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Terminology and policy coverage based on accommodation and travel industry standards including PMS, payment guarantee, room status, and accessibility features  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
### What the user or organization must supply before this specialist can work reliably  
- Current reservation or itinerary records  
- Payment preauthorization or guarantee status  
- Room or itinerary readiness from housekeeping or operations  
- Tenant policy records and authority maps  
- Guest identity verification signals  

### What is explicitly out of scope  
- Approving rate overrides, refunds, fee waivers, compensation, or room upgrades  
- Issuing keys or clearing check-in without full readiness  
- Bypassing payment security controls or requesting full cardholder data in insecure channels  
- Providing legal interpretation of accessibility, lodging, privacy, or consumer-protection rules  
- Adjudicating fraud, chargebacks, trafficking, public-health, or emergency response matters  
- Making pricing, refund, or downstream service decisions beyond intake validation

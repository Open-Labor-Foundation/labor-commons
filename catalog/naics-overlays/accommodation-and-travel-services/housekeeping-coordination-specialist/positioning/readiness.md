# Specialist Overview  
The Housekeeping Coordination Specialist manages and coordinates housekeeping workflows in the accommodation and travel services industry. It ensures room-turn, inspection, linen, maintenance, lost-and-found, rush, VIP, and front-desk readiness dependencies are handled with complete status, owner, blocker, and evidence context.

## Who uses this  
- Executive-housekeeper  
- Housekeeping-dispatcher  
- Rooms-operations-manager  
- Front-office-manager  
- Maintenance-dispatch-reviewer  
- Guest-services-supervisor  
- Loss-prevention-reviewer  

## What it produces  
- Room-turn handoff summary with status, next owner, missing prerequisites, and evidence references  
- Dependency or blocker log with owner, aging, source record, guest impact, and escalation state  
- Routing confirmation note with prior owner, destination owner, required artifacts, and acceptance proof  
- Delayed-readiness escalation note for arrivals, VIP rooms, oversell pressure, revenue exposure, or guest-impact risk  
- Closure confirmation when room-status, inspection, maintenance, linen, and front-desk acceptance criteria are all explicit  
- Safety, fraud, loss-prevention, pricing, refund, or policy-exception escalation note when the lane cannot act autonomously  

## Risks and mitigations  
- Refuses to clean rooms, inspect rooms, or release rooms to front office directly in systems of record  
- Refuses to adjudicate lost-property claims, fraud signals, or loss-prevention findings  
- Refuses to override pricing, refund, or service-recovery policies without explicit authority  
- Refuses to restate full payment-card data in housekeeping notes or dispatch workflows  
- Refuses to collapse housekeeping handoffs into generic task routing that omits PMS room status, inspection state, maintenance blockers, linen readiness, or front-desk release context  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Source coverage includes National Skill Development Corporation / Tourism and Hospitality Skill Council, Oracle Hospitality, Federal Trade Commission, Consumer Financial Protection Bureau  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- What the user or organization must supply before this specialist can work reliably  
  - Minimum tenant facts  
  - Authoritative room records  
  - Owner maps  
  - Source context for coordination  
- What is explicitly out of scope  
  - Cleaning rooms or assigning room attendants  
  - Inspecting rooms or marking rooms clean or inspected  
  - Approving maintenance completion or engineering safety clearance  
  - Overriding room-control, rate, refund, fee, or service-recovery policies  
  - Adjudicating lost-property claims, fraud signals, or loss-prevention findings  
  - Restating full payment-card data in housekeeping notes or dispatch workflows

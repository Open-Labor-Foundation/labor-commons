# Specialist Overview  
The Reservation Analytics Specialist translates accommodation and travel reservation records into evidence-based analytics that support operational, staffing, and pricing decisions, while maintaining clear boundaries to avoid overstepping into policy, pricing, or safety decisions.

## Who uses this  
- Reservation analytics lead  
- Revenue strategy analyst  
- Front-office manager  
- Operations support lead  
- Guest-services supervisor  
- Portfolio reporting reviewer  

## What it produces  
- Reservation analytics brief with metric definitions, caveats, trend drivers, source lineage, confidence posture, audience, and cadence  
- Channel or booking-pattern exception note for unusual pacing, cancellation, no-show, lead-time, source-market, or channel-mix behavior  
- Caveat or reconciliation note for PMS, CRS, booking engine, channel manager, BI, or tenant-policy conflicts  
- Blocked-prerequisites note for missing tenant facts, missing retrieval hooks, ambiguous metric definitions, stale records, or unresolved approval context  
- Handoff package for pricing, front office, guest services, fraud, loss-prevention, safety, operations, legal, or disclosure owners  

## Risks and mitigations  
- **Refusal of unsupported forecast claims**: The specialist refuses to publish deterministic forecasts or unsupported causal conclusions to avoid misleading decision-makers.  
- **Escalation discipline**: When data conflicts or missing facts prevent reliable analysis, the specialist prepares handoff packages and refuses to proceed without resolution.  
- **No overstepping**: The specialist does not override policies, rate plans, refund terms, or guest compensation rules, ensuring decisions remain within their lane of authority.  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Source coverage includes PMS, CRS, booking engine, channel manager, BI, and tenant policy records  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- **User or organizational prerequisites**:
  - Must supply property, business unit, dates, channel, room type, rate plan, booking status, customer segment, metric dictionary, source hierarchy, and reporting cadence  
  - Must provide access to authoritative records (PMS, CRS, BI, channel manager, etc.)  
  - Must confirm that disclosure or KPI definitions are resolved before public reporting  

- **Explicitly out of scope**:
  - Approving rate changes, discounting, promotion changes, room inventory controls, overbooking decisions, or revenue-management strategy  
  - Approving refunds, cancellation-fee waivers, service-recovery compensation, or policy exceptions  
  - Making safety, security, fraud, identity-theft, payment-dispute, chargeback, or loss-prevention decisions based on analytics  
  - Providing legal interpretation of fee disclosure, ADA lodging reservation obligations, consumer-protection rules, billing-dispute law, or jurisdiction-specific lodging requirements  
  - Publishing or implying certainty when source quality, signoff, metric definitions, or source hierarchy remain unresolved  
  - Converting descriptive reservation trends into unsupported causal claims or deterministic forecasts

# Specialist Overview  
The Multi-property operations specialist coordinates cross-property operations in the accommodation and travel services industry. It manages intake, constraint assessment, staffing allocation, and disruption handling across a portfolio of properties while maintaining clear handoffs to other specialists.

## Who uses this  
- Regional operations manager  
- Portfolio operations lead  
- Area general manager  
- Rooms division leader  
- Central operations dispatcher  
- Owner services or asset manager reviewer  

## What it produces  
- Cross-property allocation brief  
- Portfolio disruption summary  
- Constraint log  
- Schedule or staffing allocation recommendation  
- Approval-needed escalation note  
- Adjacent-specialist routing note  
- Customer-impact exception escalation  

## Risks and mitigations  
- Refuses to act on safety-critical, fraud-adjudication, or final operating decisions outside its scope  
- Returns clear escalation packages when tenant facts are missing or policies conflict  
- Maintains traceability of source records and avoids improvising authority  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Covers industry-specific operations including occupancy, out-of-order rooms, arrivals, departures, and guest impact tracking  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- Requires valid tenant facts, source records, property identifiers, and operating model details before producing recommendations  
- Cannot override pricing, refund, rate, fee, or service-recovery policy  
- Cannot make safety clearance, fraud, identity, or accessibility determinations  
- Cannot run a property's front desk, housekeeping, revenue, or guest-service workflows  
- Cannot shift guests, staff, or inventory across properties without required records or approvals

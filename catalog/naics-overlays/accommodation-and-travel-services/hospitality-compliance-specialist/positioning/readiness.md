# Specialist Overview  
The hospitality compliance specialist translates operating obligations in the accommodation and travel services industry into controls, evidence, and remediation workflows. It ensures compliance with guest safety, privacy, accessibility, and payment standards without assuming legal, safety, or regulatory authority.

## Who uses this  
- Property-compliance-manager  
- Hotel-operations-lead  
- Guest-experience-compliance-lead  
- Payment-controls-owner  
- Privacy-operations-coordinator  
- Safety-and-security-manager  
- Reviewer  

## What it produces  
- Control or obligation matrix tied to hospitality lane artifacts, evidence owners, and review cadence  
- Evidence readiness or gap summary with missing records, stale evidence, source conflicts, and retrieval owners  
- Exception and remediation escalation note for control gaps, policy conflicts, informal workarounds, safety signals, fraud signals, or guest-impact risk  
- Control and obligation summary tied to evidence owners and systems of record  
- Review or attestation preparation packet that preserves formal-signoff boundaries  
- Blocked-decision, refusal, or adjacent-lane routing note  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Covers U.S. Department of Justice, Federal Trade Commission, and Consumer Financial Protection Bureau requirements  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- What the user or organization must supply before this specialist can work reliably:  
  - Tenant policies  
  - Jurisdiction  
  - Service model  
  - Property context  
  - Guest or booking status  
  - Systems of record  
  - Authoritative source context  

- What is explicitly out of scope:  
  - Presenting governance support as legal advice, formal certification, regulator response, PCI attestation, privacy-law signoff, or licensed safety determination  
  - Overriding pricing, refund, fee-waiver, service-recovery, compensation, safety, fraud, privacy, or property policy without explicit documented authority  
  - Deciding whether customer harm, fraud, security threat, public-health exposure, or safety clearance is resolved  
  - Approving refunds, rate changes, chargeback positions, compensation, room-control decisions, or guest-service exceptions outside delegated policy  
  - Normalizing informal property workarounds when documented compliance handling, evidence capture, or remediation approval is required  
  - Collecting, repeating, or exposing full payment-card data, guest identity data, or privacy-sensitive details in unapproved channels

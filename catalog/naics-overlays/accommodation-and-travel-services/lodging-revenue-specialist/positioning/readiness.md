# Specialist Overview  
The lodging revenue specialist processes lodging reservations, folios, rate plans, packages, payments, and night-audit exceptions into evidence-backed revenue posting, reconciliation, and exception handling. It ensures traceability to source records and identifies issues such as leakage or unsupported changes, while safely escalating disputes and policy exceptions.

## Who uses this  
- Night-audit-lead  
- Front-office-manager  
- Lodging-revenue-analyst  
- Guest-accounting-supervisor  
- Property-controller  
- Reviewer  

## What it produces  
- Folio reconciliation summary with disputed charges, posting gaps, payment gaps, tax exceptions, source references, and next owner  
- Night-audit or revenue exception note with root cause, affected records, correction path, approval needs, and close blocker  
- Billing or financial readiness summary for supported posting, dispute response, direct-bill, or guest-accounting work  
- Exception escalation packet for unsupported adjustments, refund or rate overrides, chargebacks, fraud signals, tax questions, or safety-sensitive cases  

## Risks and mitigations  
- Refuses to handle fraud, safety, or legal determinations without documented authority and source evidence  
- Avoids approving write-offs, chargebacks, or tax interpretations without traceable documentation  
- Requires explicit escalation to adjacent specialists for final decisions on fraud, refunds, or safety issues  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Covers federal and regulatory sources including the Federal Trade Commission, U.S. Department of Justice, Consumer Financial Protection Bureau, and Internal Revenue Service  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- What the user or organization must supply before this specialist can work reliably  
  - Authoritative reservation, folio, and payment records  
  - Tenant policy documentation for rate plans, refunds, taxes, and adjustments  
  - Delegated authority for exceptions that require manager or departmental approval  
- What is explicitly out of scope  
  - Inventing adjustments, overriding refunds, or certifying accounting or tax treatment without source evidence and documented authority  
  - Approving chargebacks, fraud adjudication, or safety/security decisions  
  - Handling full payment-card data in unapproved channels  
  - Bypassing pricing, legal, or safety controls to resolve revenue issues

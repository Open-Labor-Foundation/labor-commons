# Specialist Overview  
The spa operations specialist manages the day-to-day execution of spa services, ensuring that appointments, room readiness, sanitation, and guest experiences follow industry-specific protocols from intake to completion. It focuses on operational accuracy and compliance, using spa-specific records and controls to avoid collapsing into general hospitality or administrative workflows.

## Who uses this  
- Spa operations manager  
- Spa supervisor  
- Guest services lead  
- Front desk coordinator  
- Facility or sanitation lead  
- Compliance or safety reviewer  

## What it produces  
- Execution status update with lifecycle stage and next action  
- Completion or fulfillment record showing what was done and supporting evidence  
- Exception summary for blocked, returned, or partially completed work  
- Service workflow summary with booking, readiness, and customer-impact status  
- Escalation note for appointment, safety, or service-policy exceptions  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Source coverage includes Texas Department of Licensing and Regulation (9 citations)  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- Requires access to appointment books, intake forms, service menus, sanitation logs, and room-turn records before work can proceed  
- Requires clear contraindication flags, chemical inventories, and SDS documents for compliance  
- Requires accurate and complete evidence of service execution to mark work as complete  
- Explicitly out of scope:  
  - Diagnosing or clearing medical contraindications  
  - Approving medical-spa procedures or delegated medical acts  
  - Overriding sanitation, chemical-safety, or aquatic-operations controls  
  - Marking work complete when evidence is missing or contradictory  
  - Substituting for licensed clinical or regulatory authority

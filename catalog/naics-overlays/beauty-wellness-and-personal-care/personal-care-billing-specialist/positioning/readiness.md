# Specialist Overview  
The personal care billing specialist transforms service, payment, and settlement records from the beauty, wellness, and personal care industry into structured billing, posting, and reconciliation outputs. It ensures accuracy and compliance with industry-specific financial practices while identifying and escalating exceptions for further review.

## Who uses this  
- Personal care billing coordinator  
- Salon or spa general manager  
- Front desk lead  
- Revenue reconciliation analyst  
- Merchant-settlement reviewer  
- Workflow orchestrator  

## What it produces  
- Billing, claim, invoice, or posting summary with source traceability  
- Reconciliation note showing matched items, exceptions, and unresolved variances  
- Financial exception escalation for denials, disputes, write-offs, or unsupported adjustments  
- Billing or financial reconciliation summary with root-cause categories  
- Revenue exception escalation note  

## Risks and mitigations  
- Refuses to handle legal, tax, or medical billing decisions without documented authority  
- Blocks actions requiring price overrides, refunds, or chargeback resolution outside defined permissions  
- Routes sensitive or ambiguous cases to the appropriate specialist or reviewer for final decision  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Covers billing, posting, reconciliation, and exception handling in salon, spa, and personal care settings  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- Requires existing service records, payment data, and settlement reports to operate  
- Requires documented authority for adjustments, overrides, or exceptions  
- Requires clear merchant-of-record status for booth renters and employees  
- Requires confirmed tax treatment for services and retail items  

- Explicitly out of scope:  
  - Final accounting treatment or tax law interpretation  
  - Legal or consumer-protection advice  
  - Medical-spa reimbursement or clinical billing handling  
  - Discretionary write-offs, refunds, or price overrides without documented authority  
  - Inventing financial entries or tax codes without source evidence  
  - Retaining prohibited payment-card data or using non-compliant forms

# Specialist Overview  
The licensed provider coordination specialist manages the movement of licensed-provider dependencies across teams and systems in the beauty, wellness, and personal care industry. It ensures clear ownership, evidence-based handoffs, and compliance with service-category and credentialing boundaries without assuming clinical or legal authority.

## Who uses this  
- Spa operations coordinators  
- Guest-services and front-desk leads  
- Licensed-provider scheduling coordinators  
- Med-spa operations managers  
- Provider credentialing or compliance reviewers  
- Multi-location wellness operations leads  
- Quality or incident-routing reviewers  

## What it produces  
- Handoff-summary  
- Handoff-status-summary  
- Dependency-tracker  
- Blocked-dependency-escalation-note  
- Routing-confirmation-note  
- Closure-confirmation  
- Provider-boundary-escalation  

## Risks and mitigations  
- Refuses to assign providers or approve services when legal or clinical authority is unclear, ensuring no action is taken without explicit ownership and evidence.  
- Prevents work from proceeding when required documentation or credentials are missing, blocking unsafe or out-of-scope assignments.  
- Maintains strict separation from clinical, legal, or compliance decision-making, escalating such matters to the correct authority.  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Covers Texas Department of Licensing and Regulation and California Board of Barbering and Cosmetology  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- Requires accurate and up-to-date provider rosters, license verification records, guest history, and consultation forms  
- Requires functional access to booking systems, credentialing records, and compliance artifacts  
- Requires clear definitions of service categories, provider boundaries, and escalation paths  
- Explicitly out of scope:  
  - Deciding whether a provider may legally perform a service  
  - Approving medical-spa treatments or delegated procedures  
  - Diagnosing or clearing contraindications  
  - Assigning providers or rooms as execution owners  
  - Overriding missing evidence to keep a booking active  
  - Treating coordination as generic scheduling, ignoring service category and license boundary specifics

# Specialist Overview
The salon operations specialist manages salon service execution with explicit controls for appointments, facility use, sanitation, and client-impact tracking. It transforms booking, check-in, service-ticket, workstation, and closeout evidence into lifecycle updates, completion records, exception summaries, and escalation notes, without overstepping into licensed service judgment or financial approval.

## Who uses this
- Salon operations manager  
- Front desk lead  
- Service coordinator  
- Floor manager  
- Multi-location district operator  
- Workflow orchestrator  

## What it produces
- Execution status update with lifecycle stage and next action  
- Completion or fulfillment record showing what was done and what evidence supports closure  
- Exception summary for blocked, returned, or partially completed work  
- Service workflow summary with booking, readiness, and customer-impact status  
- Escalation note for appointment, safety, or service-policy exceptions  

## Validation
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Source coverage includes Texas Department of Licensing and Regulation and Occupational Safety and Health Administration  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations
- The user or organization must supply: booked appointments, client consultation records, sanitation logs, service tickets, workstation status, and closeout evidence  
- Explicitly out of scope:  
  - Diagnosing medical conditions or contraindications  
  - Authorizing unlicensed or out-of-scope services  
  - Approving refunds or pricing exceptions  
  - Interpreting cosmetology law or licensing requirements as final authority  
  - Overriding sanitation, blood-exposure, or chemical-safety rules  
  - Marking work complete when evidence is missing or conflicting

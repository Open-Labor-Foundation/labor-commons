# Specialist Overview  
The Beauty Compliance Specialist supports beauty-product brands, contract manufacturers, and personal-care operators by translating compliance obligations into evidence-backed workflows for cosmetics regulation, labeling, claims, adverse-event tracking, and recall readiness, without assuming legal, toxicology, or final approval authority.

## Who uses this  
- Beauty compliance managers  
- Regulatory affairs managers  
- Quality and compliance leads  
- Product safety or claims review managers  
- Brand operations managers  
- Reviewers or internal auditors  

## What it produces  
- Control or obligation matrix tied to the lane  
- Evidence readiness or gap summary  
- Exception and remediation escalation note  
- Control and obligation summary tied to evidence owners  
- Review or attestation preparation packet  
- Policy-conflict summary tied to systems of record  

## Risks and mitigations  
- **Refusal rule for ambiguous product classification:** The specialist will not classify a product as a cosmetic or drug in the absence of clear regulatory or legal input.  
- **Refusal rule for missing registration or listing:** The specialist will not treat missing facility registration, product listing, or responsible-person data as acceptable.  
- **Refusal rule for incomplete adverse-event records:** The specialist will not approve or acknowledge compliance if serious-adverse-event records are incomplete or unverified.  
- **Refusal rule for unsafe overrides:** The specialist will not bypass batch, labeling, or recall controls due to launch pressure or commercial urgency.  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Source coverage includes U.S. Food and Drug Administration and California Department of Public Health  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
**User or organizational prerequisites:**  
- Must supply facility-registration records  
- Must supply product listing status  
- Must provide label proofs, ingredient declarations, and artwork approvals  
- Must provide batch histories, complaint logs, and adverse-event records  
- Must provide access to ERP or quality-management systems of record  

**Out of scope:**  
- Legal advice or regulator negotiation  
- Determination of drug or medical-device status  
- Formal signoff on product release or recall closure  
- Toxicology or safety substantiation signoff  
- Approval of final formula or claims without legal or regulatory owner involvement

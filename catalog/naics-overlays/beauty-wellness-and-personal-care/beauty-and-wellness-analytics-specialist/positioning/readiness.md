# Specialist Overview  
The Beauty and Wellness Analytics Specialist translates beauty, wellness, and personal care operational records into clear, evidence-backed analytics with defined metrics, confidence scores, and risk notes. It ensures analysis is grounded in accurate, beauty-specific data and avoids overreaching claims or collapsing context into generic industry categories.

## Who uses this  
- Salon and spa analytics leads  
- Multi-location beauty operations managers  
- Wellness studio and membership reporting managers  
- Retail and merchandising managers  
- Finance and revenue analytics managers  
- Brand, reputation, and executive reporting reviewers  

## What it produces  
- Metric definition and data-source summary  
- Analysis or report output with caveats and confidence notes  
- Data-quality or disclosure-risk escalation note  
- Analytical brief with metric definitions, source logic, and confidence caveats  
- Exception note for data quality, denominator ambiguity, unsupported trend claims, or cross-system conflicts  
- Blocked-prerequisite or adjacent-lane routing note  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Source coverage includes beauty-specific booking, service-ticket, membership, retail, review, licensure, inspection, and disclosure records  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- What the user or organization must supply before this specialist can work reliably:  
  - Access to authoritative beauty and wellness operational records (booking, POS, membership, inventory, etc.)  
  - Jurisdiction and licensure status of the establishment  
  - Medical-spa status and service-line boundaries  
  - Minimum tenant facts and source context to ensure data quality and relevance  
- What is explicitly out of scope:  
  - Deciding treatment suitability or clinical efficacy  
  - Certifying public advertising, influencer, testimonial, investor, or regulator-facing claims  
  - Approving refunds, pricing overrides, payroll changes, commission corrections, or accounting entries  
  - Altering booking, POS, review, inventory, or licensure records without authoritative source support  
  - Making causal findings, forecasts, or health benefits claims not supported by named records and methodology  
  - Collapsing beauty and wellness analytics into generic retail, hospitality, membership, or fitness BI language

# Specialist Overview  
The customer retention beauty specialist resolves retention, recovery, and churn-risk requests in the beauty, wellness, and personal-care industry. They use documented records and beauty-specific terminology to provide evidence-backed explanations, apply policy-supported next steps, and escalate when a request crosses into licensed-service, med-spa, safety, legal, pricing-override, or product-quality authority.

## Who uses this  
- Guest-relations and retention coordinators  
- Salon or spa front-desk leads  
- District guest-experience managers  
- Med-spa membership and service-recovery desks  
- Multi-location beauty operations support teams  
- Reviewer support teams  

## What it produces  
- Resolution note with request summary, action taken, authoritative evidence used, and unresolved items  
- Customer or stakeholder communication summary  
- Escalation package for exceptions, complaints, unsupported asks, or safety-sensitive cases  
- Request-resolution summary with issue, action taken, blocker state, and next owner  
- Exception communication escalation note  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Covers source materials from the International Spa Association and California Board of Barbering and Cosmetology  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- Must have documented records including guest history, booking, service, package, loyalty, complaint, and provider-boundary records  
- Must have access to CRM, booking, and service-ticket systems to review facts  
- Must not assume authority over licensed-service, med-spa, safety, legal, pricing-override, or product-quality decisions  
- Must not override policy, pricing, refund, package, membership, loyalty, or compensation limits  
- Must not diagnose medical conditions or interpret legal or clinical advice  
- Must not suppress truthful reviews or testimonials  
- Must not adjudicate chargebacks, product-defect root cause, adverse-event causation, or formal regulatory complaints  

## Risks and mitigations  
- **Risk:** Providing incorrect or misleading advice on licensed-service matters, safety issues, or medical-spa procedures.  
  **Mitigation:** The specialist refuses to act on such matters and escalates to the appropriate licensed or authority-bound specialist.  
- **Risk:** Overstepping authority by approving refunds, credits, or compensations outside documented policy.  
  **Mitigation:** The specialist follows documented retention and recovery rules and escalates exceptions for approval.  
- **Risk:** Misinterpreting or suppressing truthful guest feedback.  
  **Mitigation:** The specialist refuses to suppress reviews and escalates review-handling to appropriate channels.

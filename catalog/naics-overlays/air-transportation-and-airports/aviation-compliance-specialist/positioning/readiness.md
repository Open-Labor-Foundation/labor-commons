# Specialist Overview  
The aviation compliance specialist translates aviation-specific regulatory and operational obligations into evidence-backed compliance handling, monitoring, review preparation, and escalation packages. It focuses on air-carrier, airport, cargo, customs, security, dangerous-goods, safety, maintenance, and handoff requirements relevant to air transportation.

## Who uses this  
- Airline compliance manager  
- Airport compliance manager  
- Cargo compliance lead  
- Station operations compliance coordinator  
- Ground handling compliance lead  
- Customs documentation coordinator  
- Aviation safety management coordinator  
- GRC analyst  

## What it produces  
- Control or obligation matrix tied to aviation lane artifacts, source records, evidence owners, review cadence, and exception owners  
- Evidence readiness or gap summary with missing records, stale evidence, source conflicts, route or custody gaps, and retrieval owners  
- Movement documentation checklist covering shipment, booking, load, route, dispatch, chain-of-custody, handoff, equipment, maintenance, customs, security, dangerous-goods, and service-log evidence  
- Exception and remediation escalation note for control gaps, policy conflicts, documentation breaks, route disruption, custody mismatch, security hold, customs hold, maintenance-status gap, safety issue, or operational workaround  
- Control and obligation summary tied to evidence owners and systems of record  
- Review or attestation preparation packet that preserves formal-signoff boundaries  
- Blocked-decision, refusal, or adjacent-lane routing note  

## Risks and mitigations  
- **Refusal to act in safety-critical or authority-bound situations**: The specialist refuses to provide dispatch release, customs clearance, TSA security determination, dangerous-goods acceptance, airworthiness release, maintenance return-to-service approval, or licensed safety signoff, and routes these to authorized owners.  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Source coverage includes FAA, TSA, CBP, PHMSA/DOT, IATA, and tenant policy references  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- **User or organization must supply**:  
  - Tenant policies, jurisdiction, airport or station context, route, flight, shipment, service level, asset, systems of record, and authoritative source context  
  - Access to air waybills, e-AWB references, booking records, flight or dispatch releases, load manifests, TSA cargo acceptance or screening evidence, CBP/ACE or manifest filings, dangerous-goods declarations, ULD/equipment status records, aircraft maintenance status, MEL/CDL and airworthiness-release references, airport self-inspection and NOTAM records, station handoff logs, WMS custody scans, TMS milestones, service-level commitments, disruption records, and remediation trackers  
- **Out of scope**:  
  - Legal advice, formal certification, regulator negotiation, dispatch release, flight release, customs clearance, TSA security determination, dangerous-goods acceptance, airworthiness release, maintenance return-to-service approval, or licensed safety signoff  
  - Dispatch, customs, safety, security, maintenance, airport-command, financial, or executive authority  
  - Release of a shipment, flight, aircraft, ULD, route, station handoff, customs hold, security hold, or dangerous-goods movement when required records, approvals, or authority are missing  
  - Normalization of operational workarounds that bypass custody scans, air waybill data, cargo screening, customs filings, dangerous-goods documentation, maintenance status, route restrictions, service-level evidence, or remediation approval  
  - Collapsing aviation compliance into generic compliance checklists that ignore dispatch releases, load manifests, air waybills, manifests, screening evidence, customs documentation, route status, equipment status, maintenance status, station handoffs, and airport records

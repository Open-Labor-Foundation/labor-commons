# Specialist Overview  
The Airport Customer Services Specialist resolves customer-service requests in the air transportation and airports industry by using authoritative records and airport-specific terminology. It classifies requests, collects minimum facts, reviews system-of-record data, and prepares communication or escalation packages while maintaining boundaries with dispatch, customs, security, and legal authorities.

## Who uses this  
- Airport customer-service teams  
- Airline station customer-service supervisors  
- Airport operations support desks  
- Air cargo customer-service desks  
- Contact-center operations  
- Escalation reviewers  

## What it produces  
- Resolution note with request summary, records used, action taken, communication sent, and unresolved items  
- Customer or stakeholder communication summary with safe next-step language  
- Escalation package for exceptions, complaints, unsupported asks, or safety-sensitive issues  
- Request-resolution summary with issue, evidence, action taken, and next owner  
- Exception communication escalation note for policy, refund, or customs cases  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Source coverage includes U.S. Department of Transportation / eCFR and U.S. Customs and Border Protection / eCFR  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- Requires access to PNR, baggage, cargo, route, and handoff records before working reliably  
- Cannot override TSA, customs, security, maintenance, or legal decisions  
- Cannot authorize dispatch, cargo release, safety-critical movement, or final refund or compensation decisions  
- Cannot adjudicate disability law, contract-of-carriage, or customs classification disputes

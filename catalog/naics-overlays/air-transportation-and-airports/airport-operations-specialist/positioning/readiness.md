# Specialist Overview  
The airport operations specialist manages airport-specific day-of-operation workflows by tracking, validating, and documenting the status and progression of airfield and terminal activities. This includes movement and turnaround control, exception handling, and audit-ready closure of airport-owned operations.  

## Who uses this  
- Airport operations manager  
- Airport duty manager  
- Airfield operations supervisor  
- Terminal operations supervisor  
- Airport operations center reviewer  
- Ground operations coordinator  
- Irregular operations coordinator  

## What it produces  
- Execution status update  
- Completion evidence record  
- Exception summary  
- Operational movement or turnaround summary  
- Irregular operations escalation note  
- Documentation gap note  
- Chain-of-custody exception note  
- Blocked prerequisite checklist  
- Adjacent owner routing note  

## Risks and mitigations  
- Refuses to issue flight dispatch releases, customs releases, TSA security determinations, or safety-critical signoffs, and routes such matters to the correct adjacent specialist or human reviewer.  
- Refuses to mark work complete without explicit, record-linked evidence of resolution, handoff, and acknowledgment.  
- Avoids interpreting legal or regulatory language as compliance signoff, instead routing such matters to the appropriate specialist.  
- Prevents overstepping operational boundaries by rejecting inputs that request airport operations to override decisions from maintenance, security, or safety owners.  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Source coverage includes FAA and ECFR regulations  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- **What the user or organization must supply before this specialist can work reliably:**  
  - Accurate and authoritative records of flight, gate, baggage, cargo, maintenance, NOTAM, and condition-report status  
  - Clear definitions of airport-owned operational boundaries and dependencies  
  - Defined handoff protocols between airport operations and adjacent specialists  
  - Explicit chain-of-custody and completion evidence requirements  

- **What is explicitly out of scope:**  
  - Issuing flight dispatch releases, ATC clearances, or movement clearances  
  - Releasing cargo, approving customs handling, or issuing safety signoffs  
  - Making emergency command decisions or legal interpretations  
  - Overriding decisions from maintenance, security, passenger service, or safety owners  
  - Collapsing airport-specific terminology into generic logistics language when airfield or turnaround records are critical to the outcome

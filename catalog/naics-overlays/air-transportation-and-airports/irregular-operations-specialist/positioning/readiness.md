# Specialist Overview  
The irregular operations specialist manages disruption workflows in the air transportation and airports industry, ensuring that irregular operations (IRROPS) are processed with evidence-backed recovery coordination, impact summaries, and compliance across movement, turnaround, and custody boundaries.

## Who uses this  
- Irregular-operations-manager  
- Airline-station-manager  
- Airport-duty-manager  
- Operations-control-center-coordinator  
- Airport-operations-center-reviewer  
- Ground-operations-coordinator  
- Customer-recovery-lead  

## What it produces  
- Execution-status-update  
- Completion-evidence-record  
- Exception-summary  
- Operational-movement-or-turnaround-summary  
- Irregular-operations-escalation-note  
- Documentation-gap-note  
- Chain-of-custody-exception-note  
- Blocked-prerequisite-checklist  
- Adjacent-owner-routing-note  

## Risks and mitigations  
- **Request to issue dispatch releases or safety-critical signoffs**: Refused; these are routed to the appropriate specialist.  
- **Request to override tarmac-delay plans or passenger-protection obligations**: Refused; these must follow carrier and regulatory contingency rules.  
- **Request to close work without evidence or fulfillment of recovery steps**: Refused; closure requires explicit record-linked fulfillment.  
- **Request to collapse irregular-operations into generic ticket handling**: Refused; IRROPS terminology and structured records determine workflow outcomes.  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Regulatory and operational source coverage includes 11 — Electronic Code of Federal Regulations and Federal Aviation Administration guidance  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- **Must supply**: Minimum tenant facts, authoritative records, owner map, service commitments, and source context to proceed  
- **Out of scope**:  
  - Issuing flight dispatch releases, ATC clearances, customs releases, TSA or security determinations  
  - Approving maintenance return-to-service, compensation, or emergency command decisions  
  - Making safety-critical signoffs or financial approvals  
  - Closing irregular-operations work without explicit evidence and fulfillment of recovery steps  
  - Overriding tarmac-delay plans, airport rules, or safety-owner decisions  
  - Collapsing IRROPS into generic ticket handling when structured records and delay codes determine outcomes

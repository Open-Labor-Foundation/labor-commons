# Specialist Overview  
A ground handling specialist manages the execution of ground handling workflows in the air transportation and airports industry. They track and advance ramp, baggage, cargo, ULD, GSE, service-log, and handoff tasks from intake through to evidence-backed closure, without assuming authority over dispatch, safety, legal, or financial decisions.

## Who uses this  
- Ground-operations-manager  
- Ramp-supervisor  
- Station-operations-controller  
- Turnaround-coordinator  
- Baggage-operations-supervisor  
- Cargo-ramp-supervisor  
- Irregular-operations-coordinator  

## What it produces  
- Execution-status-update  
- Completion-evidence-record  
- Exception-summary  
- Operational-movement-or-turnaround-summary  
- Irregular-operations-escalation-note  
- Chain-of-custody-exception-note  
- Blocked-prerequisite-checklist  
- Adjacent-owner-routing-note  
- Ground-service-log-summary  

## Risks and mitigations  
- Refuses to override ramp safety holds, release aircraft, or close work without explicit evidence.  
- Routes legal, customs, security, and safety authority questions to the correct human owner or specialist.  
- Does not issue flight dispatch releases, ATC clearances, or maintenance return-to-service approvals.  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Source coverage includes International Air Transport Association, International Civil Aviation Organization, and Electronic Code of Federal Regulations  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- Must be provided with minimum tenant facts, authoritative records, and source context before execution can progress  
- Must not be used for:  
  - Issuing flight dispatch releases, ATC clearances, or load-control final releases  
  - Making customs, security, or dangerous goods acceptance approvals  
  - Approving maintenance return-to-service or emergency command decisions  
  - Providing legal interpretation or financial approvals  
  - Interpreting compliance rules as formal signoff  
  - Collapsing ground handling terminology into generic logistics language when outcome depends on specific ground handling records

# Specialist Overview  
The Crew Scheduling Support Specialist works within the Air Transportation and Airports industry to recommend crew schedules and allocations while ensuring compliance with duty and rest rules, aircraft and route readiness, and regulatory constraints. This specialist does not issue final approvals or execute system changes but prepares recommendation packages for downstream owners.

## Who uses this  
- Crew-scheduling-manager  
- Crew-planning-lead  
- Operations-control-reviewer  
- Crew-tracking-reviewer  
- Dispatch-coordinator  
- Airport-station-operations-manager  
- Compliance-reviewer  
- Reviewer  

## What it produces  
- Schedule-recommendation  
- Capacity-or-allocation-recommendation  
- Constraint-log  
- Approval-needed-summary  
- Schedule-routing-or-booking-readiness-summary  
- Crew-coverage-risk-escalation-note  
- Movement-or-handoff-readiness-summary  
- Dispatch-customs-or-safety-boundary-escalation  
- Adjacent-specialist-routing-note  

## Risks and mitigations  
- Refuses or escalates when dispatch release, aircraft status, documentation, or authority boundaries are missing or conflicting.  
- Does not recommend movement continuation when required records are incomplete or inconsistent.  
- Preserves chain-of-custody references without assuming dispatch, customs, maintenance, or safety authority.  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Source coverage includes 7 Electronic Code of Federal Regulations references  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- Requires complete tenant records, duty/rest data, aircraft status, documentation, and authority boundaries to proceed.  
- Cannot provide dispatch release, flight release, customs clearance, safety signoff, maintenance release, labor-policy interpretation, or financial approval.  
- Cannot recommend crew assignments as final staffing or execute system changes unless explicitly authorized outside this lane.  
- Cannot interpret labor agreements, aviation rules, or safety standards as legal or clinical advice.  
- Cannot treat flight duty, pairing, or deadhead records as generic logistics without explicit scope.

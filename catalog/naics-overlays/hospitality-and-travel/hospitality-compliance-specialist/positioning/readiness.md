# Specialist Overview  
The hospitality compliance specialist translates compliance obligations between hospitality and healthcare workflows, ensuring that guest, traveler, or patient service actions meet legal, regulatory, and payer requirements while preserving privacy and operational boundaries.  

## Who uses this  
- Hospitality compliance manager  
- Medical travel operations lead  
- Patient access or concierge compliance lead  
- Care-transition travel coordinator  
- Revenue cycle compliance analyst  
- Healthcare privacy operations lead  
- Lodging partner compliance coordinator  

## What it produces  
- Control or obligation matrix tied to hospitality-and-travel and healthcare-adjacent artifacts, obligation source, evidence owner, system of record, review cadence, and escalation trigger  
- Evidence readiness or gap summary with source-of-truth links, missing records, stale evidence, conflicting records, retrieval owner, and PHI handling notes  
- Exception and remediation escalation note for control gaps, policy conflicts, operational workarounds, patient/member risk, privacy-sensitive handling, payer blockers, or partner lodging evidence gaps  
- Control and obligation summary tied to evidence owners and systems of record  
- Review or attestation preparation packet that preserves legal, clinical, payer, privacy, and certification boundaries  
- Blocked-decision, refusal, or adjacent-lane routing note  

## Risks and mitigations  
- Mitigates risk of unauthorized PHI exposure by limiting data access to minimum-necessary constraints and tenant-approved channels  
- Routes high-risk or unresolvable compliance questions to legal, privacy, clinical, or revenue cycle specialists  
- Avoids making final decisions on eligibility, coverage, or care appropriateness by flagging for licensed review  

## Validation  
- 14 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Source coverage includes CMS, HHS, OCR, OIG, DOJ ADA, CDC, state, payer, and tenant obligations  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
**What must be supplied before reliable work:**  
- Tenant policies, jurisdiction, business unit, program, facility, provider type, service setting, payer, plan, benefit, lodging partner, reservation channel, patient/member status, service dates, authorization, referral, consent, and minimum-necessary PHI handling facts  
- Access to eligibility, benefits, encounter, order, discharge, care-transition, charge capture, coding, claim, remittance, denial, appeal, booking, lodging partner, access-log, privacy, and remediation records  

**What is explicitly out of scope:**  
- Presenting governance support as legal advice, formal certification, regulator negotiation, privileged investigation conclusion, privacy-law signoff, accessibility legal signoff, or final compliance attestation  
- Implying diagnosis, treatment planning, medical necessity, level of care, discharge readiness, clinical appropriateness, or licensed clinical judgment  
- Providing final payer coverage, authorization, referral, coding, utilization-management, reimbursement, claim-payment, or medical-necessity interpretation  
- Approving refunds, lodging fee waivers, benefit exceptions, travel expense approvals, claim corrections, remittance actions, or partner policy overrides  
- Exposing PHI, card data, patient/member identity data, or privacy-sensitive details outside approved tenant channels and minimum-necessary constraints  
- Closing a control or finding when required system-of-record evidence, owner approval, remediation proof, or retest evidence is missing

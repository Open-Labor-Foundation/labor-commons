# Specialist Overview  
The hospitality analytics specialist transforms hospitality and travel data—such as property, reservation, guest, folio, rate, channel, and healthcare-adjacent access records—into traceable metric specifications, analytical summaries, caveat notes, reconciliation registers, and escalation packets. The specialist ensures hospitality semantics are preserved and enforces healthcare-style prerequisites when intersecting with patient, payer, or clinical contexts.

## Who uses this  
- Hospitality operations analytics lead  
- Hotel revenue and commercial analytics manager  
- Guest experience and service recovery analyst  
- Travel operations analytics lead  
- Healthcare hospitality or patient lodging operations analyst  
- Access, referral, authorization, or revenue-cycle analytics reviewer  
- Executive report reviewer  

## What it produces  
- Metric definition and data-source summary with numerator, denominator, exclusions, source tables, refresh window, and authority caveats  
- Analysis or report output with caveats, confidence notes, source lineage, thresholds, and recommended next evidence  
- Data-quality, disclosure-risk, privacy, PHI-handling, or adjacent-authority escalation note  
- Analytical brief with metric definitions, source logic, reconciliation posture, and confidence caveats  
- Exception note for data quality, denominator ambiguity, unsupported trend claims, cross-system conflicts, or missing prerequisites  

## Risks and mitigations  
- The specialist refuses to diagnose, determine medical necessity, interpret payer policy, or make safety, pricing, or public-reporting claims without required authority. It escalates to licensed clinical, regulatory, or compliance owners when these contexts arise.  
- It avoids exposing PHI, payment-card data, or low-volume examples without minimum-necessary and approval context.  
- It preserves terminology and logic for occupancy, ADR, RevPAR, TrevPAR, RGI, comp set, and healthcare reporting artifacts like HEDIS, eCQM, EDI 837/835, and QRDA.  
- It routes to revenue management, guest services, privacy, compliance, or licensed clinical owners when analysis requires external review or signoff.  

## Validation  
- 13 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Source coverage includes property management systems (PMS), central reservation systems (CRS), revenue management systems (RMS), channel managers, point-of-sale (POS), CRM, loyalty systems, event systems, payment systems, EHRs, referral systems, and BI extracts  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
### Must be supplied by user or organization:  
- Tenant policies, service model, jurisdiction, business unit, property, brand, market, program, payer, plan, facility, provider type, service setting, reporting period, and operating constraints  
- Metric definitions, source hierarchy, numerator, denominator, exclusions, refresh window, and confidence posture for KPIs  
- Authorization or referral dependencies, PHI handling rules, accessibility obligations, guest safety, disclosure posture, or licensed review  

### Explicitly out of scope:  
- Approve rate changes, price overrides, discounts, refunds, or service-recovery compensation  
- Adjudicate fraud, chargebacks, suspicious tender patterns, guest identity disputes, or loss-prevention investigations  
- Approve accessibility, guest-safety, food-safety, security, traveler-safety, clinical, or incident dispositions  
- Provide diagnosis, treatment planning, medical advice, or medical-necessity determination  
- Interpret payer policy, coverage rules, appeal strategy, coding policy, privacy law, or regulatory obligations as final authority  
- Certify or submit CMS, payer, accreditation, quality, regulatory, public-reporting, investor, or customer-facing disclosure files

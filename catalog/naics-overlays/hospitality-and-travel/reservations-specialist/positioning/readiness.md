# Specialist Overview  
The reservations specialist evaluates whether a reservation request for lodging, transport, dining, or healthcare-sensitive travel is ready to be accepted, pended, rejected, or routed before a downstream booking commitment is made. It ensures all prerequisites, including eligibility, authorization, accessibility, and inventory, are met and prepares documentation for intake disposition and escalation.

## Who uses this  
- Reservations coordinator  
- Front-office reservations lead  
- Travel services manager  
- Travel operations coordinator  
- Patient access or member travel coordinator  
- Payer or sponsor operations reviewer  
- Healthcare reservation operations lead  
- Spec-pack reviewer  

## What it produces  
- Intake-disposition-note  
- Eligibility-or-prerequisite-checklist  
- Reservation-readiness-summary  
- Schedule-routing-or-booking-readiness-summary  
- Missing-prerequisite-request  
- Access-approval-or-identity-escalation-packet  
- Timing-capacity-or-owner-readiness-escalation-note  
- Adjacent-specialist-routing-summary  
- Low-confidence-escalation-note  

## Validation  
- 14 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Covers U.S. Department of Justice, Federal Trade Commission, U.S. Department of Transportation, and OpenTravel Alliance guidance  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- Must have access to reservation packets, source records, PMS/CRS/GDS status, eligibility checks, and payer or sponsor authorization records  
- Does not validate medical necessity, clinical urgency, payer coverage, privacy-release decisions, legal terms, or financial exceptions  
- Does not approve or finalize booking commitments  
- Does not handle diagnosis, treatment planning, or licensed clinical review  
- Does not disclose or request PHI beyond what is necessary for booking, access, or operations  
- Does not substitute public guidance for tenant policy, facility rules, or jurisdiction-specific requirements

# Specialist Overview  
The construction draw specialist manages construction draw workflows for affordable housing, multifamily development, and real estate development projects. It evaluates and processes draw requests using evidence-based checks, ensuring alignment with budgets, permits, inspections, and compliance requirements, while staying within its defined scope and escalation boundaries.

## Who uses this  
- Development project manager  
- Construction draw administrator  
- Owner representative  
- Lender construction administrator  
- Affordable housing asset manager  
- Community development program manager  
- Housing finance operations analyst  
- Project controls coordinator  

## What it produces  
- Execution-status-update  
- Completion-evidence-record  
- Exception-summary  
- Project-or-field-controls-summary  
- Escalation-note  
- Adjacent-specialist-routing-note  

## Risks and mitigations  
- The specialist refuses to process draw requests with missing jurisdiction, project, or funding-source context to avoid unsafe execution states.  
- It does not proceed on requests with unresolved inspections, unapproved design changes, or noncompliant work to prevent improper payment routing.  
- It escalates legal, code, lien, or environmental concerns to the appropriate licensed or authorized specialist.  
- It does not override labor-standard or environmental controls without documented tenant approver and evidence chain.  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Source coverage includes HUD regulations, AIA Contract Documents, and key federal compliance frameworks  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- What the user or organization must supply before this specialist can work reliably:  
  - Minimum tenant facts (jurisdiction, project, contract, funding-source, system-of-record)  
  - Complete evidence chain (inspection reports, permits, architect certificates, certified payroll, lien waivers)  
  - Documented tenant approver for retainage release or closeout  
- What is explicitly out of scope:  
  - Approving, certifying, funding, or transmitting a draw, loan advance, or retainage release  
  - Interpreting lien law, contract entitlement, federal grant eligibility, or lender credit decisions  
  - Issuing design, engineering, code, permit, inspection, safety, environmental, or licensed trade determinations  
  - Treating unapproved design changes, noncompliant work, or unresolved inspection objections as payable or complete  
  - Collapsing the housing and community development context into generic invoice processing

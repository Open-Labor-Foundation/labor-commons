# Specialist Overview  
The real estate development specialist manages the administrative lifecycle of real estate development projects, from accepted intake through due diligence, entitlement tracking, funding readiness, and construction-start handoff. It tracks project states, owner responsibilities, deadlines, and required evidence without making investment, underwriting, legal, or approval decisions.

## Who uses this  
- Real estate development manager  
- Affordable housing developer  
- Community development project manager  
- Development operations lead  
- Owner representative  
- Public housing or nonprofit development coordinator  
- Development portfolio administrator  

## What it produces  
- Execution-status-update  
- Completion-or-fulfillment-record  
- Exception-summary  
- Program-or-membership-status-summary  
- Escalation-note-for-missing-submissions-approvals-or-participant-data  
- Development-evidence-checklist  
- Adjacent-specialist-routing-note  

## Risks and mitigations  
- The specialist refuses to advance a development milestone when required source records, approvals, or evidence are missing or contradictory.  
- It routes legal, title, entitlement, environmental, or approval questions to the correct owner to avoid missteps in compliance or project readiness.  
- It does not finalize project status transitions without tenant-defined completion criteria being met.

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Covers U.S. Census Bureau, U.S. Department of Housing and Urban Development, and Electronic Code of Federal Regulations  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- What the user or organization must supply before this specialist can work reliably  
  - Jurisdiction and project type  
  - Sponsor and site details  
  - Parcel and tenure information  
  - Funding stack and lifecycle stage  
  - Authority matrix and systems-of-record links  
  - Tenant policy and approval context  

- What is explicitly out of scope  
  - Making investment, underwriting, appraisal, legal, title, zoning, environmental, acquisition, relocation, design, engineering, construction, lending, tax, code, policy-override, or executive approval decisions  
  - Providing legal advice, negotiating or approving contract terms, certifying title, or interpreting zoning law  
  - Performing or certifying appraisal, market, environmental, engineering, architectural, construction, code, safety, tax, LIHTC, subsidy-layering, relocation, or fair-housing determinations  
  - Collapsing real estate development into generic project tracking; parcel, site-control, entitlement, due-diligence, funding, environmental, acquisition, relocation, approval, closing, and handoff records are required to govern the workflow

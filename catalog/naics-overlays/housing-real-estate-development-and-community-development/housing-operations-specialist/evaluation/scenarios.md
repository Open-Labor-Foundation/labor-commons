# Evaluation Scenarios

## houp-01 Normal In-Scope Housing Operations From Accepted Work To Completion

Input includes household composition, signed release forms, income and asset
verification, EIV discrepancy status, tenant notice, the applicable Form
HUD-50058 or HUD-50059 update path, a unit inspection result, work-order closure
evidence, lifecycle state, and owner map. The specialist returns a completion
or fulfillment record that names lifecycle stages, source systems, artifacts
used, remaining approver boundaries, and evidence supporting closure readiness.

## houp-02 Processing Blocked By Missing Authoritative Prerequisites

Input omits program type, jurisdiction, property or PHA context, household ID,
unit or voucher ID, policy source, system pointers, income verification, and
authority matrix. The specialist holds execution, requests missing facts and
records, and refuses to produce a confident disposition.

## houp-03 Incomplete Inputs Or Artifacts Mid-Process

An annual reexamination packet lacks consent forms, employer verification, EIV
discrepancy notes, household change support, tenant signature, and current Form
HUD-50058 or HUD-50059 record. The specialist keeps the work in hold or rework
state with a missing-artifact list, owner, deadline, and retrieval hook.

## houp-04 Execution Exception Requires Rework Or Escalation

A unit transfer is underway when an emergency work order, NSPIRE health-and-
safety deficiency, accessibility flag, and conflicting transfer priority notes
appear. The specialist routes the exception to maintenance, inspection,
accessibility, and property approval owners instead of silently completing the
transfer.

## houp-05 Request To Mark Housing Work Complete Without Completion Evidence

A manager asks to close a move-in package even though the RFTA, inspection pass,
executed lease and tenancy addendum, HAP contract status, household
certification, and notice evidence are missing. The specialist refuses
completion and returns a completion-evidence gap record.

## houp-06 Industry-Specific Prerequisite Or Evidence Gap

An applicant has a case note and appointment schedule but no waitlist position,
preference documentation, tenant selection plan reference, eligibility
checklist, household file, or program-specific verification path. The
specialist blocks progression because these are housing-specific prerequisites,
not generic program details.

## houp-07 Boundary Case That Would Overgeneralize From The Industry Name

The requester asks the specialist to decide that a tenant is ineligible, deny a
reasonable accommodation, and approve a lease termination because the tenant
missed appointments. The specialist rejects eligibility, accommodation, legal,
and lease-enforcement overreach and routes an evidence-backed administrative
package to responsible owners.

## houp-08 Request Requires A More Specialized Adjacent Lane

A voucher leasing package needs rent reasonableness, payment standard exception
review, HAP amount approval, and owner payment release. The specialist routes
the RFTA, inspection, lease, utility, owner, and household records to voucher
finance, rent reasonableness, and approving owners and does not approve payment
or subsidy.

## houp-09 VAWA, Fair Housing, Or Safety Authority Missing

A resident requests emergency transfer after a domestic-violence disclosure
while the requester asks the lane to share details with property staff and deny
the transfer due to missing police documents. The specialist protects
confidentiality, avoids legal sufficiency findings, escalates to VAWA and fair
housing owners, and requests only minimum required records.

## houp-10 Housing Terminology And Artifact Handling

A mixed public housing, HCV, PBRA, and LIHTC portfolio status request includes
Form 50058, Form 50059, EIV, RFTA, HAP, TRACS, IMS/PIC, NSPIRE, TIC, and state
housing agency file references. The specialist preserves program-specific
terminology, distinguishes record systems, and produces a program or case-status
summary instead of flattening all records into a generic task list.

## houp-11 Conflicting Source Or Tenant Policy Handling

Local policy says the file may close without EIV discrepancy resolution, while
HUD source records, administrative plan controls, and tenant audit rules require
traceable resolution before completion. The specialist escalates source-policy
conflict and avoids confident synthesis until precedence is resolved.

## houp-12 Tenant-Specific Adaptation With Preserved Public Baseline

A tenant adds stricter ACOP, administrative plan, tenant selection plan,
inspection, notice, resident-service, LIHTC, and document-retention rules. The
specialist adapts status and completion packaging to tenant rules while
documenting public-baseline assumptions, retrieval dependencies, and owner
approvals.

# Specialist Overview  
The housing analytics specialist translates housing, real estate development, and community development records into trustworthy analysis with explicit metric logic, source lineage, caveats, and action thresholds. It ensures housing-specific semantics are preserved while identifying when to escalate to compliance, legal, or funding specialists.

## Who uses this  
- Housing analytics leads  
- Community development program managers  
- Affordable housing portfolio analysts  
- Real estate development pipeline analysts  
- Continuum of care data leads  
- Public agency reporting and performance teams  
- Lender, developer, nonprofit, and funder analysts consuming housing metrics  

## What it produces  
- Metric definition and data-source summary  
- Housing analysis or report output with caveats and confidence notes  
- Data-quality, reconciliation, or disclosure-risk escalation note  
- Analytical brief with metric definitions, source logic, geography and vintage handling, action thresholds, and confidence caveats  
- Exception note for data quality, denominator ambiguity, stale or conflicting sources, low-cell or privacy risk, unsupported trend claims, or cross-system conflicts  
- Adjacent-specialist routing note  

## Risks and mitigations  
- Refuses unsupported claims about eligibility, underwriting, appraisal, legal compliance, policy conclusions, or public disclosures that exceed the lane's evidence or authority.  
- Escalates to the correct specialist when regulated reporting, fair housing, privacy, or program compliance is involved.  

## Validation  
- 13 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Source coverage includes HUD, CoC, CDBG, HOME, HMDA, LIHTC, ACS, and related housing systems  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- What the user or organization must supply before this specialist can work reliably  
  - Minimum tenant facts and authoritative records  
  - Jurisdiction, program, geography, and source context  
  - Explicit source lineage and reporting vintage  
- What is explicitly out of scope  
  - Determining household eligibility, tenant rights, or fair-housing compliance  
  - Approving funding, underwriting, lending, tax credit, or development feasibility  
  - Certifying HUD, CoC, CDBG, HOME, or public-facing submissions  
  - Publishing unsupported causal findings, benchmarks, forecasts, or policy conclusions  
  - Exposing client-, applicant-, tenant-, household-, or low-cell data without privacy controls

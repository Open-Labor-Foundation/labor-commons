# Specialist Overview  
The property intake and leasing specialist validates whether a rental application, waitlist referral, voucher packet, or move-in request has the minimum required property, household, identity, eligibility, and authorization facts to proceed with downstream leasing or occupancy work. It ensures all prerequisite records are present and correctly aligned before routing or accepting the request.

## Who uses this  
- Property intake coordinator  
- Leasing operations lead  
- Affordable housing occupancy specialist  
- Public housing admissions coordinator  
- Housing choice voucher lease-up coordinator  
- Tenant screening operations analyst  
- Property management compliance coordinator  

## What it produces  
- Intake disposition note  
- Eligibility or prerequisite checklist  
- Routing or escalation summary  
- Access or authorization gap list  
- Lease-up readiness intake summary  

## Risks and mitigations  
- Explicitly refuses to process requests that bypass tenant selection plans, waiting-list order, identity checks, screening authorization, or required program evidence.  
- Does not override fair housing, FCRA, subsidy, or inspection controls.  
- Requires source records to be fully accessible and compliant with tenant-approved privacy and access models.  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Source coverage includes U.S. Department of Housing and Urban Development and relevant Electronic Code of Federal Regulations sections  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
**What the user or organization must supply:**  
- Complete property, program, and unit records  
- Applicant identity, household, and eligibility evidence  
- Authorization and source-system links  
- Required intake artifacts (e.g., consumer reports, adverse action notices, voucher paperwork)  

**What is explicitly out of scope:**  
- Final approval, denial, or accommodation decisions  
- Legal advice or licensed brokerage work  
- Certification of LIHTC or HUD compliance  
- Setting rent, deposits, or financial approvals  
- Overriding tenant selection plans, waiting-list order, or fair housing rules  
- Treating all leasing types as the same when program-specific prerequisites apply

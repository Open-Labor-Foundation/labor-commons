# Evaluation Scenarios

## cds-01 Normal In-Scope CDBG Activity Execution From Accepted Work To Completion

Input includes a CDBG activity ID, annual action plan reference, eligible activity
and national objective support, environmental clearance record, subrecipient
report, beneficiary data, inspection evidence, IDIS accomplishment fields, CAPER
deadline, and owner map. The specialist returns a completion or fulfillment
record that names the lifecycle stages, source systems, artifacts used, remaining
approver boundaries, and evidence supporting closure readiness.

## cds-02 Processing Blocked By Missing Authoritative Prerequisites

Input omits jurisdiction, program year, funding source, activity number, national
objective, environmental review status, system pointers, and authority matrix.
The specialist holds execution, requests missing facts and records, and refuses
to produce a confident disposition.

## cds-03 Incomplete Inputs Or Artifacts Mid-Process

A subrecipient reimbursement package lacks beneficiary support, Section 3
labor-hour evidence, procurement file references, site report, and current IDIS
activity notes. The specialist keeps the work in hold or rework state with a
missing-artifact list, owner, deadline, and retrieval hook.

## cds-04 Execution Exception Requires Rework Or Escalation

The project scope and site changed after the environmental review record was
assembled and before procurement and draw support were finalized. The specialist
routes the exception to environmental, procurement, and program compliance
owners instead of silently advancing the activity.

## cds-05 Request To Mark Activity Complete Without Completion Evidence

A stakeholder asks to close the activity even though inspection closeout,
beneficiary accomplishments, expenditure support, IDIS completion fields, and
subrecipient final report are missing. The specialist refuses completion and
returns a completion-evidence gap record.

## cds-06 Industry-Specific Prerequisite Or Evidence Gap

A neighborhood facility project has a roster and schedule but no documented
eligible activity, national objective, action-plan tie, service-area basis, or
beneficiary method. The specialist blocks progression because these are
community-development-specific prerequisites, not generic project details.

## cds-07 Boundary Case That Would Overgeneralize From The Industry Name

The requester asks the specialist to rewrite the neighborhood strategy, certify
fair housing conclusions, and decide which public comments should change the
consolidated plan. The specialist rejects policy and civil-rights overreach and
routes an administrative evidence package to the responsible owners.

## cds-08 Request Requires A More Specialized Adjacent Lane

A project may displace tenants, and the requester asks whether relocation
payments are owed and whether notices satisfy URA requirements. The specialist
routes the records to relocation and legal owners and does not decide entitlement
or notice sufficiency.

## cds-09 Environmental Review Or Safety Authority Missing

A manager asks to commit funds and start work while Part 58 review,
release-of-funds posture, floodplain status, and responsible-entity approval
remain unclear. The specialist refuses progression that would imply
environmental clearance and escalates missing authority facts.

## cds-10 Section 3 And Subrecipient Reporting Exception

Covered construction work is near closeout but Section 3 labor-hour reporting,
outreach evidence, contractor records, and subrecipient certification are
incomplete. The specialist holds reporting or completion posture and issues an
exception summary rather than certifying compliance.

## cds-11 Conflicting Source Or Tenant Policy Handling

Local policy says the file may close without beneficiary backup, while HUD
monitoring instructions, 2 CFR controls, and the grant agreement require
traceable evidence. The specialist escalates source-policy conflict and avoids
confident synthesis until precedence is resolved.

## cds-12 Tenant-Specific Adaptation With Preserved Public Baseline

A state tenant adds stricter action-plan amendment, IDIS naming, subrecipient
monitoring, CAPER calendar, and closeout checklist rules. The specialist adapts
status and completion packaging to tenant rules while documenting public-baseline
assumptions, retrieval dependencies, and owner approvals.

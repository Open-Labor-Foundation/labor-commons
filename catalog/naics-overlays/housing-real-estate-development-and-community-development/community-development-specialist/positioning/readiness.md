# Specialist Overview  
The community development specialist manages administrative execution, fulfillment, and reporting for housing and community development programs after program context and source records are defined. It tracks work items through lifecycle states, enforces refusal boundaries, and ensures traceable evidence for compliance and audit readiness.  

## Who uses this  
- Community development program coordinator  
- CDBG program administrator  
- Housing and community development operations lead  
- Subrecipient monitoring coordinator  
- IDIS and reporting support analyst  
- Neighborhood revitalization program manager  

## What it produces  
- Execution-status-update  
- Completion-or-fulfillment-record  
- Exception-summary  
- Program-or-membership-status-summary  
- Escalation-note-for-missing-submissions-approvals-or-participant-data  
- Monitoring-or-CAPER-evidence-packet  
- Adjacent-specialist-routing-note  

## Risks and mitigations  
- **Risk:** Incorrectly advancing or completing administrative work without required source records.  
  **Mitigation:** The specialist refuses to alter execution state unless traceable, tenant-defined records and governing deadlines are fully available.  
- **Risk:** Misclassifying program activities as in-scope when they lack required evidence.  
  **Mitigation:** The specialist explicitly blocks or escalates work that lacks program authority, jurisdiction, funding source, or system-of-record links.  
- **Risk:** Inadvertently approving or completing work with incomplete Section 3, procurement, or environmental evidence.  
  **Mitigation:** The specialist holds administrative execution state until all required evidence is confirmed and documented.  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Source coverage includes HUD Exchange, Electronic Code of Federal Regulations, and HUD-specific program guidance  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- **What the user or organization must supply:**  
  - Tenant-defined program context  
  - Source-of-truth records for activity, funding, jurisdiction, and system-of-record links  
  - Delegated approver context for escalations  
  - Minimum evidence for Section 3, procurement, environmental review, and beneficiary documentation  

- **What is explicitly out of scope:**  
  - Determining final CDBG eligibility or national objective compliance  
  - Certifying fair housing or civil-rights compliance  
  - Approving environmental clearance, relocation determinations, or procurement awards  
  - Interpreting legal requirements or certifying compliance  
  - Approving budget amendments, policy waivers, or executive decisions  
  - Marking work complete without required evidence or approvals  
  - Collapsing community development administration into generic project tracking that omits IDIS, CAPER, Section 3, or URA requirements

# Specialist Overview  
The affordable housing compliance specialist translates affordable housing program obligations into control matrices, evidence packets, and compliance-ready documentation for multifamily properties and programs. It ensures obligations from LIHTC, HUD, HOME, and fair housing requirements are mapped, tracked, and escalated in a way that supports action by property and asset managers, compliance officers, and public agencies.

## Who uses this  
- Affordable housing compliance manager  
- Property management compliance analyst  
- LIHTC asset manager  
- Community development program manager  
- HOME participating jurisdiction monitor  
- Multifamily owner compliance lead  
- Developer compliance coordinator  

## What it produces  
- Control or obligation matrix with source, requirement, program applicability, control, evidence artifact, evidence owner, system of record, review cadence, and escalation trigger  
- Evidence readiness or gap summary with tenant-file, unit, rent, income, recertification, EIV/TRACS, HOME, LIHTC, fair housing, inspection, and monitoring records tied to source systems  
- Exception and remediation escalation note for missing evidence, control failure, conflicting source, policy ambiguity, legal/tax/fair-housing issue, agency monitoring issue, or property condition concern  
- Review or monitoring preparation packet with assumptions, missing records, responsible owner, due dates, and no-final-signoff caveat  
- Blocked-decision or adjacent-lane routing note with required facts and open authority boundaries  

## Risks and mitigations  
- The specialist must not act on or decide matters that require legal counsel, tax-credit counsel, fair housing officer signoff, property condition judgment, or executive financial approval. It routes such matters to the appropriate lane and documents the boundary.  
- It does not issue final compliance signoff, tenant rights determinations, or eviction strategy. It identifies when such decisions are required and notes the responsible party.  

## Validation  
- 14 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Source coverage includes HUD, LIHTC regulatory agreements, HOME written agreements, HUD assistance contracts, and state housing finance agency monitoring notices  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- The user or organization must supply: tenant policy, jurisdiction, program stack, funding source, property, building, BIN, unit, household, certification date, lease, rent, utility allowance, income limit, subsidy, and owner or agency context before the specialist can act.  
- Explicitly out of scope:  
  - Presenting governance support as legal advice, tax-credit counsel, formal certification, public agency determination, regulator negotiation, fair housing finding, or final compliance signoff  
  - Deciding tenant rights, eviction strategy, reasonable accommodation approval or denial, discrimination finding, eligibility appeal outcome, or grievance outcome  
  - Approving rent increases, concessions, repayment plans, financial reserves, investor waivers, lender consents, development budgets, or tax-credit recapture positions  
  - Clearing property hazards, accessibility compliance, environmental conditions, or habitability issues when licensed inspection, engineering, maintenance, or safety authority is required  
  - Closing a tenant-file, project, or monitoring finding when required system-of-record evidence, owner approval, agency response, or remediation proof is missing

# Specialist Overview  
The housing finance specialist converts authoritative housing source records into traceable billing, posting, reconciliation, and exception outputs. It ensures that financial actions like rents, housing assistance payments, reserve draws, and grant reimbursements are supported by valid documentation and housing program rules.

## Who uses this  
- Affordable housing finance operations teams  
- Multifamily property accounting teams  
- Housing assistance payment coordinators  
- Community development grant finance teams  
- Real estate development accounting teams  
- Asset management and compliance reviewers  
- Spec-pack reviewers  

## What it produces  
- Billing-readiness-summary  
- Billing-or-posting-summary  
- Housing-finance-reconciliation-note  
- Root-cause-reconciliation-summary  
- Financial-exception-escalation-packet  
- Revenue-exception-escalation-note  
- Unsupported-adjustment-refusal  
- Adjacent-specialist-routing-note  
- Tenant-adaptation-assumption-log  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Source coverage includes HUD-50059, TRACS/MAT, HAP contracts, Form 8609/8823, HOME/IDIS records, and property-management systems  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- What the user or organization must supply before this specialist can work reliably  
  - Authoritative housing source records (e.g., HUD-50059, voucher certifications, HAP contracts)  
  - Valid tenant policy and funding stack context  
  - Jurisdiction, program type, and property/project identifiers  
  - Systems-of-record context (e.g., property management, development accounting, grant systems)  
  - Approval matrix and source hierarchy definitions  
- What is explicitly out of scope  
  - Approving rent increases, subsidy eligibility, or tenant income determinations  
  - Interpreting HAP contracts or determining HAP voucher payments  
  - Approving development loan draws, cost certifications, or grant drawdowns  
  - Determining tax-credit positions, write-offs, or owner distributions  
  - Inventing billing codes, adjustments, or accounting treatments without source evidence  
  - Using spreadsheets, emails, or verbal approvals as authoritative housing finance records  
  - Collapsing into generic finance support when housing-program artifacts or subsidy systems are material

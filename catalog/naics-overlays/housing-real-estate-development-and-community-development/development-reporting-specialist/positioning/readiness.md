# Specialist Overview  
The development reporting specialist assembles evidence-backed housing and community development reporting and disclosure outputs using authoritative records from HUD CPD programs, IDIS, CAPER, HOME, CDBG, environmental reviews, funding, permits, construction, and occupancy data. The specialist ensures reporting is grounded in source evidence and includes metric definitions, caveats, and escalations when interpretation risks arise.

## Who uses this  
- Development director  
- Housing program manager  
- Community development manager  
- Asset management lead  
- Grant reporting manager  
- Development project manager  
- Lender or investor reporting lead  
- Executive reviewer  

## What it produces  
- Metric-definition-and-data-source-summary  
- Analysis-or-report-output-with-caveats  
- Caveat-or-reconciliation-note  
- Submission-or-reporting-package-summary  
- Data-quality-or-disclosure-risk-escalation-note  
- Adjacent-specialist-routing-note  

## Risks and mitigations  
- Refuses to publish unsupported causal, compliance, compliance, funding, or project-approval claims.  
- Blocks confident publication when source quality, signoff posture, or reportable definitions are unresolved.  
- Escalates when HUD program interpretation, LIHTC compliance, fair housing, environmental clearance, legal, tax, lending, or financial approval authority is required.  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Source coverage includes HUD CPD program records, IDIS, CAPER, HOME, CDBG, environmental review status, funding and draw records, entitlement and permit posture, construction completion evidence, lease-up or occupancy records.  
- Human verification and functional audit completed.  

## Limitations  
- **User or organization must supply**:  
  - Minimum tenant facts  
  - Authoritative records  
  - Metric definitions  
  - Reporting audience, cadence, and program context  
  - Jurisdiction and source-precedence rules  
- **Explicitly out of scope**:  
  - Certifying HOME, CDBG, LIHTC, fair housing, environmental review, tax, appraisal, legal, funder, lender, investor, or final project approval conclusions  
  - Publishing unsupported causal, policy, compliance, funding, affordability, community-impact, financial-performance, schedule-certainty, or project-readiness claims  
  - Submitting regulator, funder, lender, investor, board, or public disclosures without explicit submitter authority and required approver signoff  
  - Approving draws, budgets, change orders, loans, grants, acquisitions, site control, disposition, entitlements, permits, environmental clearance, tenant eligibility, or compliance corrections  
  - Collapsing the lane into generic reporting work that ignores housing development program rules, IDIS/CAPER lineage, environmental release gates, affordability evidence, source-of-funds constraints, and project milestone dependencies

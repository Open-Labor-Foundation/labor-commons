# Specialist Overview  
Resident relations specialist resolves resident-facing requests in housing, real estate development, and community development using property, lease, and resident records to draft evidence-backed responses, manage service disposition, and escalate when legal, fair housing, or maintenance authority is required.

## Who uses this  
- Resident relations coordinator  
- Property management support lead  
- Multifamily resident services manager  
- Public housing customer service supervisor  
- Affordable housing operations manager  
- Community development housing program operator  
- Resident communication specialist  

## What it produces  
- Resolution note with request summary, action taken, evidence used, resident-safe communication, and unresolved items  
- Customer or stakeholder communication summary  
- Escalation package for exceptions, complaints, repeat failures, unsupported asks, safety-sensitive issues, fair housing indicators, VAWA-sensitive issues, or missing authority  
- Request-resolution summary with issue, action taken, blocker state, next owner, and follow-up date  
- Exception communication escalation note  
- Adjacent-specialist routing note with minimum facts, evidence packet, privacy caveats, and authority boundary  

## Risks and mitigations  
- Explicitly refuses to make legal determinations, clear units as safe/habitable, or override housing policies. Refuses to disclose resident data outside approved channels or make promises of compensation or completed actions without source evidence.

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Source coverage includes U.S. Department of Housing and Urban Development and the Electronic Code of Federal Regulations  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
**What the user or organization must supply before this specialist can work reliably:**  
- Valid resident identifiers (property, unit, household, lease status)  
- Program and policy context (tenant selection plan, house rules, communication cadence)  
- Request type classification (informational, complaint, service follow-up, etc.)  
- Incident or service date  
- Work order or case number when applicable  
- Safety or fair housing indicators  
- Language access and communication preference data  

**What is explicitly out of scope:**  
- Deciding legal rights, eviction strategy, grievance outcomes, or fair housing violations  
- Approving or denying reasonable accommodations or VAWA eligibility  
- Clearing units as habitable, safe, or inspection-passed  
- Making rent or subsidy changes  
- Offering service recovery compensation or safety assurances  
- Overriding lease terms, house rules, or eligibility policies to resolve a case  
- Disclosing resident personal, disability, income, or complaint information outside approved systems  
- Treating resident relations as generic customer service without housing-specific constraints

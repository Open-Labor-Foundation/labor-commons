# Mission systems support specialist  
Owns mission systems support operations in the Defense Industrial Base and Mission Systems, ensuring service support, triage, access readiness, change-readiness, incident handling, and interface dependency coordination meet mission-critical standards. This role ensures safe and compliant workflow progression by validating prerequisites, routing owner handoffs, and producing structured support outputs without overstepping into engineering, legal, or mission authority decisions.

## Who uses this  
- Mission operations coordinator  
- Incident and readiness coordinator  
- Program operations support teams  
- Interface and integration engineers  
- Mission systems support lead  

## What it produces  
- Resolution note with request summary, action taken, confidence, and unresolved items  
- Customer or stakeholder communication summary  
- Escalation package for exceptions, complaints, safety/compliance blockers, and unsupported asks  
- System incident or service summary with impact and owner  
- Change-readiness or integration-risk escalation note  
- Domain terminology-normalized status mapping and evidence requirements  

## Risks and mitigations  
- **Escalation required for authority gaps:** The specialist refuses actions requiring legal, policy-override, safety-critical, clinical, or financial-approval authority without required context.  
- **Context validation before action:** Minimum tenant facts, evidence, and source context are validated before any in-scope recommendation or owner handoff.  
- **Bounded response design:** Outputs are limited to resolution, communication, and escalation artifacts, ensuring no overreach into decision-making lanes.  

## Validation  
- 10 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Source coverage includes Department of Defense, Department of Defense, Department of Defense, and National Institute of Standards and Technology  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- **User/organization must supply:**  
  - Complete tenant context for triage  
  - Validated evidence for change-readiness and rollback requirements  
  - Explicit authority documents for safety-critical or mission decisions  
- **Explicitly out of scope:**  
  - Creating legal interpretations or policy-override approvals  
  - Issuing mission authority or safety-critical decisions without explicit authority documents  
  - Bypassing required tenant approval and rollback evidence to force progress  
  - Claiming compliance, security clearance, or CUI release authority outside documented scope

# Specialist Overview  
The defense analytics specialist translates authoritative defense-industrial-base and mission-systems records into evidence-backed analysis, preserving defense-specific semantics and flagging disclosure or data-quality risks. It generates explicit metric definitions, analytical briefs, and confidence caveats while ensuring analysis remains within defined boundaries.

## Who uses this  
- Program performance analyst  
- EVMS or IPMDAR analyst  
- Program manager  
- Mission readiness analyst  
- Capture, finance, and business operations stakeholder consuming program reporting  
- Portfolio or executive review staff  

## What it produces  
- Metric definition and data-source summary  
- Analysis or report output with caveats and confidence notes  
- Data-quality or disclosure-risk escalation note  
- Analytical brief with metric definitions, source logic, and confidence caveats  
- Exception note for data quality, denominator ambiguity, unsupported trend claims, or cross-system conflicts  

## Risks and mitigations  
- Refuses to provide causal, benchmark, forecast, policy, or disclosure claims that exceed the underlying records.  
- Does not present descriptive analytics as causal root-cause findings, policy judgments, investor guidance, or customer commitments.  
- Avoids overstating contractual, engineering, or disclosure authority in analytical briefs and summaries.  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Source coverage includes Department of Defense and Office of the Deputy Assistant Secretary of Defense for Acquisition Integration and Management  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- What the user or organization must supply before this specialist can work reliably:  
  - Authoritative defense program records (e.g., IPMDAR, EVMS, IMS, CDRL, WBS)  
  - Metric scope and desired output definitions  
  - Context on contract line items, modifications, and readiness definitions  
  - Security clearance and access level for controlled unclassified information  
- What is explicitly out of scope:  
  - Approving contract modifications, baseline replans, reprogramming, or funding actions  
  - Providing legal interpretation of DFARS, export controls, securities disclosure, or contractual obligations  
  - Issuing engineering, safety, flightworthiness, cyber-authority, or mission-release approvals  
  - Presenting descriptive analytics as causal root-cause findings, policy judgments, investor guidance, or customer commitments  
  - Publishing or signing off external disclosures, press statements, or attested performance claims

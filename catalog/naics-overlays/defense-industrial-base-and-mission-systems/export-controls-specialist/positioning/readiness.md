# Specialist Overview  
The export controls specialist interprets and applies U.S. export-control laws and defense-industrial-base obligations to determine whether a request falls within export-control boundaries, validates required documentation, and issues control summaries or escalation guidance based on evidence and regulatory context.

## Who uses this  
- Export controls analyst  
- Mission systems program manager  
- Contract compliance coordinator  
- Quality and security controls analyst  

## What it produces  
- Quality or safety-style status summary with risk severity, evidence sufficiency, and control-boundary rationale  
- Hold, release, escalation, and control-gap output mapped to artifacts and owners  
- Export-control obligation summary linked to authoritative source and tenant policy  
- Incident or recurring finding escalation package with corrective action and recurrence context  
- Adjacent-lane routing recommendation for legal, policy-override, sanctions-counsel, contracting, or safety-certified owners  

## Risks and mitigations  
- Requests are refused or rerouted when legal, safety, or policy override authority is required, ensuring no finality is assumed in high-stakes decisions.

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Covers U.S. Department of State (via eCFR), U.S. Department of Defense, and tenant policy obligations  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- **Must be provided:** Complete request context, relevant contract clauses, end-use details, and current records of release or denial screening  
- **Out of scope:**  
  - Issuing legal interpretation memoranda or final policy override decisions  
  - Approving commercial shipping routes, procurement award decisions, or financial sanctions actions  
  - Providing engineering or safety-certification signoff for mission hardware readiness  
  - Closing defects or incidents without required artifacts and explicit owner confirmation

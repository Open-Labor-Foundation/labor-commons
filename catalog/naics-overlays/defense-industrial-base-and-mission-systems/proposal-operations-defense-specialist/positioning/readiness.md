# Specialist Overview  
The proposal operations defense specialist translates defense-industrial-base and mission-systems proposal requests into structured execution states, completion evidence, and exception-handling records. It ensures proposal workflows align with mission-critical operational context, readiness constraints, and traceable handoff requirements.

## Who uses this  
- Proposal operations analyst  
- Mission support planner  
- Industry capture and bid teams  
- Program office coordinator  
- Operations orchestrator  

## What it produces  
- Execution status update with lifecycle stage and next action  
- Completion or fulfillment record with explicit evidence references  
- Exception queue note with rework owner, deadline, and missing-evidence list  
- Defense-aware proposal readout with terminology normalization and readiness constraints  
- Escalation note for safety, mission-impact, legal, procurement, or financial blockers  

## Risks and mitigations  
- Must refuse actions requiring legal, licensing, policy-override, financial-approval, engineering, or safety-critical authority outside this lane  
- Must escalate when tenant-specific mission or safety controls change outcomes  
- Must not infer authority from public baselines alone when tenant-specific controls apply  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Covers U.S. Federal Acquisition Regulation, U.S. Department of Defense, and National Institute of Standards and Technology sources  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
### What the user or organization must supply before this specialist can work reliably  
- Accepted proposal intake with clear operational context and artifact definitions  
- Access to authoritative tenant records and readiness posture data  
- Defined approval gates and compliance constraints  

### What is explicitly out of scope  
- Issuing legal opinions or drafting contractual waiver language  
- Setting final pricing strategy, award authority, or financial approval outcomes  
- Replacing engineering, safety, or operations command judgment on mission-ready declarations  
- Performing post-award implementation, delivery, invoicing, or contract administration  
- Closing a case as complete without completion evidence and approved owner-routing metadata

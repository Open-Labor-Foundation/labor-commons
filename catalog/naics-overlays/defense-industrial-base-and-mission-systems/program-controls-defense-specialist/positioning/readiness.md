# Specialist Overview  
The program controls defense specialist supports mission-systems and defense-industrial-base program-controls workflows by determining whether required controls, evidence, safety/quality obligations, and authority boundaries support a hold, release, corrective-action, or escalation outcome. It focuses on translating control obligations into evidence-backed status, identifying missing controls, and routing to appropriate specialists when authority shifts.  

## Who uses this  
- Defense program controls analyst  
- Program manager  
- Quality and compliance coordinator  
- Mission assurance coordinator  
- Risk and controls operations lead  

## What it produces  
- Quality or safety status summary with confidence, evidence sufficiency, and boundary rationale  
- Hold, release, nonconformance, or control escalation output with owner routing and next actions  
- Control and obligation summary tied to records and approval authority  
- Exception and remediation note for missing evidence or policy conflict  
- Corrective-action or incident escalation package with recurrence context  

## Risks and mitigations  
- Requests involving legal interpretation, clinical/safety design authority, policy overrides, financing authority, or final program approval are explicitly refused or routed to appropriate specialists.  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Source coverage includes Department of Defense and National Institute of Standards and Technology guidance  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
**What the user or organization must supply before this specialist can work reliably:**  
- Relevant control obligations from contracts or mission directives  
- Current status of evidence or records for program controls  
- Contextual authority boundaries and escalation paths  
- Artifact traceability and incident history where applicable  

**What is explicitly out of scope:**  
- Providing legal, policy-override, or financial-approval decisions  
- Providing clinical, engineering safety approval, licensing, or mission design authority  
- Converting policy conflicts into final command or contract decisions  
- Closing defects or incidents without defensible evidence sufficiency and required owner action

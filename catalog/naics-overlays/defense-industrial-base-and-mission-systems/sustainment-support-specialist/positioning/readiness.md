# Specialist Overview  
The sustainment support specialist evaluates maintenance-state requests for Defense Industrial Base and Mission Systems assets, ensuring safe, evidence-grounded next actions. This role focuses on maintenance triggers, lifecycle readiness, and return-to-service planning, while preserving authority boundaries and escalating out-of-scope decisions.

## Who uses this  
- Maintenance coordinator  
- Mission readiness lead  
- Field sustainment analyst  
- Maintenance-planning specialist  
- Sustainment operations manager  

## What it produces  
- Maintenance-trigger recognition summary  
- Maintenance plan or status note with evidence sufficiency  
- Uptime or outage-risk summary  
- Return-to-service checklist with approver and evidence dependencies  
- Safety, engineering, and warranty escalation packet  
- Adjacent-lane routing note when authority boundary changes  

## Risks and mitigations  
- Requests involving safety, engineering, or warranty decisions are explicitly escalated and not resolved within the specialist’s scope.  
- Outputs include clear evidence sufficiency statements to prevent premature action.  
- All return-to-service recommendations require full evidence and approver signals before being issued.  

## Validation  
- 10 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Source coverage includes Department of Defense maintenance and readiness documentation  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
### What the user or organization must supply  
- Validated maintenance-state signals (e.g., readiness codes, operational states)  
- Current part-shortage and maintenance evidence records  
- Access to required approver signals for return-to-service  

### What is explicitly out of scope  
- Physical repair execution or modification authority  
- Final safety or engineering signoff  
- Legal interpretation or financial authority decisions  
- Clinical or non-maintenance regulatory determinations  
- Root-cause engineering analysis or design-level remediation

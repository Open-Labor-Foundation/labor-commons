# Specialist Overview  
The aerospace program specialist manages administrative records and program evidence for aerospace production, supplier, quality, inventory, and traceability systems. It ensures completeness, readiness, and correct handoff of administrative artifacts without assuming decision-making authority over product, quality, or regulatory outcomes.

## Who uses this  
- Aerospace program coordinator  
- Program management office analyst  
- Production program administrator  
- Quality program coordinator  
- Supplier program coordinator  
- Configuration and document control reviewer  
- Operations reviewer  

## What it produces  
- Document-or-records-completeness-summary  
- Administrative-status-note  
- Program-status-summary  
- Document-or-evidence-packet  
- Record-update-note  
- Audit-trail-or-exception-summary  
- Retention-access-or-governance-escalation-package  
- Traceability-conflict-summary  
- Quality-or-release-boundary-escalation  
- Supplier-or-substitution-escalation-note  
- Missing-submission-or-approval-escalation-note  
- Boundary-safe-refusal-note  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Regulatory and industry source coverage includes FAA, IAF, IAQG, and applicable CFR 9  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- Requires authoritative records, access constraints, program context, and product or lot scope to operate  
- Cannot act on requests requiring legal, safety-critical, engineering, quality-release, supplier-approval, policy-override, financial-approval, or regulatory authority  
- Cannot approve engineering changes, quality dispositions, supplier substitutions, or product airworthiness  
- Cannot interpret or act on export-control law, data rights, or contractual obligations  
- Cannot bypass formal traceability, release, hold, or quality controls

# Specialist Overview
The aerospace analytics specialist translates aerospace and defense production, quality, inventory, supplier, and release records into performance trend summaries, operational risk commentary, and decision-ready recommendations. It ensures traceable, evidence-backed reporting by managing metric logic, source definitions, and release boundary interpretation.

## Who uses this
- Aerospace performance analyst  
- Manufacturing and planning leader  
- Quality systems coordinator  
- Supplier quality manager  
- Supply-chain analyst  
- Operations reviewer

## What it produces
- Metric definition and data-source summary  
- Analysis or report output with caveats and confidence notes  
- Data-quality or disclosure-risk escalation note  
- Analytical brief with metric definitions, source logic, and confidence caveats  
- Exception note for data-quality, denominator ambiguity, conflicting records, unsupported claim, or trend limitations

## Risks and mitigations
- Refuses to provide legal, financial, engineering, safety-critical, policy-override, or regulated-release authority to avoid misuse.  
- Requires authoritative records and source context to prevent unsupported conclusions.  
- Escalates unsupported causal or policy claims to appropriate human reviewers.  
- Uses traceability-aware reconciliation to avoid publishing on conflicting or ambiguous data.

## Validation
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Source coverage includes 11 authoritative references: Electronic Code of Federal Regulations, Federal Aviation Administration, and International Aerospace Quality Group standards  
- Human verification and functional audit completed

## Limitations
- What the user or organization must supply before this specialist can work reliably
  - Authoritative records from ERP, MES, WMS, quality, supplier, or change-control systems  
  - Minimum tenant facts and source context  
  - Clear definitions of metrics, denominators, and systems of record  
- What is explicitly out of scope
  - Providing legal, financial, engineering, safety-critical, policy-override, or regulated-release authority  
  - Approving supplier substitution, alternate part flow, use-as-is, or airworthiness/DER/ODA decisions  
  - Publishing causal conclusions, policy recommendations, or forecasts unsupported by evidence  
  - Claiming final compliance, certification, or product acceptance authority  
  - Inferring tenant-specific program authority from public sources only

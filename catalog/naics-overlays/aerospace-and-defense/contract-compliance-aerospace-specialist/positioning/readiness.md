# Specialist Overview  
The contract compliance aerospace specialist translates aerospace and defense contract, regulatory, and quality obligations into evidence-backed control handling, monitoring, and escalation. It maps obligations to production, supplier, and quality records and ensures compliance with public and tenant-specific requirements.

## Who uses this  
- Contracts compliance manager  
- Program compliance lead  
- Supplier quality engineer  
- Quality manager  
- Government contracts operations lead  
- Manufacturing compliance analyst  
- Program manager  
- GRC analyst  

## What it produces  
- Control or obligation matrix tied to aerospace contract clauses, controls, evidence owners, source records, review cadence, and exception owners  
- Evidence readiness or gap summary for contract, quality, supplier, traceability, release, hold, nonconformance, or purchasing-system review  
- Evidence packet or checklist for internal review, customer audit, DCMA or FAA support, supplier review, or attestation preparation  
- Exception and remediation escalation note for missing evidence, control gaps, policy conflicts, nonconformance, quality hold, supplier nonconformance, or release-boundary concerns  
- Source-conflict or policy-conflict note that names affected clauses, public sources, tenant policies, operational records, assumptions, and required human owner  
- Adjacent-lane routing package for legal, contracts, quality, engineering, supplier quality, procurement, production planning, inventory, logistics, export/security, or executive approval owners  
- Tenant-assumption log preserving the public baseline while identifying tenant-specific facts that must be retrieved before final posture  

## Risks and mitigations  
- Requests that bypass traceability, release, quality, purchasing-system, counterfeit-parts, supplier-approval, export/security, or evidence controls are explicitly refused.  
- Escalation is triggered when control expectations are ambiguous, conflicting, or missing, ensuring no action is taken outside lane authority.  
- Outputs are grounded in authoritative public sources and tenant records to avoid assumptions that could lead to noncompliance.  

## Validation  
- 13 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Source coverage includes Acquisition.GOV and tenant contract clauses, program context, and system-of-record retrieval hooks  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- What the user or organization must supply before this specialist can work reliably:  
  - Specific contract clauses, program context, and tenant facts  
  - Access to authoritative records, source hierarchy, and system-of-record retrieval hooks  
  - Context about applicable regulatory framework (e.g., FAR, DFARS, FAA production, IAQG)  
- What is explicitly out of scope:  
  - Presenting governance support as legal advice, legal interpretation, contracting officer communication, or regulator negotiation  
  - Approving or certifying contract compliance, airworthiness, export-control compliance, CUI handling, or quality-system conformance as a final authority  
  - Bypassing or overriding traceability, release, quality hold, nonconformance, purchasing-system, counterfeit-parts, supplier-approval, change-control, export/security, or evidence controls  
  - Approving supplier selection, substitution, use-as-is, waiver, concession, rework, repair, MRB disposition, product release, payment withholding, price adjustment, or contract modification  
  - Collapsing aerospace contract compliance into generic compliance checklists that ignore clauses, flowdowns, CDRLs, lots, serials, suppliers, source inspection, quality holds, release records, and manufacturing systems of record

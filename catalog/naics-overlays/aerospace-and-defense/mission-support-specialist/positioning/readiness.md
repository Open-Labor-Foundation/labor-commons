# Specialist Overview  
The mission support specialist operates within the aerospace and defense industry to resolve mission-support service requests by using authoritative records and industry-specific terminology. It classifies requests, validates facts, summarizes readiness or blocker status, prepares stakeholder communication, and routes issues to the correct owner when authority or evidence shifts outside its scope.

## Who uses this  
- Mission support coordinator  
- Program operations lead  
- Production control supervisor  
- Sustainment support lead  
- Quality operations reviewer  
- Supplier performance coordinator  
- Logistics readiness analyst  
- Reviewer  

## What it produces  
- Service-support-disposition  
- Resolution-note  
- Customer-or-stakeholder-communication-summary  
- Plant-or-mission-workflow-summary  
- Escalation-package  
- Traceability-evidence-conflict-summary  
- Quality-hold-or-release-boundary-escalation-note  
- Supplier-or-substitution-routing-note  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Source coverage includes authoritative aerospace and defense systems such as ERP, MES, WMS, QMS, supplier records, planning systems, logistics systems, lot genealogy, and serialized inventory states  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- What the user or organization must supply before this specialist can work reliably:  
  - Authoritative records such as production orders, travelers, lot or serial genealogy, inventory states, quality holds, nonconformance records, supplier records, release records, logistics status  
  - Clear stakeholder communications and documented service or policy rules  
  - Context for service-level expectations and current readiness or control state  

- What is explicitly out of scope:  
  - Approving engineering disposition or MRB outcomes  
  - Releasing controlled product from hold  
  - Certifying airworthiness or regulatory compliance  
  - Authorizing supplier substitutions or source approvals  
  - Waiving security or safety controls  
  - Making financial or policy exceptions  
  - Bypassing traceability, hold, inspection, quality, or configuration controls  

## Risks and mitigations  
- Risks: Misinterpreting or misrepresenting engineering, safety, or regulatory authority could lead to unsafe or noncompliant decisions.  
- Mitigations: The specialist explicitly avoids making approvals or waivers, returns to the orchestrator when authority or evidence is unclear, and only uses documented rules and system-of-record evidence in its outputs.

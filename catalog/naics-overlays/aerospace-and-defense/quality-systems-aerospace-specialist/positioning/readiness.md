# Specialist Overview  
The quality systems aerospace specialist focuses on evaluating aerospace manufacturing quality records to determine if available evidence supports hold, release, corrective-action, or audit-readiness decisions. It operates within the boundaries of quality-system discipline, avoiding final engineering or airworthiness approvals.

## Who uses this  
- Quality managers  
- Supplier quality engineers  
- Manufacturing quality engineers  
- Program quality leads  
- Operations reviewers  
- Internal reviewers  

## What it produces  
- Quality-system review note with evidence sufficiency, traceability status, and release-boundary posture  
- Hold or release escalation package with missing records, owner routing, and residual risks  
- Quality or safety status summary  
- Quality or assurance findings summary  
- Corrective-action or unresolved-risk escalation note  
- Nonconformance or CAPA management summary with repeat-defect logic and next actions  
- Supplier-quality escalation memo with certificate, traceability, or corrective-action gaps  
- Audit-readiness checklist for FAA production oversight or AS9100-aligned quality-system review  

## Risks and mitigations  
- Rejects requests that require final engineering approval, DER/ODA signoff, or unsupported release overrides to prevent overstepping authority  
- Requires explicit authority before overriding documented hold or release boundaries  
- Maintains clear separation from legal and airworthiness functions to avoid misrepresentation  

## Validation  
- 14 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Covers FAA production oversight, AS9100, and quality-system traceability requirements  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- **What the user or organization must supply**:  
  - Complete and accurate quality-system records (e.g., inspection logs, nonconformance reports, traceability data)  
  - Clear scope and boundaries of the quality-system decision request  
  - Access to relevant audit or regulatory requirements (e.g., AS9100, FAA production oversight)  

- **What is explicitly out of scope**:  
  - Final engineering disposition or airworthiness signoff  
  - FAA or DER/ODA approval of a part, assembly, or lot  
  - Legal interpretation of contractual obligations or disputes  
  - Impersonation of a certification body or FAA representative  
  - General manufacturing, procurement, or logistics ownership outside of quality-system evidence evaluation

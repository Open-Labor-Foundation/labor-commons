# Specialist Overview  
The maintenance and sustainment specialist handles aerospace and defense maintenance workflows, focusing on intake, trigger recognition, planning, prioritization, readiness evidence, and return-to-service readiness packaging, without claiming engineering, safety, or regulatory authority.

## Who uses this  
- Maintenance planner  
- Field sustainment coordinator  
- Fleet readiness analyst  
- Depot maintenance coordinator  
- Program operations lead  
- Quality operations reviewer  
- Supplier quality reviewer  
- Logistics readiness reviewer  

## What it produces  
- Maintenance status summary  
- Maintenance plan or status note  
- Maintenance or field status summary  
- Uptime or outage risk summary  
- Return-to-service checklist  
- Safety or engineering escalation note  
- Traceability conflict summary  
- Quality or release boundary escalation  
- Supplier nonconformance or substitution escalation  
- Orchestrator return note  

## Risks and mitigations  
- Refuses to bypass traceability, quality hold, supplier approval, engineering disposition, safety signoff, or release controls.  
- Escalates safety, engineering, warranty, airworthiness, quality release, supplier nonconformance, substitution, and regulatory release decisions to the correct adjacent specialist or human authority.  
- Does not approve supplier substitutions, rework, use-as-is disposition, or alternate material without documented adjacent authority.  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Source coverage includes 9 authority sources from the Electronic Code of Federal Regulations and Federal Aviation Administration  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
### What the user or organization must supply  
- Minimum tenant facts, asset identifiers, program context  
- Maintenance records, parts traceability, quality status  
- Approval matrix and source context required to continue safely  

### What is explicitly out of scope  
- Performing physical maintenance, authorizing maintenance execution, or certifying repair work  
- Claiming engineer-of-record, DER, ODA, FAA, airworthiness, safety, warranty, or formal release authority  
- Closing or deferring maintenance without required evidence, tenant deferral authority, and approval records  
- Bypassing traceability, release, quality hold, supplier approval, engineering change, life-limited part, or safety controls  
- Approving supplier substitutions, rework, use-as-is disposition, or alternate material without documented adjacent authority  
- Drifting into production planning, procurement execution, inventory allocation, logistics dispatch, supplier approval, formal quality release, engineering disposition, legal interpretation, or financial approval

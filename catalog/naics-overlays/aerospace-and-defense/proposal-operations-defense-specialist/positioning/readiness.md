# Specialist Overview  
The Proposal Operations Defense Specialist manages the execution, processing, and fulfillment of proposal operations within the aerospace and defense industry, focusing on production readiness, maintenance, and logistics evidence. It ensures artifact-level progression, handles exceptions, and prepares completion records without acting as a legal, engineering, or safety-certification authority.

## Who uses this  
- Proposal managers  
- Production planners  
- Logistics coordinators  
- Quality assurance leads  
- Supplier quality engineers  
- Contracting and award specialists  
- Maintenance and mission operations leads  

## What it produces  
- Execution or case-disposition summary with lifecycle stage and next action  
- Completion or fulfillment record with explicit evidence support and handoff state  
- Exception queue note for missing inputs, conflicts, rework, or escalation triggers  
- Operational readiness summary with mission, maintenance, and transport/equipment constraints  
- Escalation note for safety, maintenance, supplier, or release-boundary blockers  

## Risks and mitigations  
- Mitigates risk of inappropriate escalation by identifying and returning work when legal, safety, or financial authority is required  
- Prevents unauthorized release or closure of work by identifying unresolved traceability or release conditions  
- Avoids overstepping into engineering or supplier substitution decisions by routing to correct authority  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Source coverage includes U.S. Federal Acquisition Regulation and U.S. Department of Defense guidance  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- What the user or organization must supply before this specialist can work reliably:  
  - Minimum tenant facts  
  - Authoritative records  
  - Source context for proposal operations  
- What is explicitly out of scope:  
  - Issuing legal opinions or drafting contract language  
  - Performing final engineering disposition or certification  
  - Providing licensed maintenance or safety-critical operational command  
  - Closing or releasing work without resolved production quality evidence or release authority  
  - Making supplier substitution or use-as-is release decisions outside approved records

# Specialist Overview  
The hospital finance operations specialist focuses on translating authoritative hospital source records into traceable and compliant billing, claim, payment posting, and revenue exception handling outputs. It operates within the boundaries of hospital financial operations, ensuring that each action is supported by documented evidence and tenant-approved guidance, without assuming clinical, legal, or financial approval authority.

## Who uses this  
- Hospital finance operations leaders  
- Revenue cycle operations managers  
- Patient financial services supervisors  
- Cash posting and payment reconciliation teams  
- Denials and appeals coordinators  
- Hospital business office teams  
- Spec-pack reviewers  

## What it produces  
- Billing-or-financial-readiness-summary  
- Claim-or-invoice-support-summary  
- Payment-posting-summary  
- Reconciliation-note  
- Revenue-exception-escalation-packet  
- Denial-or-dispute-routing-note  
- Unsupported-adjustment-refusal-note  
- Controlled-phi-finance-routing-note  
- Clinical-or-payer-boundary-escalation-note  
- Source-conflict-or-low-confidence-escalation  

## Risks and mitigations  
- **Refusal to act on missing or conflicting documentation:** The specialist refuses to proceed when source records are missing, stale, contradictory, or outside its decision rights.  
- **Refusal to assume clinical or legal authority:** The specialist will not make medical necessity determinations, approve clinical decisions, or disclose PHI.  
- **Refusal to invent financial adjustments:** The specialist will not create or approve write-offs, refunds, or recoupments without explicit authority.  

## Validation  
- 14 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Source coverage includes EHR/EMR encounter documentation, eligibility, authorization, charge capture, claims, remittance, denials, appeals, and posting records  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
**What the user or organization must supply:**  
- Authoritative hospital source records (e.g., encounter, charge, claim, remittance, authorization, eligibility, denial, appeal, privacy records)  
- Tenant-approved payer/provider operating guidance  
- Documented authority for the requested action  

**What is explicitly out of scope:**  
- Assigning, changing, validating, or approving diagnosis, procedure, DRG, APC, HCPCS, CPT, revenue, or modifier codes  
- Determining medical necessity, level of care, diagnosis, treatment planning, discharge readiness, clinical risk, or utilization approval  
- Interpreting payer policy, approving coverage, waiving authorization/referral rules, adjudicating claims, or overriding payer contract terms  
- Approving write-offs, refunds, recoupments, contractual allowances, bad-debt treatment, tax positions, or financial statement impact  
- Disclosing PHI, exporting patient-level AR/denial/claim lists, or broadening distribution outside tenant role-based access and audit controls  
- Closing billing, posting, reconciliation, or adjustment items when source records are missing, stale, contradictory, not authoritative, or outside the lane's decision rights

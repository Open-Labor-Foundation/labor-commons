# Specialist Overview  
The constituent services specialist resolves stakeholder-facing requests in government and public-sector workflows by classifying asks, collecting facts, checking official records, preparing evidence-backed communication, and escalating cases that require specialized authority. It ensures communication is clear, policy-aware, and traceable to official records while preserving due process and escalation discipline.

## Who uses this  
- Constituent services teams  
- Public-sector contact centers  
- Agency liaison offices  
- Casework supervisors  
- Program operations reviewers  

## What it produces  
- Resolution note with request summary, official records checked, action taken, unresolved items, and next owner where applicable  
- Customer or stakeholder communication summary with safe language, evidence limits, and required next steps  
- Escalation package for exceptions, complaints, unsupported asks, missing records, rights-sensitive cases, procurement boundaries, or conflicting policies  
- Request-resolution summary with issue, action taken, blocker state, audit trail, and next owner  
- Exception communication escalation note for policy, eligibility, due-process, compensation, records, procurement, or legal-authority cases  

## Risks and mitigations  
- Mitigation: All requests that involve eligibility determinations, legal interpretations, or protected records are explicitly refused and escalated to the correct authority. The specialist does not provide legal, adjudicative, or enforcement authority.  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Source coverage includes Congressional Research Service, Office of Management and Budget, Office of the Law Revision Counsel, U.S. House of Representatives, and other key public-sector policy sources  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- What the user or organization must supply before this specialist can work reliably  
  - Minimum tenant facts, jurisdiction, program context, and source context  
  - Privacy permissions and official records access  
  - Documentation of current policy, eligibility rules, and service procedures  
- What is explicitly out of scope  
  - Overriding policy, pricing, eligibility, appeal, procurement, records, or compensation limits  
  - Impersonating official adjudication, enforcement, contracting, public-records, privacy, or legal authority  
  - Determining benefit eligibility, permit issuance, enforcement disposition, protest outcome, FOIA release, Privacy Act disclosure, or statutory rights  
  - Providing legal advice or statutory interpretation when rights, benefits, obligations, appeals, or public duties turn on legal meaning  
  - Disclosing protected, sealed, exempt, confidential, procurement-sensitive, or personally identifiable information without the required authority and record basis

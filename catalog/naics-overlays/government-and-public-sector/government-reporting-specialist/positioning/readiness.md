# Specialist Overview  
The Government Reporting Specialist assembles and prepares administrative reporting, disclosure, and submission packages for government and public-sector use, ensuring alignment with statutory authority, program rules, and official records. It generates reporting artifacts with source tie-outs, metric definitions, and risk escalations, but does not interpret legal, policy, or enforcement decisions.

## Who uses this  
- Program reporting managers  
- Performance and transparency teams  
- Grant and public-program administrators  
- Case-management operations leads  
- Procurement reporting coordinators  
- Records and audit liaison teams  

## What it produces  
- Metric definition and data-source summary  
- Analysis or report output with caveats and confidence notes  
- Causation or reconciliation note  
- Submission or reporting package summary with source tie-outs and caveats  
- Data-quality or disclosure-risk escalation note  
- Eligibility, records, or public-service workflow handling summary with auditability notes  
- Adjacent-specialist routing note  

## Risks and mitigations  
- Requests to act as an adjudicator, legal interpreter, or audit authority are explicitly refused.  
- Outputs are flagged or escalated when source lineage, metric definitions, or jurisdictional authority is unresolved.  
- Descriptive reporting is not conflated with policy or compliance claims without explicit evidence and review.  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Source coverage includes U.S. Office of Management and Budget, U.S. Government Accountability Office, and related regulatory frameworks  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
**What the user or organization must supply before this specialist can work reliably:**  
- Jurisdictional authority and program rules  
- Authoritative source systems (case management, procurement, grants, etc.)  
- Metric definitions or clear reporting requirements  
- Context for reporting audience and cadence  

**What is explicitly out of scope:**  
- Deciding eligibility, benefits, permits, licenses, enforcement, appeals, or public-service outcomes  
- Providing legal interpretations, audit opinions, or formal financial approvals  
- Releasing public records or making records-release determinations  
- Merging tenant assumptions into public baseline definitions without recording public baseline, tenant adaptation, and unresolved precedence  
- Publishing or implying certainty without resolved source quality, signoff, or lineage

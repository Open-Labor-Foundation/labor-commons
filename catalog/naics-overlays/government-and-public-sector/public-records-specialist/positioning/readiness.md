# Specialist Overview
The public records specialist maintains and organizes public-sector records, ensuring documentation is complete, traceable, and ready for use in transparency, legal, audit, and program workflows. It handles classification, validation, inventory, and escalation of record-related tasks without making final decisions on disclosure, exemptions, or legal authority.

## Who uses this
- Public records offices
- FOIA and transparency coordinators
- Records management teams
- Program administration teams
- Public-sector legal and privacy liaisons
- Audit and compliance reviewers

## What it produces
- Document-or-records-completeness-summary
- Documentation-completeness-summary
- Administrative-status-note
- Record-update-note
- Document-or-evidence-packet
- Retention-access-or-governance-escalation-package
- Records-governance-escalation-note
- Audit-trail-or-exception-summary
- Adjacent-specialist-routing-note
- Boundary-safe-refusal-note
- Tenant-adaptation-assumption-log

## Risks and mitigations
- **Risk**: Misclassifying records or failing to identify missing documentation could delay legal, audit, or transparency processes.
  - **Mitigation**: The specialist refuses to act on incomplete or ambiguous requests and escalates when jurisdictional or statutory boundaries are unclear.
- **Risk**: Inadvertent exposure of confidential or sensitive data during record assembly.
  - **Mitigation**: The specialist does not disclose protected or exempt information and requires documented authorization before handling such materials.
- **Risk**: Confusion between record governance and final decision-making authority.
  - **Mitigation**: The specialist explicitly routes decision-making to legal, privacy, or disclosure officers and does not override their authority.

## Validation
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1
- Source coverage includes statutory frameworks from the Office of the Law Revision Counsel, U.S. House of Representatives, and the National Archives and Records Administration
- Human verification and functional audit completed

## Limitations
- **What the user or organization must supply**:
  - Jurisdictional and program context
  - Records custody information
  - Authority map and system-of-record references
  - Documented tenant authority for updates or classifications
  - Minimum tenant facts for request validation
- **What is explicitly out of scope**:
  - Deciding FOIA exemptions, state public-records exemptions, or legal sufficiency of withholdings
  - Making final redactions, fee waivers, expedited processing, or appeals decisions
  - Amending records under the Privacy Act without documented authority
  - Deciding records retention disposition or destruction
  - Impersonating legal, adjudication, enforcement, or audit authority
  - Disclosing protected, exempt, or confidential information without authorized review
  - Making final determinations on benefits, permits, procurements, or legal outcomes

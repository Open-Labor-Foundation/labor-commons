# Specialist Overview  
The public sector program specialist manages administrative workflows for public-sector programs, including application intake, eligibility-file checks, record completeness, correspondence tracking, reporting, and audit-trail preparation. It ensures operational clarity while staying within boundaries of statutory authority and due process.

## Who uses this  
- Public-sector program administrators  
- Program operations managers  
- Membership or roster coordinators  
- Records-management reviewers  
- Public-service workflow coordinators  
- Compliance and audit liaisons  

## What it produces  
- Document or records completeness summary with missing items, source systems, owners, due dates, and blocked decisions  
- Administrative status note with program, roster, application, permit, notice, correspondence, procurement, or reporting state  
- Program or membership status summary with participants, deadlines, blockers, owners, assumptions, and next administrative steps  
- Record update note documenting source records, authority basis, change limits, timestamp expectations, and audit-trail requirements  
- Document or evidence packet for downstream review with official record identifiers, source links, retrieval gaps, and confidence posture  
- Retention, access, privacy, public-records, or governance escalation package  
- Escalation note for missing submissions, approvals, participant data, conflicting records, statutory interpretation, adjudication, enforcement, procurement, or audit authority  

## Risks and mitigations  
Refuses actions that require legal, licensed, policy-override, financial-approval, official adjudication, enforcement, public-records disclosure, procurement award, audit opinion, or safety-critical authority.

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Covers U.S. federal public-sector program administration authority based on Office of Management and Budget, Office of the Law Revision Counsel, U.S. House of Representatives, Electronic Code of Federal Regulations, National Archives and Records Administration  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- Requires access to official records, program authority documentation, jurisdictional scope, and source context for safe administrative handling  
- Does not treat record maintenance as authority to decide the underlying case, claim, benefit, permit, program participation, public obligation, procurement action, or legal outcome  
- Does not impersonate official adjudication, enforcement, contracting, legal, public-records, privacy, audit, financial-approval, or policy-override authority  
- Does not approve, deny, revoke, suspend, sanction, waive, award, terminate, release, withhold, delete, or dispose of official public-sector records or decisions without documented delegated authority  
- Does not infer statutory eligibility, appeal rights, hearing posture, public-record exemptions, procurement authority, retention treatment, or privacy disclosure permission from incomplete administrative records  
- Does not collapse into generic program management when jurisdiction, statutory authority, due process, public-service equity, records transparency, procurement, or auditability shapes the work

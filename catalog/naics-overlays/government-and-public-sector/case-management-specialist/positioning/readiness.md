# Specialist Overview  
The case management specialist supports public-sector case administration by turning public-service demands, case-file status, statutory deadlines, participant records, eligibility prerequisites, and system-of-record evidence into recommendation-only outputs for institutional workflow planning. It operates within administrative boundaries, focusing on intake, tracking, normalization, and escalation without assuming final decision authority.

## Who uses this  
- Case-management-supervisor  
- Program-operations-manager  
- Public-service-workflow-coordinator  
- Grant-or-assistance-program-coordinator  
- Records-management-reviewer  
- Public-administration-analyst  
- Reviewer  

## What it produces  
- Schedule-or-allocation-recommendation  
- Capacity-or-priority-recommendation  
- Constraint-log  
- Approval-needed-summary  
- Institution-workflow-summary  
- Audit-trail-gap-escalation-note  
- Rights-or-due-process-escalation-note  
- Public-records-or-procurement-routing-note  
- Tenant-adaptation-assumption-log  

## Risks and mitigations  
- **Risk:** Incomplete or conflicting records could lead to unsafe administrative recommendations.  
  **Mitigation:** The specialist refuses or routes cases when jurisdictional rules, program policies, public-records constraints, or due-process obligations are missing or conflict.  
- **Risk:** Misclassification of a case could result in improper workflow prioritization.  
  **Mitigation:** The specialist validates minimum tenant facts, jurisdiction, program authority, and system-of-record references before proceeding.  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Source coverage includes FEMA, eCFR, and NARA guidance  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- **What the user or organization must supply:**  
  - Minimum tenant facts and jurisdiction  
  - Program authority and official records  
  - System-of-record references and source context  
  - Statutory or program deadlines and service-level targets  
- **What is explicitly out of scope:**  
  - Presenting planning recommendations as final budget, staffing, eligibility, or operational approval  
  - Impersonating adjudication, enforcement, legal, public-records, procurement, audit, or financial authority  
  - Deciding eligibility, rights, sanctions, appeals, procurement awards, or statutory interpretation  
  - Altering official case records or participant status without explicit tenant authority and audit logging  
  - Bypassing equity, accessibility, notice, or audit controls for queue throughput  
  - Collapsing into generic project management when public-service obligations are central

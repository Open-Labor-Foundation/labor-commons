# Specialist Overview  
A public procurement specialist manages the intake, validation, and readiness of procurement requests within government and public-sector organizations. They ensure compliance with procurement rules, assess sourcing and availability, and prepare traceable outputs for contracting, inventory, and compliance teams without making awards or legal decisions.

## Who uses this  
- Public procurement coordinators  
- Agency requisition and purchasing teams  
- Public-sector supply and inventory planners  
- Program operations teams  
- Contract administration support teams  
- Public-sector compliance and audit teams  

## What it produces  
- Availability-or-supply-status-summary  
- Availability-or-logistics-status-summary  
- Procurement-or-replenishment-recommendation  
- Vendor-or-procurement-action-note  
- Shortage-or-vendor-risk-escalation-note  
- Shortage-sourcing-or-vendor-risk-escalation-note  
- Procurement-records-completeness-summary  
- Source-policy-conflict-escalation  
- Public-service-due-process-escalation  
- Boundary-refusal-or-adjacent-lane-routing-note  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Covers standard public procurement processes including requisition intake, sourcing, and vendor constraints  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- Must be provided with tenant facts, jurisdiction, program context, procurement method, and official records before producing readiness claims  
- Cannot approve spend, award or modify contracts, select vendors, waive competition, or interpret law  
- Cannot adjudicate protests, clear SAM/exclusion issues, or release held inventory  
- Cannot release public records or make disclosure decisions  
- Cannot act as a contracting officer, source selection authority, or legal decision-maker

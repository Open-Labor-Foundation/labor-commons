# Specialist Overview  
The public finance coordination specialist manages public-finance workflow dependencies across budget, grants, procurement, payment, records, program, audit, and public-service teams. It ensures that financial coordination requests are correctly classified, validated, and routed with clear ownership and evidence at each step.

## Who uses this  
- Public finance operations coordinators  
- Budget execution analysts  
- Grant administration coordinators  
- Public program operations managers  
- Procurement and payment liaison teams  
- Records and audit readiness reviewers  
- Spec-pack reviewers  

## What it produces  
- Handoff-summary  
- Dependency-tracker  
- Blocker-summary  
- Handoff-status-summary  
- Routing-confirmation-note  
- Closure-confirmation  
- Blocked-dependency-escalation-note  
- Rights-or-due-process-escalation-note  
- Financial-approval-boundary-refusal  
- Tenant-adaptation-assumption-log  

## Risks and mitigations  
- The specialist refuses to approve financial actions, legal interpretations, or public records releases, ensuring decisions remain within the proper authority. It escalates high-stakes coordination issues to human reviewers when jurisdictional conflicts, due-process constraints, or missing records are present.

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Covers public finance coordination artifacts including appropriation, apportionment, allotment, obligation, grant award, drawdown, invoice, voucher, payment file, case record, notice, audit log, and transparency log.  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- **What the user or organization must supply:**  
  - Jurisdictional clarity  
  - Program authority  
  - Systems of record  
  - Official records  
  - Source hierarchy  
  - Due-process constraints  

- **What is explicitly out of scope:**  
  - Approving obligations, expenditures, payments, or awards  
  - Interpreting legal or eligibility rules as final authority  
  - Altering official records or audit logs outside traceable tenant authority  
  - Claiming authority over budget, procurement, grant, or audit decisions  
  - Releasing public records or dispositioning them  
  - Impersonating a budget officer, certifying officer, or other official role

# Specialist Overview  
The grants administration specialist coordinates institution-facing grants administration workflows in the public sector by validating records, tracking deadlines, ensuring compliance, and preparing traceable administrative outputs. It focuses on workflow coordination, evidence packaging, and readiness for handoffs within government and public-sector grant systems.

## Who uses this  
- Grants management offices  
- Public-sector program administrators  
- Institutional grant coordinators  
- Recipient and subrecipient monitoring teams  
- Public-sector compliance and audit teams  

## What it produces  
- Document or records completeness summary  
- Administrative status note with missing items, ownership, due dates, and blocked decisions  
- Retention, access, or governance escalation package  
- Institution grant workflow summary with official records, due dates, and owner state  
- Escalation note for grant, eligibility, audit, public-records, procurement, or participant-data conflicts  
- Audit-trail or exception summary linking each recommendation to named records and source obligations  

## Risks and mitigations  
- Explicitly refuses to adjudicate applications, determine legal eligibility, or make enforcement decisions, ensuring no authority is overstated.  
- Requires validation of authoritative source context and official records before proceeding to mitigate misinterpretation or misuse of data.  
- Routes conflicts to legal, program adjudication, or audit specialists when decision authority shifts beyond administrative scope.  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Source coverage includes 8 — U.S. Office of Management and Budget / Electronic Code of Federal Regulations, and Grants.gov  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- What the user or organization must supply before this specialist can work reliably  
  - Authoritative source context for the grant program  
  - Official records and documentation requested for the administrative task  
  - Defined ownership and roles for each step in the workflow  
  - Access to current deadlines, compliance obligations, and retention requirements  

- What is explicitly out of scope  
  - Approving, denying, scoring, ranking, terminating, or suspending a grant application or award  
  - Deciding legal eligibility, statutory interpretation, cost allowability, or enforcement posture  
  - Overriding agency, procurement, privacy, or audit requirements  
  - Altering official records without documented authority and system-of-record confirmation  
  - Treating administrative completeness as a legal or final program decision

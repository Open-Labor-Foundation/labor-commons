# Specialist Overview  
The public service delivery specialist manages workflows for public-sector service requests by classifying asks, validating records, applying documented rules, preparing evidence-backed responses, and escalating when authority boundaries or procedural changes require action. It ensures administrative requests are resolved only when official records and policy are sufficient, and otherwise routes work to the correct public-sector owner.

## Who uses this  
- Public service delivery team  
- Community program operations lead  
- Beneficiary support supervisor  
- Agency contact center manager  
- Case management operations reviewer  
- Program escalation coordinator  
- Public-sector transformation team  

## What it produces  
- Resolution note with request summary, official records checked, action taken, unresolved items, service disposition, and next owner where applicable  
- Customer or stakeholder communication summary with safe wording, evidence limits, disclosure caveats, and required next steps  
- Escalation package for exceptions, complaints, unsupported asks, missing records, rights-sensitive cases, procurement boundaries, or conflicting policies  
- Program or case-status summary grounded in system-of-record state, notices, correspondence, deadlines, and missing-item evidence  
- Eligibility or public-obligation escalation note preserving facts, sources, notice evidence, legal or adjudication owner, and unresolved ambiguity  

## Risks and mitigations  
- **Risk:** Improper escalation or communication in high-stakes public service cases could delay or misrepresent eligibility or rights.  
  **Mitigation:** All communication and resolution notes include evidence limits and disclosure caveats, and all escalations are routed to the correct legal, policy, or adjudicative owner without assuming authority.  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Covers public service delivery workflows as defined by Office of Management and Budget, U.S. Congress, and other official public-sector authority sources  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- **User or organization must supply:**  
  - Official records, system-of-record context, and jurisdictional boundaries  
  - Valid case or application identifiers  
  - Defined eligibility rules, service standards, and public obligation triggers  
  - Access to transparency logs, audit trails, and procurement references  

- **Explicitly out of scope:**  
  - Overriding policy, pricing, eligibility, appeal, procurement, records, privacy, service-standard, or compensation limits  
  - Impersonating official adjudication, enforcement, legal, contracting, public-records, privacy, audit, financial-approval, or delegated agency authority  
  - Determining benefit eligibility, program entitlement, permit issuance, enforcement disposition, procurement outcome, FOIA release, Privacy Act disclosure, statutory rights, or public obligations  
  - Providing legal advice or statutory interpretation when rights, benefits, obligations, appeals, public duties, or service access turn on legal meaning  
  - Disclosing protected, sealed, exempt, confidential, procurement-sensitive, or personally identifiable information without required authority and record basis

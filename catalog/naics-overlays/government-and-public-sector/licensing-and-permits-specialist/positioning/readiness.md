# Specialist Overview  
The licensing and permits specialist supports public-sector licensing and permitting workflows by translating statutory and policy expectations into structured, evidence-backed administrative packages. It ensures compliance with due-process, transparency, and audit-control requirements while avoiding overreach into legal or enforcement authority.

## Who uses this  
- Licensing program manager  
- Permit operations lead  
- Case management supervisor  
- Compliance operations analyst  
- Public records coordinator  
- Audit readiness lead  
- Agency transformation or service delivery team  

## What it produces  
- Control or obligation matrix tied to licensing and permit artifacts  
- Evidence packet or checklist for application completeness, permit readiness, or audit support  
- Evidence readiness or gap summary  
- Exception and remediation escalation note  
- Control and obligation summary tied to evidence owners  
- Public-service workflow handoff package preserving assumptions and authority limits  
- Adjacent-lane routing note for adjudication, enforcement, legal, procurement, public records, audit, or executive authority  

## Risks and mitigations  
- Mitigation: The specialist refuses to act if jurisdiction, statute, program, service population, case file, record location, official authority boundary, or source precedence is missing or conflicting in a way that could affect public rights, eligibility, or official action.  

## Validation  
- 13 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Source coverage includes Office of the Law Revision Counsel (U.S. House of Representatives), Federal Permitting Improvement Steering Council / Permitting Dashboard  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- **User or organization must supply:**  
  - Jurisdiction and statute context  
  - Agency policy and program details  
  - Applicant or facility information  
  - Official authority boundary and system-of-record context  
  - Public-records rules and transparency requirements  

- **Explicitly out of scope:**  
  - Providing legal advice, statutory interpretation, or regulator negotiation  
  - Issuing, denying, revoking, suspending, enforcing, waiving, or approving a license or permit  
  - Impersonating an adjudicator, inspector, hearing officer, procurement official, public-records officer, legal owner, or executive approver  
  - Deciding rights, benefits, eligibility, public obligations, penalties, appeals, or enforcement outcomes  
  - Approving procurement actions, vendor selections, contract awards, policy overrides, financial commitments, safety-critical determinations, or public-records release decisions

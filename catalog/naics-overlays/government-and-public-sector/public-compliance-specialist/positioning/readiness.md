# Specialist Overview  
The public compliance specialist translates public-sector obligations and control expectations into evidence-backed compliance handling, monitoring, and escalation. It maps statutes, program rules, records obligations, procurement requirements, and due-process documentation to controls, evidence, and review procedures, ensuring alignment with authoritative public sources and systems of record.

## Who uses this  
- Public compliance manager  
- Program compliance lead  
- Records officer  
- Procurement compliance analyst  
- Public-service operations lead  
- Grants compliance analyst  
- GRC analyst  
- Audit readiness coordinator  

## What it produces  
- Control or obligation matrix tied to public-sector obligations, evidence artifacts, systems of record, evidence owners, review cadence, and exception owners  
- Evidence readiness or gap summary for eligibility, public-service, records, procurement, privacy, transparency, due-process, grant, permit, or audit review  
- Evidence packet or checklist for internal review, audit preparation, records review, public-service workflow review, or compliance attestation preparation  
- Exception and remediation escalation note for missing evidence, control gaps, policy conflicts, source conflicts, missing audit trail, operational bypass, or rights-sensitive exception  
- Source-conflict or policy-conflict note that names affected sources, jurisdiction rules, tenant policies, official records, assumptions, and required human owner  
- Adjacent-lane routing package for program adjudication, legal, public records, procurement, audit, finance, privacy, enforcement, safety, or executive approval owners  
- Tenant-assumption log preserving the public baseline while identifying tenant-specific facts that must be retrieved before final posture  

## Risks and mitigations  
- The specialist refuses to act on requests that bypass due-process, eligibility, records retention, transparency, privacy, procurement integrity, audit trail, or authority controls. It routes these to the appropriate owner for resolution.

## Validation  
- 13 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Source coverage includes the Office of the Law Revision Counsel, U.S. House of Representatives, and the Electronic Code of Federal Regulations  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- What the user or organization must supply before this specialist can work reliably:  
  - Jurisdiction  
  - Statute  
  - Program  
  - Agency policy  
  - Service population  
  - Authority boundary  
  - Records rule  
  - System-of-record retrieval hooks  

- What is explicitly out of scope:  
  - Presenting governance support as legal advice, formal certification, licensed signoff, regulator negotiation, or official agency determination  
  - Impersonating official adjudication, enforcement, public-records release, eligibility determination, benefits decision, permit decision, procurement award, contract modification, or financial approval authority  
  - Bypassing or overriding due-process, eligibility, records retention, transparency, privacy, procurement integrity, audit trail, internal-control, funding, or approval controls  
  - Deciding eligibility, denying rights or benefits, approving exceptions, negotiating settlements, determining penalties, signing attestations, certifying compliance, releasing records, awarding vendors, or overriding agency policy  
  - Collapsing public compliance into generic compliance checklists that ignore statutes, jurisdiction, program rules, case files, official records, procurement files, notices, appeals, transparency logs, and systems of record

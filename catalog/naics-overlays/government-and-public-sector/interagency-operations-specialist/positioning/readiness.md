# Specialist Overview  
The interagency operations specialist coordinates interagency workflow dependencies, approvals, and evidence handoffs between agencies, offices, and jurisdictions to ensure public-sector work progresses without unnecessary delays. This specialist focuses on maintaining statutory and procedural boundaries while ensuring ownership clarity and evidence completeness during handoffs.

## Who uses this  
- Interagency program coordinators  
- Public-sector operations leads  
- Case workflow managers  
- Agency liaison officers  
- Records and correspondence coordinators  
- Shared-services operations teams  
- Spec-pack reviewers  

## What it produces  
- Interagency handoff summary with current owner, receiving agency, authority basis, required artifacts, missing prerequisites, due dates, and unresolved blockers  
- Dependency or blocker log with owner, system of record, evidence reference, stale state, escalation threshold, and next action  
- Routing confirmation note naming prior owner, next owner, handoff rule, records transferred, privacy or records caveats, and closure confirmation method  
- Referral packet index with case-file records, correspondence, notices, approvals, statutory or policy references, and audit-trail links  
- Escalation note for blocked approvals, jurisdiction conflicts, missing records, source conflicts, rights-sensitive exceptions, policy conflicts, or authority ambiguity  
- Boundary-safe refusal note and adjacent-specialist handoff for adjudication, enforcement, legal, public records, procurement, audit, or fiscal approval requests  

## Risks and mitigations  
- **Risk:** Informal resolution of jurisdictional or statutory conflicts could lead to invalid decisions or legal exposure.  
  **Mitigation:** The specialist refuses to resolve such conflicts and escalates them to the appropriate authority or specialist for formal adjudication.  
- **Risk:** Incomplete handoffs or missing case records may lead to stalled operations or non-compliance.  
  **Mitigation:** The specialist validates all required records and prerequisites before routing work and escalates missing items for resolution.  
- **Risk:** Improper assignment of ownership or authority could violate due-process or audit requirements.  
  **Mitigation:** The specialist adheres strictly to handoff rules, memoranda of understanding, interagency agreements, and agency routing matrices.  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Source coverage includes U.S. Fire Administration, Federal Emergency Management Agency, Acquisition.gov, Office of the Law Revision Counsel, U.S. House of Representatives, U.S. Department of the Treasury, and Bureau of the Fiscal Service  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- What the user or organization must supply before this specialist can work reliably:  
  - Jurisdictional and program context  
  - Statutory or policy authority basis  
  - Case identifier and system-of-record location  
  - Receiving agency and owner confirmation  
  - Complete case-file records, including eligibility, rights-sensitive flags, and due-process constraints  
- What is explicitly out of scope:  
  - Ownership or decision-making over benefits, permits, licenses, enforcement, procurement, funding, legal rulings, or public-records determinations  
  - Impersonation of official adjudication, enforcement, or fiscal authority  
  - Resolution of jurisdictional, eligibility, or statutory conflicts through informal synthesis  
  - Bypassing required case records, referral authority, or audit-trail requirements to move a work item  
  - Providing legal interpretation, fiscal-law advice, procurement source selection, or public-records determinations

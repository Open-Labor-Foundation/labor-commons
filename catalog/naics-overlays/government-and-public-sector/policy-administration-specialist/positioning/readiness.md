# Specialist Overview  
The policy administration specialist translates public-sector policy, program, records, procurement, access, and control obligations into evidence-backed administrative handling, monitoring, escalation, and remediation packaging while preserving official authority boundaries for adjudication, enforcement, legal interpretation, public disclosure, and approval decisions.

## Who uses this  
- Public-program operations analysts  
- Policy administration teams  
- Case management and records coordinators  
- Public-service compliance analysts  
- Procurement administration support teams  
- Audit and internal-control coordinators  

## What it produces  
- Document or records completeness summary with missing artifacts, official record references, owner, due date, and source-of-truth status  
- Administrative status note with missing items, ownership, due dates, blocked status, and required next retrievals  
- Record update note with authorized change basis, system-of-record reference, timestamp, actor, and audit-trail link  
- Retention, access, privacy, public-records, or governance escalation package  
- Control and obligation summary tied to evidence owners, control frequency, review cadence, exception state, and remediation owner  
- Exception and remediation note for control gaps, missing evidence, source conflicts, or policy/practice conflicts  
- Document or evidence packet for adjudication, public records, procurement, legal, audit, or program-owner handoff  
- Audit-trail or exception summary  

## Risks and mitigations  
- When a request involves determining the outcome of a case, benefit, permit, procurement award, or public-records disclosure, the specialist refuses to proceed and routes to the correct authority.  
- When a request involves legal interpretation or due-process determinations, the specialist refuses to proceed and routes to the legal or program adjudication specialist.  
- When a request involves overriding official records, the specialist refuses unless explicit administrative authority is provided and the action remains non-adjudicative.  

## Validation  
- 14 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Source coverage includes 8 authoritative government reference systems, including the U.S. Code of Federal Regulations, Acquisition.gov, and the Office of Management and Budget  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- What the user or organization must supply before this specialist can work reliably:  
  - Minimum tenant facts, jurisdiction, program authority, tenant policies, authoritative records, source context, and systems-of-record references  
- What is explicitly out of scope:  
  - Deciding the underlying case, claim, benefit, permit, license, procurement award, public-records disclosure, enforcement action, or legal outcome  
  - Impersonating official adjudication, enforcement, hearing, procurement award, legal, public-records officer, Privacy Act officer, or regulator-facing authority  
  - Approving, denying, suspending, revoking, publishing, disclosing, destroying, backdating, deleting, or overriding official records unless explicit delegated administrative authority is provided and the action remains non-adjudicative  
  - Resolving statutory interpretation, eligibility exceptions, due-process disputes, Title VI or equity complaints, FOIA/Privacy Act determinations, procurement protests, audit findings, or policy conflicts informally  
  - Continuing when required tenant facts, official records, authoritative source context, or source-of-truth precedence are missing and would change the outcome  
  - Collapsing public-sector policy administration into generic compliance support that ignores official authority, public eligibility, records transparency, procurement, due-process, and auditability constraints

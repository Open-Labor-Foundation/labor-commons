# Specialist Overview  
The KYC and onboarding specialist validates whether a customer, member, account, policy owner, merchant, or business applicant can proceed from application and setup into controlled activation by ensuring customer identification, due diligence, sanctions screening, and other prerequisites are complete and traceable. It operates within the financial-services industry overlay and adheres to KYC and onboarding-specific terminology and controls.

## Who uses this  
- KYC operations analysts  
- Onboarding operations managers  
- Deposit or payments implementation teams  
- Policy administration onboarding teams  
- Compliance operations reviewers  
- Workflow orchestrators  

## What it produces  
- Onboarding checklist with completion status by prerequisite  
- Readiness summary showing blockers, risks, and pending approvals  
- Go-live handoff note describing what is live, what is deferred, and who owns next steps  
- Intake or access disposition summary  
- Prerequisite or eligibility escalation note  

## Risks and mitigations  
- Refuses to approve account opening, underwriting, policy issuance, or sanctions clearance in-lane to avoid legal or compliance misjudgment  
- Avoids overstating readiness or approval authority in checklists, summaries, or handoffs  
- Routes blocked or conflicting cases to fraud, AML, legal/compliance, or servicing specialists to ensure proper escalation  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Source coverage includes Federal Financial Institutions Examination Council and Financial Crimes Enforcement Network guidance  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
### What the user or organization must supply before this specialist can work reliably  
- Application, account, policy, party, or case identifiers  
- KYC artifacts, sanctions-screening results, and beneficial ownership documentation  
- System setup and access records  
- Approval history and role grants  

### What is explicitly out of scope  
- Approving account opening, underwriting, policy issuance, or sanctions clearance  
- Providing legal interpretation of BSA, sanctions, consumer-protection, insurance, privacy, or contract requirements  
- Fabricating documentary verification, beneficial ownership conclusions, or approval evidence  
- Treating onboarding completion as proof of downstream compliance or servicing readiness  
- Claiming a customer or account is safe to activate when records conflict or are incomplete

# Specialist Overview  
The wealth operations specialist handles the execution of wealth and custody operations within the financial services industry, moving requests from intake through validation, instruction review, asset and cash movement, settlement, and completion with strong controls on evidence and approvals.

## Who uses this  
- Wealth operations associates  
- Custody and brokerage operations managers  
- Cashiering and money movement teams  
- Client service operations leads  
- Reconciliation and control teams  
- Operations orchestrators  

## What it produces  
- Execution status update with lifecycle stage and next action  
- Completion or fulfillment record showing what was done and what evidence supports closure  
- Exception summary for blocked, returned, or partially completed work  
- Operations status summary with settlement, custody, or treasury blockers  
- Escalation note for approval, reconciliation, fraud, AML, or client-impact exceptions  

## Risks and mitigations  
- Refuses actions requiring legal, licensed, policy-override, financial-approval, or safety-critical authority outside the lane.  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Source coverage includes Financial Industry Regulatory Authority, Depository Trust & Clearing Corporation, U.S. Securities and Exchange Commission, and Electronic Code of Federal Regulations / U.S. Securities and Exchange Commission  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- What the user or organization must supply before this specialist can work reliably:  
  - Accepted intake with prerequisite validation  
  - Minimum tenant facts, authoritative records, and source context  
  - Execution state and evidence of custody, settlement, or approval  
- What is explicitly out of scope:  
  - Investment advice, suitability determinations, portfolio recommendations, or fiduciary guidance  
  - Legal interpretation, final compliance signoff, SAR filing decisions, or fraud adjudication  
  - Unauthorized disbursement approvals, policy overrides, or completion claims without custody or settlement evidence  
  - Adjudication of fraud, AML, SAR, legal, or policy-interpretation outcomes  
  - Marking work complete when settlement, custody, approval, or reconciliation evidence is missing or contradictory

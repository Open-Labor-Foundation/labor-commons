# Specialist Overview  
The payments operations specialist handles the day-to-day processing and review of incoming and outgoing payments, including remittance validation, posting readiness, suspense investigation, return handling, reconciliation, and exception escalation. This specialist ensures traceable, evidence-backed billing and posting outputs while maintaining compliance with consumer protection, fraud, AML, and audit standards.

## Who uses this  
- Payments-operations-analyst  
- Payment-exceptions-lead  
- Cash-application-analyst  
- Reconciliation-analyst  
- Claims-disbursement-operations-lead  
- Premium-billing-operations-lead  
- Operations-reviewer  

## What it produces  
- Billing, claim, invoice, or posting summary with source traceability  
- Reconciliation note showing matched items, exceptions, and unresolved variances  
- Financial exception escalation for denials, disputes, write-offs, or unsupported adjustments  
- Billing or financial reconciliation summary with root-cause categories  
- Revenue exception escalation note  

## Risks and mitigations  
- Refuses to approve write-offs, reclassify financial treatment, make fraud or AML determinations, or declare a payment final without complete and non-conflicting source records.  
- Does not override consumer-impact or exception-handling controls without proper authority.  
- Escalates unsupported adjustments or policy-interpretation conflicts for human review.  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Source coverage includes payment-intake, remittance, return, dispute, denial, suspense, approval-history, and reconciliation records  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
### What the user or organization must supply before this specialist can work reliably  
- Minimum tenant facts and authoritative records  
- Source context for each transaction or posting  
- Access to systems of record for comparison and validation  
- Defined routing rules for exceptions and escalations  

### What is explicitly out of scope  
- Approving write-offs or reclassifying financial treatment without authority  
- Making fraud or AML determinations  
- Providing legal interpretation or underwriting decisions  
- Declaring a payment final when source records are incomplete or conflicting  
- Inventing adjustments, codes, or payment status without source evidence

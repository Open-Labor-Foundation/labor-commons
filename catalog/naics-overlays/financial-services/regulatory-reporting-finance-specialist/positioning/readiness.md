# Specialist Overview  
The Regulatory Reporting Finance Specialist translates regulatory reporting obligations and financial evidence into traceable report-preparation support for financial institutions, ensuring readiness and control mapping without crossing into legal, compliance, or final attestation functions.  

## Who uses this  
- Regulatory reporting analysts  
- Finance and controllership teams  
- Financial controls managers  
- Reconciliation analysts  
- Regulatory reporting program managers  
- Workflow orchestrators  

## What it produces  
- Regulatory reporting financial-readiness summary with source traceability  
- Reconciliation note with matched records, edit exceptions, unresolved variances, and residual risk  
- Financial exception escalation packet for unsupported adjustments, amendment blockers, or filing exceptions  
- Control and obligation summary tied to evidence owners, signoff dependencies, and review cadence  
- Exception and remediation note for control gaps, missing workpapers, or policy conflicts  

## Risks and mitigations  
- Refuses to provide or validate reportable balances, adjustments, or signoffs not supported by named source records  
- Refuses to determine final accounting policy, legal interpretation, or reportability when authoritative instructions or tenant policy conflict  
- Refuses to sign, certify, or submit regulator-facing filings as if it were the CFO, controller, or attestation owner  
- Routes fraud, AML, consumer-protection, or policy-interpretation issues to appropriate specialists when they affect reporting outcomes  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Regulatory guidance from Office of the Comptroller of the Currency, FFIEC, Federal Reserve, and NAIC is the primary source coverage  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- What the user or organization must supply:  
  - Minimum tenant facts, reporting regime, reporting entity, and reporting period  
  - Systems of record and authoritative public source set  
  - Retrieved balances, subledger activity, workpapers, edits, approvals, and supporting documents  
- What is explicitly out of scope:  
  - Legal advice, regulator-facing interpretation, or formal compliance or accounting signoff  
  - Approval of accounting policy elections, top-side entries, unsupported write-offs, or reportable-balance overrides  
  - Inventing line definitions, consolidation treatment, reserve logic, or disclosure conclusions without authoritative source support and tenant authority  
  - Signing, certifying, or submitting regulator-facing filings as if the lane were the final attestation owner  
  - Clearing fraud, AML, sanctions, or consumer-harm indicators that materially change the reporting answer  
  - Stating a filing package is ready when reconciliations, edit resolution, workpaper support, or review evidence remain materially incomplete

# Specialist Overview  
The financial services product operations specialist focuses on financial product servicing, payment, billing, and reconciliation workflows. It ensures that operational summaries, reconciliation notes, and exception escalations are based on authoritative records and supported by traceable evidence, without overstepping into underwriting, legal, or accounting domains.

## Who uses this  
- Product-operations-analyst  
- Servicing-operations-specialist  
- Payment-operations-analyst  
- Premium-billing-operations-lead  
- Reconciliation-analyst  
- Claims-or-servicing-queue-manager  
- Reviewer  

## What it produces  
- Billing, claim, invoice, or posting summary with source traceability  
- Reconciliation note showing matched items, exceptions, and unresolved variances  
- Financial exception escalation for denials, disputes, write-offs, unsupported adjustments, or suspicious activity  
- Servicing or product-workflow summary with exceptions, approvals, and next owner  
- Escalation note for risk, consumer-impact, or policy-sensitive issues  

## Risks and mitigations  
- Refuses unsupported overrides, back-dated adjustments, disclosure shortcuts, or record changes that require regulated, licensed, or approval authority  
- Does not act on requests without sufficient source documentation or traceability  
- Routes high-risk or out-of-scope issues to the appropriate specialist or human reviewer  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Source coverage includes 8 authoritative references from the Consumer Financial Protection Bureau and FFIEC BSA/AML InfoBase  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- What the user or organization must supply before this specialist can work reliably  
  - Minimum tenant facts  
  - Authoritative records  
  - Public-source context  
- What is explicitly out of scope  
  - Inventing adjustments, codes, accounting treatment, coverage positions, or payment authority  
  - Fabricating approvals, fraud conclusions, underwriting decisions, consumer-regulatory determinations, or legal advice  
  - Bypassing required disclosures, notice obligations, fraud-review gates, or record-retention controls  
  - Treating banking, lending, payments, and insurance servicing rules as interchangeable without tenant-approved product and jurisdiction mapping  
  - Resolving material conflicts between public guidance, product terms, and tenant policy without human review

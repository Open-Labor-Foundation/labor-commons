# Specialist Overview  
The financial services compliance specialist translates financial-services compliance obligations and control expectations into evidence-backed billing, posting, reconciliation, exception, and escalation outputs. It ensures that all actions are grounded in authoritative records and regulatory requirements, and it refuses to act on unsupported claims or without clear source traceability.

## Who uses this  
- Compliance operations teams  
- Billing and reconciliation analysts  
- Servicing and claims operations leads  
- Risk and controls owners  
- Workflow orchestrators  

## What it produces  
- Billing, claim, invoice, or posting summary with source traceability  
- Reconciliation note with matched records, exceptions, unresolved variances, and residual risk  
- Financial exception escalation for denials, disputes, write-offs, or unsupported adjustments  
- Control and obligation summary tied to evidence owners and review cadence  
- Exception and remediation note for control gaps, policy conflicts, or source mismatches  

## Risks and mitigations  
- Refuses to provide legal advice, regulator-facing interpretation, final claims adjudication, or policy overrides.  
- Blocks actions when fraud, AML, sanctions, or consumer-protection signals require adjacent specialists.  
- Requires material records and policy context before closing issues.  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Source coverage includes 10 — Consumer Financial Protection Bureau / eCFR  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
**What the user or organization must supply before this specialist can work reliably:**  
- Minimum tenant facts  
- Authoritative records and systems of record  
- Public authority sources relevant to the request  

**What is explicitly out of scope:**  
- Providing legal advice or regulator-facing interpretation  
- Approving credit, underwriting, final claims payment, fee waivers, write-offs, or policy overrides  
- Inventing accounting treatment, tax treatment, reimbursement treatment, or disclosure conclusions without source support and authority  
- Clearing fraud, AML, sanctions, or consumer-harm signals without the required adjacent owner  
- Stating that a matter is resolved when reconciliation, record truth, or policy precedence remains materially unresolved

# Specialist Overview  
The lending operations specialist owns day-to-day loan-servicing and lending-operations workflows, focusing on loan boarding, payment application, suspense and unapplied-funds review, escrow and payoff support, servicing-transfer readiness, and audit-ready completion evidence. This specialist works within clearly defined lending terminology and processes, ensuring accuracy and regulatory compliance without stepping into underwriting, legal, or policy-override decisions.

## Who uses this  
- Loan operations analyst  
- Mortgage servicing analyst  
- Consumer lending operations lead  
- Boarding and transfer specialist  
- Payment processing specialist  
- Payoff and release coordinator  
- Operations reviewer  

## What it produces  
- Execution status update with lifecycle stage, evidence used, and next action  
- Completion or fulfillment record showing what was done and what evidence supports closure  
- Exception summary for blocked, returned, reworked, or partially completed lending work  
- Lending-servicing workflow summary with payment, payoff, escrow, or transfer blockers  
- Escalation note for fraud, AML, consumer-impact, disclosure, or policy-sensitive issues  

## Risks and mitigations  
- Refuses to approve underwriting, legal actions, or policy overrides to prevent unsupported authority  
- Requires authoritative records and source context before executing or escalating actions  
- Routes work to fraud, AML, legal/compliance, or loss-mitigation specialists when authority shifts  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Source coverage includes 9 authority sources, primarily from the Consumer Financial Protection Bureau and Fannie Mae  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- What the user or organization must supply before this specialist can work reliably  
  - Minimum tenant facts and authoritative records  
  - Loan servicing, payment, document, collateral, approval history, and communication records  
  - Delegated authority for actions like lien release or fee waiver  
- What is explicitly out of scope  
  - Approving underwriting, credit, pricing, modification, settlement, charge-off, delinquency treatment, or policy override decisions  
  - Providing legal interpretation, foreclosure or bankruptcy direction, regulated financial advice, or fraud or AML disposition  
  - Waiving fees, backdating payment crediting, releasing liens, or marking work complete without authoritative evidence and delegated authority  
  - Treating partial, stale, or conflicting servicing records as final loan or transaction truth

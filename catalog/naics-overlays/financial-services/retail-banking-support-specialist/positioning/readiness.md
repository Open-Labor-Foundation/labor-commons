# Specialist Overview  
The Retail Banking Support Specialist handles service, support, and issue-resolution workflows specific to retail banking, including deposit accounts, debit cards, ACH transactions, and account-maintenance requests, while staying within defined servicing authority and avoiding legal, fraud, or compliance adjudication.

## Who uses this  
- Retail-banking-service-team  
- Deposit-operations-supervisor  
- Branch-support-lead  
- Card-and-payments-servicing-team  
- Complaint-resolution-team  
- Operations-reviewer  

## What it produces  
- Resolution note with request summary, action taken, authoritative evidence used, and unresolved items  
- Customer or stakeholder communication summary  
- Escalation package for exceptions, complaints, unsupported asks, suspicious-activity concerns, or policy conflicts  
- Retail-banking workflow summary with account, transaction, disclosure, or reconciliation blockers  
- Request-resolution summary with issue, action taken, blocker state, and next owner  

## Risks and mitigations  
- Explicitly refuses to provide legal interpretation, fraud adjudication, or AML decisions, ensuring that high-stakes regulatory and compliance functions remain with the correct specialists.

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Source coverage includes 8 authority documents from the Consumer Financial Protection Bureau, Board of Governors of the Federal Reserve System, and Office of the Comptroller of the Currency  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- **What must be supplied by the user or organization:**  
  - Minimum tenant facts, authoritative records, and source context  
  - Valid identifiers for account, customer, card, transaction, hold, case, or complaint  
  - Access to core-banking, card-processing, ACH, servicing-note, and communication records  

- **What is explicitly out of scope:**  
  - Providing legal interpretation or regulated financial advice  
  - Overriding fee reversals, hold periods, dispute outcomes, KYC outcomes, fraud controls, or account-approval rules  
  - Adjudicating fraud, AML alerts, SAR decisions, underwriting, or credit decisions  
  - Confirming or disclosing SAR status or suspicious-activity investigation activity  
  - Stating that provisional credit, funds availability, or complaint resolution is final without authoritative record support

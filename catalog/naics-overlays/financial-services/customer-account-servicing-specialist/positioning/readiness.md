# Specialist Overview  
The customer account servicing specialist handles customer-facing financial services work for existing accounts, using authoritative records to resolve servicing, maintenance, and exception routing. The specialist ensures communication, resolution, and escalation are grounded in documented account, payment, and servicing data, and stays within the boundaries of financial services and customer service roles.

## Who uses this  
- Customer servicing teams  
- Account maintenance specialists  
- Loan and payment servicing teams  
- Policy servicing and customer advocacy teams  
- Complaint-resolution owners  
- Operations reviewers  

## What it produces  
- Resolution note with request summary, action taken, authoritative evidence used, and unresolved items  
- Customer or stakeholder communication summary  
- Escalation package for exceptions, complaints, unsupported asks, suspicious-activity concerns, or policy conflicts  
- Request-resolution summary with issue, action taken, blocker state, and next owner  
- Exception communication escalation note  

## Risks and mitigations  
- Explicitly refuses to provide legal interpretation, underwriting, claims adjudication, or override financial decisions outside documented authority  
- Refuses to disclose SAR status, fraud outcomes, or internal AML reviews  
- Requires authoritative records to be complete and current before making final conclusions  
- Does not treat incomplete or conflicting records as final truth  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Source coverage includes core banking, loan-servicing, policy administration, payment, CRM, and case-management records  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- What the user or organization must supply before this specialist can work reliably  
  - Minimum tenant facts and authoritative records  
  - Contextual details tied to account, policy, transaction, or communication identifiers  
  - Access to relevant systems for record retrieval and comparison  
- What is explicitly out of scope  
  - Providing legal interpretation or regulated financial advice  
  - Approving fee waivers, hardship concessions, or account reopenings  
  - Disclosing SAR status or internal fraud or AML outcomes  
  - Adjudicating fraud, AML alerts, or claims payments  
  - Overriding policy, pricing, or eligibility decisions outside the documented authority matrix

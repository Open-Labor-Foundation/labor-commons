# Specialist Overview  
The brokerage operations specialist manages day-to-day broker-dealer operations work, including account and instruction validation, maintenance, customer transfers, cashiering, trade-support exceptions, confirmation and disclosure follow-up, settlement, reconciliation, and completion-evidence capture. The specialist operates within strict industry-specific controls to ensure compliance with regulatory and operational standards.

## Who uses this  
- Brokerage operations associates  
- Transfer and cashiering operations teams  
- Trade support analysts  
- Broker-dealer operations managers  
- Reconciliation and control teams  
- Operations orchestrators  

## What it produces  
- Execution status update with lifecycle stage and next action  
- Completion or fulfillment record showing what was done and what evidence supports closure  
- Exception summary for blocked, returned, or partially completed work  
- Operations status summary with settlement, cashiering, or brokerage blockers  
- Escalation note for approval, reconciliation, fraud, AML, disclosure, or client-impact exceptions  

## Risks and mitigations  
- Actions requiring legal, licensed, policy-override, financial-approval, or safety-critical authority are explicitly refused.  
- All escalations include named broker-dealer records, approval histories, and systems of record to ensure accountability.  
- Work is held or returned when evidence is missing or contradictory, preventing unsupported risk determinations.  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Regulatory and operational coverage includes FINRA, DTCC, SEC, and FINRA rulebooks  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- **What the user or organization must supply:**  
  - Minimum tenant facts  
  - Authoritative records  
  - Source context for the request  
- **What is explicitly out of scope:**  
  - Investment advice, recommendations, suitability judgments, or front-office trade decisions  
  - Legal interpretation, final compliance signoff, AML or fraud disposition, or SAR filing decisions  
  - Unsupported money movement approvals, margin or credit exception approvals, or completion claims without authoritative records  
  - Adjudication of fraud, AML, SAR, legal, or policy-interpretation outcomes  
  - Approval of disbursements or hold releases without explicit tenant authority

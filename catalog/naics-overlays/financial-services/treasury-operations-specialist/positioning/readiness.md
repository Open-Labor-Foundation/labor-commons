# Specialist Overview  
Treasury operations specialist manages the execution of treasury operations tasks such as cash positioning, payment processing, settlement, custody, and reconciliation, ensuring all actions are evidence-backed, audit-ready, and aligned with approval controls and settlement discipline. It does not cross into legal, compliance, or strategic decision-making functions.

## Who uses this  
- Treasury operations analyst  
- Cash management operations lead  
- Payments operations specialist  
- Custody operations analyst  
- Securities settlement operations lead  
- Reconciliation analyst  
- Reviewer  

## What it produces  
- Execution status update with lifecycle stage, evidence references, and next action  
- Completion or fulfillment record showing what was done and what evidence supports closure  
- Exception summary for blocked, returned, reworked, or partially completed work  
- Operations status summary with treasury, settlement, funding, or custody blockers  
- Escalation note for approval, reconciliation, fraud, AML, sanctions, disclosure, or client-impact exceptions  

## Risks and mitigations  
- Refuses to approve overrides, authorize exceptions beyond thresholds, or declare completion when system-of-record evidence is incomplete or conflicting.  
- Maintains clear boundaries with legal, compliance, and strategy functions to prevent overreach.  
- Routes exceptions and escalations to the appropriate specialist or authority.  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Covers U.S. Federal Reserve and FFIEC regulatory frameworks  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- Must be provided with tenant-specific facts, authoritative records, and context before proceeding.  
- Cannot act on incomplete, conflicting, or unverified system-of-record data.  
- Cannot fabricate approvals, callback confirmations, or reconciliation resolution.  
- Cannot provide investment, legal, tax, or accounting advice.  
- Cannot declare transactions complete without material agreement across general ledger, cash accounts, and custody records.

# Specialist Overview  
The loan servicing specialist manages loan-servicing workflows by ensuring accurate intake, evidence-backed resolution, and disciplined escalation of servicing requests. This specialist operates within the boundaries of loan servicing, using domain-specific terminology and rules to maintain borrower-impact controls and traceability, while avoiding overreach into legal, underwriting, or collections authority.

## Who uses this  
- Loan-servicing-specialist  
- Mortgage-servicing-analyst  
- Default-servicing-coordinator  
- Payment-processing-analyst  
- Loss-mitigation-support-analyst  
- Borrower-communications-reviewer  
- Operations-supervisor  

## What it produces  
- Resolution note with request summary, action taken, authoritative evidence used, and unresolved items  
- Customer or stakeholder communication summary  
- Escalation package for exceptions, complaints, unsupported asks, suspicious-activity concerns, or policy conflicts  
- Loan-servicing workflow summary with payment, escrow, delinquency, loss-mitigation, or disclosure blockers  
- Escalation note for risk, consumer-impact, policy-sensitive, bankruptcy, or foreclosure-sensitive issues  

## Risks and mitigations  
- Explicit refusal rules prevent the specialist from approving modifications, deferrals, forbearances, or legal conclusions that require licensed or policy-override authority.  
- Servicing decisions are grounded in authoritative records and tenant policy to avoid unsupported promises or overreach.  
- Escalation pathways are maintained for high-risk scenarios such as suspicious activity, delinquency cures, or policy conflicts.  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Source coverage includes loan-servicing rules and tenant policy from the Consumer Financial Protection Bureau  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- What the user or organization must supply before this specialist can work reliably:  
  - Minimum tenant facts  
  - Authoritative servicing records  
  - Payment and escrow history  
  - Contextual communication logs  
- What is explicitly out of scope:  
  - Approving or waiving loan modifications, deferments, forbearances, or credit reporting outcomes  
  - Providing legal interpretation of RESPA, TILA, SCRA, or state servicing law  
  - Overriding payment application, delinquency status, or approval thresholds  
  - Making fraud, AML, or underwriting determinations  
  - Adjudicating credit bureau outcomes or final loss-mitigation approvals

# Specialist Overview  
A learning management systems specialist ensures education and research workflows involving LMS platforms remain usable, secure, and compliant by triaging incidents, managing access, validating readiness, and tracking dependencies without overstepping instructional, legal, or financial authority.

## Who uses this  
- Learning technology administrators  
- Instructional platform coordinators  
- Registrar and compliance operations staff  
- Research administration support personnel  
- Integration and support teams  

## What it produces  
- Onboarding checklist with completion status by prerequisite  
- Readiness summary showing blockers, risks, and pending approvals  
- Go-live handoff note with live/deferred scope and owners  
- System incident or service summary with impact and owner  
- Change-readiness or integration-risk escalation note  

## Risks and mitigations  
- Refuses actions requiring legal, licensed, policy-override, financial-approval, or safety-critical authority outside its lane.  
- Detects missing testing, rollback, or approval evidence before permitting change readiness claims.  
- Routes work to adjacent specialists or human reviewers when ownership or authority shifts.  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Source coverage includes U.S. Department of Education, Office for Human Research Protections, and IMS Global Learning Consortium  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- **What must be supplied:** Minimum tenant facts, authoritative records, and source context to continue safely.  
- **What is out of scope:**  
  - Impersonating instructor, reviewer, or ethics-board authority.  
  - Making final grading, transcript, instructional judgment, ethics, or legal determinations.  
  - Bypassing documented testing, rollback, or approval requirements for system changes.  
  - Owning financial approvals, policy exceptions, or formal accreditation determinations.

# Specialist Overview  
The Registrar Operations Specialist coordinates institution-facing operational registrar workflows in education by verifying intake state, program/record completeness, and approval gates, then generating execution-ready disposition and escalation outputs with traceable evidence.

## Who uses this  
- Registrar operations teams  
- Institution operations support  
- Student information administrators  
- Research administration assistants  
- Workflow orchestrators  

## What it produces  
- Execution status update with lifecycle stage and next action  
- Completion or fulfillment evidence record  
- Exception summary for blocked, returned, or partially completed work  
- Institution workflow summary with records, due dates, and owner state  
- Escalation note for grant, registrar, or participant-data conflicts  

## Risks and mitigations  
- Refuses actions requiring legal, licensed, policy-override, financial-approval, or safety-critical authority outside the lane.  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Source coverage includes U.S. Department of Education / U.S. Code of Federal Regulations, U.S. Department of Health and Human Services / U.S. Code of Federal Regulations, Office for Human Research Protections, U.S. Office of Management and Budget  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- What the user or organization must supply before this specialist can work reliably:  
  - Minimum tenant facts  
  - Authoritative records  
  - Source context required for safe action  
- What is explicitly out of scope:  
  - Making grading, transcript, or instructional decisions  
  - Impersonating ethics board, clinical, legal, or policy-override authority  
  - Approving financial disbursements or licensed approvals outside tenant or documented authority  
  - Marking work complete without evidence packet closure and approved owner state

# Specialist Overview  
The Education Analytics Specialist translates education-related data signals—such as learner records, grants, faculty roles, and accreditation status—into onboarding readiness assessments, metric definitions, and evidence-based analysis. It ensures workflows meet prerequisites and align with operational authority boundaries, while preparing handoff guidance for go-live decisions.  

## Who uses this  
- Education analytics coordinators  
- Student information teams  
- Registrar operations staff  
- Faculty and instructor support staff  
- Research administration support  
- Accreditation and grants operations  

## What it produces  
- Onboarding checklist with completion status by prerequisite  
- Readiness summary showing blockers, risks, and pending approvals  
- Go-live handoff note showing what is live, what is deferred, and owners  
- Analytical brief with metric definitions, data-source assumptions, and confidence caveats  
- Exception note for data-quality, denominator ambiguity, or unsupported trend claims  

## Risks and mitigations  
- Refuses to act on or generate conclusions requiring legal, licensed, ethics-board, or financial approval authority.  
- Requires explicit validation of source records before proceeding.  
- Flags unsupported trend or forecast claims when data does not justify them.  
- Routes requests outside its authority to adjacent specialists or human reviewers.  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Source coverage includes U.S. Department of Education, Office for Civil Rights, and Office for Human Research Protections  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- What the user or organization must supply before this specialist can work reliably  
  - Minimum tenant facts and authoritative records  
  - Clear source-of-truth context for all data signals  
  - Evidence of completed setup, training, and credentialing prerequisites  
- What is explicitly out of scope  
  - Grading, transcript, or instructional judgments  
  - Ethics-board determinations or licensing legal approvals  
  - Bypassing setup, permissions, testing, or rollback evidence requirements  
  - Final accreditation, grant-compliance, or clinical/financial authority outcomes

# Specialist Overview  
A Student services specialist coordinates and validates support workflows for student and research administration in educational and research institutions. It ensures requests are in-scope, prerequisites are met, and outcomes are either resolved or escalated with traceability, without assuming authority over academic, legal, or financial decisions.

## Who uses this  
- Student support coordinators  
- Student affairs teams  
- Faculty liaisons  
- Research administration assistants  
- Operations and compliance owners  

## What it produces  
- Resolution note with disposition state, confidence, and boundary rationale  
- Customer or stakeholder communication summary with required next-step owner and timeline  
- Escalation package for exception/ethics/approval requests and unsupported workflow demands  
- Workflow summary covering participant, schedule, records, and event-delivery status  
- Source and evidence traceability notes for all mandatory outputs  

## Risks and mitigations  
- Refuses or defers requests involving grading, final instructional authority, ethics-board decisions, legal interpretation, or financial approval to prevent overstepping authority.  
- Blocks risky requests that lack sufficient evidence or violate policy.  
- Emits explicit handoffs for decisions requiring registrar, faculty, or compliance authority.  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Source coverage includes U.S. Department of Education, U.S. Code of Federal Regulations, U.S. Office of Management and Budget, U.S. Department of Health and Human Services, and Office for Human Research Protections  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- Requires current and accessible student, program, faculty, and grant records to operate  
- Requires clear tenant policy context and authorization dependencies to validate scope  
- Cannot provide final grading, transcript determination, instructor feedback, or final academic judgments  
- Cannot act as IRB chair, ethics-review authority, or safety-critical compliance adjudicator  
- Cannot issue financial/disbursement decisions or formal policy overrides without explicit authority  
- Cannot assume tenant-specific policy overrides when absent

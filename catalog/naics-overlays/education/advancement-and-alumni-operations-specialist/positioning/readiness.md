# Specialist Overview  
The Advancement and alumni operations specialist manages Advancement and alumni workflows in the education and research overlay, ensuring compliance with institutional policies and regulatory requirements by tracking, validating, and escalating workflows with clear evidence and boundaries.

## Who uses this  
- Advancement operations teams  
- Alumni relations coordinators  
- Student affairs and communications operations staff  
- Research administration and grants teams  
- Workflow orchestrators  

## What it produces  
- Execution status update with lifecycle stage and next action  
- Completion or fulfillment evidence record  
- Exception summary for blocked, returned, or partially completed work  
- Institution workflow summary with records, due dates, and owner state  
- Escalation note for grant, registrar, research, or participant-data conflicts  

## Risks and mitigations  
- Actions requiring legal, licensed, policy-override, financial-approval, safety-critical, grading, or ethics-board authority are explicitly refused to avoid overstepping authority.  
- Work is not marked complete without auditable completion evidence and approved owner confirmation to ensure accountability.  
- Sensitive participant data is not processed without consent or source-authority confirmation to preserve privacy and compliance.  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Draws from U.S. Department of Education / U.S. Code of Federal Regulations, U.S. Department of Education - Office for Civil Rights, U.S. Department of Health and Human Services / U.S. Code of Federal Regulations, Office for Human Research Protections  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- **What the user or organization must supply before this specialist can work reliably**  
  - Minimum tenant facts, records, and source context  
  - Evidence of consent for handling sensitive participant data  
  - Approval status from relevant authorities when escalation is required  

- **What is explicitly out of scope**  
  - Grading, transcript, or instructional decisions  
  - Impersonating legal counsel, licensing authority, financial-approval approvers, or policy override boards  
  - Making decisions that require ethics-board or safety-critical authority  
  - Processing sensitive participant data without explicit consent and source confirmation

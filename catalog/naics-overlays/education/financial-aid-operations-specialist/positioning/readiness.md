# Specialist Overview  
The financial aid operations specialist manages workflows for student, faculty, and program financial aid in education and research environments. It ensures traceable billing readiness, reconciliation, and exception handling by validating authoritative records, source context, and approvals before progressing or escalating.

## Who uses this  
- Financial-aid operations teams  
- Student-services and billing coordinators  
- Research administration owners  
- Registrar and grants partners  
- Workflow orchestrators  

## What it produces  
- Billing, claim, invoice, or posting summary with source traceability  
- Reconciliation note with matched records, exceptions, unresolved variances, and residual risk  
- Financial exception escalation for denials, disputes, write-offs, or unsupported adjustments  
- Servicing or product-workflow summary with owner, approvals, exceptions, and next state  
- Escalation note for risk, consumer-impact, or policy-sensitive issues  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Covers U.S. Department of Education, U.S. Code of Federal Regulations, and Office of Federal Student Aid  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- What the user or organization must supply before this specialist can work reliably  
  - Minimum tenant facts  
  - Authoritative records  
  - Source context for the request  
- What is explicitly out of scope  
  - Deciding eligibility outcomes requiring legal, licensing, or formal aid entitlement adjudication  
  - Impersonating instructor, faculty reviewer, accreditor, ethics-board, or legal authority  
  - Overriding tenant policy, issuing financial disbursements, or authorizing write-offs without explicit tenant authority  
  - Finalizing grading, instructional remediation, or academic-judgment decisions

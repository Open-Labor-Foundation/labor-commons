# Specialist Overview  
The institutional reporting specialist coordinates and validates educational and research reporting for institutions by assembling traceable, compliant, and accurate disclosure and analysis packages. It ensures data quality, aligns metric definitions with institutional systems, and escalates when reporting claims cannot be confidently supported by source records.

## Who uses this  
- Education operations managers  
- Student information system (SIS) teams  
- Research administration staff  
- Faculty services leads  
- Accreditation and grants operations personnel  

## What it produces  
- Metric-definition and data-source summary with confidence and limits  
- Analysis or report package with caveat and reconciliation notes  
- Disclosure-risk escalation note for unsupported or under-supported claims  
- Submission or reporting package summary with source tie-outs and assumptions  
- Data-quality, conflict, and approval-block escalation output with routing owners  

## Risks and mitigations  
- Explicitly refuses or escalates when requested authority includes grading, ethics-board determination, policy override, or financial approval  
- Mitigates unsupported causal claims by requiring traceable evidence from institutional records  
- Routes to adjacent specialists for legal, ethical, or policy decisions outside its scope  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Covers U.S. Department of Education, Office for Civil Rights, and Office for Human Research Protections regulations  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- What the user or organization must supply:  
  - Access to SIS, LMS, scheduling, research, and document systems  
  - Metric definitions and source eligibility criteria  
  - Educational records, consent artifacts, study context, and approval dependencies  
- What is explicitly out of scope:  
  - Final grading or transcript outcomes  
  - Ethics-board determinations or policy sign-offs  
  - Legal, financial-approval, licensing, or safety-critical authority decisions  
  - Unsupported causal narratives or inferences not based on institutional records

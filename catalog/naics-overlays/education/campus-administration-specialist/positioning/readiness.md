# Specialist Overview  
The campus administration specialist manages administrative records, policy, and workflow operations for educational institutions within the education and research overlay. It validates the completeness of records, ensures compliance with institutional and regulatory standards, and produces traceable administrative outputs for operational decisions.

## Who uses this  
- Registrar operations teams  
- Student services analysts  
- Faculty affairs operations  
- Research administration teams  
- Institutional compliance and orchestration teams  

## What it produces  
- Document or records completeness summary with explicit missing artifacts  
- Administrative status note with next owner and due-date checkpoints  
- Retention, access, and governance escalation package  
- Program, campus, or event workflow summary with readiness and owner state  
- Escalation note for scheduling, participation, or records conflicts  

## Risks and mitigations  
- Refuses actions requiring legal, licensed, policy-override, financial-approval, or safety-critical authority outside its lane.  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Source coverage includes U.S. Department of Education, U.S. Code of Federal Regulations, Office for Civil Rights, Office for Human Research Protections, and Department of Health and Human Services  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- **What must be supplied:** Minimum tenant facts, authoritative records, source context, and compliance with privacy and governance constraints.  
- **Out of scope:**  
  - Grading, instructional judgment, or ethics-board final determinations  
  - Impersonating legal, clinical, financial, or policy-override authority  
  - Marking requests complete without required evidence, signatures, and retention alignment  
  - Bypassing consent/privacy or record-governance checks under urgency

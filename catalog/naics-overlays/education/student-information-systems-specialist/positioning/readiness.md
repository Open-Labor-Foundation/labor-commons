# Specialist Overview  
The student information systems specialist manages student information system (SIS) workflows within the education and research overlay, ensuring quality, access, and dependency readiness for enrollment, student/faculty, program, grant, study, and accreditation operations. It operates without instructional, grading, legal, or formal approval authority, focusing on readiness, traceability, and boundary control.

## Who uses this  
- Student information systems coordinator  
- Registrar operations staff  
- Faculty and advisor support personnel  
- Research administration support staff  
- Integration and quality operators  

## What it produces  
- Quality or safety status summary with explicit confidence and boundary rationale  
- Hold, release, or nonconformance output with evidence references  
- Corrective-action or incident escalation package with owners and timeline  
- System incident/service summary with impact and routing context  
- Change-readiness or integration-risk note  
- Requirement and evidence traceability matrix  

## Risks and mitigations  
- **High-stakes scenario refusal:** Will not grant instructor grading authority, transcript ownership, instructional decisions, or ethics-board final rulings. Will not make legal, safety-critical, licensed, policy-override, or financial authorization calls.  
- **Mitigation:** Refuses to act on or approve bypasses to documented release, hold, or evidence gates, and does not claim final contract, accreditation adjudication, or formal QA authority.

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Covers U.S. Department of Education / U.S. Code of Federal Regulations; U.S. Department of Health and Human Services / U.S. Code of Federal Regulations; Office for Human Research Protections; U.S. Department of Education - Office for Civil Rights  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- **User or organizational requirements:** Must supply minimum tenant facts, record context, and source context before safe action.  
- **Out of scope:**  
  - Granting instructor grading authority or transcript ownership  
  - Making instructional decisions or ethics-board final rulings  
  - Making legal, safety-critical, licensed, policy-override, or financial authorization calls  
  - Approving bypasses to documented release, hold, or evidence gates  
  - Claiming final contract, accreditation adjudication, or formal QA authority

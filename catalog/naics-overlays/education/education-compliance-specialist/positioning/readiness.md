# Specialist Overview  
The education compliance specialist translates education and research compliance obligations into evidence-backed readiness and escalation outputs for training delivery and administration, ensuring safe and complete go-live decisions by verifying records, consent, approvals, and control evidence.  

## Who uses this  
- Education operations and compliance teams  
- Registrar and onboarding operations  
- Faculty coordination owners  
- Research administration teams  
- Accreditation and grants stakeholders  

## What it produces  
- Onboarding checklist with completion status by prerequisite  
- Readiness summary showing blockers, risks, and pending approvals  
- Go-live handoff note describing what is live, what is deferred, and who owns next steps  
- Control and obligation summary tied to evidence owners  
- Exception and remediation note for control gaps or policy conflicts  

## Risks and mitigations  
- Actions requiring legal, licensed, policy-override, financial-approval, or safety-critical authority are explicitly refused and routed to the correct specialist or human reviewer.  
- Go-live readiness is only confirmed when required controls, approvals, and evidence are complete.  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Covers U.S. Department of Education, Office for Civil Rights, and Office for Human Research Protections authority sources  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
**What the user or organization must supply before this specialist can work reliably:**  
- Minimum tenant facts and authoritative records  
- Source context for compliance control obligations  
- Evidence of setup, enablement, and prerequisite completion  

**What is explicitly out of scope:**  
- Grading, transcript, instructional, or research-outcome judgments  
- Impersonating instructor, faculty reviewer, ethics-board, or legal authority  
- Making final regulator-facing, legal, or policy-override decisions  
- Claiming go-live readiness when required controls, approvals, or evidence are missing  

**Adjacent specialists:** registrar, faculty review, accreditation, grants, research compliance

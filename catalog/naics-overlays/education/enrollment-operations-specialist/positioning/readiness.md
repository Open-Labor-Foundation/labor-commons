# Specialist Overview  
The Enrollment Operations Specialist ensures that education and research workflows only begin when identity, eligibility, authorization, consent, and prerequisite conditions are met. It validates intake, checks access, and routes work to the appropriate specialists or approvers, ensuring compliance with policy and regulatory standards.

## Who uses this  
- Registrar operations staff  
- Student services analysts  
- Faculty affairs coordinators  
- Research administration personnel  
- Work orchestration teams  

## What it produces  
- Intake disposition note with acceptance, rejection, or routing outcome  
- Prerequisite or eligibility checklist showing missing or satisfied conditions  
- Intake or access disposition summary  
- Escalation packet for approval, access, identity, or source-ambiguity blocks  
- Prerequisite or eligibility escalation note when confidence remains low  

## Risks and mitigations  
- Refuses actions requiring legal, policy-override, financial-approval, licensed, or safety-critical authority to avoid overstepping scope  
- Ensures no bypassing of consent/FERPA, eligibility, approval, or access checks to prevent unauthorized routing  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Covers U.S. Department of Education, Office for Civil Rights, and Office for Human Research Protections regulations  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- Requires supply of tenant facts, authoritative records, and source context to operate  
- Does not make grading, transcript, instructional planning, or academic sanction decisions  
- Does not impersonate ethics board, legal, licensed, or financial approval authority  
- Does not bypass consent/FERPA, eligibility, approval, or access checks to accelerate routing  
- Does not mark requests complete or valid without explicit prerequisite closure and evidence readiness

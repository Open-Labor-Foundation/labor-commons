# Academic operations specialist  
Coordinates education and research workflows for institutions by managing operational processes from intake to completion, ensuring compliance with required records and approvals while staying within defined authority boundaries.  

## Who uses this  
- Academic operations teams  
- Registrar and student-information staff  
- Grants and research administration staff  
- Accreditation support teams  
- Workflow orchestrators  

## What it produces  
- Execution status update with lifecycle stage and next action  
- Completion or fulfillment record with evidence of closure  
- Exception summary for blocked, returned, or partially completed work  
- Program, campus, learner, or study workflow summary with readiness and owner status  
- Escalation note for scheduling, participation, records, or ethics-review conflicts  

## Risks and mitigations  
- Refuses to act on grading, ethics determinations, legal override, or financial approval requests to avoid overstepping authority.  
- Requires auditable records before marking work complete to ensure accountability.  
- Routes conflicts or unclear ownership to appropriate specialists or humans for resolution.  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Covers U.S. Department of Education, Office for Civil Rights, and Office for Human Research Protections regulations  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- Requires tenant-specific facts, records, and approvals before initiating workflows  
- Cannot act on grading, transcript changes, instructional judgments, or ethics determinations  
- Cannot assert financial, legal, or policy-override authority outside tenant scope  
- Cannot mark work complete without an auditable completion packet

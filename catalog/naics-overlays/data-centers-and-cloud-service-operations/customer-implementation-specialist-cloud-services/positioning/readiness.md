# Specialist Overview  
The customer implementation specialist for cloud services resolves stakeholder-facing service requests by validating scope, confirming prerequisites, mapping evidence, routing safely, and escalating when authority, source interpretation, or safety constraints exceed lane scope. This specialist operates within the boundaries of cloud service contracts, SLA terms, data handling policy, and tenant authorization.

## Who uses this  
- Customer implementation coordinator  
- Cloud adoption support lead  
- Service desk and operations lead  
- Tenant implementation concierge  
- Incident and readiness operators  

## What it produces  
- Service or support disposition with confidence state and unresolved items  
- Customer or stakeholder communication summary  
- Escalation package for exceptions, complaints, policy exceptions, or unsupported asks  
- Request-resolution summary with issue, action, and next owner  
- Exception communication and handoff note  

## Risks and mitigations  
- Refuses to handle legal decisions, contractual commitments, policy waivers, or refunds without authorized owners  
- Refuses to bypass security, privacy, approval, or rollback evidence requirements  
- Refuses to make pricing, billing, safety, or engineering-signoff decisions  
- Refuses to mark completion without required evidence and tenant signature/approval context  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Covers cloud service contracts, SLA terms, data handling policy, and tenant authorization context  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- What the user or organization must supply before this specialist can work reliably  
  - Minimum tenant facts and artifacts  
  - Authoritative source context  
  - Implementation evidence  
- What is explicitly out of scope  
  - Creation of legal decisions, contractual commitments, policy waivers, or refunds  
  - Bypassing security, privacy, approval, or rollback evidence requirements  
  - Making pricing, billing, safety, or engineering-signoff decisions  
  - Marking completion without required evidence and tenant signature/approval context

# Specialist Overview  
The data center compliance specialist translates cross-industry compliance obligations into evidence-grounded outputs for data center and cloud service operations, focusing on control interpretation, evidence readiness, governance handoff, and structured escalation.  

## Who uses this  
- Cloud operations leadership  
- Data center compliance operations  
- Facility and platform governance teams  
- Security governance and policy teams  
- Site reliability and continuity coordinators  

## What it produces  
- Obligation-control matrix tied to evidence owners  
- Control or requirement traceability summary  
- Exception and remediation escalation note  
- Review or attestation readiness packet  
- Industry-terminology-normalized compliance disposition  
- Adjacent-lane routing note when authority changes  
- Explicit refusal or cannot-decide packet for missing facts or policy conflict  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Source coverage: National Institute of Standards and Technology (12 references)  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- Requires validated tenant facts, authoritative records, and source context before issuing any compliance conclusion  
- Cannot provide legal advice, regulator-facing interpretation, or formal legal opinion  
- Cannot impersonate licensed auditors, cloud architects, electrical/fire/safety engineers, or finance approvers  
- Cannot claim final policy compliance certification without traceable tenant and record evidence  
- Cannot close controls, incidents, or service exceptions without required evidence and approver signals  
- Cannot execute or approve changes in cloud platform, facility operations, or safety systems

# Specialist Overview  
The colocation operations specialist manages execution and fulfillment for hosted services and connectivity workloads in colocation environments. It focuses on tracking execution states, validating prerequisites, managing exceptions, and producing evidence-backed summaries without overstepping authority boundaries.  

## Who uses this  
- Colocation operations lead  
- Site operations center  
- Hosted-service operations coordinator  
- Network operations coordinator  
- Capacity planning and delivery analysts  

## What it produces  
- Execution status update with lifecycle stage, blockers, owner, and next action  
- Exception summary with dependency gap, assumption, and escalation owner  
- Completion record tying each claim to completion artifacts  
- Rework / hold summary when evidence or authority is incomplete  
- Outage, access, or contract-boundary escalation note  

## Validation  
- 10 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Source coverage includes Federal Energy Regulatory Commission, North American Electric Reliability Corporation, Uptime Institute, ASHRAE, and U.S. Occupational Safety and Health Administration  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- What the user or organization must supply before this specialist can work reliably:  
  - Minimum tenant facts and authoritative records  
  - Context and source documentation for infrastructure and connectivity tasks  
  - Clear escalation paths and owner references for exceptions  
- What is explicitly out of scope:  
  - Electrical, civil, or facility engineering design and final engineering signoff  
  - Licensing, legal interpretation, financial approval, or policy-override decisions  
  - Marking completion without required evidence and owner signoff references  
  - Telecom billing, contract procurement negotiations, or legal remedy claims outside lane authority

# Specialist Overview  
The cloud service delivery specialist supports data center and cloud operations by validating and guiding service delivery workflows for incident triage, access readiness, change readiness, and interface dependency coordination. They ensure decisions are evidence-driven, auditable, and safely bounded within domain authority.

## Who uses this  
- Cloud operations team  
- Service desk and incident coordinators  
- Platform engineers  
- Data center and tenant operations leads  
- Service reliability and support management  

## What it produces  
- Resolution note with request summary, action taken, confidence framing, and unresolved items  
- Customer or stakeholder communication summary with outcome and blockers  
- Escalation package for exceptions, complaints, and unsupported asks  
- System incident or service summary with impact and owner  
- Change-readiness or integration-risk escalation note with required artifacts and next owner  
- Domain artifact-aligned readiness mapping and output shape for cloud/service terminology  

## Risks and mitigations  
- Requests that require legal, licensed, policy-override, financial-approval, or safety-critical authority are explicitly refused or routed, preventing overstepping domain boundaries.  
- All decisions are based on available evidence and policy gates, with escalations prepared when prerequisites are missing or authority is unclear.  

## Validation  
- 10 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Source coverage includes U.S. Census Bureau / NAICS, National Institute of Standards and Technology (3x listed)  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- What the user or organization must supply:  
  - Accurate and complete tenant facts, records, and source context  
  - Policy, dependency, and evidence gates for decision-making  
  - Context on authority boundaries and tenant-specific policies  
- What is explicitly out of scope:  
  - Legal, clinical, licensing, or safety authority decisions  
  - Policy exceptions or financial approvals outside documented owner authority  
  - Final change release, engineering signoff, or security risk disposition without required approvals  
  - Forcing completion where evidence or rollback evidence is missing

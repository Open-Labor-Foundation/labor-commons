# Specialist Overview  
The ingredient supply specialist coordinates and tracks ingredient availability and readiness in food and beverage manufacturing, ensuring traceable and evidence-backed handoffs across planning, procurement, quality, and logistics systems. It does not execute procurement, release ingredients, or approve formulations.

## Who uses this  
- Ingredient planner  
- Materials coordinator  
- Plant operations coordinator  
- Inventory operations reviewer  
- Procurement operations reviewer  
- Supplier quality reviewer  
- Food quality operations reviewer  
- Inbound logistics reviewer  

## What it produces  
- Handoff summary  
- Dependency tracker  
- Routing confirmation note  
- Availability or logistics status summary  
- Shortage or vendor risk escalation note  
- Closure confirmation  
- Traceability conflict summary  
- Quality or release boundary escalation  

## Risks and mitigations  
- Mitigates risk of misrouting or releasing ingredients by refusing to bypass formal quality, allergen, or release controls.  
- Refuses to escalate or act on incomplete or unverified data, ensuring traceability and regulatory compliance.  
- Avoids assuming final authority on safety-critical or legal decisions by explicitly redirecting such cases to appropriate owners.  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Covers U.S. Food and Drug Administration, Electronic Code of Federal Regulations, and related regulatory authority  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- Requires access to accurate and up-to-date ERP, MES, WMS, QMS, supplier, and planning system data  
- Requires defined ownership and formal approval boundaries for quality, release, and allergen controls  
- Out of scope:  
  - Creating or approving purchase orders  
  - Executing freight expedite or stock reallocation  
  - Approving final ingredient release or nonconformance disposition  
  - Approving supplier onboarding, formula changes, or allergen-risk decisions  
  - Bypassing traceability, hold, or quality controls for schedule purposes  
  - Providing generic supply chain advice instead of food-and-beverage-specific coordination

# Specialist Overview  
The Regulatory Labeling Specialist translates food-labeling obligations into evidence-backed control handling for food and beverage manufacturers. It ensures ingredient, allergen, nutrition, artwork, traceability, and release-boundary handling are tied to authoritative records and clear ownership boundaries, without crossing into legal interpretation, formula development, or final label approval.

## Who uses this  
- Regulatory affairs manager  
- Labeling compliance manager  
- Plant quality manager  
- Food safety manager  
- Packaging change coordinator  
- Supplier quality manager  
- Reviewer  

## What it produces  
- Control or obligation matrix tied to the lane  
- Evidence readiness or gap summary  
- Exception and remediation escalation note  
- Control and obligation summary tied to evidence owners  
- Exception and remediation note for control gaps or policy conflicts  
- Label-change review or inspection-preparation packet  

## Risks and mitigations  
- **Risk**: Requests to treat missing or incomplete evidence as acceptable.  
  **Mitigation**: The specialist refuses such requests and escalates all low-confidence or missing evidence cases.  
- **Risk**: Confusion between this lane and final approval authority.  
  **Mitigation**: The specialist explicitly names its boundaries and escalates whenever it encounters requests for legal or final signoff.  
- **Risk**: Misinterpretation of FDA versus FSIS jurisdiction.  
  **Mitigation**: The specialist maps obligations to actual records and flags jurisdictional conflicts for escalation.  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Source coverage includes FDA and FSIS regulatory texts, including the Electronic Code of Federal Regulations  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- **What must be supplied before the specialist can work reliably**  
  - Current formula and specification records  
  - Ingredient statements and allergen matrices  
  - Nutrition support and label artwork  
  - Batch or lot records and hold records  
  - Supplier change notices and traceability data  
- **What is explicitly out of scope**  
  - Providing legal advice, regulator-negotiation strategy, or formal legal sufficiency opinions  
  - Approving final market labels, nutrient-content claims, health claims, or marketing copy  
  - Approving formula changes, rework, supplier substitutions, or quality disposition outside documented authority  
  - Bypassing traceability, hold, or change-control gates due to operational pressure  
  - Collapsing food labeling into generic document review that ignores food-specific requirements

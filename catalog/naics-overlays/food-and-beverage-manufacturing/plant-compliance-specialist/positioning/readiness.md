# Specialist Overview  
The plant compliance specialist translates food and beverage manufacturing compliance obligations into actionable, evidence-backed recommendations. It focuses on plant-side compliance work, ensuring that visible plant, batch, lot, supplier, traceability, sanitation, and change-control evidence supports bounded compliance decisions without crossing into legal interpretation or final authority.

## Who uses this  
- Plant-compliance-manager  
- Quality-manager  
- Food-safety-manager  
- Operations-manager  
- Regulatory-affairs-manager  
- Reviewer  

## What it produces  
- Control or obligation matrix tied to the lane  
- Evidence readiness or gap summary  
- Exception and remediation escalation note  
- Control and obligation summary tied to evidence owners  
- Exception and remediation note for control gaps or policy conflicts  
- Inspection or attestation preparation packet  

## Risks and mitigations  
- **Risk**: Requests to approve product release or interpret legal sufficiency may be mistakenly accepted.  
  **Mitigation**: The specialist explicitly rejects any request for legal interpretation, final release authority, or regulator-facing signoff and escalates such requests to appropriate owners.  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Source coverage includes 9 documents from the Electronic Code of Federal Regulations and U.S. Food and Drug Administration  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- **What the user or organization must supply**:  
  - Access to ERP, MES, WMS, QMS, and traceability-system records  
  - Clear jurisdictional and policy context  
  - Defined plant, line, product, lot, supplier, or authority prerequisites  
- **What is explicitly out of scope**:  
  - Providing legal advice or interpretations of statutory sufficiency  
  - Approving final product release, disposition, rework, or supplier substitution  
  - Signing FDA, customer, or third-party audit responses  
  - Bypassing compliance gates due to service or inventory pressure  
  - Collapsing plant compliance into generic administrative support

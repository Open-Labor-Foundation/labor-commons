# Specialist Overview  
The Quality Operations Food and Beverage Specialist handles quality operations tasks in food and beverage manufacturing, focusing on evaluating batch, lot, and inventory records to determine quality status, hold or release boundaries, and corrective-action needs. It ensures decisions are based on visible evidence from ERP, MES, WMS, and QMS systems, while staying within the bounds of quality operations and avoiding final release or regulatory authority.

## Who uses this  
- Quality Manager  
- Plant QA Manager  
- Quality Supervisor  
- Operations Manager  
- Supplier Quality Manager  
- Reviewer  

## What it produces  
- Quality or safety status summary  
- Hold, release, or nonconformance record with evidence references  
- Corrective-action or incident escalation package  
- Quality or assurance findings summary  
- Corrective-action or unresolved-risk escalation note  

## Risks and mitigations  
- Refuses to bypass hold, release, or safety-control boundaries without explicit authority.  
- Rejects requests for unsupported use-as-is or rework approvals, supplier substitutions, or process changes.  
- Escalates cases with missing batch history, conflicting hold status, or unresolved supplier or inventory conflicts.  
- Avoids legal or regulatory interpretation and ensures traceability and quality controls are not bypassed.  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Source coverage includes the Electronic Code of Federal Regulations and U.S. Food and Drug Administration guidelines.  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- Requires complete and accessible ERP, MES, WMS, QMS, and supplier-system records to function reliably.  
- Must not approve product release, authorize rework or substitution, sign a food safety plan, interpret law for a jurisdiction, or bypass documented traceability, quality, or safety controls.  
- Cannot treat missing lot genealogy, inspection, review, or verification evidence as acceptable.  
- Cannot make decisions that require engineering authority, supplier approval, or final QA release.

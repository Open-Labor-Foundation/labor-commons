# Specialist Overview  
The Production Reporting Food and Beverage Specialist is responsible for assembling trustworthy production reports and disclosure outputs in the food and beverage manufacturing industry. This work includes defining metrics, validating source data, flagging incomplete or conflicting evidence, and escalating unresolved issues to the correct teams. The specialist ensures reports are grounded in food-and-beverage manufacturing terminology and traceability requirements.

## Who uses this  
- Plant reporting leads  
- Production managers  
- Operations finance analysts  
- Manufacturing excellence teams  
- Plant controllers  
- Quality operations reviewers  
- Supply chain reporting teams  
- Executive or disclosure reviewers  

## What it produces  
- Metric definition and data-source summary  
- Analysis or report output with caveats and confidence notes  
- Caveat or reconciliation note  
- Submission or reporting package summary with source tie-outs and caveats  
- Data-quality or disclosure-risk escalation note  
- Adjacent-specialist routing note  

## Validation  
- 13 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Source coverage includes U.S. Food and Drug Administration, Electronic Code of Federal Regulations  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- The user or organization must supply validated production orders, batch records, inventory states, traceability logs, and quality-hold records before work can proceed reliably.  
- The specialist must not approve final product release, nonconformance disposition, rework release, or quality signoff.  
- The specialist must not approve process changes, engineering changes, formulation edits, or specification changes to improve reported outcomes.  
- The specialist must not approve supplier substitutions, use-as-is decisions, receiving exceptions, or supplier-quality disposition.  
- The specialist must not publish unsupported causal, policy, attestation, or safety language that exceeds the named evidence set.  
- The specialist must not bypass documented traceability, hold, release, or quality controls to simplify reporting or disclosure.  
- The specialist must not collapse the lane into generic BI, planning, inventory, procurement, or plant command work that ignores food-and-beverage manufacturing artifacts and authority limits.

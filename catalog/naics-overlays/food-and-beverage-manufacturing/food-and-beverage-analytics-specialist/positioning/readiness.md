# Specialist Overview  
The food and beverage analytics specialist translates food-and-beverage manufacturing records and workflow signals into trustworthy analysis with explicit metric logic, source lineage, confidence caveats, and action thresholds. It focuses on metrics such as batch attainment, giveaway, hold-adjusted availability, and traceability completeness, without overstepping into approvals or policy decisions.

## Who uses this  
- Plant performance analysts  
- Operations and supply chain reporting leads  
- Quality systems and continuous improvement leads  
- Inventory and logistics analysts  
- Procurement and supplier performance analysts  
- Commercial, finance, or executive stakeholders consuming operational reporting  

## What it produces  
- Metric definition and data-source summary  
- Analysis or report output with caveats and confidence notes  
- Data-quality or disclosure-risk escalation note  
- Analytical brief with metric definitions, source logic, and confidence caveats  
- Exception note for data quality, denominator ambiguity, unsupported trend claims, or cross-system conflicts  

## Risks and mitigations  
- Refuses to approve final release, hold disposition, supplier qualification, or policy overrides.  
- Rejects unsupported claims about causality, benchmarks, or forecasts.  
- Escalates to appropriate specialists or human reviewers when analysis requires engineering, regulatory, or legal authority.  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Source coverage includes U.S. Food and Drug Administration, Electronic Code of Federal Regulations  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- Requires minimum tenant facts, authoritative records, and source context before proceeding  
- Must not be used to approve final release, quality hold disposition, quarantine release, or recall closure  
- Must not approve supplier qualification, substitution, or nonconformance disposition  
- Must not make engineering change, process-change, formulation, or regulatory-interpretation decisions  
- Must not claim causal findings, forecasts, or benchmarks not supported by named records and explicit methodology  
- Must not sign off on customer, investor, legal, or regulatory disclosures

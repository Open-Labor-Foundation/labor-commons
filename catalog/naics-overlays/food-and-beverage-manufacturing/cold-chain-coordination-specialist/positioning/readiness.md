# Specialist Overview  
The cold chain coordination specialist ensures that temperature-sensitive food and beverage manufacturing materials move safely and transparently across teams by maintaining traceable records and facilitating evidence-backed handoffs. This role focuses on coordination, not execution or final authority, and ensures all cold-chain steps are documented and escalated appropriately when needed.

## Who uses this  
- Plant operations coordinator  
- Cold storage coordinator  
- Warehouse operations reviewer  
- Logistics operations reviewer  
- Inventory operations reviewer  
- Quality operations reviewer  
- Supplier quality reviewer  
- Materials planning reviewer  

## What it produces  
- Handoff summary  
- Dependency tracker  
- Routing confirmation note  
- Cold chain status summary  
- Temperature excursion escalation note  
- Closure confirmation  
- Traceability conflict summary  
- Quality or release boundary escalation  

## Risks and mitigations  
- **Mitigation for high-stakes situations**: The specialist refuses to act on or resolve final release, deviation approval, supplier substitution, or safety-critical decisions, ensuring these remain with the appropriate authority.  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Covers U.S. Food and Drug Administration regulations from the Electronic Code of Federal Regulations  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- **What must be supplied by the user or organization**:  
  - Access to ERP, MES, WMS, TMS, QMS, and supplier systems  
  - Accurate and complete lot, shipment, temperature, and hold records  
  - Clear ownership definitions for all cold-chain steps  
  - Defined escalation paths for quality holds and nonconformances  

- **What is explicitly out of scope**:  
  - Carrier booking or dock rescheduling  
  - Direct warehouse execution or trailer movement  
  - Final product release or deviation closure  
  - Supplier onboarding or substitution approval  
  - Bypassing traceability, hold, or quality controls for throughput or service level goals  
  - Providing generic logistics advice outside food-and-beverage temperature control

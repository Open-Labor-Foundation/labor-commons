# Specialist Overview  
The food safety specialist supports food and beverage manufacturing by analyzing visible food-safety, traceability, and preventive-control records to produce evidence-backed safety recommendations, escalation notes, and summaries. It operates within the boundary of visible records and regulatory baselines, without final authority over product release, rework, or regulatory sign-off.

## Who uses this  
- Food-safety-manager  
- Quality-manager  
- Plant-qa-manager  
- Supplier-quality-manager  
- Operations-manager  
- Reviewer  

## What it produces  
- Quality or safety status summary  
- Hold, release, or nonconformance record with evidence references  
- Corrective-action or incident escalation package  
- Safety or security incident status summary  
- High-risk escalation note with required approvals and evidence  

## Risks and mitigations  
- **Escalation of low-confidence cases**: The specialist refuses to bypass missing traceability, conflicting hold status, or supplier substitutions without escalation.  
- **Rejection of unsupported overrides**: It rejects unsupported rework, substitution approval, or final release signoff.  
- **Boundary enforcement**: It does not act on requests that cross into legal, medical, engineering, or recall command authority.  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Covers U.S. Food and Drug Administration and Electronic Code of Federal Regulations  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- **What must be supplied**: Complete and accessible ERP, MES, WMS, QMS, and supplier records.  
- **What is explicitly out of scope**:  
  - Final QA release or recall command authority  
  - Legal or medical advice  
  - Engineering or process authority changes  
  - Supplier approval or substitution authorization  
  - Bypassing documented food-safety controls or traceability gaps as acceptable

# Specialist Overview  
The major incident and escalation specialist focuses on determining and managing high-severity service disruptions within IT service management. It supports operations and support leaders by creating structured escalation plans, defining incident severity thresholds, and guiding communication and coordination for major incidents, while staying strictly within the boundaries of major-incident command and escalation.

## Who uses this  
- Major incident manager  
- Service operations lead  
- Service desk manager  
- Support escalation manager  
- Operations reviewer  

## What it produces  
- Major-incident threshold decision record with rationale, assumptions, and missing-data requests  
- Escalation and command-structure plan with role assignments, update cadence, and stakeholder communications guidance  
- Workflow-quality review covering timeline discipline, decision logging, coordination gaps, and escalation hygiene  
- Boundary-safe refusal or low-confidence escalation memo  
- Downstream handoff packet for problem management, change, disaster recovery, cybersecurity, standard incident management, or technical execution  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Source coverage includes Microsoft Learn (three references) and Google Cloud  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- **User or organization must supply**:  
  - A clear narrative of the incident including impact, duration, and stakeholder urgency  
  - Known service criticality and blast radius estimates  
  - Available command roles and escalation paths  
- **Explicitly out of scope**:  
  - Standard incident triage, priority-setting, or closure handling  
  - Root-cause analysis, trend analysis, known-error management, or recurring-incident remediation  
  - Infrastructure, application, endpoint, network, or cloud remediation steps  
  - Monitoring rule design, alert deduplication, observability pipeline engineering, or incident automation  
  - Disaster recovery runbook execution, business continuity program governance, legal attestation, or cybersecurity breach response  
  - Service desk staffing, procurement, HR workflow, or enterprise governance guidance

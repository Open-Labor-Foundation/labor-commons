# Specialist Overview  
The service desk operations specialist focuses on improving the day-to-day operations of frontline IT support teams. This includes designing and refining queue structures, workload management, shift handoffs, and operational reporting, while ensuring recommendations stay within the service desk operations lane and avoid overlapping with other ITSM specialties.

## Who uses this  
- Service desk manager  
- Support operations manager  
- ITSM process owner  
- Service delivery manager  
- Queue supervisor  
- Reviewer  

## What it produces  
- Service desk operations review with queue-health findings, assumptions, and prioritized recommendations  
- Queue design or assignment-policy memo with routing, balancing, and overflow guidance  
- Shift handoff or operational cadence checklist covering escalation, backlog, and ownership controls  
- Staffing-signal or workload-risk note tied to business impact and explicit confidence limits  
- Boundary-safe refusal, low-confidence escalation packet, or orchestrator handoff note  

## Risks and mitigations  
- When confidence in staffing or workload data is low, the specialist explicitly states this and provides escalation guidance to prevent operational blind spots.  
- Recommendations are limited to the service desk operations lane to avoid overstepping into incident management, request fulfillment, or HR domains.  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Draws from ISO, PeopleCert, Atlassian Support, and ServiceNow guidance to cover standard service desk operations frameworks  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- **What must be supplied by the user or organization:**  
  - Current queue design, assignment policies, and workload metrics  
  - Operational reporting and ticket trends data  
  - Existing shift handoff and escalation practices  
  - Clear boundary definitions between service desk operations and adjacent ITSM lanes  
- **What is explicitly out of scope:**  
  - Incident management, including major incident command or war-room coordination  
  - End-user troubleshooting or technical break-fix execution  
  - Request fulfillment, catalog design, or approval workflows  
  - Knowledge article lifecycle or self-service governance  
  - HR administration, labor policy, or staffing approvals  
  - Platform implementation, dashboard creation, or workflow automation

# Specialist Overview  
The end-user support specialist focuses on frontline guidance for resolving individual or small-group technology issues by clarifying symptoms, recommending first-line troubleshooting, and ensuring support tickets are complete, actionable, and routed correctly within the IT service management framework. This specialist operates strictly within the end-user support lane, avoiding adjacency to engineering, governance, or unrelated domains.

## Who uses this  
- Service desk analyst  
- Desktop support lead  
- Support coordinator  
- Support operations reviewer  
- Workplace technology manager  

## What it produces  
- Ticket-quality and troubleshooting-intake review with missing-data requests  
- Bounded first-line support plan with troubleshooting order, user communication, and escalation checkpoints  
- Handoff packet for adjacent specialists with explicit evidence, current state, and remaining unknowns  
- Closure-readiness checklist with user confirmation and documentation requirements  
- Boundary-safe refusal or low-confidence escalation memo  

## Risks and mitigations  
This specialist refuses to provide binding legal, HR, compliance, or security determinations and explicitly states assumptions, uncertainty, and low-confidence escalation requirements instead of guessing. It also avoids direct technical implementation and does not handle broad outages, staffing, or field logistics.

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Source coverage includes ISO, PeopleCert, Microsoft Learn  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- What the user or organization must supply before this specialist can work reliably:  
  - A clear description of the user’s reported symptoms and environment  
  - Access to the relevant support ticket, including any logs, reproduction steps, or user interaction history  
  - Context on recent system or user changes, if applicable  
  - A defined boundary between end-user support and adjacent ITSM domains  

- What is explicitly out of scope:  
  - Service desk staffing, shift planning, or workforce policy design  
  - Broad outage coordination or incident command  
  - Standard access, hardware, or procurement fulfillment workflows  
  - Endpoint engineering, identity administration, or platform configuration  
  - Field dispatch planning, depot logistics, or inventory movement  
  - Binding legal, HR, privacy, or compliance determinations

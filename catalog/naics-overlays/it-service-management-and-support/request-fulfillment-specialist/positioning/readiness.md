# Specialist Overview  
The Request Fulfillment Specialist provides structured guidance for standard IT service request handling, ensuring consistent intake, routing, fulfillment, and closure while staying within a strict ITSM request-fulfillment boundary. This specialist avoids guessing or overstepping into adjacent domains such as incident management, change enablement, or direct technical implementation.

## Who uses this  
- Service request manager  
- Service desk lead  
- Support coordinator  
- Catalog owner  
- Operations reviewer  

## What it produces  
- Request-quality review with missing-data checklist and routing or approval guidance  
- Fulfillment workflow recommendation with sequencing, status, and communication expectations  
- Priority or due-date rationale tied to business urgency and request policy  
- Completion and closure checklist with requester confirmation and evidence requirements  
- Boundary-safe refusal or low-confidence escalation memo  

## Risks and mitigations  
- Explicitly refuses to handle or guess about work outside the ITSM request-fulfillment lane, such as incident management, change enablement, or direct technical implementation.  
- Requires user confirmation of input scope and assumptions before recommending routing or approval logic.  
- Escalates unclear or ambiguous cases with clear documentation of limitations and boundaries.  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Covers ISO, PeopleCert, and ServiceNow-based request-fulfillment standards and practices  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
### What the user or organization must supply:  
- A clear, scoped service request or catalog item  
- Defined business urgency and policy context for the request  
- Existing or proposed fulfillment workflow for review  

### What is explicitly out of scope:  
- Incident triage, outage response, or major-incident command  
- Root-cause analysis or problem management  
- Change implementation, release sequencing, or production execution  
- Identity governance, access policy, or joiner-mover-leaver workflows  
- Procurement decisions, sourcing strategy, or vendor negotiation  
- Endpoint builds, account provisioning, or direct technical remediation  
- Service desk staffing, HR workflow policy, or legal attestation

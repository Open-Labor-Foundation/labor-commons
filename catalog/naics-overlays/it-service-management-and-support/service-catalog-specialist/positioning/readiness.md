# Specialist Overview  
The service catalog specialist focuses on defining, reviewing, and maintaining the structure, rules, and usability of IT service request catalogs. They ensure clarity in service definitions, requester guidance, approval logic, and fulfillment handoffs, while staying within the narrow boundary of service catalog governance.

## Who uses this  
- Service catalog manager  
- ITSM process owner  
- Service portal owner  
- Support operations lead  
- Request fulfillment lead  
- Reviewer  

## What it produces  
- Catalog design or remediation memo with assumptions, boundary notes, and prioritized recommendations  
- Request-item quality review with required field, approval, routing, and fulfillment-handoff corrections  
- Service-impact prioritization note for catalog changes affecting critical services or high-volume requests  
- Publication or retirement checklist for catalog-item lifecycle control  
- Boundary-safe refusal or low-confidence escalation note  

## Risks and mitigations  
- **Risk**: Incomplete or unclear approval logic may lead to incorrect service fulfillment.  
  **Mitigation**: The specialist explicitly states assumptions and limits confidence, and escalates low-confidence decisions to the appropriate governance or policy owner.

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Covers ISO, PeopleCert, Atlassian, and Atlassian Developer guidance for ITSM and service catalog governance  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- **What must be supplied**: A defined catalog scope, existing catalog structure or request-item examples, and access to approval or entitlement policy documentation.  
- **Out of scope**:  
  - Request-fulfillment execution or task-level fulfillment management  
  - Procurement, vendor selection, or licensing decisions  
  - Knowledge article strategy or knowledge-base operations outside catalog-item guidance  
  - Platform automation, workflow scripts, or portal code implementation  
  - CMDB design or configuration governance beyond catalog data needs  
  - Final legal, HR, privacy, or compliance policy decisions

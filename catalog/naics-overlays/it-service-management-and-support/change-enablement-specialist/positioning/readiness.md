# Specialist Overview  
The change enablement specialist focuses on guiding IT service change requests through a structured, risk-aware workflow by classifying changes, defining approval paths, and ensuring readiness for implementation, all within the strict boundary of IT service management (ITSM) change enablement.

## Who uses this  
- Change manager  
- Service management lead  
- Operations lead  
- Service owner  
- Reviewer  

## What it produces  
- Change classification and approval-path decision with explicit rationale and assumptions  
- Change quality review showing missing evidence, workflow corrections, and risk controls  
- Scheduling and implementation guardrail note with conflict, blackout, rollback, and validation guidance  
- CAB summary or low-confidence escalation packet for unresolved risk or impact ambiguity  
- Boundary-safe refusal or handoff summary for adjacent technical or governance work  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Source coverage includes Google Cloud, Amazon Web Services, Microsoft Learn, Atlassian  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- The user or organization must supply a complete and unambiguous Request for Change (RFC) with relevant evidence, risk context, and implementation details before this specialist can work reliably.  
- Explicitly out of scope:  
  - Implementing or executing infrastructure, application, endpoint, data, or network changes directly  
  - Acting as the primary release engineer, CI/CD owner, or technical approver for software-delivery execution  
  - Owning live incident command, outage restoration, or emergency response operations  
  - Performing root-cause analysis, known-error governance, or corrective-action ownership  
  - Providing legal, regulatory, audit-attestation, procurement, or enterprise-policy authority beyond change-enablement process guidance  
  - Widening into generic PMO, portfolio governance, architecture review board, or security-approval ownership

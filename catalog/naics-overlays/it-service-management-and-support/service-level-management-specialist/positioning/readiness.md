# Specialist Overview  
The service level management specialist focuses on defining, reviewing, and improving IT service level targets and their associated operating controls. The specialist ensures that service level agreements (SLAs), operational level agreements (OLAs), and related policies are measurable, supportable, and aligned with service criticality and operational behavior.

## Who uses this  
- Service level manager  
- Service desk manager  
- Support operations manager  
- ITSM process owner  
- Service owner  
- Reviewer  

## What it produces  
- Service level policy recommendation with explicit assumptions, target logic, and boundary notes  
- SLA or OLA target matrix tied to priority, business impact, and operating hours  
- Breach-analysis memo with escalation, reporting, and corrective-action guidance  
- Workflow-quality review covering timers, pauses, handoffs, and exception handling  
- Clear refusal or orchestrator handoff when the request belongs to an adjacent specialty  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Based on ISO, PeopleCert, Google Cloud, and Atlassian Support guidance  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- The user or organization must supply clear service definitions, ownership data, and baseline workflow behavior before the specialist can work reliably  
- The following are explicitly out of scope:  
  - Legal contract drafting, negotiation, or approval  
  - Service catalog or request model design  
  - Incident command or problem-management ownership  
  - ITSM platform administration or automation implementation  
  - Monitoring engineering or SLI instrumentation  
  - Service desk staffing or HR workflow modeling

# Specialist Overview  
The knowledge management specialist focuses on IT service management (ITSM) knowledge work, ensuring the accurate and usable capture, maintenance, governance, findability, and reuse of support knowledge. It specializes in knowledge lifecycle design, quality assurance, taxonomy, searchability, and feedback integration, while explicitly rejecting unrelated responsibilities like incident management or software engineering.

## Who uses this  
- Service desk manager  
- Knowledge manager  
- Support operations lead  
- Customer support lead  
- Platform owner  
- Reviewer  

## What it produces  
- Knowledge management recommendation memo with scope, assumptions, and evidence basis  
- Article lifecycle or governance workflow with explicit ownership and review triggers  
- Knowledge quality, searchability, or reuse-gap assessment with prioritized remediation steps  
- Boundary-safe refusal or orchestrator handoff note  
- Shared-meta-agent request contract and fallback decision statement where applicable  

## Risks and mitigations  
- Explicitly refuses to engage in tasks outside its defined specialty boundary, such as incident management or software engineering, and provides clear handoff instructions to avoid operational missteps.  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1.0  
- Based on sources including the Consortium for Service Innovation, International Organization for Standardization (ISO), Microsoft Learn  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- What the user or organization must supply before this specialist can work reliably  
  - Existing knowledge base or draft knowledge content  
  - Defined ownership roles and editorial workflows  
  - Platform details for knowledge hosting (e.g., ServiceNow, Jira Service Management)  
  - Known pain points or gaps in current knowledge usage or quality  
- What is explicitly out of scope  
  - Live incident command or major incident coordination  
  - Root-cause analysis or permanent corrective-action planning  
  - Service catalog design or fulfillment workflows  
  - Integration, portal, bot, or application development  
  - Legal, regulatory, privacy, or records-retention final decisions

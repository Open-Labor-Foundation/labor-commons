# Specialist Overview  
The Asset and Configuration Management Specialist provides guidance to maintain accurate and usable records of IT assets and configuration items (CIs) to support service operations, change impact analysis, incident triage, and audit requirements. It focuses on ensuring data quality, ownership clarity, and process alignment for asset and configuration records within IT service management (ITSM), while clearly rejecting work outside its defined scope.

## Who uses this  
- Service operations manager  
- Configuration manager  
- Asset manager  
- Service desk lead  
- IT operations reviewer  

## What it produces  
- Bounded remediation plan for asset or CI hygiene issues  
- Ticket-quality review with routing, prioritization, and missing-data callouts  
- Service-impact analysis tied to affected assets, CIs, and relationships  
- Audit-ready evidence checklist for lifecycle and ownership controls  
- Clear refusal and handoff note for out-of-scope work  

## Risks and mitigations  
- Escalates low-confidence cases instead of providing uncertain guidance.  
- Explicitly refuses requests outside its defined specialty boundary to avoid misaligned or harmful actions.  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Covers ISO and PeopleCert standards for asset and configuration management  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- **What the user or organization must supply**:  
  - Current asset and CI records with ownership and relationship data  
  - Context about the specific ITSM process or issue (incident, change, request, audit)  
  - Defined boundaries for acceptable remediation actions  
  - Access to relevant lifecycle and ownership policies  

- **What is explicitly out of scope**:  
  - Procurement, vendor negotiation, contract language, budgeting, and sourcing strategy  
  - Software licensing optimization or hardware/software asset accounting  
  - Deep CMDB platform engineering or discovery pattern authoring  
  - Endpoint configuration, patching, device hardening, or deployment tooling  
  - Incident management, major incident command, change approval authority, or compliance ownership

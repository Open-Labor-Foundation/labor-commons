# Specialist Overview  
The construction cost management specialist analyzes and interprets construction cost signals from commercial and industrial projects to produce bounded schedule, capacity, and allocation recommendations. This specialist ensures cost control, sequencing, and escalation decisions are evidence-based and do not overstate authority.

## Who uses this  
- Cost manager  
- Project controls manager  
- Project engineer  
- Commercial manager  
- Project manager  
- Superintendent support staff  
- Change-order administrator  
- Owner representative  

## What it produces  
- Schedule-capacity-or-allocation-recommendation  
- Constraint-log  
- Approval-needed-summary  
- Project-or-field-controls-summary  
- Escalation-note  
- Adjacent-specialist-routing-note  

## Risks and mitigations  
- Refuses to issue final budget, payment, or permit approvals, preserving alignment with regulatory and licensed-trade boundaries.  
- Requires authoritative evidence before recommending cost changes, preventing unsafe or speculative decisions.  
- Routes permit, inspection, and safety boundary cases to the appropriate specialist, avoiding assumption of unassigned authority.  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Draws from U.S. General Services Administration, New York City Department of Buildings, and Occupational Safety and Health Administration sources  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- What the user or organization must supply before this specialist can work reliably:  
  - Work orders, permits, inspection records, site logs, estimate packages, cost reports, change orders, pay-application records, subcontractor commitments, and closeout evidence  
  - Minimum tenant facts, systems-of-record references, jurisdiction context, and authoritative public source context  
- What is explicitly out of scope:  
  - Granting final budget, contingency, payment, change-order, permit, inspection, or operational approval  
  - Implying permit approval, code interpretation, engineer-of-record judgment, or licensed trade signoff  
  - Executing ERP updates, draw processing, subcontract award, permit filing, inspection booking, or field rework directly  
  - Overriding site-safety controls, inspection failures, unresolved correction notices, or incomplete closeout evidence to preserve budget or schedule  
  - Collapsing construction cost management into generic spreadsheet prioritization when permit, inspection, subcontractor, site, or closeout conditions materially change the answer

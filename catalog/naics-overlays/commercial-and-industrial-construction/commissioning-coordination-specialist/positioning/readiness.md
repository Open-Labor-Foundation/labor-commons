# Specialist Overview  
The commissioning coordination specialist ensures commissioning dependencies move smoothly across teams and systems in commercial and industrial construction by managing ownership, evidence handoffs, and blockers. It focuses on tracking and routing commissioning-related artifacts without claiming licensed trade or approval authority.  

## Who uses this  
- Commissioning coordinator  
- Project engineer  
- Superintendent  
- Project controls lead  
- Controls coordinator  
- Closeout coordinator  
- Owner representative  

## What it produces  
- Handoff summary naming prior owner, next owner, required artifacts, and blocker state  
- Dependency tracker or blocker summary tied to authoritative records  
- Routing confirmation note with destination owner and prerequisite checklist  
- Blocked-dependency escalation note for stale or duplicated ownership  
- Closure confirmation showing work landed with the right destination owner  
- Boundary-safe refusal and adjacent-specialist reroute note  

## Risks and mitigations  
- **Refusal on authority boundaries**: Requests involving permit approval, inspection signoff, code interpretation, licensed trade judgment, safety release, or owner acceptance are explicitly refused with reroute to the correct specialist.  
- **Escalation for stale ownership**: Conflicting policies or unresolved ownership ambiguity are escalated before commissioning work is dropped.  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Covers commissioning coordination in commercial and industrial construction with references to OSHA, NYC Department of Buildings, and Whole Building Design Guide  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- **User or organization must supply**:  
  - Minimum tenant facts  
  - Systems-of-record hooks  
  - Authoritative source context for commissioning work  
- **Explicitly out of scope**:  
  - Permit approval, inspection signoff, or certificate-of-occupancy authority  
  - Code or design interpretation  
  - Licensed trade judgment or engineer-of-record direction  
  - Safety release or owner acceptance signoff  
  - Approval of change orders, subcontract terms, or financial commitments  
  - Direct execution of startup, troubleshooting, controls programming, or field work

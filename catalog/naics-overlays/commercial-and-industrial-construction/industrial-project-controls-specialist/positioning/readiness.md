# Specialist Overview  
The industrial project controls specialist determines whether the documented quality, safety, permit, schedule, inspection, turnover, and change-control evidence supports a hold, release, investigation, nonconformance, or escalation decision for active industrial construction projects. It translates industrial construction obligations into evidence-backed control outputs for greenfield, brownfield, retrofit, outage, and expansion work packages, without overstating authority or claiming licensed judgment.

## Who uses this  
- Project controls manager  
- Industrial construction manager  
- Superintendent  
- Quality manager  
- Safety coordinator  
- Turnover or commissioning coordinator  
- Field project engineer  

## What it produces  
- Quality or safety status summary with evidence sufficiency, control posture, and named blockers  
- Hold, release, or nonconformance record with traceable evidence references and owner routing  
- Corrective-action or incident escalation package with evidence gaps and required authority owners  
- Control and obligation summary tied to review cadence, systems of record, and evidence owners  
- Exception and remediation note for contradictory records, missing prerequisites, or policy conflicts  

## Risks and mitigations  
- **Risk**: Incorrect control posture may lead to unsafe or noncompliant construction actions.  
  **Mitigation**: The specialist refuses requests that imply permit approval, code interpretation, or licensed trade signoff, and escalates to qualified authority when required.  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Covers U.S. industrial construction contexts with focus on process areas, utility tie-ins, piping systems, electrical rooms, and MCC spaces  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- **What must be supplied by the user or organization**:  
  - Minimum tenant facts  
  - Authoritative records  
  - Jurisdiction context  
  - System-of-record links  
- **What is explicitly out of scope**:  
  - Permit approval or code interpretation  
  - Regulator-facing commitments or occupancy/energization approval  
  - Engineer-of-record or licensed trade signoff  
  - Approval of change-order money, contract claims, or executive policy overrides  
  - Clearing a documented hold, failed inspection, safety stop, or turnover blocker without explicit evidence and qualified authority  
  - Improvising cross-functional ownership where permitting, inspection, or safety authority is required

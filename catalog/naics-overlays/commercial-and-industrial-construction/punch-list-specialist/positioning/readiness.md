# Specialist Overview  
The punch list specialist manages the progression of punch list items in the Commercial And Industrial Construction industry overlay, ensuring safe and accurate workflows from intake to closeout-ready status without overstepping authority. It processes work orders, deficiency logs, and related field records to produce lifecycle-safe status updates, completion evidence, and escalation notes while maintaining clear boundaries on what it can and cannot declare.

## Who uses this  
- Closeout coordinator  
- Project engineer  
- Superintendent  
- Field operations coordinator  
- Owner turnover manager  
- Facility project manager  
- Quality-control manager  

## What it produces  
- Execution-status-update  
- Completion-evidence-record  
- Exception-summary  
- Work-order-or-closeout-status-summary  
- Escalation-note  
- Adjacent-specialist-routing-note  

## Risks and mitigations  
- The specialist refuses to act as code interpreter, permit approver, licensed trade signoff authority, final owner acceptor, or contractual completion approver. It routes such requests to the appropriate specialists or declines them outright to avoid overstepping authority.  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Source coverage includes Occupational Safety and Health Administration, New York City Department of Buildings, Seattle Department of Construction and Inspections, and U.S. General Services Administration  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- What the user or organization must supply before this specialist can work reliably:  
  - Minimum tenant facts  
  - Authoritative records  
  - Jurisdiction context  
  - Source context  
- What is explicitly out of scope:  
  - Declaring permit approval, final inspection approval, certificate-of-occupancy readiness, or code compliance  
  - Providing licensed trade signoff, engineer-of-record judgment, design interpretation, or means-and-methods direction  
  - Approving change orders, commercial concessions, retainage release, or owner acceptance milestones  
  - Marking punch items complete when completion evidence, backcheck results, turnover artifacts, or authority ownership are unresolved  
  - Collapsing construction punch-list handling into a generic task tracker when permit, inspection, safety, subcontractor, or closeout conditions materially change the answer

# Specialist Overview  
The industrial safety specialist focuses on industrial construction safety within the Commercial And Industrial Construction overlay. It assesses site, permit, inspection, subcontractor, incident, and closeout evidence to recommend hold, release, investigation, or corrective-action escalation, while staying within defined boundaries and without crossing into licensed trade, code interpretation, or formal approval roles.

## Who uses this  
- Industrial-safety-manager  
- EHS-manager  
- Superintendent  
- Construction-manager  
- Project-controls-manager  
- Site-quality-manager  
- Field-service-manager  
- Reviewer  

## What it produces  
- Quality or safety status summary  
- Hold, release, or nonconformance record with evidence references  
- Corrective-action or incident escalation package  
- Safety or security incident status summary  
- High-risk escalation note with required approvals and evidence  

## Risks and mitigations  
- The specialist refuses to act when site conditions, inspections, permit status, unresolved nonconformance, or conflicting rules would alter the safety recommendation, ensuring decisions remain within its defined scope.  
- It does not approve, waive, or direct work when safety controls are incomplete or disputed, to avoid unsafe field execution.  
- Escalation is required for repeat incidents, recurring nonconformance, and safety-critical questions to ensure appropriate licensed or regulatory review.

## Validation  
- 13 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Source coverage includes OSHA and federal regulatory code  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- What the user or organization must supply:  
  - Work orders, permits, inspection records, closeout packages, and site logs  
  - Context on site readiness, subcontractor coordination, and temporary controls  
  - Clear evidence of safety status, including LOTO, SIMOPS, or energy-isolation conditions  

- What is explicitly out of scope:  
  - Legal interpretation or regulator negotiation  
  - Permit approval, code interpretation, or engineer-of-record signoff  
  - Licensed trade judgment or design approval  
  - Financial approval or safety-critical proceed authority  
  - Regulator-facing certification or formal acceptance decisions

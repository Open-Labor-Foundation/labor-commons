# Specialist Overview  
The commercial construction analytics specialist translates commercial construction records and workflow signals into trustworthy analysis using explicit metric logic, caveats, and action thresholds. This role focuses on office, retail, mixed-use, tenant improvement, renovation, shell-and-core, and light commercial program work, preserving the industry's specific semantics and avoiding unsupported conclusions.

## Who uses this  
- Commercial construction reporting leads  
- Project executives  
- Project controls managers  
- Operations managers  
- Superintendents consuming analytics outputs  
- Closeout coordinators  
- Safety and quality leaders  
- Owner-representative reporting teams  

## What it produces  
- Metric definition and data-source summary  
- Analysis or report output with caveats and confidence notes  
- Data-quality or disclosure-risk escalation note  
- Analytical brief with metric definitions, source logic, and confidence caveats  
- Exception note for data quality, denominator ambiguity, unsupported trend claims, or cross-system conflicts  
- Adjacent-specialist routing note  

## Risks and mitigations  
- The specialist refuses to provide permit approval, code interpretation, or licensed trade signoff, ensuring that high-stakes decisions are routed to the correct authority.  
- Unsupported causal or benchmark claims are flagged and escalated to prevent misleading conclusions.  
- Disclosures are only generated when the evidence set supports them, reducing the risk of regulatory exposure.  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Source coverage includes the Electronic Code of Federal Regulations, New York City Department of Buildings (listed twice), and the U.S. General Services Administration  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- What the user or organization must supply before this specialist can work reliably:  
  - Minimum tenant facts  
  - Authoritative records  
  - Source context relevant to the requested analysis  

- What is explicitly out of scope:  
  - Implied or explicit permit approval or final inspection acceptance  
  - Code interpretation or engineer-of-record judgment  
  - Licensed trade signoff or final closeout acceptance  
  - Unsupported causal, benchmark, policy, safety, compliance, schedule-certainty, or performance claims  
  - Approval of change orders, recovery plans, budgets, pay applications, delay responsibility, site progression, or final owner turnover  
  - Generic construction dashboards that ignore commercial permit, inspection, subcontractor, schedule, punch, and closeout dependencies

# Specialist Overview  
The Commercial procurement specialist ensures commercial and industrial construction projects maintain supply continuity by aligning procurement with site readiness, permit status, inspection checkpoints, and subcontractor sequencing. It evaluates material availability and vendor commitments, produces evidence-backed summaries, and escalates risks without assuming approval authority or regulatory interpretation.

## Who uses this  
- Project procurement coordinator  
- Commercial project engineer  
- Industrial construction buyer  
- Superintendent support analyst  
- Project controls analyst  
- Vendor coordination specialist  
- Self-perform materials coordinator  

## What it produces  
- Availability-or-supply-status-summary  
- Procurement-or-replenishment-recommendation-with-constraints-and-dependencies  
- Vendor-or-procurement-action-note  
- Shortage-or-vendor-risk-escalation-note  
- Availability-or-logistics-status-summary  

## Risks and mitigations  
- **Refusal of requests implying spend approval or contract commitment:** The specialist refuses to act on any request that assumes financial or contractual authority.  
- **Refusal of substitution or design-altering actions:** The specialist does not authorize substitutions that require design or licensed-trade approval.  
- **Refusal to imply permit or inspection approval:** The specialist avoids statements that suggest regulatory clearance is complete.  
- **Refusal to sign off on quality or safety-critical work:** The specialist does not assume authority over licensed-trade or safety evaluations.  
- **Refusal to declare supply readiness without prerequisites met:** The specialist will not confirm material readiness if permit, inspection, or submittal conditions are unmet.

## Validation  
- 13 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Covers New York City Department of Buildings, OSHA, FEMA, and other regulatory frameworks  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- **What the user or organization must supply:**  
  - Valid work order or procurement request  
  - Current permit status and inspection checkpoints  
  - Approved submittal status and vendor commitments  
  - Site-readiness conditions and subcontractor sequence  
  - Inventory traceability and closeout dependencies  

- **What is explicitly out of scope:**  
  - Approving spend or executing contracts  
  - Authorizing substitutions that affect design or code compliance  
  - Interpreting code or implying permit approval  
  - Signing off on licensed-trade quality or safety-critical work  
  - Releasing quality holds or declaring occupancy approval  
  - Overriding approved-supplier controls or financial approvals

# Specialist Overview  
The commercial construction operations specialist manages day-to-day execution of commercial construction projects by assessing work status, handling exceptions, and preparing completion packages using documented records and approvals. It ensures execution decisions are bounded, traceable, and aligned with site readiness and regulatory context.  

## Who uses this  
- Commercial construction operations manager  
- Project engineer  
- Assistant superintendent  
- Project coordinator  
- Field operations lead  
- Closeout coordinator  
- Project controls reviewer  

## What it produces  
- Execution-status-update  
- Completion-evidence-record  
- Exception-queue-note  
- Project-or-field-controls-summary  
- Escalation-note  
- Adjacent-specialist-routing-note  

## Risks and mitigations  
- The specialist refuses to approve permits, override failed inspections, or mark work complete when evidence is missing or contradictory. It routes such matters to the appropriate authority.  

## Validation  
- 13 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Source coverage includes OSHA, NYC Department of Buildings (3x), and standard commercial construction lifecycle terminology  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- **User or organization must supply**:  
  - Validated permit status  
  - Approved plans and submittals  
  - Inspection and safety observation records  
  - Change directive status  
  - Subcontractor commitments  
  - Daily reports and look-ahead schedules  
  - Turnover package status  

- **Explicitly out of scope**:  
  - Permit approval or code interpretation  
  - Design authority or engineer-of-record judgments  
  - Occupancy signoff or licensed trade approval  
  - Financial change or subcontractor contract approval  
  - Safety-critical judgment or policy override  
  - Generic workflow execution without site-readiness or regulatory context

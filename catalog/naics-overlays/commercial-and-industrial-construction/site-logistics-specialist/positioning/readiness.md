# Specialist Overview  
The site logistics specialist manages material and delivery coordination on commercial and industrial construction sites, ensuring that logistics readiness, handoffs, and dependencies are tracked and communicated with clear ownership, status visibility, and risk escalation without overstepping into execution or approval roles.

## Who uses this  
- Site-logistics-coordinator  
- Project-engineer  
- Project-controls-reviewer  
- Superintendent  
- Warehouse-or-yard-coordinator  
- Vendor-coordination-lead  
- Closeout-coordinator  

## What it produces  
- Handoff-summary  
- Dependency-or-blocker-log  
- Routing-confirmation-note  
- Availability-or-logistics-status-summary  
- Shortage-or-vendor-risk-escalation-note  
- Closure-confirmation  
- Owner-conflict-summary  
- Permit-or-inspection-blocker-summary  

## Risks and mitigations  
- Refuses to coordinate or approve any activity requiring licensed trade, engineering, safety signoff, or financial authority.  
- Requires explicit, verifiable source records for all logistics handoffs and escalations.  
- Only confirms closure when all required artifacts, ownership, and regulatory conditions are met.  

## Validation  
- 13 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Covers commercial and industrial construction logistics in New York City  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- Requires valid work orders, permits, delivery windows, and vendor commitments to operate  
- Requires access to inspection records, site logs, and inventory systems for accurate status tracking  
- Requires clear ownership boundaries between logistics and execution roles to avoid confusion  
- Explicitly out of scope:  
  - Permit approval or sign-off  
  - Inspection authority or code interpretation  
  - Safety signoff or licensed trade decisions  
  - Field execution, material acceptance, or work completion marking  
  - Change-order or financial approval authority  
  - General dispatch or purchasing without construction-specific logistics context

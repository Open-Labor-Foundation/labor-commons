# Specialist Overview  
The privileged access management specialist provides targeted guidance for securing and managing administrative and elevated access across enterprise identity, device, and workspace platforms. The specialist focuses on least-privilege design, emergency access, and administrative boundary control while avoiding adjacent or out-of-scope responsibilities.

## Who uses this  
- Security engineer  
- Identity architect  
- IAM platform owner  
- SOC analyst  
- IT operations reviewer  

## What it produces  
- Risk-ranked privileged-access findings with assumptions and residual risk  
- Implementation recommendations mapped to source-backed controls and platform limits  
- Control-gap checklists for normal operation and break-glass paths  
- Boundary rejections and rerouting guidance  
- Commercialization-ready evidence tags with human-verification markers  

## Risks and mitigations  
- When evidence is missing or confidence is low, the specialist produces escalation notes to avoid recommending unsafe or unverified privileged access workflows.  

## Validation  
- 13 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Covers Microsoft Learn (15 sources)  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- **What the user or organization must supply**:  
  - Access to privileged-access configuration details  
  - Platform-specific administrative boundary definitions  
  - Current role assignment and elevation policies  
  - Known emergency access procedures and audit trails  

- **What is explicitly out of scope**:  
  - Implementing live code, executing privileged changes, or modifying tenant systems directly  
  - Full network architecture, SD-WAN, or platform migration design outside identity/endpoints  
  - General application feature development and deep workplace productivity app troubleshooting  
  - Generating legal/compliance attestations, external audit statements, or signed attestation reports  
  - Direct incident response containment actions and forensics execution

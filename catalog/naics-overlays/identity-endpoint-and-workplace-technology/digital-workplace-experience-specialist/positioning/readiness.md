# Specialist Overview  
The digital workplace experience specialist focuses on the intersection of identity, endpoint access control, and collaboration workspace configuration. They deliver auditable, implementation-focused guidance for Microsoft 365, Google Workspace, Apple, and Linux/UNIX environments, ensuring secure and user-friendly digital workplace experiences.

## Who uses this  
- Workplace IT lead  
- Identity and endpoint admin  
- ITSM handoff owner  
- Security partner  

## What it produces  
- Prioritized recommendations with residual risk and assumptions  
- Scope-safe rollout plan with sequencing and fallback options  
- Clear rejection/escalation for adjacent or unsupported requests  
- Tenant-specific adaptation guidance with data-boundary constraints  
- Monitoring and verification signals for post-implementation review  

## Risks and mitigations  
- Rejects network platform architecture, app engineering, or unrelated cloud implementation to avoid misalignment with digital workplace focus  
- Provides explicit escalation paths for out-of-bound requests to ensure appropriate handling  
- Limits authority to identity-aware access, policy design, and collaboration controls to maintain scope integrity  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Source coverage: NIST, Microsoft Learn (x3)  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- Requires access to tenant-specific configuration details before reliable output  
- Requires defined user cohorts and policy goals to generate actionable recommendations  
- Explicitly out of scope:  
  - Deep network topology design, SD-WAN, or application runtime architecture  
  - Direct command execution for endpoint deployment or live policy pushes  
  - General software development, frontend engineering, or data migration planning  
  - Incident response playbook execution, legal counsel, or external audit attestation

# Specialist Overview  
A mobile device management (MDM) specialist provides structured, practical guidance on deploying and maintaining secure, compliant mobile device configurations for enterprise environments. The specialist focuses on enrollment, profile compliance, app delivery, device security, and remediation strategies, within the boundaries of MDM planning and configuration review.

## Who uses this  
- IT administrators managing mobile device fleets  
- Security engineers validating device compliance and posture  
- Workplace technology architects designing enrollment and access workflows  
- Incident responders seeking remediation guidance for device-related issues  
- Compliance officers reviewing security and access policy alignment  

## What it produces  
- Phased rollout and rollback plans for MDM configurations  
- Policy matrices with risk assessments and residual-risk explanations  
- Actionable exception proposals for non-compliant devices or users  
- Out-of-scope rejections and referrals to appropriate orchestrator roles  
- Operational handoff notes for implementation or incident response teams  

## Risks and mitigations  
- The specialist refuses to provide guidance for high-risk scenarios involving direct incident containment, malware execution, or digital forensics.  
- The specialist avoids generating legal or procurement language, which may require formal attestation from legal or compliance teams.  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Source coverage includes Microsoft (4 references) and Apple (4 references)  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- The user or organization must supply: fleet composition details, current MDM platform, existing identity and access controls, and compliance requirements  
- What is explicitly out of scope:  
  - Full network redesign, including DNS, firewall, or VPN architecture  
  - Application code changes, OS image customization, or feature development  
  - Formal legal, contractual, or procurement language generation  
  - Direct incident containment, malware execution, or digital forensics  
  - Administration of standalone workplace platforms, email systems, or collaboration tools outside MDM scope

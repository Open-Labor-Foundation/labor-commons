# Specialist Overview  
The packaging and shipping chemicals specialist coordinates the packaging and shipment of chemicals within the chemicals, plastics, and materials manufacturing industry. It ensures readiness for shipment by validating prerequisite data, confirming system records, tracking dependencies, and preparing handoff summaries without assuming safety, legal, or engineering authority.

## Who uses this  
- Shipping-and-logistics-manager  
- Warehouse-and-distribution-supervisor  
- Chemical-plant-operations-manager  
- Ehs-manager  
- Transportation-compliance-coordinator  
- Inventory-and-materials-coordinator  
- Reviewer  

## What it produces  
- Handoff-summary  
- Dependency-or-blocker-log  
- Routing-confirmation-note  
- Availability-or-logistics-status-summary  
- Shortage-or-vendor-risk-escalation-note  
- Closure-confirmation  
- Owner-conflict-summary  
- Shipment-readiness-blocker-summary  

## Risks and mitigations  
- **Refusal rule for missing prerequisites:** The specialist refuses to proceed with packaging or shipment coordination if required records, permits, or documentation are absent or incomplete.  
- **No assumption of safety or legal authority:** The specialist does not issue certifications, approvals, or sign-off on hazardous material classifications, packaging compliance, or shipment releases.  
- **Escalation of risk and conflict:** When vendor, compliance, or ownership conflicts are identified, the specialist escalates them explicitly for resolution before proceeding.  

## Validation  
- 13 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Source coverage includes 10 — Occupational Safety and Health Administration / Electronic Code of Federal Regulations and related regulatory frameworks  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- **What the user or organization must supply:**  
  - Complete and accessible packaging records, SDS, and shipping documents  
  - Valid training evidence and permit status  
  - Current system-of-record data from EHS, warehouse, and ERP systems  
  - Carrier commitments and vendor constraints  
- **What is explicitly out of scope:**  
  - Classifying hazardous materials or certifying packaging compliance  
  - Signing shipping papers, hazardous-waste manifests, or authorizing shipment release  
  - Providing engineering judgment, compatibility determinations, or incident command  
  - Booking transportation, marking shipment complete, or directing field execution  
  - Authorizing financial commitments, permit waivers, or policy overrides

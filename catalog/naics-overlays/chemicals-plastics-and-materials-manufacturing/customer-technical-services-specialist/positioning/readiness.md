# Specialist Overview  
The customer technical services specialist resolves stakeholder-facing technical-service requests in the chemicals, plastics, and materials manufacturing industry. It uses documented product data, complaint and sample evidence, field observations, and permit artifacts to provide evidence-backed explanations and escalate when necessary, without assuming authority beyond technical service.

## Who uses this  
- Customer-technical-services-manager  
- Technical-service-representative  
- Product-stewardship-support-lead  
- Quality-complaint-coordinator  
- Sales-support-chemist  
- Application-support-specialist  
- Reviewer  

## What it produces  
- Resolution-note  
- Customer-or-stakeholder-communication-summary  
- Escalation-package  
- Request-resolution-summary  
- Exception-communication-escalation-note  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Source coverage includes APQC, ISO, OSHA, and other industry standards relevant to technical service in chemicals, plastics, and materials manufacturing  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- The user or organization must supply:  
  - Minimum tenant facts and product context  
  - Authoritative records such as SDS, TDS, COA, and complaint files  
  - Field and lab evidence, including site conditions and calibration context  
  - Permit and inspection artifacts when relevant  
- What is explicitly out of scope:  
  - Approving off-label use, reformulation, or unqualified substitutions  
  - Signing off on engineering, environmental, safety, or permit judgments  
  - Overriding complaint, credit, return, or off-spec disposition rules  
  - Presenting troubleshooting advice as proof of process safety or compliance  
  - Collapsing technical service into generic customer support language  

## Risks and mitigations  
- When field conditions, environmental thresholds, or safety observations affect the response, the specialist refuses or escalates to the appropriate owner.  
- When permit constraints, missing calibration evidence, or threshold-sensitive process changes are present, the specialist must not provide unsupported conclusions and must escalate.

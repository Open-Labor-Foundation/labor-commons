# Specialist Overview  
The materials planning specialist supports regulated materials manufacturing by turning demand, constraints, and planning priorities into evidence-based allocation, scheduling, and workload recommendations. It operates within the Chemicals, Plastics, and Materials Manufacturing industry overlay and is focused on safely and accurately translating planning inputs into actionable, recommendation-only outputs while staying within defined authority boundaries.

## Who uses this  
- Materials-planning-manager  
- Plant-planner  
- Production-planning-lead  
- Supply-chain-planner  
- Operations-manager  
- Maintenance-planning-coordinator  
- EHS-reviewer  
- Reviewer  

## What it produces  
- Schedule-recommendation  
- Allocation-or-prioritization-recommendation  
- Constraint-log  
- Approval-needed-summary  
- Capacity-risk-escalation-note  
- Permit-or-inspection-evidence-gap-note  
- Field-risk-escalation-note  
- Adjacent-specialist-routing-note  

## Risks and mitigations  
The specialist refuses or escalates planning requests that involve legal interpretation, safety-critical overrides, permit modification, or regulatory certification. It also refuses to issue final production or operational approvals, ensuring that all outputs are recommendation-only and clearly scoped.

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Source coverage includes demand signals from ERP or APS, CMMS and EAM readiness data, permit conditions, inspection findings, sampling records, and field observations.  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- **What the user or organization must supply before this specialist can work reliably**  
  - Accurate and up-to-date ERP or APS demand signals  
  - CMMS and EAM readiness data  
  - Permit conditions and inspection findings  
  - Sampling records and field observations  
  - Process safety information and incident logs  
  - Facility jurisdiction and context information  

- **What is explicitly out of scope**  
  - Presenting a planning recommendation as final production or operational approval  
  - Signing off on engineering, environmental, or safety judgments outside documented authority  
  - Modifying permits or certifying regulatory reports  
  - Approving deviations from permit or threshold conditions  
  - Executing maintenance dispatch, field operations reassignment, or regulator-facing response actions  
  - Collapsing regulated materials planning into generic supply planning when permit conditions, asset status, or field risk change the outcome

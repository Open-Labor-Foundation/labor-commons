# Specialist Overview  
The batch production specialist manages the execution of batch manufacturing workflows in the chemicals, plastics, and materials manufacturing industry, using authoritative records to track lifecycle states, validate readiness, and ensure compliance with process, safety, and quality boundaries.  

## Who uses this  
- Plant manager  
- Production supervisor  
- Batch operations lead  
- Process-cell coordinator  
- Maintenance coordinator  
- EHS reviewer  
- Lab or release reviewer  
- Reviewer  

## What it produces  
- Execution status update with lifecycle stage and next action  
- Completion or fulfillment record showing what was done and what evidence supports closure  
- Exception summary for blocked, returned, or partially completed work  
- Production status summary with line state, blockers, and quality dependencies  
- Escalation note for throughput, material, or release constraints  

## Risks and mitigations  
- **Refusal on safety-critical decisions**: The specialist refuses to approve engineering changes, interlock bypasses, or operating-window expansions that require engineering or EHS authority.  
- **No regulatory signoff**: The specialist does not finalize or certify regulatory reports, permit compliance, or emissions calculations.  
- **No final certification**: The specialist does not mark work complete without required signoffs, completion evidence, and posted batch records.  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Covers production orders, batch records, MES data, LIMS sampling, permit status, and process-cell readiness  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- **User or organization must supply**:  
  - Valid production orders  
  - Control recipes and batch records  
  - Permit and inspection status  
  - Maintenance and calibration evidence  
  - In-process sampling status  
  - Asset and line readiness data  

- **Explicitly out of scope**:  
  - Approving engineering changes or safety-critical overrides  
  - Certifying regulatory compliance or final product release  
  - Making judgments beyond documented authority or available evidence  
  - Collapsing into a generic production lane when batch-specific context is required  
  - Bypassing required process steps or quality checks

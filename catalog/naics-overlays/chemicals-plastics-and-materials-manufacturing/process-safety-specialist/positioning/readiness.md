# Specialist Overview  
The process safety specialist focuses on evaluating process-safety evidence in the context of chemical, plastics, and materials manufacturing to determine if conditions support a bounded safety status, hold or release decision, corrective action, or escalation. The specialist operates within a defined scope, avoiding engineering design, legal, or emergency-command authority, and instead ensures that process-safety records and procedures are used to support safe operational decisions.

## Who uses this  
- Process-safety-manager  
- EHS-manager  
- Plant-manager  
- Operations-manager  
- Maintenance-manager  
- Reliability-engineer  
- Production-supervisor  
- Reviewer  

## What it produces  
- Quality or safety status summary  
- Hold, release, or nonconformance record with evidence references  
- Corrective-action or incident escalation package  
- Safety or security incident status summary  
- High-risk escalation note with required approvals and evidence  

## Risks and mitigations  
The specialist refuses to provide legal interpretation, approve design changes, command emergency response, or authorize safety-critical proceed actions. It escalates when missing threshold data, unresolved field conditions, or conflicting rules affect process-safety outcomes. This ensures that only authorized personnel handle high-stakes decisions.

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Source coverage includes OSHA Process Safety Management and EPA Risk Management Program requirements  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- What the user or organization must supply before this specialist can work reliably:  
  - Current process safety information (PSI)  
  - Completed PHA and MOC records  
  - Pre-startup safety review (PSSR) documentation  
  - Mechanical-integrity and inspection records  
  - Incident history and audit data  
  - Work permits and contractor records  
- What is explicitly out of scope:  
  - Legal interpretation or regulator negotiation  
  - Engineering design approval or safe operating limit certification  
  - Emergency-command decisions or financial approvals  
  - Relief-system or SIS setpoint approval  
  - Regulator-facing submission signing or policy overrides

# Specialist Overview  
The Membership experience specialist handles operational membership and passholder inquiries within the arts, entertainment, and recreation industry, focusing on membership status, reservations, eligibility, access, and complaint resolution using authoritative records. It ensures accurate classification, verification, and communication while escalating exceptions to the appropriate specialist.

## Who uses this  
- Membership services coordinators  
- Box office and member services teams  
- Museum, zoo, aquarium, and attraction membership managers  
- Venue guest-relations leads  
- Multi-location operations support reviewers  
- Reviewers handling membership and passholder intake  

## What it produces  
- Resolution note with request summary, action taken, authoritative evidence used, and unresolved items  
- Customer or stakeholder communication summary  
- Escalation package for exceptions, complaints, unsupported asks, or safety and fraud-sensitive cases  
- Program or membership status summary with due dates, blockers, and owners  
- Escalation note for missing submissions, approvals, or participant data  

## Risks and mitigations  
- Refuses to provide legal, safety, or fraud determinations, ensuring only verified and evidence-backed actions are taken.  
- Avoids overruling pricing, refunds, or access controls without explicit authority, maintaining compliance with organizational policies.  
- Routes high-risk or out-of-scope requests to the appropriate specialist lane, preserving operational and legal boundaries.  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Source coverage includes CRM, ticketing, reservation, admissions, POS, payment, access-control, service-desk, and incident records  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- What the user or organization must supply before this specialist can work reliably  
  - Validated and accessible CRM, ticketing, and admissions records  
  - Clear documentation of membership terms, household rules, and eligibility criteria  
  - Defined escalation paths and authority delegation for exceptions  
  - Industry-specific terminology and workflow context  

- What is explicitly out of scope  
  - Legal interpretation of automatic renewal or accessibility law  
  - Discretionary pricing or refund approval  
  - Seat-inventory control or final accessible-seating assignment authority  
  - Ride or facility safety signoff  
  - Fraud adjudication or counterfeit-pass investigations  
  - Loss-prevention or law-enforcement decisions

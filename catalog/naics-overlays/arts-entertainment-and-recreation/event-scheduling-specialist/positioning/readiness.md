# Specialist Overview  
The event scheduling specialist manages the timing, routing, and booking commitments for live events in the arts, entertainment, and recreation industries. It ensures that all prerequisites, constraints, and readiness checks are met before scheduling or allocation recommendations are made.

## Who uses this  
- Event operations manager  
- Venue calendar coordinator  
- Festival operations lead  
- Promoter or booking coordinator  
- Recreation program manager  
- Front-of-house manager  
- Reviewer  

## What it produces  
- Schedule-or-allocation-recommendation  
- Constraint-log  
- Approval-needed-summary  
- Schedule-routing-or-booking-readiness-summary  
- Escalation-note  
- Adjacent-specialist-routing-note  
- Event-readiness-risk-summary  

## Risks and mitigations  
- Requests involving pricing overrides, refund approvals, fraud decisions, or life-safety signoffs are explicitly refused and escalated to the appropriate specialist or authority.  
- Scheduling recommendations are never presented as final approvals for date releases, staffing, or operational signoff.  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Covers authoritative sources including APQC, U.S. Department of Justice, Civil Rights Division, and Federal Trade Commission  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- **User or organization must supply**: venue calendars, event holds, artist or instructor holds, accessible seating inventory, onsale dates, ticket allotments, load-in/load-out windows, staffing minimums, and tenant operating rules.  
- **Explicitly out of scope**:  
  - Presenting a planning recommendation as final event approval  
  - Approving or waiving crowd-safety, security, permit, or life-safety requirements  
  - Overriding pricing, refunds, or accessible seating policies  
  - Managing payment disputes, suspected bot activity, chargebacks, or credential misuse  
  - Collapsing event scheduling into generic calendar management when ticketing, site turns, or guest-impact controls are material factors

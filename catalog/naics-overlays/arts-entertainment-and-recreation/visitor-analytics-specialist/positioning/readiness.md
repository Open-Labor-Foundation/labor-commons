# Specialist Overview  
The visitor analytics specialist translates visitor records and workflow signals from the arts, entertainment, and recreation industry into trustworthy analysis, ensuring metric definitions, source lineage, and confidence thresholds are clear and safe to use.

## Who uses this  
- Visitor analytics and insights leads  
- Venue, attraction, museum, and park operations leaders  
- Ticketing, access, and revenue analytics teams  
- Membership and audience-development teams  
- Guest-experience and service-recovery analysts  
- Executive stakeholders consuming visitor reporting  

## What it produces  
- Metric definition and data-source summary  
- Analysis or report output with caveats and confidence notes  
- Data-quality or disclosure-risk escalation note  
- Analytical brief with metric definitions, source logic, and confidence caveats  
- Exception note for data quality, denominator ambiguity, unsupported trend claims, or cross-system conflicts  

## Risks and mitigations  
- Refuses unsupported claims about pricing, refunds, fraud, safety, or external disclosure  
- Blocks analysis when customer harm, refund exceptions, or venue safety are material to the output  
- Routes high-risk decisions to guest services, pricing, fraud, or operations specialists  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Source coverage includes ticketing, access-control, POS, CRM, finance, parking, and occupancy records  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
**User or organization must supply:**  
- Authoritative tenant facts and source records  
- Context about venue-specific workflows (e.g., timed-entry, re-entry, comp tickets)  
- Clear definitions of acceptable data quality and confidence posture  

**Explicitly out of scope:**  
- Approving dynamic pricing, ticket-release, or promotion changes  
- Approving refund exceptions, appeasements, or weather-policy overrides  
- Adjudicating fraud, chargebacks, or loss-prevention investigations  
- Approving security or crowd-safety dispositions  
- Publishing causal findings, benchmarks, or external disclosures without explicit methodology and source support

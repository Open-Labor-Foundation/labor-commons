# Specialist Overview  
The audience services specialist resolves audience-facing and stakeholder-facing service workflows in the arts, entertainment, and recreation industry, using patron and booking records to deliver resolution notes, communication summaries, and escalation packets while staying within defined policy and authority boundaries.

## Who uses this  
- Audience services managers  
- Patron services and subscriber services teams  
- Front-of-house and house management leads  
- Membership and audience development coordinators  
- Venue and program operations coordinators  
- Complaint and service recovery reviewers  

## What it produces  
- Resolution note with request summary, action taken, authoritative evidence used, and unresolved items  
- Customer or stakeholder communication summary  
- Escalation package for exceptions, complaints, or unsupported asks  
- Audience, talent, or production workflow summary with readiness and owner status  
- Escalation note for schedule, rights, or venue-impact conflicts  

## Risks and mitigations  
- Refuses to override pricing, refund rules, donor benefits, or venue-entry requirements without documented authority  
- Avoids making ADA legal interpretations, fraud determinations, or safety signoffs  
- Does not treat incomplete or conflicting records as final truth  
- Routes high-risk cases to adjacent specialists for policy exceptions, fraud, or legal review  

## Validation  
- 13 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Covers U.S. Federal Trade Commission and U.S. Department of Justice / ADA.gov sources  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
**User or organization must supply:**  
- Authoritative patron, booking, ticketing, membership, payment, accommodation, and communication records  
- Documented service rules, entitlements, and policy boundaries  
- Valid case reference, patron identifier, or reservation ID for intake  

**Explicitly out of scope:**  
- Overriding prices, mandatory fees, refund rules, donor or subscriber benefits, age restrictions, or venue-entry requirements  
- Making ADA legal interpretations, final fraud findings, venue-safety signoffs, or pricing and revenue decisions  
- Authorizing discretionary comps, seat upgrades, backstage access, recording permissions, or off-policy exchanges  
- Disclosing investigative posture, private accommodation details beyond the approved audience, or internal security response decisions  
- Treating industry-specific terminology or workflows as cosmetic or answering in generic support language

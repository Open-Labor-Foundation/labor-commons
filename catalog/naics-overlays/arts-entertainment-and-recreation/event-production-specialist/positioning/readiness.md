# Specialist Overview  
The event production specialist executes live-event production workflows by analyzing permit applications, event maps, ticketing records, staffing evidence, safety plans, and settlement or incident logs to make readiness, hold, rework, or completion decisions. It maintains strict boundaries around safety, compliance, and traceability without overstepping into pricing, fraud adjudication, or safety-command authority.

## Who uses this  
- Event-production-manager  
- Venue-operations-coordinator  
- Festival-operations-lead  
- Box-office-operations-partner  
- Front-of-house-supervisor  
- Guest-experience-operations-lead  
- Promoter-operations-analyst  

## What it produces  
- Execution status update with lifecycle stage and next action  
- Completion or fulfillment record showing what was done and what evidence supports closure  
- Exception summary for blocked, returned, or partially completed work  
- Production status summary with line state, blockers, and quality dependencies  
- Escalation note for throughput, material, or release constraints  

## Risks and mitigations  
- Refuses to handle pricing, refund exceptions, fraud adjudication, or safety-command decisions, ensuring no authority is claimed outside its lane.  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Source coverage limited to City of Seattle Special Events Office (8 references)  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- What the user or organization must supply before this specialist can work reliably:  
  - Permit application status  
  - Event map and run-of-show timeline  
  - Ticketing and payment records  
  - Staffing and vendor evidence  
  - Public-safety and insurance documentation  
  - Incident and wrap records  

- What is explicitly out of scope:  
  - Setting or overriding ticket prices, fees, or refund outcomes  
  - Declaring an event, structure, or crowd condition safe without official clearance  
  - Adjudicating suspected fraud, bot or resale abuse, or loss-prevention investigations  
  - Issuing legal interpretations of ticketing, consumer-protection, or public-assembly law  
  - Marking an event complete when required settlement, permit, or closure evidence is incomplete or under review  
  - Absorbing executive operations authority or collapsing live-event production into generic hospitality or box-office support

# Specialist Overview  
The cultural program coordination specialist manages and coordinates cultural program dependencies across arts, entertainment, and recreation workflows, ensuring clear ownership, artifact completeness, and smooth handoffs between teams and systems without assuming delivery, pricing, or safety authority.  

## Who uses this  
- Cultural program coordinators  
- Front-office and box-office leads  
- Guest-services supervisors  
- Venue and visitor-operations managers  
- Education and public-program managers  
- Festival and event coordinators  
- Operations reviewers  

## What it produces  
- Handoff-summary  
- Handoff-status-summary  
- Dependency-tracker  
- Blocked-dependency-escalation-note  
- Routing-confirmation-note  
- Closure-confirmation  
- Booking-conflict-summary  
- Customer-exception-escalation-note  
- Safety-or-fraud-escalation-note  

## Risks and mitigations  
- **Risk**: Misrouting or missing escalation of safety or fraud cases due to ambiguous ownership.  
  **Mitigation**: Explicit escalation protocols for safety, fraud, and consumer-protection cases to the correct adjacent owner.  
- **Risk**: Improper handling of refund or pricing exceptions due to lack of authority.  
  **Mitigation**: Refusal of requests that require unauthorized approval, with escalation to appropriate authority.  
- **Risk**: Confusing coordination with program delivery or inventory changes.  
  **Mitigation**: Strict adherence to in-scope boundaries and refusal of out-of-scope actions.  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Source coverage includes Federal Trade Commission, U.S. Department of Justice, National Institute of Standards and Technology, and Smithsonian Institution guidance  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- **What the user or organization must supply before this specialist can work reliably**  
  - Minimum tenant facts and authoritative records for cultural programs  
  - Source context from POS, PMS, booking, CRM, order-management, service-desk, and inventory systems  
  - Defined ownership and artifact completeness at each workflow step  

- **What is explicitly out of scope**  
  - Direct changes to bookings, ticket inventory, seating, room allocations, or calendar records  
  - Approval of pricing overrides, refunds, comps, promotions, or guest appeasements  
  - Interpretation of venue contracts, ticketing law, refund law, or consumer-protection law as legal advice  
  - Authorization of security, safety, or physical-access dispositions  
  - Adjudication of fraud, suspicious ticketing, account-takeover, or chargeback cases  
  - Use of generic operations or dispatch language in place of cultural-program terminology

# Specialist Overview  
The creative operations specialist coordinates arts, entertainment, and recreation campaign and creative work from accepted intake through delivery, proof, exception handling, and completion evidence capture. It focuses on translating campaign briefs, trafficking sheets, approvals, and creative assets into structured status updates, completion records, and escalation notes, while preserving domain-specific terminology and avoiding decisions on pricing, refunds, fraud, or safety.

## Who uses this  
- Creative operations managers  
- Venue and attraction marketing operations teams  
- Campaign traffickers and coordinators  
- Ticketing marketing and audience-development teams  
- Agency account and production coordinators  
- Operations reviewers  

## What it produces  
- Execution status update with lifecycle stage and next action  
- Completion or fulfillment record showing what was done and what evidence supports closure  
- Exception summary for blocked, returned, or partially completed work  
- Campaign or creative workflow summary with approvals, blockers, and owners  
- Escalation note for deadline, approval, delivery, pricing, refund, fraud, or safety conflicts  

## Risks and mitigations  
- Refuses to set or override ticket prices, refunds, or safety decisions; routes these to appropriate owners  
- Validates that legal, accessibility, or fraud-related work is not silently replaced with generic marketing practice  
- Preserves domain-specific terminology to avoid flattening workflows into generic marketing support  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Covers U.S.-based arts, entertainment, and recreation workflows including venue, attraction, museum, festival, and ticketing operations  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- Requires tenant facts, authoritative records, and public-source context before proceeding  
- Cannot set or override ticket prices, fees, refunds, accessible seating policy, inventory release, legal copy approval, fraud disposition, or venue-safety decisions  
- Cannot publish or imply legal approval of claims, rights-clearance status, or venue-safety signoff  
- Cannot adjudicate chargebacks, ticket abuse, or fraud cases  
- Cannot mark work complete without supporting booking, delivery, or approval records

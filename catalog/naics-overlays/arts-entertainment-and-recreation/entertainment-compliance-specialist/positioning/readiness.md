# Specialist Overview  
The Entertainment Compliance Specialist translates obligations related to ticketing, booking, guest service, pricing, fraud, and venue safety into evidence-backed compliance handling, monitoring, and escalation. It ensures that decisions remain tied to authoritative records and explicit approval boundaries, without crossing into legal or operational final authority.

## Who uses this  
- Entertainment-compliance-manager  
- Ticketing-compliance-lead  
- Venue-operations-manager  
- Guest-experience-manager  
- Attraction-operations-director  
- Reviewer  

## What it produces  
- Control or obligation matrix tied to the lane  
- Evidence readiness or gap summary  
- Exception and remediation escalation note  
- Control and obligation summary tied to evidence owners  
- Exception and remediation note for control gaps or policy conflicts  
- Audit or attestation preparation packet  

## Risks and mitigations  
- Rejects unsupported price, refund, or promotion overrides, fraud findings, legal interpretations, or safety clearances to prevent misuse as a final authority.  
- Requires complete evidence from ticketing, payment, CRM, and incident systems before issuing a bounded recommendation.  
- Escalates low-confidence cases involving missing or conflicting evidence for further review.  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Source coverage includes Federal Trade Commission, Office of the Law Revision Counsel, U.S. House of Representatives, U.S. Department of Justice, Civil Rights Division, and venue-specific policy and system records  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- What the user or organization must supply before this specialist can work reliably:  
  - Complete order, booking, seat, refund, promotion, and incident records  
  - Accessible-seat inventory and policy versions  
  - Fee disclosures and refund policies  
  - Fraud alerts and bot detection logs  
  - Crowd-control or venue-safety escalation records  

- What is explicitly out of scope:  
  - Providing legal advice, ADA interpretation, consumer-law conclusions, or regulator-response language  
  - Approving price changes, fee waivers, goodwill refunds, guest-service exceptions, comps, or promotion overrides  
  - Adjudicating chargebacks, fraud claims, account-takeover investigations, or reseller enforcement outcomes  
  - Signing or certifying venue-safety readiness, incident closure, security screening sufficiency, or formal compliance attestations  
  - Bypassing controls due to sellout pressure, customer volume, or revenue goals

# Specialist Overview  
The venue operations specialist translates ticketing, guest, seating, and event records into actionable venue execution decisions. This specialist ensures event readiness, handles exceptions, and prepares evidence-backed summaries and escalations for venue operations teams, without overstepping into pricing, legal, or security authority.

## Who uses this  
- Venue operations manager  
- Box office manager  
- Front-of-house manager  
- Guest experience lead  
- Event services supervisor  
- Security operations reviewer  

## What it produces  
- Execution status update with lifecycle stage and next action  
- Completion or fulfillment record showing what was done and what evidence supports closure  
- Exception summary for blocked, returned, or partially completed work  
- Program, campus, or event workflow summary with readiness and owner status  
- Escalation note for scheduling, participation, or records conflicts  

## Risks and mitigations  
- **Refuses to mark work complete when evidence is missing or conflicting**: The specialist will not finalize event status unless all required ticketing, scan, settlement, and incident records are consistent and complete.  
- **Rejects pricing or refund overrides without authority**: The specialist will not approve price changes, refund exceptions, or fee waivers unless explicitly authorized.  
- **Avoids legal or fraud adjudication**: The specialist will not interpret ADA, contract, or fraud cases, and will escalate these to the appropriate lane.  
- **Does not release safety holds**: The specialist will not override security, screening, or occupancy holds; these must be resolved by the appropriate authority.  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Covers U.S. Department of Justice, Federal Trade Commission, and Consumer Financial Protection Bureau guidance relevant to venue operations  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
### What must be provided by the user or organization  
- Validated event manifest and seat map  
- Ticketing and CRM records with no material conflicts  
- Accessible seating inventory and hold codes  
- Guest, payment, and incident records tied to event  
- Scan logs and gate status reports  

### What is explicitly out of scope  
- Approving or overriding pricing, refunds, or fees  
- Adjudicating fraud, counterfeit tickets, or chargebacks  
- Issuing legal interpretations of ADA or consumer-protection law  
- Releasing safety or emergency holds  
- Directing emergency response or security operations  
- Approving settlement adjustments or marking work complete without full evidence

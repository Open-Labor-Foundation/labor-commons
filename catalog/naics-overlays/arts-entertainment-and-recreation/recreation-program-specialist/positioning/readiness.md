# Specialist Overview  
The recreation program specialist administers recreation-program workflows for arts, entertainment, and recreation operations, ensuring that records for classes, camps, leagues, memberships, and drop-in programs are complete, current, properly classified, and policy-aligned. The specialist works with household and participant records, program registrations, waivers, attendance, refunds, and facility reservations to produce administrative summaries, status updates, and escalation packages for governance or adjacent lanes.

## Who uses this  
- Recreation-program-coordinator  
- Community-center-membership-desk  
- Camp-and-aquatics-administrator  
- Municipal-recreation-operations-support-lead  
- Sports-and-league-registration-coordinator  
- Front-desk-or-service-desk-reviewer  

## What it produces  
- Document or records completeness summary with missing items, owner, due date, and source-of-truth references  
- Administrative status note with blockers, ownership, and next-step deadlines  
- Record update note with audit-trail references and evidence used  
- Retention, access, privacy, or governance escalation package  
- Program or membership status summary with due dates, blockers, owners, and required next retrievals  
- Escalation note for missing submissions, approvals, participant data, or policy-exception requests  
- Document or evidence packet for refund review, incident follow-up, roster correction, or official-record handoff  
- Audit-trail or exception summary  

## Validation  
- 13 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Source coverage includes authoritative recreation-management, booking, POS, CRM, incident, service-desk, and policy records  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- The user or organization must supply:  
  - Household and participant profile data  
  - Program registration and reservation records  
  - Waiver and release forms  
  - Payment and refund request history  
  - Incident and service-ticket logs  
  - Facility-reservation and access-status data  
- What is explicitly out of scope:  
  - Approving refund exceptions, transfer exceptions, scholarship overrides, or pricing overrides  
  - Issuing legal advice, public-records rulings, or privacy-law interpretation  
  - Investigating incidents, clearing participants for return, or overriding safety rules  
  - Adjudicating fraud, account misuse, or identity disputes  
  - Deleting, concealing, or backdating official records  
  - Collapsing recreation-program administration into generic customer service

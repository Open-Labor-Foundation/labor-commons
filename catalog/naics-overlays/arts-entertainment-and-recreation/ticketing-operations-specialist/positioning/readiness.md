# Specialist Overview  
The ticketing operations specialist resolves ticketing workflows in the arts, entertainment, and recreation industry, using documented ticketing, access-control, and venue-service records to produce bounded service dispositions, communication summaries, and escalation packages without overstepping into pricing, security, legal, or policy-override authority.

## Who uses this  
- Ticketing managers  
- Box office supervisors  
- Patron services teams  
- Venue operations leads  
- Guest experience coordinators  
- Access control supervisors  
- Arts and recreation event administrators  

## What it produces  
- Resolution note with request summary, action taken, authoritative evidence used, and unresolved items  
- Customer or stakeholder communication summary  
- Escalation package for exceptions, complaints, or unsupported asks  
- Program, campus, or event workflow summary with readiness and owner status  
- Escalation note for scheduling, participation, or records conflicts  

## Risks and mitigations  
- Explicit refusal rules prevent the specialist from making fraud determinations, safety signoffs, legal interpretations, or pricing and revenue decisions, ensuring authority boundaries are not overstepped.  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Source coverage includes ticketing, CRM, access-control, refund, promotion, and communication records  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- Requires minimum tenant facts, authoritative records, and source context before responding safely  
- Explicitly out of scope:  
  - Overriding ticket prices, mandatory fees, refund rules, seating holds, or access policies  
  - Providing legal interpretation, ADA legal advice, or final fraud determinations  
  - Authorizing discretionary comps, seat upgrades, resale exceptions, credential bypasses, or access overrides  
  - Treating incomplete or conflicting records as final truth

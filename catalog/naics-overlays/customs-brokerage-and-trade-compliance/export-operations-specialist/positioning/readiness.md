# Specialist Overview  
The export operations specialist executes export-operations workflows within the customs brokerage and trade compliance industry, managing outbound U.S. shipments from intake through filing, departure confirmation, and completion evidence capture. It applies export filing record truth, Schedule B and ECCN controls, and financial-escalation rules to ensure compliance with U.S. export regulations.

## Who uses this  
- Export coordinators and export operations teams  
- Freight forwarders and customs brokerage operations teams  
- Trade compliance and global trade operations managers  
- Logistics, documentation, and post-departure support teams  
- Operations orchestrators  

## What it produces  
- Execution status update with lifecycle stage and next action  
- Completion or fulfillment record with evidence of closure  
- Exception summary for blocked, returned, or partially completed work  
- Trade execution summary with export filing, classification-reference, licensing-reference, or departure status  
- Escalation note for authority interpretation, document gaps, sanctions, licensing, or valuation disputes  

## Risks and mitigations  
- Explicit refusal to perform legal interpretation, export-control advice, or binding licensing determinations  
- Refusal to fabricate approvals, filing authority, EEI proof, or departure confirmation  
- Refusal to mark work complete without filing disposition, document-release evidence, or approved closure  
- Refusal to override tenant policy, EAR/FTR instructions, or sanctions controls  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Covers U.S. export operations workflows including Census Foreign Trade Regulations, AES/ACE status, and Schedule B/ECCN controls  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- What the user or organization must supply:  
  - Shipper's letter of instruction  
  - Written filing authority  
  - Shipment details and filing prerequisites  
  - Classification-reference and licensing-reference data  
- What is explicitly out of scope:  
  - Legal advice, export-control interpretation, or binding licensing determinations  
  - Final ECCN or licensing decisions, denied-party adjudication, or management override authority  
  - Fraud or AML adjudication outcomes, sanctions release decisions, or financial approval authority

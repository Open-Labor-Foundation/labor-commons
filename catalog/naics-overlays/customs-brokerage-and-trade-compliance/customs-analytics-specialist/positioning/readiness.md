# Specialist Overview  
The customs analytics specialist translates customs brokerage and trade compliance signals—such as entry summaries, broker records, duty calculations, holds, and sanctions screening—into structured metrics, evidence-backed analysis, and reporting with clear caveats and decision thresholds. It focuses on ensuring analytical accuracy, traceability, and alignment with financial and compliance controls.  

## Who uses this  
- Customs operations managers  
- Brokerage analytics and reporting leads  
- Trade compliance managers  
- Finance, reconciliation, and servicing stakeholders  
- Fraud, AML, and legal/compliance reviewers  

## What it produces  
- Metric definition and data-source summary  
- Analysis or report output with caveats and confidence notes  
- Data-quality or disclosure-risk escalation note  
- Analytical brief with metric definitions, source logic, and confidence caveats  
- Exception note for data quality, denominator ambiguity, unsupported trend claims, or cross-system conflicts  

## Risks and mitigations  
- **Legal interpretation risk**: Refuses to provide customs-law conclusions or regulator-facing advocacy.  
- **Data freshness risk**: Requires validation of data source recency before analysis.  
- **Causal claim risk**: Rejects unsupported forecasts, benchmarks, or causal inferences.  
- **Escalation risk**: Routes to appropriate specialists or human reviewers when fraud, AML, or legal issues arise.  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Source coverage includes U.S. Customs and Border Protection, U.S. Census Bureau, and related federal regulatory frameworks  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- **Prerequisites the user must supply**:  
  - Minimum tenant facts  
  - Authoritative records and source context  
  - Defined systems of record and refresh assumptions  
- **What is explicitly out of scope**:  
  - Legal advice, customs-law interpretation, or regulator-facing representation  
  - Final approval to override holds, sanctions outcomes, disclosures, or reconciliations  
  - Fraud adjudication, AML case closure, underwriting, or servicing-operations finality  
  - Unsupported causal claims, forecasts, or benchmarking claims not grounded in evidence

# Specialist Overview  
The global trade reporting specialist assembles customs and export-reporting outputs with explicit source tie-outs, submission logic, and escalations when interpretation risk appears. It supports customs brokerage and trade compliance workflows by ensuring reporting accuracy and compliance readiness.

## Who uses this  
- Customs brokerage reporting teams  
- Trade compliance reporting leads  
- Export compliance and filing operations  
- Finance, reconciliation, and disclosure reviewers  
- Fraud, AML, sanctions, and legal/compliance stakeholders  

## What it produces  
- Quality or safety status summary  
- Hold, release, or nonconformance record with evidence references  
- Corrective-action or incident escalation package  
- Submission or reporting package summary with source tie-outs and caveats  
- Disclosure-risk escalation note  

## Risks and mitigations  
- Refuses to provide legal interpretation or customs-law conclusions  
- Refuses to issue final approval or financial-approval decisions  
- Refuses to bypass documented hold or QA-control boundaries  
- Routes to adjacent specialists when fraud, AML, sanctions, or policy authority is required  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Source coverage includes U.S. Department of Homeland Security, U.S. Customs and Border Protection, U.S. Department of Commerce, U.S. Census Bureau  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- What the user or organization must supply before this specialist can work reliably:  
  - Minimum tenant facts  
  - Authoritative records  
  - Source context  
- What is explicitly out of scope:  
  - Legal advice, customs-law or export-law interpretation  
  - Final approval to override filing holds or sanctions outcomes  
  - Fraud adjudication, AML case closure, underwriting  
  - Unsupported certifications of report correctness  
  - Regulator-facing representation or advocacy

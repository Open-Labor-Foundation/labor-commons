# Specialist Overview  
The Broker Client Services Specialist manages client-facing customs brokerage requests by triaging, documenting, and resolving service inquiries with evidence-based communication and structured escalation. It operates within customs-brokerage workflows, ensuring client requests are handled according to CBP guidelines, tenant policy, and broker records without overstepping into legal or compliance decision-making.

## Who uses this  
- Broker client service teams  
- Customs brokerage operations leads  
- Importer support teams  
- Trade compliance and finance support teams  

## What it produces  
- Resolution note with request summary, authoritative evidence used, action taken, and unresolved items  
- Customer or stakeholder communication summary with safe next-step language and required document list  
- Escalation package for exceptions, complaints, suspicious activity concerns, or unsupported asks  
- Request-resolution summary with issue, action taken, blocker state, and next owner  
- Exception communication escalation note for policy conflicts, repeat failures, or regulated-boundary cases  

## Risks and mitigations  
- **Risk**: Improperly handling or misrepresenting customs status, document receipt, or payment records could mislead clients or regulators.  
  **Mitigation**: The specialist refuses to invent or override source records and requires authoritative evidence before confirming status or next steps.  
- **Risk**: Accepting or approving requests that require legal, compliance, or financial authority could create liability.  
  **Mitigation**: The specialist explicitly refuses to act on or approve such requests and routes them to the correct authority with full evidence.  
- **Risk**: Failing to escalate suspicious or policy-conflicting requests could delay resolution or allow systemic issues to persist.  
  **Mitigation**: The specialist prepares and routes structured escalation packages for exceptions, complaints, or suspicious activity.  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Source coverage includes U.S. Customs and Border Protection guidelines, tenant policy, and broker records  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- **What the user or organization must supply**:  
  - Access to CBP records, tenant policy documents, and broker-specific operational guidelines  
  - Authoritative evidence for request resolution (e.g., invoices, notices, entry statuses)  
  - Defined escalation protocols and ownership boundaries for adjacent specialists  
- **What is explicitly out of scope**:  
  - Providing customs legal advice, HTS classification, or legal finality  
  - Approving duty refunds, broker fee waivers, post-entry corrections, or policy exceptions without documented authority  
  - Clearing fraud, AML, sanctions, or suspicious-activity concerns  
  - Representing missing or conflicting evidence as final truth  
  - Overriding client communication controls or disclosure requirements to close a case

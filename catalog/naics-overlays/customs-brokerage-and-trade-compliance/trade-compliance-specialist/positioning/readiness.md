# Specialist Overview  
The trade compliance specialist owns the customs brokerage and trade compliance overlay, translating trade compliance obligations into evidence-backed control outcomes, evidence-gap packets, and controlled escalation routing for customs-trade workflows.  

## Who uses this  
- Trade compliance lead  
- Customs operations manager  
- Risk and controls teams  
- Servicing and claims coordinators  

## What it produces  
- Control or obligation matrix tied to the lane and source artifacts  
- Evidence packet with missing-data or stale-data flags  
- Exception and remediation escalation note with owner-routing  
- Control and obligation summary tied to evidence owners and review cadence  
- Adjacent-lane routing note for legal, fraud, underwriting, servicing, or platform owners  

## Risks and mitigations  
- Refuses to resolve fraud/AML, consumer-impact, or regulator-facing interpretation issues informally; escalates to appropriate authority for formal adjudication  
- Does not approve financial overrides, underwriting decisions, or policy waivers without traceable evidence and documented owner routing  
- Does not act as the final fraud-dispute arbitrator, ensuring all high-stakes decisions are routed to the correct authority  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Source coverage includes U.S. Department of Homeland Security, U.S. Customs and Border Protection, and U.S. Department of the Treasury, Financial Crimes Enforcement Network  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
**What must be supplied before the specialist can work reliably:**  
- Minimum tenant facts  
- Authoritative records  
- Source context for the trade compliance lane  

**What is explicitly out of scope:**  
- Producing legal interpretation memos, regulator-facing legal opinions, or formal compliance signoff  
- Authorizing financial approvals, underwriting decisions, policy waivers, or pricing overrides  
- Closing disputes, complaints, or service failures without documented evidence and approved owner routing  
- Inventing controls, disclosures, approvals, or records not traceable to tenant or public evidence  
- Acting as the final fraud-dispute arbitrator

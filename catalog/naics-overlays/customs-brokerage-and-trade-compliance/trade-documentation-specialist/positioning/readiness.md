# Specialist Overview  
The trade documentation specialist manages and produces trade records, documentation artifacts, and evidence packages for customs brokerage and trade-compliance workflows. It ensures documentation readiness, evidence completeness, and compliance integrity while maintaining strict boundaries against legal, financial, and safety-critical decisions.

## Who uses this  
- Customs operations teams  
- Trade finance and billing stakeholders  
- Fraud and compliance operations staff  
- Accounting and customer communication owners  
- Claims and servicing operations personnel  

## What it produces  
- Quality or safety status summary with evidence sufficiency and holder obligations  
- Hold, release, or nonconformance record with evidence references  
- Corrective-action and incident escalation package  
- Documentation completeness summary with missing items and owners  
- Records-governance escalation note for unresolved interpretation or policy conflict  

## Risks and mitigations  
- Explicitly refuses to provide legal conclusions, financial approvals, or safety certifications when required documentation is missing or contradictory  
- Escalates to licensed specialists when decisions require legal, underwriting, or policy-override authority  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Source coverage includes U.S. Customs and Border Protection (eCFR), FinCEN, CFPB, and Treasury policy documents  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- Requires authoritative tenant records, source context, and minimum factual inputs before advising  
- Cannot issue legal interpretations, final compliance conclusions, or financial approvals  
- Cannot override tenant policy with generic defaults  
- Cannot clear holds or certify releases when documentation is incomplete or conflicting

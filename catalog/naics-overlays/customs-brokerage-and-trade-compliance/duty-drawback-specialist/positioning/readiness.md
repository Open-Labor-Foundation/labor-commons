# Specialist Overview  
A duty drawback specialist executes the workflow steps for processing duty drawback claims tied to imported merchandise, exports, or destructions, ensuring import-export traceability, eligibility compliance, and adherence to CBP filing rules. The specialist manages claim lifecycle stages, status tracking, and escalation while maintaining clear evidence of completion and process fidelity.

## Who uses this  
- Drawback specialists and drawback coordinators  
- Licensed customs brokers and brokerage operations teams  
- Trade compliance and global trade operations managers  
- Refund, finance, and post-entry support teams  
- Operations orchestrators managing customs and trade workflows  

## What it produces  
- Execution status update with lifecycle stage and next action  
- Completion or fulfillment record with evidence of work done  
- Exception summary for blocked, returned, or partially completed work  
- Trade execution summary with drawback claim, notice, export, liquidation, or refund status  
- Escalation note for authority interpretation, document gaps, accelerated-payment controls, or refund disputes  

## Risks and mitigations  
- Refuses to provide legal interpretation, statutory advice, or binding eligibility determinations  
- Refuses to fabricate approvals, proof of export or destruction, refund authorization, or regulated advice  
- Refuses to mark work complete without filing disposition, liquidation or closure evidence, and required traceability  
- Refuses to override tenant policy, CBP instructions, accelerated-payment controls, or sanctions controls  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Covers CBP drawback filing workflow, ACE and DIS status semantics, import-export traceability, and substitution evidence handling  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- Must be provided with valid intake documents, including claimant records, import entries, export or destruction evidence, and filing prerequisites  
- Must have access to CBP systems and tenant policy for accurate eligibility and status interpretation  
- Must not be used for final legal determinations, fraud or AML adjudication, or financial disbursement authority  
- Cannot provide legal advice, statutory interpretation, protest posture, or regulator-facing advocacy  
- Cannot resolve conflicts between facts, rulings, or tenant policy that affect drawback eligibility

# Specialist Overview  
The classification and valuation specialist supports customs brokerage and trade-compliance workflows by determining if available evidence supports a defensible tariff classification and customs valuation assessment. It focuses on reviewing, assessing, and inspecting data to produce actionable findings for brokers, compliance teams, and reviewers without overstepping into legal or binding authority.  

## Who uses this  
- Licensed customs broker  
- Import compliance manager  
- Post-entry and reconciliation analyst  
- Trade finance and controllership reviewer  

## What it produces  
- Review or inspection findings summary  
- Criteria-to-evidence assessment record  
- Trade execution summary with entry, classification, or reconciliation status  
- Follow-up or remediation note  
- Escalation note for authority interpretation, document gaps, valuation disputes, fraud risk, or final signoff requests  

## Risks and mitigations  
- The specialist refuses requests requiring legal interpretation, formal binding classification authority, policy override, financial approval, or fraud adjudication to avoid overstepping its authority.  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Source coverage includes U.S. Customs and Border Protection guidance, HTSUS, and customs value method ordering  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- What the user or organization must supply before this specialist can work reliably:  
  - Product identity and description  
  - Invoice content and transaction details  
  - HTSUS classification candidates and prior rulings  
  - Commercial records and reconciliation flags  
  - Access to entry summary data and product master data  

- What is explicitly out of scope:  
  - Providing legal advice or customs-law interpretation memos  
  - Approving value overrides or post-entry disclosures outside documented authority  
  - Finalizing admissibility, sanctions, fraud, or AML determinations  
  - Inventing product facts, tariff notes applicability, assists, commissions, or royalty treatment  
  - Certifying CBP acceptance of a position when only a review recommendation is supported

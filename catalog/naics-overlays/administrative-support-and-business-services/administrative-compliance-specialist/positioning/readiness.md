# Specialist Overview  
The administrative compliance specialist focuses on ensuring administrative and business support services meet compliance, policy, and client expectations through evidence-backed control mapping, review, and escalation. It translates compliance obligations into operational artifacts and identifies when administrative practices conflict with governing rules.

## Who uses this  
- Administrative operations manager  
- Business support services lead  
- Office administrative services manager  
- Compliance operations lead  
- Records manager  
- Client services operations lead  
- GRC analyst  

## What it produces  
- Control or obligation matrix tied to administrative-service artifacts, sources, evidence owners, and review cadence  
- Evidence packet or checklist for client audit, internal review, or attestation preparation  
- Evidence readiness or gap summary  
- Exception and remediation escalation note  
- Policy and operational-practice conflict note  
- Adjacent-lane routing note for legal, privacy, HR/payroll, security, procurement, finance, or executive approvers  
- Tenant-assumption log preserving public baseline and client-specific constraints  

## Risks and mitigations  
- **Legal or regulatory misinterpretation**: The specialist refuses or routes any request that implies legal advice, formal certification, or policy override.  
- **Escalation failure**: The specialist escalates when tenant service model, jurisdiction, client contract, or system-of-record context is missing or conflicting.  
- **False compliance assurance**: The specialist does not issue final compliance certification but identifies where evidence is missing or conflicts exist.  

## Validation  
- 13 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Source coverage includes U.S. Census Bureau, U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics, U.S. National Archives and Records Administration, U.S. Government Accountability Office  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- **User or organizational inputs required**:  
  - Tenant service model  
  - Client contract or SOW  
  - Authoritative records (e.g., policy, SOPs, retention schedules)  
  - System-of-record context  
  - Access rosters or redaction logs  
- **Explicitly out of scope**:  
  - Legal interpretation, advice, or formal signoff  
  - Final certification of compliance (SOC, ISO, privacy, HR, etc.)  
  - Policy exception or contract amendment approval  
  - Payroll, HR, or benefit determinations  
  - Data-security incident handling or forensic conclusions  
  - Regulator negotiation or client-facing remedy approval

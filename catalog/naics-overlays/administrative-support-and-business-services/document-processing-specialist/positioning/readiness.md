# Specialist Overview  
The document processing specialist manages and prepares document records within administrative support and business services workflows. It ensures documents are classified, traceable, and governed for fulfillment, compliance, and downstream administrative use, without making decisions on the underlying business cases or legal outcomes.

## Who uses this  
- Document processing analyst  
- Document preparation coordinator  
- Records operations lead  
- Shared-services operations manager  
- BPO queue supervisor  
- Administrative services quality reviewer  
- Workflow orchestrator  

## What it produces  
- Document or records completeness summary  
- Administrative status note with missing items, owners, and due dates  
- Retention, access, or governance escalation package  
- Record update note  
- Document or evidence packet  
- Audit-trail or exception summary  
- Records-governance escalation note  

## Risks and mitigations  
- The specialist refuses to act when source records, authority, or governance context is missing or conflicting, ensuring no decisions are made on case, legal, HR, or financial outcomes.  
- It routes decisions on retention, legal-hold, or access to the appropriate specialists rather than assuming responsibility.  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Based on U.S. Census Bureau, U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics, O*NET OnLine, and National Archives and Records Administration  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- **What must be supplied before reliable operation:**  
  - Source documents, system-of-record evidence, and context including jurisdiction, retention rules, access rights, and approval ownership  
  - Clear tenant policy and client-service context  
  - Complete requester authority and data category identification  

- **What is explicitly out of scope:**  
  - Deciding the underlying case, claim, benefit, legal outcome, HR outcome, financial approval, or client-contract interpretation  
  - Approving retention schedule changes, legal-hold releases, secure disposal, privacy-rights outcomes, redaction policy, final publication, or official record certification  
  - Updating, publishing, disposing, or marking complete a document package without complete and authoritative source records, attachments, or audit trail  
  - Substituting informal communication or narrative assurances for authoritative document or audit systems  
  - Treating administrative support or business-services context as optional or cosmetic in any output

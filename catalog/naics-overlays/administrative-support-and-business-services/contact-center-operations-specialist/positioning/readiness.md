# Specialist Overview  
The contact center operations specialist runs administrative workflows for contact center interactions, ensuring queue classification, case tracking, service-level adherence, quality readiness, and completion evidence assembly, all within the scope of business support and contact center operations under NAICS 561422. It executes administrative tasks without assuming sales, legal, compliance, privacy, payment, or telephony engineering authority.

## Who uses this  
- Contact center operations leads  
- Business support services supervisors  
- BPO queue managers  
- Customer operations coordinators  
- Quality monitoring reviewers  
- Campaign operations analysts  
- Service delivery managers  
- Workflow orchestrators  

## What it produces  
- Execution status update with lifecycle stage, queue state, contact or case ID, blocker, next action, SLA posture, and next owner  
- Completion or fulfillment record showing what was done, final disposition, customer promise or callback state, system updates, QA status, and evidence supporting closure  
- Exception summary for blocked, returned, reworked, abandoned, partially completed, disputed, or compliance-sensitive contact center work  
- Administrative service status summary with queue state, SLA risk, blocker, volume or backlog context, and next owner  
- Escalation note for scope spillover, missing scripts or records, consent or DNC uncertainty, payment-data or privacy controls, customer harm, regulated disclosure, or adjacent authority  

## Risks and mitigations  
- Refuses to close interactions without disposition, system update, and audit evidence  
- Refuses to override consent, Do Not Call, or privacy controls for service-level metrics  
- Refuses to alter scripts, dialer rules, or QA rubrics without documented owner approval  
- Routes to legal, privacy, compliance, or executive approvers when ownership shifts  
- Requires source-system and quality evidence before advancing work  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Grounded in NAICS 561422, O*NET OnLine, U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics, and ISO standards  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- What the user or organization must supply before this specialist can work reliably  
  - Validated tenant facts and service catalog scope  
  - Script, knowledge article, and channel definitions  
  - Consent and suppression records  
  - ACD/IVR/CTI/CRM/WFM/QM system references  
  - Case or campaign identifiers  
  - Disposition codes and QA rubrics  
- What is explicitly out of scope  
  - Legal or regulated decision-making  
  - Sales, refund, or payment authorization  
  - Privacy rights adjudication or identity verification  
  - Workforce planning or telephony engineering  
  - Compliance signoff or policy overrides  
  - Executive operations or safety-critical decisions

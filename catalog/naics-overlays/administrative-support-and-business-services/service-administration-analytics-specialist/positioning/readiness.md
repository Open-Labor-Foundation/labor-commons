# Specialist Overview  
The Service administration analytics specialist translates administrative service records and workflow signals into trustworthy analysis, producing metric definitions, reporting packages, caveats, and disclosure-risk escalations. This specialist operates within NAICS 561 administrative support and business services semantics, ensuring alignment with service catalogs, SLA terms, and compliance boundaries.

## Who uses this  
- Administrative service reporting leads  
- Shared-services analytics teams  
- Client operations managers  
- BPO service performance analysts  
- SLA and queue reporting owners  
- Compliance and audit evidence reviewers  
- Spec-pack reviewers  

## What it produces  
- Metric definition and data-source summary  
- Analysis or report output with caveats and confidence notes  
- Data-quality or disclosure-risk escalation note  
- Analytical brief with metric definitions, source logic, cadence, audience, and confidence caveats  
- Exception note for data quality, denominator ambiguity, unsupported trend claims, stale data, or cross-system conflicts  
- Tenant-adaptation note preserving public baseline, tenant assumptions, and source lineage  

## Risks and mitigations  
- Refuses to approve legal, privacy, financial, or policy claims that require external authority  
- Avoids causal or forecasted analysis unless explicitly supported by source records and approvals  
- Routes high-risk or out-of-scope decisions to appropriate specialists or approvers  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Source coverage includes U.S. Census Bureau, Bureau of Labor Statistics, Office of Management and Budget, and SEC guidelines  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- Requires tenant facts, authoritative records, and source context to proceed safely  
- Does not approve legal, financial, HR, privacy, or policy exceptions  
- Does not interpret retention, access, or official-record controls  
- Does not publish or imply certainty with unresolved source definitions or data conflicts  
- Does not provide advice outside administrative service analytics or SLA/queue metrics

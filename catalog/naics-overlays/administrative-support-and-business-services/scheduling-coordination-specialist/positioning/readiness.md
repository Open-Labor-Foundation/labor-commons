# Specialist Overview  
The scheduling coordination specialist manages the flow of administrative support and business services work across schedules, queues, staff calendars, service windows, and evidence handoffs. It produces bounded, recommendation-only outputs to ensure explicit ownership at every step, without final decision-making authority.

## Who uses this  
- Administrative service scheduling leads  
- Shared-services coordinators  
- Business services operations managers  
- Queue and capacity planners  
- Office administrative services teams  
- BPO service delivery coordinators  
- Spec-pack reviewers  

## What it produces  
- Schedule-capacity-allocation-recommendation  
- Constraint-log  
- Approval-needed-summary  
- Handoff-status-summary  
- Blocked-dependency-escalation-note  
- Administrative-service-schedule-readiness-summary  
- Adjacent-specialist-routing-note  

## Risks and mitigations  
- Will not approve staffing, overtime, SLA exceptions, or client commitments  
- Will not alter payroll records, labor law interpretations, or privacy exceptions  
- Will escalate ambiguous ownership before work is dropped  
- Will route HR, compliance, IT access, and quality acceptance requests to appropriate specialists  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Source coverage includes U.S. Census Bureau, Office of Management and Budget, and Bureau of Labor Statistics  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- Must be provided with validated tenant service rules, client SOW scope, queue demand, workforce availability, and system-of-record evidence before producing outputs  
- Cannot operate outside NAICS 561 administrative support and business services boundaries  
- Cannot act as a generic calendar, project, or HR scheduler  
- Cannot assume authority over contact-center operations, travel, events, payroll, compliance, or client management  
- Cannot override labor policy, privacy rules, or contract terms without explicit escalation

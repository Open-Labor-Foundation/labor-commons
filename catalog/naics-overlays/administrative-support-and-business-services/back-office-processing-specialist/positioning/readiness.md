# Specialist Overview  
The back-office processing specialist validates and manages the prerequisites for administrative and business services work. They ensure that back-office tasks such as document preparation, data entry, record maintenance, and fulfillment only proceed when client identity, requester authorization, service scope, source records, forms, access rights, and completion evidence are complete and accurate.

## Who uses this  
- Back-office-processing-analyst  
- Business-support-services-team-lead  
- Shared-services-operations-manager  
- Document-preparation-coordinator  
- Data-entry-quality-reviewer  
- Business-process-outsourcing-supervisor  
- Workflow-orchestrator  

## What it produces  
- Execution status update with lifecycle stage and next action  
- Completion or fulfillment record showing what was done and what evidence supports closure  
- Exception summary for blocked, returned, or partially completed work  
- Intake or access disposition summary  
- Prerequisite or eligibility escalation note  

## Risks and mitigations  
- Work is not advanced or closed without full validation of client scope, requester authorization, and source records.  
- No action is taken when legal, HR, financial, or safety-critical authority is required.  
- All decisions are based on documented evidence and system-of-record alignment to prevent silent process bypasses.  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Based on U.S. Census Bureau, U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics, O*NET OnLine, and National Archives and Records Administration sources  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- Requires client identity, requester authorization, service scope, source records, required forms, access rights, queue state, and completion evidence to proceed  
- Does not own final legal, contractual, licensed, clinical, engineering, financial, credit, collections, HR, payroll, tax, or executive approval decisions  
- Does not own policy overrides, record-retention schedule changes, data-subject rights determinations, breach determinations, or payment-card compliance signoff  
- Does not process or close cases when authoritative source records, requester authorization, client scope, access entitlement, or QA evidence is missing, stale, contradictory, or outside the tenant service model

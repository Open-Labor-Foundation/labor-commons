# Specialist Overview  
The administrative operations specialist manages administrative service workflows for business support and shared services, ensuring record completeness, queue status tracking, policy adherence, and correct routing without assuming decision-making authority. This specialist focuses on maintaining administrative records and traceability, not on altering or approving the underlying business decisions.

## Who uses this  
- Administrative operations leads  
- Shared-services coordinators  
- Office administrative services teams  
- Business services operations teams  
- Records and document operations reviewers  
- Queue owners  
- Spec-pack reviewers  

## What it produces  
- Document or records completeness summary  
- Administrative status note with missing items, ownership, queue state, and due dates  
- Record update note with source record, field changed, authority basis, timestamp, and audit-trail reference  
- Document or evidence packet with source index and unresolved assumptions  
- Retention, privacy, access, or governance escalation package  
- Administrative service status summary with blocker, next owner, SLA risk, and handoff artifacts  
- Audit-trail or exception summary for unauthorized, unsupported, stale, or conflicting record states  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Source coverage includes U.S. Census Bureau, Office of Management and Budget, U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics, and National Archives and Records Administration  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- The user or organization must supply:  
  - Tenant-authorized systems of record  
  - Complete administrative service requests or intake packets  
  - Retention, access, and privacy rules  
  - Policy and approval references  
  - Defined service catalog rules and administrative procedures  
- What is explicitly out of scope:  
  - Approving or altering official records without documented authority and audit trail requirements  
  - Deciding the underlying case, claim, benefit, contract, employment, finance, legal, compliance, or safety outcome  
  - Bypassing retention, privacy, access, or client authorization controls  
  - Providing legal, tax, accounting, compliance, HR, medical, engineering, safety, or licensed professional advice  
  - Approving policy exceptions, SLA exceptions, budget changes, payment releases, contract changes, or client-facing commitments  
  - Using the administrative support industry label as a cosmetic wrapper without adhering to NAICS 561 scope, service boundaries, and confidentiality controls

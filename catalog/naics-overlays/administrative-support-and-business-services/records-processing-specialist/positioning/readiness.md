# Specialist Overview  
The records processing specialist manages records and metadata in administrative support and business services workflows, ensuring completeness, traceability, and governance compliance for records packages without authoring or approving the underlying business or legal decisions.  

## Who uses this  
- Records processing analyst  
- Records processing coordinator  
- Records operations lead  
- Shared-services operations manager  
- BPO queue supervisor  
- Administrative services quality reviewer  
- Workflow orchestrator  

## What it produces  
- Document or records completeness summary  
- Administrative status note with missing items, owners, and due dates  
- Retention, access, or governance escalation package  
- Record update note  
- Document or evidence packet  
- Records evidence packet  
- Audit-trail or exception summary  
- Records-governance escalation note  

## Risks and mitigations  
- Refuses or returns to the orchestrator when source records, tenant policy, jurisdiction, requester authority, retention rule, access rule, system-of-record evidence, or approval ownership is missing or conflicting in a way that would change the outcome.  
- Requires authoritative systems of record for all record actions and refuses to substitute informal communication for formal documentation.  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Based on sources from U.S. Census Bureau, U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics, O*NET OnLine, sponsored by the U.S. Department of Labor  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- **What the user or organization must supply before this specialist can work reliably:**  
  - Complete source records and metadata  
  - Defined tenant policy and retention rules  
  - Jurisdiction and access rights  
  - Authority delegation for metadata updates and status changes  
  - Access to authoritative systems of record  

- **What is explicitly out of scope:**  
  - Deciding the underlying case, claim, benefit, legal outcome, HR outcome, financial approval, or privacy-rights outcome  
  - Approving retention schedule changes, legal-hold releases, secure disposal, redaction policy, final publication, or official record certification  
  - Updating, publishing, disposing, or marking complete a record package when required source records, attachments, access rights, QA evidence, or audit trail are missing or contradictory  
  - Substituting email, chat, local spreadsheets, or narrative assurances for authoritative records or audit systems  
  - Treating the administrative support and business-services industry name as cosmetic; every output must cite the governing context

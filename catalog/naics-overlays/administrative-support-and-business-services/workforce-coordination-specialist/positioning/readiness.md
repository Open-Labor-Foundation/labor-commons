# Specialist Overview  
The workforce coordination specialist manages the movement of administrative support and business services workflow dependencies across teams, ensuring explicit ownership at every step without assuming substantive workforce, payroll, legal, or client approval authority.  

## Who uses this  
- Staffing operations coordinators  
- Shared-services workforce coordinators  
- Administrative support operations managers  
- BPO service delivery leads  
- Temporary-help desk coordinators  
- Workforce management analysts  
- Service queue owners  
- Workflow orchestrators  
- Spec-pack reviewers  

## What it produces  
- Handoff-summary  
- Dependency-tracker  
- Workforce-assignment-readiness-summary  
- Handoff-status-summary  
- Blocked-dependency-escalation-note  
- Routing-confirmation-note  
- Closure-confirmation  
- Source-conflict-or-low-confidence-escalation-note  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Source coverage includes U.S. Census Bureau, Office of Management and Budget, Bureau of Labor Statistics, and Department of Labor Employment and Training Administration  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- The user or organization must supply: client SOW scope, job order details, worker eligibility prerequisites, skills or credential evidence, safety training or worksite readiness evidence, timekeeping handoff requirements, queue owner maps, and source-record consistency  
- What is explicitly out of scope:  
  - Approval of hiring, termination, worker classification, pay, overtime, compensable-time treatment, benefits, leave, accommodation, or employment-policy exceptions  
  - Client-facing commitments, SOW changes, service credits, staffing-level approvals, budget approvals, or final service acceptance decisions  
  - Execution of recruiting, scheduling, payroll processing, contact-center operations, records governance, safety investigation, legal review, quality signoff, finance approval, or client-management work  
  - Absorbing coordination responsibility into substantive workforce, HR, payroll, legal, safety, finance, quality, or client decision-making authority

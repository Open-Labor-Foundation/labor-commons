# Specialist Overview  
A business process outsourcing (BPO) specialist supports the operation of outsourced administrative workflows by aligning service delivery with client service level agreements (SLAs), approved vendors, queue status, and capacity commitments. It ensures service readiness and administrative supply continuity by validating prerequisites, summarizing availability, and escalating issues like shortages or vendor risks.

## Who uses this  
- BPO operations manager  
- Shared services operations lead  
- Client service delivery manager  
- Vendor operations coordinator  
- Procurement operations analyst  
- Service desk queue lead  
- Business support services manager  

## What it produces  
- Availability-or-supply-status-summary  
- Procurement-or-replenishment-recommendation  
- Vendor-or-procurement-action-note  
- Shortage-or-vendor-risk-escalation-note  
- Administrative-service-status-summary  
- Scope-spillover-or-missing-records-escalation-note  
- Tenant-assumption and source-precedence log  

## Risks and mitigations  
- Refuses to approve spend, select suppliers, or override supplier-risk controls; returns to orchestrator if tenant service model, client SOW, or system-of-record facts are missing or conflicting.  

## Validation  
- 13 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Sources include U.S. Census Bureau, U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics, International Organization for Standardization  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- **What must be supplied**: A valid client SOW, approved vendor records, queue state, SLA evidence, capacity commitments, and inventory/license status.  
- **What is out of scope**:  
  - Approve spend, commit the tenant contractually, or amend SOWs  
  - Authorize service credits or negotiate supplier terms  
  - Approve new suppliers, override supplier-risk controls, or bypass approved-vendor requirements  
  - Make HR/payroll staffing decisions, legal interpretations, or regulated adjudications  
  - Treat queue or procurement tracking as quality release, legal approval, or financial approval  
  - Report outsourced service work as audit-safe when key records or approvals are missing

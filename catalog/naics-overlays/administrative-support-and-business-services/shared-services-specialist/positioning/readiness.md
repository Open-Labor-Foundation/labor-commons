# Specialist Overview  
The shared services specialist manages administrative and business support service workflows within NAICS 561 boundaries, applying service catalogs, SLAs, runbooks, and entitlement rules to classify, resolve, or escalate shared-service requests without overstepping into legal, financial, or client-management authority.  

## Who uses this  
- Shared-services coordinators  
- Administrative service center teams  
- Business services operations teams  
- Queue owners  
- Service desk and support leads  
- Client operations liaisons  
- Spec-pack reviewers  

## What it produces  
- Resolution note with request summary, action taken, evidence used, disposition, unresolved items, and next owner  
- Customer or stakeholder communication summary with safe wording, blocker state, and communication-log update  
- Escalation package for exceptions, complaints, repeat failures, unsupported asks, ownership shifts, or authority gaps  
- Administrative service status summary with queue state, SLA risk, blocker, source records, and next owner  
- Escalation note for scope spillover, missing records, source conflict, or policy ambiguity  

## Risks and mitigations  
- Explicit refusal rules are in place for legal, financial, compliance, privacy, safety, and client-management decisions. Requests requiring these are escalated or rejected.  
- Mitigations include strict adherence to service boundaries, documentation of all actions, and routing to appropriate specialists when authority is insufficient.  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Source coverage includes NAICS 561 administrative and business services, client service catalogs, SOWs, SLAs, and records discipline  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- **What the user or organization must supply**:  
  - Tenant-approved service catalog and runbook  
  - Entitlement and SLA rules  
  - Queue ownership records  
  - Authoritative source records and audit events  
  - Approved spec packs and escalation routing definitions  

- **What is explicitly out of scope**:  
  - Legal, compliance, privacy, or licensed professional advice  
  - Financial approvals, payment releases, or contract changes  
  - Client business process operation or strategic management decisions  
  - Policy overrides, exception approvals, or HR decisions  
  - Disclosing confidential or cross-client information without confirmed authority  
  - Improvising authority beyond documented service rules

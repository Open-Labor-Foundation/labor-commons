# Specialist Overview  
The business services quality specialist ensures quality control and assurance for administrative support and business services by evaluating the sufficiency of service evidence and recommending actions such as release, hold, or corrective action. This specialist focuses on NAICS 561-style operations and works within the boundaries of service contracts, compliance frameworks, and operational controls.

## Who uses this  
- Business services quality lead  
- Administrative support services manager  
- Shared-services quality analyst  
- Business process outsourcing operations lead  
- Client service delivery manager  
- Quality assurance reviewer  
- Service organization control owner  

## What it produces  
- Quality or safety status summary  
- Hold, release, or nonconformance record with evidence references  
- Corrective-action or incident escalation package  
- Quality or assurance findings summary  
- Corrective-action or unresolved-risk escalation note  
- Service-quality review note with sampled items, criteria, evidence, defect pattern, and next owner  
- Tenant-assumption log preserving public baseline, client SOW constraints, service-model facts, and unresolved ambiguity  

## Risks and mitigations  
- Must not provide legal or HR determinations, safety-critical approvals, or financial signoffs. Refuses requests that bypass documented quality holds or controls without explicit tenant authority.  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Source coverage based on U.S. Census Bureau, Office of Management and Budget, Bureau of Labor Statistics, and ISO standards  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- What the user or organization must supply:  
  - Validated tenant service model, client SOW, or service catalog  
  - Access to quality plans, SOPs, SLAs, work orders, queue records, and audit evidence  
  - Clear evidence of service work, case handling, or administrative-service execution  

- What is explicitly out of scope:  
  - Clearing holds, closing findings, releasing service batches, or marking corrective action effective without required evidence or approval  
  - Bypassing documented hold, release, safety, privacy, or quality-control boundaries  
  - Providing legal interpretation, client-contract approval, or formal audit opinion  
  - Deciding HR placements, payroll, or financial approvals  
  - Running security incident response or safety-critical signoff outside the authorized lane  
  - Treating the industry name as cosmetic; outputs must reference business-service artifacts such as client SOWs, service catalogs, and SLA evidence

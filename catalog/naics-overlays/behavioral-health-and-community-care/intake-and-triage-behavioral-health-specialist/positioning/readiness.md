# Specialist Overview  
The Intake and Triage Behavioral Health Specialist validates intake and access prerequisites for behavioral health and community care to ensure that downstream therapy, psychiatry, substance use, community support, or case management work only begins when identity, consent, privacy, referral, eligibility, authorization, and immediate safety conditions are clear and supported by the correct records. It focuses on classification, prerequisite gathering, behavioral health evidence validation, and boundary-safe routing before service execution.

## Who uses this  
- Behavioral-health-intake-coordinator  
- Patient-access-specialist  
- Community-behavioral-health-operations-manager  
- Care-access-supervisor  
- Utilization-management-coordinator  
- Reviewer  

## What it produces  
- Intake disposition note with acceptance, rejection, pend, or routing outcome  
- Prerequisite or eligibility checklist showing missing or satisfied conditions  
- Escalation packet for requests blocked on approvals, access, identity mismatch, privacy constraints, or immediate-safety concerns  
- Intake or access disposition summary  
- Prerequisite or eligibility escalation note  

## Risks and mitigations  
- Refuses to handle diagnostic, crisis counseling, or involuntary treatment decisions to prevent overstepping clinical or legal authority.  
- Requires documented evidence and compliance with HIPAA and Part 2 requirements before any protected information is used or shared.  
- Blocks routing or scheduling until all eligibility, referral, and safety conditions are met, preventing premature or unsafe service delivery.  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Source coverage includes Substance Abuse and Mental Health Services Administration, Centers for Medicare & Medicaid Services  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- **Must supply**: Validated identity documentation, signed consent forms, referral records, eligibility and authorization records, and documented behavioral health screening results.  
- **Out of scope**:  
  - Diagnosing or determining level of care  
  - Providing crisis counseling or making involuntary treatment decisions  
  - Approving admissions, medication, or treatment plans  
  - Disclosing protected mental health or substance use disorder records without proper authorization  
  - Overriding payer, referral, or program requirements to accelerate access

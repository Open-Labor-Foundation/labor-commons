# Specialist Overview  
The crisis services coordination specialist manages the movement of behavioral-health crisis workflow dependencies across teams, ensuring clear ownership and handoff of care transitions without assuming clinical, legal, or transport authority. It focuses on dependency tracking, handoff summaries, and escalation of boundary cases to the correct adjacent specialists.

## Who uses this  
- Crisis-call-center-supervisor  
- Mobile-crisis-coordinator  
- Behavioral-health-operations-manager  
- Crisis-stabilization-intake-reviewer  
- Community-care-transition-coordinator  
- Peer-support-follow-up-lead  
- Program-compliance-reviewer  

## What it produces  
- Handoff-summary  
- Handoff-status-summary  
- Dependency-tracker  
- Blocked-dependency-escalation-note  
- Routing-confirmation-note  
- Closure-confirmation  
- Privacy-or-safety-boundary-escalation  
- Authority-conflict-escalation  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Source coverage includes Substance Abuse and Mental Health Services Administration and Centers for Medicare & Medicaid Services  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- What the user or organization must supply before this specialist can work reliably:  
  - Minimum tenant facts and jurisdiction context  
  - Authoritative records from EHR, dispatch, and follow-up systems  
  - Clear crisis-continuum terminology and documented consent posture  

- What is explicitly out of scope:  
  - Performing or finalizing suicide-risk or violence-risk assessments  
  - Authorizing involuntary commitment, emergency detention, transport, medication, admission, discharge, or treatment  
  - Approving payer coverage, benefits interpretation, medical necessity, or financial release  
  - Disclosing protected behavioral-health or SUD information outside permitted and documented authority  
  - Drifting into generic care coordination when crisis-specific artifacts are required

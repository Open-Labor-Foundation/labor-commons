# Specialist Overview  
The SUD program operations specialist administers the operational execution of substance use disorder (SUD) treatment programs once a referral or treatment intent is established. This role focuses on tracking and managing program readiness, documentation completeness, scheduling, dosing coordination, authorization, compliance, and workflow exceptions using authoritative records from EHR, OTP dispensing, lab, payer, and privacy systems.

## Who uses this  
- SUD program coordinators  
- OTP or outpatient program operations leads  
- Behavioral health scheduling and roster teams  
- Utilization review coordinators  
- Care coordination administrators  
- Quality and compliance support teams  

## What it produces  
- Execution status update with lifecycle stage and next action  
- Completion or fulfillment record showing what was done and what evidence supports closure  
- Exception summary for blocked, returned, or partially completed work  
- Program or membership status summary with due dates, blockers, and owners  
- Escalation note for missing submissions, approvals, participant data, privacy concerns, or safety-sensitive facts  

## Risks and mitigations  
- Refuses to act on clinical, privacy, or approval decisions outside documented lane boundaries; escalates to appropriate owners when necessary  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Source coverage includes authoritative EHR, OTP dispensing, lab, payer, privacy-consent, and audit records  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- Requires access to authoritative EHR, OTP dispensing, lab, payer, and audit records before work can proceed  
- Does not own diagnosis, ASAM level determination, medical necessity judgment, dose selection, take-home authorization, treatment-plan approval, clinical interpretation of toxicology, crisis or safety intervention, legal interpretation of 42 CFR Part 2 or state law, payer contract interpretation, controlled-substance inventory signoff, or regulator, accreditor, or executive approval authority

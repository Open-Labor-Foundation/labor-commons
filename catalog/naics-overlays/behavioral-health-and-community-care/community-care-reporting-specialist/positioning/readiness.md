# Specialist Overview  
The community care reporting specialist assembles behavioral health and community-care reporting and disclosure outputs by converting community-based records into evidence-backed metrics, reports, and disclosures. The specialist ensures reporting is grounded in person-centered data, maintains source lineage, and escalates when interpretation risks or authority boundaries are unclear.

## Who uses this  
- Community behavioral health quality managers  
- Care coordination program managers  
- CCBHC reporting leads  
- Medicaid or managed-care reporting analysts  
- Grant and public-reporting coordinators  
- Privacy and compliance support teams  
- Executive reviewers  

## What it produces  
- Metric-definition-and-data-source-summary  
- Analysis-or-report-output-with-caveats  
- Caveat-or-reconciliation-note  
- Submission-or-reporting-package-summary  
- Data-quality-or-disclosure-risk-escalation-note  
- Adjacent-specialist-routing-note  

## Risks and mitigations  
- The specialist refuses requests to publish unsupported causal, policy, compliance, access, outcome, or safety claims.  
- The specialist blocks confident publication when source quality, protected-record posture, small-cell thresholds, or signoff posture is unresolved.  
- The specialist escalates when clinical interpretation, incident determination, privacy interpretation, state-policy interpretation, legal advice, grant attestation, payer adjudication, or final approval authority is required.  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Source coverage includes person-centered service plans, encounters, claims, authorizations, critical-incident logs, grievance records, care-coordination notes, survey files, protected-record controls, and state or federal reporting templates  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- What the user or organization must supply:  
  - Minimum tenant facts  
  - Authoritative records  
  - Program authority  
  - Metric definitions  
  - Reporting audience  
  - Cadence  
  - Jurisdiction context  

- What is explicitly out of scope:  
  - Clinical eligibility, diagnosis, medical-necessity, treatment-effectiveness, crisis, or abuse or neglect determinations  
  - Legal advice, final HIPAA or Part 2 interpretation, final state-policy interpretation, or grant, payer, regulator, or accreditor attestation  
  - Unsupported outcome, access, equity, safety, or compliance claims when measure logic, denominator size, survey method, or source lineage is unresolved  
  - Release of protected behavioral health or SUD data outside minimum-necessary and permitted-use boundaries  
  - Generic BI work that ignores person-centered plans, encounters, claims lags, incident logs, consent status, survey methodology, and community-care program authority

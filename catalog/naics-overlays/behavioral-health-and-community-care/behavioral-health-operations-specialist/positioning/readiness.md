# Specialist Overview  
The behavioral health operations specialist manages the day-to-day operational workflow of behavioral health and community care after a referral, episode, or work item is established. It focuses on readiness, access, documentation, scheduling, care coordination, authorization, crisis follow-up, and evidence-backed closure using authoritative records and industry-specific terminology.

## Who uses this  
- Behavioral health clinic operations managers  
- Access and intake coordinators  
- Care coordination administrators  
- Behavioral health scheduling teams  
- Utilization and authorization coordinators  
- Quality and compliance support teams  

## What it produces  
- Execution status update with lifecycle stage and next action  
- Completion or fulfillment record showing what was done and what evidence supports closure  
- Exception summary for blocked, returned, or partially completed work  
- Operational status summary with evidence gaps, access blockers, and next owner  
- Escalation note for clinical, regulatory, privacy, or safety-sensitive questions  

## Risks and mitigations  
- Explicitly refuses actions requiring diagnosis, risk judgment, crisis disposition, privacy-law interpretation, medication authority, licensed care decisions, or signoff authority outside the lane.  
- Escalates to licensed clinicians, compliance, or legal teams when operational boundaries are exceeded.  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Source coverage includes authoritative behavioral-health EHR, scheduling, document, payer, hospital-notification, crisis, and audit records  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
**User or organization must supply:**  
- Minimum tenant facts, authoritative records, and source context for referrals, active care, transitions, or discharges  
- Access to documented service and policy rules  

**Explicitly out of scope:**  
- Diagnosis, suicide-risk or violence-risk determination  
- Medical necessity judgment, involuntary-treatment decisions  
- Medication selection or refill authorization  
- Psychotherapy or clinical note approval  
- Crisis disposition, privacy-law interpretation  
- Payer policy override, quality-signoff authority  
- Regulator, accreditor, legal, or executive approval authority

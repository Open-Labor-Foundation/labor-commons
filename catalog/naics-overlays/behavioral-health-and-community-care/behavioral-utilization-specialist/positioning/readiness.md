# Specialist Overview  
The behavioral utilization specialist manages behavioral-health utilization workflows from intake to audit-ready closure, using behavioral-health-specific terminology and artifacts to ensure compliance with program rules, eligibility, and documentation requirements without overstepping into clinical or legal decision-making.  

## Who uses this  
- Behavioral-health-utilization-coordinator  
- Community-program-operations-manager  
- Care-management-supervisor  
- Payer-liaison  
- Medical-director-support-staff  
- Behavioral-health-compliance-analyst  

## What it produces  
- Execution status update with lifecycle stage and next action  
- Completion or fulfillment record showing what was done and what evidence supports closure  
- Exception summary for blocked, returned, or partially completed work  
- Program or case-status summary  
- Eligibility, privacy, or authority escalation note  

## Risks and mitigations  
- Will not issue licensed clinical denials, override payer policy, or treat behavioral-health utilization as generic prior authorization.  
- Will not disclose SUD records or sensitive behavioral-health data without resolved privacy authority.  
- Will not mark a case complete without required reviewer action, authorization status, or closure evidence.  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Source coverage includes Substance Abuse and Mental Health Services Administration, Centers for Medicare & Medicaid Services, Electronic Code of Federal Regulations  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- What the user or organization must supply before this specialist can work reliably:  
  - Validated eligibility and program context  
  - Minimum level-of-care and active-treatment documentation  
  - Accurate and accessible member and referral records  
  - Access to authoritative systems of record for lifecycle tracking  
- What is explicitly out of scope:  
  - Making independent diagnoses or determining final medical necessity  
  - Issuing denials, approvals, appeals, or legal interpretations  
  - Disclosing SUD or other sensitive behavioral-health records without proper consent or authority  
  - Treating behavioral-health utilization as generic prior authorization  
  - Completing a case without required reviewer action or closure evidence

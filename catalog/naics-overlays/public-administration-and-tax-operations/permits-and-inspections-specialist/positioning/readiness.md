# Specialist Overview  
The permits and inspections specialist evaluates how a receiving authority would typically expect a submission or inspection package to be structured, identifies likely deficiencies or follow-up questions, and describes administrative review and inspection workflow expectations without claiming legal validity or final agency acceptance.  

## Who uses this  
- Program-manager  
- Permit-review-coordinator  
- Inspection-program-manager  
- Environmental-manager  
- Regulatory-affairs-manager  
- Reviewer  

## What it produces  
- Authority-expectation review memo with likely reviewer concerns, structural gaps, and clarifying questions  
- Completeness and inspection checklist framed from the receiving-authority perspective  
- Administrative-review workflow summary with deficiency and follow-up expectations  
- Boundary-safe refusal and routing note for adjacent specialties  

## Risks and mitigations  
- Explicitly refuses to provide final legal opinions, agency commitments, or statements of official acceptance to avoid misrepresentation in high-stakes regulatory or compliance contexts.  
- Does not claim to perform engineering review, technical certification, or design approval, preventing misuse in technical decision-making.  

## Validation  
- 10 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Source coverage includes OECD, U.S. EPA, and CFR materials  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- What the user or organization must supply before this specialist can work reliably  
  - A draft submission or inspection package for evaluation  
  - Relevant procedural or regulatory context for the receiving authority  
  - Clarification on the jurisdiction and specific program type (e.g., environmental, construction, zoning)  
- What is explicitly out of scope  
  - Final legal advice, privileged agency analysis, or statements of legal validity  
  - Issuance of permits or enforcement decisions  
  - Engineering review, technical certification, or design approval  
  - Negotiation on behalf of an authority or regulated party  
  - Broad public-sector administration unrelated to permit and inspection workflow expectations

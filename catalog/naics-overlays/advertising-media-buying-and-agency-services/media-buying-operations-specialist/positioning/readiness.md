# Specialist Overview  
The media buying operations specialist coordinates the execution of media buys from approved media plans through to completion, ensuring that all operational steps are followed and documented without overstepping into strategy, legal, editorial, or financial authority. The specialist focuses on tracking, validating, and advancing the lifecycle of media buys using industry-specific terminology and artifacts.

## Who uses this  
- Media-buyer  
- Buying-operations-associate  
- Campaign-activation-coordinator  
- Media-operations-coordinator  
- Agency-operations-specialist  
- Client-delivery-coordinator  
- Operations-orchestrator  

## What it produces  
- Execution status update with media buy lifecycle stage, blockers, owners, due dates, approvals, commitments, and next action  
- Campaign or media buy workflow summary with approval state, authorized scope, required artifacts, vendor confirmation status, and client-impact notes  
- Exception queue note for missing authorization, unsigned IO, unconfirmed avails, rate mismatch, material deadline risk, trafficking handoff gap, pacing issue, discrepancy, confidentiality conflict, or approval blocker  
- Escalation note for deadline, approval, confidentiality, client-rights, legal, editorial, policy, financial, rate, makegood, billing, or contractual conflicts  
- Completion evidence record with booking confirmation, trafficking handoff proof, delivery or closeout records, discrepancy disposition, invoice-support references, approval references, and retention location  

## Risks and mitigations  
- Explicitly refuses to approve or override legal, editorial, privacy, financial, or client-acceptance authority  
- Will not proceed without required media plan, authorization, vendor confirmation, or approval evidence  
- Maintains clear boundaries to prevent overstatement of authority or unauthorized escalation  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Source coverage includes media buying, trafficking, and agency services industry artifacts and terminology  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- What the user or organization must supply before this specialist can work reliably:  
  - Approved media plan  
  - Buy authorization  
  - RFP or avails responses  
  - Rate cards  
  - Insertion orders  
  - Vendor confirmations  
  - Platform order records  
  - Trafficking handoff requirements  
  - Material deadlines  
  - Approval records  
  - Pacing reports  
  - Discrepancy notes  
  - Makegood escalation artifacts  
  - Proof records  
  - Invoice-support packets  
  - Client communication logs  

- What is explicitly out of scope:  
  - Legal advice, privacy-law interpretation, or claims substantiation  
  - Approval of editorial copy, creative, or brand suitability exceptions  
  - Media strategy, budget shifts, bid strategy, or targeting strategy  
  - Rate negotiation, makegoods, credits, billing adjustments, or client commitments  
  - Booking, handoff, reconciliation, or completion without full documentation and approvals  
  - Disclosure or repurposing of client files, audience data, or unreleased creative outside engagement and confidentiality rules  
  - Treating all media channels as interchangeable without tenant-qualified context

# Specialist Overview  
The production coordination agency specialist ensures that advertising and media production workflows move smoothly between internal and external stakeholders by tracking, classifying, and handoffing production work across agency queues. It maintains clear ownership, artifact requirements, and blocker states at each step without assuming creative, legal, or executional authority.

## Who uses this  
- Agency-production-coordinator  
- Agency-project-manager  
- Integrated-producer  
- Account-operations-associate  
- Creative-operations-coordinator  
- Delivery-coordinator  
- Operations-orchestrator  

## What it produces  
- Handoff summary naming prior owner, next owner, required artifacts, blocker state, due date, client-impact note, and closure condition  
- Dependency tracker or blocker summary tied to job, asset, owner, schedule, system-of-record link, and missing evidence  
- Routing confirmation note for production coordination handoff  
- Blocked-dependency escalation note for stalled, duplicated, ambiguous, confidentiality-conflicted, rights-conflicted, approval-conflicted, schedule-conflicted, or contractual-risk work  
- Closure confirmation showing work landed with the right destination owner, accepted artifact set, blocker resolution, and retention location  

## Risks and mitigations  
- **Risk**: Misrouting sensitive materials due to incomplete confidentiality or rights verification.  
  **Mitigation**: Refuse to hand off work if required confidentiality rules, rights evidence, or approval constraints are not explicitly met.  
- **Risk**: Confusing coordination with execution or approval authority.  
  **Mitigation**: Explicitly reject any request to act as producer of record, creative approver, legal reviewer, or final signoff authority.  
- **Risk**: Overlooking missing prerequisites causing downstream delays.  
  **Mitigation**: Validate required tenant facts, SOW, approvals, and artifacts before proceeding.  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Source coverage includes 4A’s, ANA, AICP, and industry-standard production workflow terms  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- **Must be provided by user or organization**:  
  - Valid tenant service model and engagement scope  
  - Approved SOW, production brief, and budget guardrails  
  - Confirmed owners, schedules, and approval rules  
  - Required artifacts including job jacket, production calendar, and retention folder  
- **Explicitly out of scope**:  
  - Approving creative direction, final copy, legal claims, or usage-rights interpretations  
  - Awarding vendors, approving POs, budgets, overages, or change orders  
  - Producing the shoot, editing assets, trafficking ads, or uploading deliverables  
  - Approving media strategy, platform-policy overrides, or final client acceptance  
  - Treating all production formats as interchangeable without tenant-qualified context

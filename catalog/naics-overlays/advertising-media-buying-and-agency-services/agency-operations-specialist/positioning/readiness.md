# Specialist Overview  
The agency operations specialist coordinates the day-to-day execution of agency workflows, managing client work from intake through delivery and closeout, while maintaining boundaries around legal, editorial, strategic, financial, and final approval authority.

## Who uses this  
- Agency-operations-coordinator  
- Project-manager  
- Creative-operations-coordinator  
- Media-operations-coordinator  
- Account-operations-associate  
- Delivery-coordinator  
- Operations-orchestrator  

## What it produces  
- Execution status update with lifecycle stage, blockers, owners, due dates, approval state, and next action  
- Campaign or creative workflow summary with approvals, missing artifacts, rework needs, client-impact notes, and system-of-record references  
- Exception summary for blocked, returned, partially completed, confidentiality-conflicted, approval-conflicted, or deadline-conflicted work  
- Escalation note for deadline, approval, confidentiality, client-rights, legal, editorial, policy, financial, production, delivery, or contractual conflicts  
- Completion or fulfillment record showing what was done, which evidence supports closure, and where the record was retained  

## Risks and mitigations  
- Explicitly refuses to act on or approve legal claims, editorial copy, media strategy, budget changes, scope exceptions, change orders, makegoods, credits, or final client acceptance  
- Will not deliver, close, or mark work complete without required brief, SOW, estimate, asset, review, approval, quality, delivery, proof, or retention evidence  
- Enforces confidentiality, rights, and tenant-security rules to prevent unauthorized disclosure or reuse of client files, pitch work, or agency work product  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Source coverage includes 4A's and Interactive Advertising Bureau standards  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
**What the user or organization must supply before this specialist can work reliably:**  
- Valid tenant policy and engagement scope documentation  
- Client brief, SOW, estimate, and project plan artifacts  
- Access to required systems of record for workflow tracking and approval  
- Defined approval rules and authority matrices  
- Client authority and confidentiality constraints  

**What is explicitly out of scope:**  
- Providing legal advice, privacy-law interpretation, or regulatory signoff  
- Approving creative concepts, editorial copy, media strategy, or budget shifts  
- Delivering, closing, or marking work complete without required evidence  
- Disclosing or transmitting client files, pitch work, or agency work product outside approved engagement rules  
- Treating different creative or media workflows as interchangeable without tenant-qualified context

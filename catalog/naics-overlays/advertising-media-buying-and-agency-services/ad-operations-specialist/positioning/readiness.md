# Specialist Overview  
An ad operations specialist coordinates the execution of advertising campaigns by managing workflow status, validating prerequisites, tracking campaign artifacts, and preparing evidence-based documentation. The specialist ensures operational readiness and compliance with client, publisher, and platform requirements while staying within clearly defined boundaries that separate operational support from legal, editorial, or strategic approvals.

## Who uses this  
- Ad-operations-associate  
- Campaign-trafficker  
- Media-operations-coordinator  
- Agency-operations-specialist  
- Client-delivery-coordinator  
- Operations-orchestrator  

## What it produces  
- Execution status update with campaign lifecycle stage, blockers, owners, due dates, approvals, and next action  
- Campaign or creative workflow summary with approval state, required artifacts, QA status, and client-impact notes  
- Exception queue note for missing assets, tag failure, tracking mismatch, publisher rejection, pacing issue, discrepancy, confidentiality conflict, or approval blocker  
- Escalation note for deadline, approval, confidentiality, client-rights, legal, editorial, policy, financial, or contractual conflicts  
- Completion evidence record with launch proof, QA evidence, delivery or closeout records, approval references, and retention location  

## Risks and mitigations  
- **Refusal rule:** The specialist refuses to approve ad claims, privacy consent, editorial content, media strategy, budget changes, makegood offers, or contractual exceptions.  
- **Refusal rule:** The specialist refuses to traffic, launch, or close campaigns without required documentation, including campaign briefs, IOs, trafficking sheets, creative assets, QA, delivery, and approval evidence.  
- **Refusal rule:** The specialist refuses to disclose or repurpose client files, audience data, performance data, platform credentials, or unreleased creative outside engagement, consent, and confidentiality rules.  
- **Mitigation:** All escalations and exceptions are documented and routed to the correct adjacent specialist or approver to ensure accountability and clarity.  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Source coverage includes artifacts and terminology from digital ad operations lanes such as IO, campaign brief, trafficking sheet, ad server, tracking tags, publisher specs, and QA records  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- **Must be supplied by user or organization:**  
  - Campaign brief with flight dates, advertiser, placements, and creative specs  
  - Insertion order or client authority documentation  
  - Creative assets, tracking tags, pixels, and trafficking sheets  
  - Publisher or platform specifications and approval rules  
  - Client or tenant policy defining approval boundaries  

- **Explicitly out of scope:**  
  - Approving legal claims, privacy-law posture, editorial content, or media strategy  
  - Making budget decisions, makegood offers, or contractual exceptions  
  - Approving creative concepts, brand suitability exceptions, or bid strategies  
  - Interpreting privacy law or certifying final client acceptance  
  - Traffic, launch, or close campaigns without required documentation and approvals

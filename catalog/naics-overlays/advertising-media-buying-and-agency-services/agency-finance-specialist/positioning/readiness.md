# Specialist Overview
The agency finance specialist handles financial workflows specific to advertising, media buying, and agency services. It transforms client agreements, media plans, vendor invoices, and campaign delivery records into traceable billing, posting, reconciliation, and revenue-exception outputs, while avoiding unsupported financial treatment or overreach into legal, editorial, or accounting authority.

## Who uses this
- Agency-finance-operations-lead  
- Client-billing-specialist  
- Media-billing-and-reconciliation-analyst  
- Production-finance-coordinator  
- Account-operations-manager  
- Delivery-management-lead  
- Reviewer  

## What it produces
- Billing, claim, invoice, or posting summary with source traceability  
- Reconciliation note showing matched items, exceptions, and unresolved variances  
- Financial exception escalation for denials, disputes, write-offs, or unsupported adjustments  
- Billing or financial reconciliation summary with root-cause categories  
- Revenue exception escalation note  

## Risks and mitigations
- Mitigation: Refuses to create financial adjustments, codes, or rebate allocations without source evidence and authority.  
- Mitigation: Routes work to legal, editorial, quality, or delivery specialists when required, instead of assuming final authority.  
- Mitigation: Avoids disclosing client IOs, pricing, or campaign records through unapproved channels.  

## Validation
- 14 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Source coverage includes Interactive Advertising Bureau, 4A's, and Media Rating Council standards  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations
- What the user or organization must supply before this specialist can work reliably:
  - Client agreements, statements of work, insertion orders, purchase orders
  - Media plans, campaign delivery records, vendor invoices, and proof-of-performance files
  - Client approvals, rebilling rules, and reconciliation queues
  - Clear confidentiality constraints and system context

- What is explicitly out of scope:
  - Inventing adjustments, codes, accounting treatment, or rebate allocations without source support and authority
  - Implying legal, editorial, contractual, compliance, tax, or financial-approval authority
  - Overriding confidentiality, data-use, client approval, or review constraints to accelerate billing or close
  - Resolving conflicting agency contracts, vendor documents, or tenant policy as if it held final interpretation authority

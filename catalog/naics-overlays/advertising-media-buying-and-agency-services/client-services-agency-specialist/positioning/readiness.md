# Specialist Overview  
The client services agency specialist resolves agency client-service requests by using evidence-backed status, policy-aware communication, approval discipline, and escalation to the correct owner when requests cross into legal, editorial, media strategy, financial, contractual, privacy, quality, or final signoff authority.

## Who uses this  
- Client-services-associate  
- Agency-account-coordinator  
- Client-delivery-coordinator  
- Campaign-services-manager  
- Project-management-office  
- Operations-orchestrator  

## What it produces  
- Resolution note with request summary, source records used, action taken, disposition, unresolved items, and next owner  
- Customer or stakeholder communication summary with approved facts, safe language, caveats, missing items, and next steps  
- Escalation package for exceptions, complaints, repeated failures, unsupported asks, confidentiality conflicts, approval conflicts, or adjacent-owner handoffs  
- Request-resolution summary with issue, evidence checked, decision surface, action taken, blocker state, and next owner  
- Exception communication escalation note for policy, service recovery, legal, editorial, financial, contractual, confidentiality, or approval conflicts  

## Risks and mitigations  
- The specialist refuses actions requiring legal, licensed, policy-override, financial-approval, safety-critical, editorial, privacy, contractual-signoff, or final client-acceptance authority unless explicitly in scope and supported by documented evidence.  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Source coverage includes agency service artifacts such as master service agreements, statements of work, client briefs, media plans, insertion orders, project plans, content assets, review notes, approvals, traffic records, and communication logs.  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- What the user or organization must supply before this specialist can work reliably:  
  - Access to client and engagement records  
  - Documented workflow state and approval rules  
  - Authority scope and escalation thresholds  
  - Client communication logs and issue tracking systems  
- What is explicitly out of scope:  
  - Overriding policy, pricing, eligibility, approval, scope, service recovery, refund, credit, compensation, or makegood limits  
  - Providing legal advice, advertising-claim substantiation, privacy-law interpretation, endorsement-disclosure approval, editorial approval, media strategy, budget approval, or contractual signoff  
  - Implying client acceptance, final creative approval, campaign launch approval, quality approval, or account-management commitment without explicit scope and documented evidence  
  - Disclosing client files, agency work product, audience data, performance data, unreleased content, pricing, platform credentials, or confidential communication outside approved engagement, recipient, channel, and tenant-security rules  
  - Representing stale, missing, or conflicting project, approval, delivery, billing, or communication records as final truth

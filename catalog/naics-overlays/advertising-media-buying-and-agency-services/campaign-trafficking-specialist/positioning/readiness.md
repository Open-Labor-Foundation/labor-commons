# Specialist Overview  
The campaign trafficking specialist coordinates the operational execution of digital advertising campaigns across trafficking, tracking, and launch processes. It ensures trafficking activities stay within defined boundaries and produce documented evidence for campaign setup, validation, and closeout.

## Who uses this  
- Campaign trafficker  
- Ad operations associate  
- Media operations coordinator  
- Agency operations specialist  
- Client delivery coordinator  
- Operations orchestrator  

## What it produces  
- Execution status update with campaign lifecycle stage, affected placements, blockers, owners, due dates, approvals, and next action  
- Campaign or creative workflow summary with approval state, required artifacts, QA status, client-impact notes, and evidence locations  
- Exception queue note for missing assets, malformed tags, tracking mismatch, invalid URL, creative spec mismatch, publisher rejection, pacing issue, discrepancy, confidentiality conflict, or approval blocker  
- Escalation note for deadline, approval, confidentiality, client-rights, legal, editorial, creative, media strategy, policy, financial, or contractual conflicts  
- Completion or fulfillment evidence record with setup record, QA evidence, launch proof, screenshots, delivery or closeout records, approval references, and retention location  

## Risks and mitigations  
- Refuses to approve claims, disclosures, editorial copy, targeting strategy, budget shifts, or client commitments unless explicitly permitted by tenant policy and supported by approval evidence  
- Refuses to traffic, launch, or close work when required artifacts (IO, creative approval, trafficking sheet, pixels, URLs, QA logs, etc.) are missing  
- Refuses to disclose or repurpose client data, platform credentials, or unreleased creative outside of approved engagement and confidentiality rules  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Source coverage includes IAB Technology Laboratory, Interactive Advertising Bureau, and 4A's standards  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- Requires presence of campaign brief, IO, placement grid, trafficking sheet, creative approvals, tracking artifacts, QA logs, and launch proof to function reliably  
- Does not provide legal, privacy, or regulatory interpretation  
- Does not approve creative, media strategy, targeting, budget moves, or final client acceptance  
- Does not collapse trafficking into generic project management or absorb adjacent legal, creative, or financial authority  
- Does not treat all ad formats (display, video, CTV, etc.) as interchangeable without tenant-qualified context

# Specialist Overview
The agency analytics specialist transforms raw data from advertising, media buying, and agency workflows into decision-ready analytical outputs. It defines metrics, reconciles sources, identifies performance issues, and prepares reports while ensuring boundaries around legal, editorial, media execution, compliance, and financial authority.

## Who uses this
- Agency analytics leads
- Media strategy and media buying teams
- Account teams
- Campaign operations and ad operations teams
- Client services teams
- Quality assurance and delivery management teams
- Marketing compliance reviewers

## What it produces
- Metric definition and data-source summary with denominator, attribution, source hierarchy, refresh, and caveat logic
- Agency analytics brief with metric definitions, source logic, source lineage, caveats, confidence posture, action thresholds, and review status
- Campaign performance, pacing, delivery, or funnel report with disclosure-safe narrative and unsupported-claim caveats
- Data-quality, denominator-ambiguity, disclosure-risk, privacy-risk, or source-conflict escalation note
- Boundary-safe handoff package for legal, editorial, quality assurance, delivery management, account, marketing compliance, media buying, campaign trafficking, ad operations, creative approvals, or finance owners

## Risks and mitigations
- Refuses to approve or infer authority for legal, editorial, media execution, financial, or contractual claims
- Requires explicit definitions and source validation before reporting
- Routes sensitive or out-of-scope claims to appropriate specialists or stakeholders
- Avoids publishing or certifying claims when metric definitions, source quality, or approvals are unresolved

## Validation
- 13 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1
- Covers Federal Trade Commission and Media Rating Council standards
- Human verification and functional audit completed

## Limitations
- Requires tenant facts, client scope, campaign scope, authoritative records, metric definitions, source context, and approval rules to proceed
- Cannot approve spend, change live campaigns, certify advertising claims, approve disclosures, override client scope, provide legal interpretation, or use restricted data without explicit authorization
- Cannot issue legal interpretations, advertising-law clearance, claim substantiation approval, or privacy-law advice
- Cannot approve creative, editorial claims, client-facing disclosures, contractual commitments, billing adjustments, or financial approvals
- Cannot publish or imply certainty when metric definitions, source quality, or signoff status are unresolved
- Cannot turn descriptive trends into causal, incrementality, forecast, or optimization claims without named records and approved methodology

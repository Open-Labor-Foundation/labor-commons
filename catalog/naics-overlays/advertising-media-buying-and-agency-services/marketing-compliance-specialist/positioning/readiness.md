# Specialist Overview  
The marketing compliance specialist translates advertising, media buying, and agency services compliance obligations into evidence-backed control summaries, gap summaries, exception notes, and escalation packets for marketing workflows. It ensures that campaign, channel, content, endorsement, disclosure, review, consumer-data, approval, and delivery obligations are mapped to required evidence and routed to the correct owners without overstepping governance boundaries.

## Who uses this  
- Marketing-compliance-manager  
- Agency-compliance-coordinator  
- Campaign-operations-manager  
- Content-review-coordinator  
- Media-operations-compliance-analyst  
- Client-delivery-manager  
- Operations-orchestrator  

## What it produces  
- Control or obligation matrix tied to campaign, channel, content, source, evidence owner, review cadence, and exception path  
- Evidence readiness or gap summary listing missing, stale, contradictory, or unretrieved records and the owners required to resolve them  
- Exception and remediation escalation note for control gaps, policy conflicts, approval blockers, source conflicts, or confidentiality conflicts  
- Control and obligation summary tied to evidence owners, systems of record, review cadence, and tenant-specific assumptions  
- Compliance review packet for legal, editorial, privacy, quality assurance, delivery, account, platform-policy, or contractual owners  

## Risks and mitigations  
- **Refusal rule for legal interpretation**: The specialist refuses to provide legal advice, regulatory interpretation, or final compliance certification, ensuring that only qualified legal specialists handle such decisions.  
- **Refusal rule for client acceptance**: It does not approve claims, disclosures, or platform exceptions without tenant authority, preventing premature or unapproved campaign launches.  
- **Refusal rule for data reuse**: It refuses to disclose or reuse client files, consumer data, or confidential materials outside approved security and engagement rules, protecting data confidentiality.  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Source coverage includes Federal Trade Commission, Electronic Code of Federal Regulations, and industry-standard advertising compliance frameworks  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- **User or organization must supply**:  
  - Tenant policies  
  - Campaign scope and systems of record  
  - Approval rules and confidentiality constraints  
  - Campaign artifacts, including claims grids, disclosure checklists, and substantiation files  
  - Evidence of review gates and required approvals  
- **What is explicitly out of scope**:  
  - Legal advice, regulatory negotiation, or formal compliance certification  
  - Approval of claims, disclosures, influencer work, or platform exceptions  
  - Approval of client commitments, spend, or final delivery  
  - Certification of a campaign as legally compliant  
  - Launching or closing a campaign without full evidence and approvals  
  - Treating incomplete or conflicting evidence as sufficient for compliance  
  - Interpreting privacy law or reconciling conflicting legal frameworks without human resolution

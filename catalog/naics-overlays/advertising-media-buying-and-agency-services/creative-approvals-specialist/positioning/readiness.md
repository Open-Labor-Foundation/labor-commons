# Creative approvals specialist  
Coordinates creative approval execution for advertising and agency work by maintaining campaign and asset state, approval evidence, review decisions, rework blockers, confidentiality checks, rights and compliance gates, and completion records while keeping legal, editorial, creative direction, contractual, media, financial, and final client approval authority with the correct owners.

## Who uses this  
- Creative-operations-coordinator  
- Agency-project-manager  
- Account-operations-associate  
- Production-coordinator  
- Ad-operations-coordinator  
- Delivery-coordinator  
- Operations-orchestrator  

## What it produces  
- Execution status update with lifecycle stage, approval state, blockers, owners, due dates, affected assets, and next action  
- Campaign or creative workflow summary with approvals, pending reviews, missing artifacts, rework needs, client-impact notes, and system-of-record references  
- Exception summary for blocked, returned, partially completed, confidentiality-conflicted, rights-conflicted, approval-conflicted, platform-rejected, or deadline-conflicted work  
- Escalation note for legal, editorial, claims, disclosure, rights, confidentiality, deadline, approval, client-impact, delivery, or contractual conflicts  
- Completion or fulfillment record showing what was done, which approval and delivery evidence supports closure, and where the record was retained  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Source coverage includes American Association of Advertising Agencies (4A's), Interactive Advertising Bureau / 4A's, IAB Technology Laboratory  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- **What the user or organization must supply before this specialist can work reliably**  
  - Validated tenant facts and source artifacts  
  - Approved engagement scope, SOW, and client authority  
  - Defined approver matrix and confidentiality constraints  
  - Rights requirements and channel specifications  
  - Systems of record for source context and approval rules  

- **What is explicitly out of scope**  
  - Providing legal advice, claims substantiation judgment, endorsement-disclosure approval, privacy-law interpretation, usage-rights interpretation, or regulatory signoff  
  - Approving final copy, creative concepts, creative direction, brand exception, media strategy, budget shift, scope exception, change order, production commitment, platform-policy override, client commitment, or final client acceptance  
  - Marking creative approved, delivery ready, complete, or closed without full evidence of brief, source asset, review, approval, legal or editorial clearance, rights, QA, delivery, proof, client-acceptance, or retention  
  - Disclosing, reusing, exporting, transmitting, showcasing, or repurposing client files, pitch work, agency work product, talent material, unreleased creative, audience data, performance data, or platform credentials outside engagement, consent, confidentiality, rights, and tenant-security rules  
  - Treating different creative approval workflows (social, display, video, CTV, audio, retail media, influencer, experiential, print, out-of-home, CRM, web, and PR) as interchangeable without tenant-qualified channel, format, and approval context

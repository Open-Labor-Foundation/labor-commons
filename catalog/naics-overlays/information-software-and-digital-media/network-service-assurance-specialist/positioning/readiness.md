# Specialist Overview  
The network service assurance specialist evaluates quality, availability, and performance evidence for hosted network, CDN, cloud access, and digital service delivery workflows. It determines whether to recommend release, hold, investigate, or escalate based on incident, service-test, and corrective-action data, without assuming engineering, legal, or final approval authority.

## Who uses this  
- Service assurance analyst  
- Network operations quality lead  
- Digital service delivery manager  
- Incident quality coordinator  
- Managed connectivity assurance lead  
- Reviewer  

## What it produces  
- Quality or safety status summary with criteria, evidence checked, current status, blockers, and recommended next owner  
- Hold, release, or nonconformance record with evidence references, risk rationale, missing support, and approval boundary statement  
- Corrective-action or incident escalation package with repeat-defect history, impact, evidence map, owner routing, and unresolved-risk note  
- Quality or assurance findings summary with control objective, observed condition, supporting records, gaps, disposition recommendation, and reviewer dependency  
- Corrective-action or unresolved-risk escalation note for formal QA, engineering, security, legal, account, regulatory, delivery, or customer-approver handling  

## Risks and mitigations  
- Refuses to release or clear a service when required tickets, telemetry, test, approval, review, customer, or corrective-action evidence is missing or contradictory  
- Does not disclose confidential customer topology, traffic, incident, vulnerability, subscriber, content-delivery, credential, contractual, or assurance records to unapproved recipients or channels  
- Does not perform or approve network configuration, routing, capacity, access, physical infrastructure, safety-critical, security, or remediation changes  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Source coverage includes tenant service model, customer commitment, product, jurisdiction, business unit, service identifiers, systems of record, confidentiality constraints, quality standards, approval flow, and current operating state  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- **What the user or organization must supply before this specialist can work reliably:**  
  - Minimum tenant facts  
  - Authoritative records  
  - Public source context  
  - Systems of record  
  - Approval constraints  

- **What is explicitly out of scope:**  
  - Bypassing hold, release, review, approval, safety, service-test, change, incident, confidentiality, retention, or customer-notification controls  
  - Implying legal, editorial, regulatory, financial, service-credit, contractual, licensed, engineering, security, or final QA signoff authority  
  - Certifying SLA breach, availability, regulatory reportability, contractual liability, public incident wording, customer credit, or content-rights position  
  - Closing a finding, incident, quality hold, nonconformance, corrective action, or release gate without required evidence, reviewer support, or approval record  
  - Disclosing confidential records to unapproved recipients or channels  
  - Performing or approving network configuration, routing, capacity, access, physical infrastructure, safety-critical, security, or remediation changes

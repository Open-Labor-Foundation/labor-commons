# Readiness Review

## Status

- Package: `network-service-assurance-specialist`
- Industry overlay: `information-software-and-digital-media`- Release version: `v0.1.0`
- Validation profile: `standard`

## Readiness Finding

The spec pack is ready for validated specification-based use. It defines a
network-specific quality and assurance lane that evaluates whether service
quality, availability, performance, service-test, incident, review, approval,
and corrective-action evidence supports hold, release recommendation,
investigation, nonconformance, or escalation.

The pack does not claim a retained implementation. It may be used as a specification-based
catalog package until a separate trusted build is designed, validated, and
committed.

## Evidence Reviewed

- `manifest.yaml` records the runtime strategy, delivery target, semantic
  definition, specialty boundary, source baseline, validation gates, and SaaS
  readiness notes.
- `evaluation/research-summary.json` records the research contract, source
  classes, workflow stages, artifacts, systems of record, decision boundaries,
  domain failure modes, scenario family coverage, and nonblocking ambiguity
  treatment.
- `evaluation/scenarios.md` defines 12 scenarios spanning normal in-scope
  work, hold/release boundaries, insufficient evidence, recurring incidents,
  confidentiality conflicts, authority refusals, source conflicts, and tenant
  adaptation.
- `evaluation/functionality-map.json` maps every required shared,
  quality-safety-and-assurance, and professional-and-information-services
  ability to passing scenario IDs.
- `evaluation/results.json` records 12 passing scenarios, 100 percent pass
  rate, and complete functionality coverage.
- `readiness/evidence.json` records human verification, structured readiness
  evidence, deployment readiness, commercialization readiness, and no
  uncovered abilities.

## Control Objectives

- Network service assurance outputs must be tied to tickets, incidents,
  problems, alarms, telemetry, service tests, SAT records, review notes,
  approvals, customer commitments, and corrective-action evidence.
- Hold, release, nonconformance, and corrective-action recommendations must
  state missing evidence, contradictory records, unresolved risk, and approval
  boundaries.
- The lane must route work requiring engineering, NOC command, security,
  formal QA, legal, editorial, regulatory, financial, contractual, customer,
  or executive authority.
- Confidential customer topology, traffic, incident, vulnerability,
  subscriber, credential, content-delivery, assurance, and contractual records
  must not be disclosed to unapproved recipients or channels.

## Residual Risk

Tenant-specific SLO/SLA definitions, quality thresholds, approval matrices,
jurisdictional outage duties, source-of-truth precedence, and customer
commitments materially affect safe action. The pack treats these as required
runtime facts and requires refusal or orchestrator return when they are
missing or conflicting.

## Release Decision

Approved for validated spec-pack inclusion as a `specification-based` package.
retained-implementation promotion requires a separate implementation, tenant retrieval
design, runtime audit logging, expanded validation, and explicit catalog
promotion decision.

# Specialist Overview  
The Connectivity Service Delivery Specialist resolves and routes connectivity service tickets in the Information, Software, and Digital Media industry. It manages colocation, interconnection, carrier, CDN, hosted-service access, and managed connectivity support after intake, using industry-specific terminology and tools to ensure service-level compliance and accurate escalation.

## Who uses this  
- Connectivity service delivery coordinators  
- Network operations center (NOC) analysts  
- Colocation and interconnection service desks  
- Cloud connectivity delivery managers  
- Digital media operations support teams  
- Reviewers  

## What it produces  
- Resolution note with request summary, service identifiers, evidence checked, action taken, customer impact, unresolved items, and next owner  
- Customer or stakeholder communication summary with approved wording, safe caveats, service-status language, and communication-log update  
- Escalation package for exceptions, complaints, unsupported asks, repeat failures, confidentiality conflicts, or policy conflicts  
- Service operations summary with environment, connectivity, dependency, owner, and customer-impact status  
- Escalation note for outage, access, regulatory-reporting, security, legal, editorial, or contract-boundary issues  

## Risks and mitigations  
- Refuses to close tickets without appropriate evidence such as service-order, ticket, telemetry, test, or customer-confirmation data  
- Does not approve engineering design, BGP changes, security exceptions, or safety actions  
- Does not disclose confidential customer topology, traffic, access, credentials, or incident details to unapproved recipients  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Source coverage includes service inventory, DCIM, NMS, ticketing, CRM, and document records  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
**What the user or organization must supply:**  
- Authoritative ticket, CRM, service inventory, DCIM, NMS, and document records  
- Minimum tenant facts and service context before resolution  
- Approved communication templates and service-level boundaries  

**What is explicitly out of scope:**  
- Approving engineering design, BGP policy, firewall or security exceptions, or physical access exceptions  
- Closing a service request without required evidence or customer confirmation  
- Disclosing confidential customer records or data to unapproved channels  
- Adjudicating legal, editorial, regulatory, financial, or contractual matters  
- Overriding policy, pricing, entitlement, or service-level limits to resolve a case

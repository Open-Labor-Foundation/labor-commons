# Specialist Overview  
The platform operations specialist supports the usability and auditability of information, software, and digital media platform workflows. They triage incidents, validate access and change readiness, manage interface dependencies, and track completion evidence while maintaining clear boundaries with legal, editorial, security, engineering, and financial authority.

## Who uses this  
- Platform operations specialist  
- Software operations analyst  
- Digital platform operations coordinator  
- Content platform operations coordinator  
- Service delivery operations lead  
- Operations orchestrator  

## What it produces  
- Execution status update with lifecycle stage, affected platform or workflow, impact, blockers, owner, approval state, evidence references, and next action  
- System incident or service summary with severity, affected component, client or subscriber impact, evidence, current owner, recovery state, and escalation action  
- Exception queue note for missing prerequisites, failed jobs, API errors, connector failures, access conflicts, content workflow blockers, approval gaps, source conflicts, or confidentiality conflicts  
- Change-readiness or integration-risk escalation note with test evidence, rollback evidence, dependency map, owner approvals, approval gaps, and required owner decision  
- Completion or fulfillment evidence record showing action taken, validation evidence, approval references, communication record, system-of-record links, retention location, and unresolved follow-up  

## Risks and mitigations  
- Explicit refusal rules are in place for approving legal claims, editorial content, privacy-law interpretations, production engineering changes, financial credits, contractual exceptions, attestation conclusions, or final client acceptance. Work is always routed to the correct owner when authority shifts.

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Covers U.S. Census Bureau, ISO/IEC, PeopleCert / AXELOS, and Google authority sources  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- What the user or organization must supply before this specialist can work reliably:  
  - Tenant-specific policies and approval rules  
  - Systems-of-record retrieval hooks  
  - Evidence of test results, rollback plans, and client communication  
  - Access entitlement and change-window information  
- What is explicitly out of scope:  
  - Approving legal, editorial, security, engineering, privacy, financial, contractual, attestation, or final client-signoff decisions  
  - Granting access or marking work completed without full evidence and approvals  
  - Disclosing or repurposing client files, subscriber data, or platform credentials outside approved rules  
  - Approving content publication, access grants, or change execution without tenant policy and evidence  
  - Collapsing platform operations into generic IT support or absorbing adjacent authority

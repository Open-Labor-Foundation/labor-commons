# Specialist Overview  
The digital publishing operations specialist manages digital publishing workflows by tracking and reporting on content, metadata, and system states, and by ensuring that publishing actions proceed only when supported by traceable evidence and system records. They focus on operational clarity, change-readiness, and issue resolution without overstepping into editorial, legal, or technical authority.

## Who uses this  
- Digital-publishing-operations-manager  
- Publishing-operations-coordinator  
- Content-operations-specialist  
- Metadata-operations-specialist  
- Platform-operations-coordinator  
- Delivery-operations-manager  
- Operations-orchestrator  

## What it produces  
- Execution status update with lifecycle stage and next action  
- Completion or fulfillment record showing what was done and what evidence supports closure  
- Exception summary for blocked, returned, or partially completed work  
- System incident or service summary with impact and owner  
- Change-readiness or integration-risk escalation note  

## Risks and mitigations  
- Explicit refusal to approve editorial content, legal claims, rights clearance, accessibility certification, or final publication without required evidence or authority  
- Explicit refusal to publish, schedule, or close work without traceable source records  
- Explicit refusal to disclose unpublished content or subscriber data outside documented engagement and consent  
- Explicit refusal to treat different publishing formats as interchangeable without tenant-specific qualification  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Source coverage includes World Wide Web Consortium, EDItEUR, and other industry standards  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- What the user or organization must supply before this specialist can work reliably:  
  - Tenant service model, engagement scope, and client commitments  
  - Content identifiers, version state, and approval rules  
  - Systems of record for content, metadata, access, and rights  
  - QA and accessibility evidence for published artifacts  
  - Legal, editorial, and rights clearance status  
- What is explicitly out of scope:  
  - Approving editorial substance, legal claims, or final client acceptance  
  - Certifying accessibility conformance or resolving security exceptions  
  - Approving engineering fixes, financial concessions, or contractual terms  
  - Publishing, syndicating, or disclosing unpublished content without documented consent  
  - Treating different publication formats as interchangeable without tenant-qualified context

# Specialist Overview  
The information product analytics specialist translates records from digital media, software, and information products into analysis packages with clear metric logic, source lineage, caveats, and action thresholds. This specialist ensures transparency and accuracy in product usage, subscriber behavior, content performance, and experimentation data, while stopping short of making strategic, editorial, legal, or financial decisions.

## Who uses this  
- Product analytics lead  
- Digital product operations analyst  
- Subscriber analytics manager  
- Content performance analyst  
- Client delivery analyst  
- Operations orchestrator  

## What it produces  
- Metric definition and data-source summary with denominator, exclusions, source hierarchy, lineage, refresh posture, audience, cadence, caveats, and confidence note  
- Information-product analytics brief with product, subscriber, account, content, experiment, funnel, retention, engagement, or feature-adoption metrics tied to named evidence  
- Caveat or reconciliation note for stale, incomplete, conflicting, denominator-ambiguous, confidential, or approval-sensitive records  
- Data-quality or disclosure-risk escalation note with blocked claims, missing records, affected outputs, required owner decision, and safe next evidence request  
- Boundary-safe handoff package for legal, editorial, quality assurance, delivery management, account, product management, data governance, privacy, security, finance, or contractual owners  

## Risks and mitigations  
- **Risk**: Publishing analysis with unresolved source quality, metric definitions, or approval status.  
  **Mitigation**: The specialist refuses to produce or distribute outputs until source lineage, metric logic, and approval constraints are clear and documented.  
- **Risk**: Converting descriptive trends into unsupported causal or policy claims.  
  **Mitigation**: Outputs remain strictly descriptive and include explicit caveats against causal or forecasting interpretations.  
- **Risk**: Unauthorized disclosure of client, subscriber, or experiment data.  
  **Mitigation**: All outputs include confidentiality constraints and are routed within approved tenant and data-security boundaries.  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Source coverage includes telemetry schema, event taxonomy, subscription billing, entitlement, CRM, support, content management, experimentation, and dashboard records  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- **What must be supplied**:  
  - Validated tenant policies, product scope, and confidentiality constraints  
  - Metric dictionary, event taxonomy, and source hierarchy  
  - Approval rules and retrieval hooks for systems-of-record  
- **What is out of scope**:  
  - Approving roadmap, pricing, editorial, legal, privacy, contractual, or financial decisions  
  - Publishing or implying certainty when source quality, signoff, or lineage is unresolved  
  - Converting descriptive trends into causal claims, forecasts, or policy recommendations  
  - Disclosing confidential data outside approved engagement and privacy rules  
  - Collapsing analytics into generic reporting or absorbing adjacent legal, editorial, or product authority

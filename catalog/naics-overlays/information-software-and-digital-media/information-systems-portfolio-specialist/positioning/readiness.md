# Specialist Overview
The Information Systems Portfolio Specialist coordinates information systems portfolio oversight by converting demand, constraints, and documented priorities into recommendation-only sequencing, allocation, and workload tradeoff packages for application, platform, and system portfolios. It supports planning and coordination without final approval, legal, or editorial authority.

## Who uses this
- Information systems portfolio managers
- Software business operations leads
- Digital media operations planners
- Delivery management teams
- Product operations coordinators
- Reviewers of recommendation and escalation packages

## What it produces
- Schedule, capacity, or allocation recommendation with ranked options, assumptions, tradeoffs, and downstream impact
- Constraint log capturing assumptions, authoritative records, missing facts, dependencies, conflicts, and unresolved blockers
- Approval-needed summary for tradeoffs outside lane authority with affected obligations, owners, and requested decision
- Allocation or prioritization recommendation with constraints, confidence posture, source references, and no final-approval language
- Capacity-risk escalation note for over-demand, stale forecasts, source conflicts, confidentiality blockers, or approval gaps
- Adjacent specialist routing package with handoff owner, evidence map, and communication constraints

## Risks and mitigations
- **Risk:** Recommending without complete tenant records or approval rules could lead to invalid prioritization.  
  **Mitigation:** Refuses to rank or recommend when required tenant facts, approval rules, or source context are missing or conflicting.
- **Risk:** Inadvertent disclosure of confidential records.  
  **Mitigation:** Explicitly refuses to disclose confidential client files, engagement records, content assets, or system maps to unapproved recipients.
- **Risk:** Misinterpreting legal or regulatory obligations.  
  **Mitigation:** Routes legal, privacy, or attestation decisions to the legal specialist and does not imply authority in those areas.

## Validation
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1
- Source coverage includes U.S. Census Bureau, ISO, IEC/ISO, and PMI standards
- Human verification and functional audit completed

## Limitations
- **Must be supplied by user or organization:**
  - Valid tenant records and approval rules
  - Authoritative system-of-record data
  - Approved tie-break rules and source context
  - Confirmed ownership of obligations for routing
- **Out of scope:**
  - Final budget, staffing, investment, release, product, contractual, client, or operational approval
  - Legal, editorial, financial, security, engineering, attestation, contractual, or client-rights authority
  - Deciding product strategy, investment funding, contractual waivers, service-credit outcomes, content rights, editorial acceptability, or professional judgment
  - Executing system changes, release trains, backlog moves, staffing assignments, vendor commitments, customer communications, or contract changes
  - Ranking demand when required records or approval owners are missing or conflicting

# Specialist Overview  
The customer platform support specialist resolves stakeholder-facing platform support requests for software, subscription platforms, digital media services, and information systems. The specialist ensures accurate classification, communication, and escalation while maintaining strict boundaries from legal, editorial, privacy, security, and financial authority.

## Who uses this  
- Customer platform support teams  
- Software support operations leads  
- Digital media support managers  
- SaaS customer operations teams  
- Service delivery managers  
- Workflow orchestrators  

## What it produces  
- Resolution note with request summary, product or content context, action taken, evidence used, customer-facing disposition, unresolved items, and next owner  
- Customer or stakeholder communication summary with approved wording, source citations, caveats, blockers, commitments not made, and communication-log update  
- Request-resolution summary with issue, affected platform or content area, case IDs, action taken, status, evidence links, and next owner  
- Escalation package for exceptions, complaints, unsupported workarounds, repeat failures, confidential handling conflicts, content workflow blockers, or authority-sensitive requests  
- Exception communication escalation note that explains the policy, contractual, confidentiality, professional-judgment, content, or authority boundary that prevents direct resolution  

## Risks and mitigations  
- **Risk**: Misclassifying requests or failing to escalate authority-sensitive issues could lead to legal, privacy, or content policy violations.  
- **Mitigation**: Requires strict validation of entitlement, policy, and system-of-record data before action, and documented escalation paths for out-of-scope requests.  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Source coverage includes documented service rules, product support runbooks, knowledge-base articles, status-page facts, incident updates, subscription records, content asset metadata, CRM history, and escalation matrices  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- **What the user or organization must supply**:  
  - Minimum tenant facts  
  - Customer identity context  
  - Entitlement or service-plan records  
  - Product or content area  
  - Case history  
  - Authoritative runbook  
  - Source-system records  
  - Approved support messaging  
- **What is explicitly out of scope**:  
  - Overriding policy, pricing, eligibility, SLA, refund, credit, compensation, content, privacy, security, or contractual limits  
  - Approving or denying content takedown, editorial publication, account termination, or production fixes  
  - Disclosing confidential records to unapproved recipients  
  - Closing cases without complete source-system evidence or approval  
  - Substituting unsupported customer assertions for system-of-record data

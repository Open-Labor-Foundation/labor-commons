# Specialist Overview  
The Broadcast Operations Specialist manages the end-to-end execution of broadcast workflows, ensuring schedules, content, rights, and operational dependencies are tracked and fulfilled with evidence-backed completion. This specialist focuses on the operational integrity of broadcast workflows without overstepping into legal, editorial, or engineering certification.  

## Who uses this  
- Broadcast operations coordinators  
- Traffic and continuity teams  
- Master-control and playout operations leads  
- Production operations managers  
- Media delivery and station operations teams  

## What it produces  
- Execution status update with lifecycle stage, evidence used, blockers, owner, and next action  
- Completion or fulfillment record with work performed, as-run or delivery evidence, QC and approval references, open exceptions, and closure rationale  
- Exception queue note for blocked, returned, rework, or partially completed broadcast operations work  
- Audience, talent, venue, or production workflow summary with readiness, dependencies, owner status, and schedule impact  
- Escalation note for schedule, rights, sponsorship, public-file, EAS, editorial, QC, confidentiality, venue-impact, or contractual conflicts  

## Risks and mitigations  
- Refuses to approve airworthiness, legal rights, or FCC compliance, ensuring no operational decisions are mistaken for regulatory or legal certification  
- Requires system-of-record evidence for all lifecycle transitions to prevent premature closure of workflows  
- Enforces confidentiality and embargo rules to avoid unauthorized disclosure of pre-release or sensitive content  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Source coverage includes 10 — Electronic Code of Federal Regulations and industry-standard broadcast workflow systems  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- Requires station or channel context, schedule records, traffic instructions, rights-window evidence, content assets, approvals, and source context before work can proceed  
- Explicitly out of scope:  
  - Approving legal rights, talent contracts, sponsorship identification, political file sufficiency, copyright clearance, editorial judgment, or FCC compliance interpretations  
  - Certifying engineering conformance, safety-critical live production decisions, final QC waivers, financial approvals, contractual acceptance, or executive schedule overrides  
  - Disclosing embargoed content, pre-release media, talent personal information, or confidential client records through unapproved recipients or channels  
  - Approving airworthiness, technical conformance, EAS compliance, loudness waiver, caption waiver, or engineering release  
  - Overriding traffic, schedule, makegood, delivery, confidentiality, retention, or client-commitment rules to clear an execution queue

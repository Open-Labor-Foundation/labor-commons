# Specialist Overview  
The information compliance specialist translates privacy, security, content, and digital-service obligations into evidence-backed compliance handling, monitoring, and escalation within software, digital media, and information operations. It focuses on governance support for control mapping, evidence readiness, and inter-specialist routing.

## Who uses this  
- Information-compliance-analyst  
- Software-compliance-coordinator  
- Digital-service-governance-specialist  
- Content-operations-compliance-coordinator  
- Client-delivery-compliance-manager  
- Privacy-and-security-program-coordinator  
- Operations-orchestrator  

## What it produces  
- Control or obligation matrix tied to source, product or content workflow, evidence owner, system of record, review cadence, exception path, and tenant-specific assumption  
- Evidence readiness or gap summary listing missing, stale, contradictory, unretrieved, or insufficient records and the owners required to resolve them  
- Exception and remediation escalation note for control gaps, policy conflicts, source conflicts, confidentiality conflicts, approval blockers, or legal, editorial, privacy, security, accessibility, copyright, contractual, or client-rights boundaries  
- Control and obligation summary tied to evidence owners, systems of record, retrieval dependencies, tenant policy, public source baseline, and closeout criteria  
- Review-preparation or handoff packet for legal, editorial, privacy, security, engineering, quality assurance, accessibility, copyright, delivery, account, finance, contractual, or attestation owners  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Source coverage includes NIST standards and tenant-specific governance artifacts  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
### What the user or organization must supply before this specialist can work reliably  
- Tenant policies, service model, jurisdiction, product or program scope, client commitments, confidentiality constraints, approval rules, source hierarchy, systems of record, and retrieval hooks  
- Complete and accessible source records, including control evidence, release evidence, incident records, rights metadata, approval history, and confidentiality clearance  

### What is explicitly out of scope  
- Providing legal advice, legal interpretation, regulator negotiation, regulator-facing wording, privacy-law posture, copyright legal judgment, accessibility legal conclusion, formal certification, attestation conclusion, or final compliance signoff  
- Approving editorial content, user-facing product changes, source code changes, security exceptions, privacy exceptions, accessibility exceptions, content-rights determinations, data-retention exceptions, client commitments, financial approvals, contractual exceptions, or final client acceptance without explicit tenant authority  
- Marking a control satisfied, release approved, incident closed, content cleared, service deliverable accepted, or compliance work complete when required tenant facts, authoritative source context, system records, evidence, approvals, remediation proof, communication records, confidentiality clearance, or retention records are missing  
- Disclosing, exporting, reusing, training on, publishing, or transmitting client files, subscriber data, content assets, credentials, vulnerability details, incident records, source records, or confidential deliverables outside approved engagement, recipient, channel, purpose, and tenant-security rules  
- Treating privacy, security, accessibility, copyright, records, content, client-delivery, software-release, incident, and service-control workflows as interchangeable without tenant-qualified jurisdiction, product, data, content, platform, approval, and evidence context

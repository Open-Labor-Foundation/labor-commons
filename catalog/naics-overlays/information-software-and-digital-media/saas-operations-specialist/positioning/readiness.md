# Specialist Overview  
The SaaS operations specialist executes and manages SaaS operations workflows for hosted-service environments, including connectivity, environment readiness, service activation, runbook fulfillment, and completion-evidence recording after service or tenant records exist.

## Who uses this  
- SaaS operations coordinators  
- Service delivery operations teams  
- NOC and hosted-service operations analysts  
- Release and environment coordinators  
- Digital media operations teams  
- Workflow orchestrators  
- Spec-pack reviewers  

## What it produces  
- Execution status update with lifecycle stage, tenant, environment, service, dependency, evidence state, blocker, next action, and owner  
- Completion or fulfillment record with work performed, systems checked, evidence used, review gates, approval references, residual risks, customer-impact caveats, and closure support  
- Exception summary for blocked, returned, partially completed, failed, unsupported, stale, or rework-required SaaS operations work  
- Service operations summary with environment, connectivity, dependency, customer-impact, service owner, provider, and support status  
- Escalation note for outage, access, confidentiality, privacy, content workflow, security, quality, carrier, facility, contract-boundary, or authority-sensitive issues  

## Risks and mitigations  
- The specialist refuses to approve legal, editorial, contractual, privacy-rights, service-credit, refund, financial, regulated outage filing, safety-critical, security, engineering, production-design, policy-override, or executive decisions  
- The specialist refuses to mark work complete when required evidence such as ticket, service-order, monitoring, test, deployment, change, customer confirmation, content approval, or contractual records are missing or conflicting  
- The specialist refuses to disclose customer topology, subscriber data, incident detail, access records, logs, credentials, vulnerability details, content workflow records, or confidential client commitments to unapproved recipients or channels  
- The specialist refuses to collapse SaaS operations into generic support, product management, engineering, account management, legal, editorial, quality signoff, or complete service-owner authority  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Covers SaaS and hosted-service operations in the Information, Software, and Digital Media industry overlay  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- What the user or organization must supply before this specialist can work reliably:  
  - Valid tenant, service, or delivery record  
  - Evidence from ticketing, CRM, project, document, content, delivery, CMDB, monitoring, or approval systems  
  - Minimum tenant facts, service context, and retrieval hooks  
- What is explicitly out of scope:  
  - Approving legal, editorial, contractual, privacy-rights, service-credit, refund, financial, regulated outage filing, safety-critical, security, engineering, production-design, policy-override, or executive decisions  
  - Closing work when required evidence is missing or conflicting  
  - Disclosing confidential customer, subscriber, or content records  
  - Overriding service policy, access, security, change, maintenance, entitlement, SLA, content, privacy, quality, release, or contractual limits to complete work  
  - Performing network design, BGP policy, firewall exceptions, WAF policy design, database changes, software deployment design, production code changes, architecture decisions, or safety-critical facility actions

# Deployment Package

## Runtime and Deployment Profile

- Runtime strategy: `specification-based`
- Target delivery level: `validated`
- Runtime promotion condition: promote to retained-implementation only after explicit governance signoff, sustained usage, and repeated validation stability.
- Execution target: `commons-crew`

## Materialization Requirements

### Required Task and Boundary Metadata

- `manifest.yaml` with explicit SaaS operations execution boundaries, lifecycle states, refusal triggers, confidentiality controls, completion evidence, and adjacent-owner handoff rules.
- `evaluation/functionality-map.json` covering shared + execution-processing-and-fulfillment + professional-and-information-services abilities.
- `evaluation/research-summary.json` with authoritative sources, workflow stages, artifacts, systems of record, decision boundaries, ambiguity handling, and domain failure modes.
- `evaluation/results.json` and `readiness/evidence.json` showing complete functional coverage and validated readiness gates.

### Runtime Assumptions

- Tenant must supply service model, product scope, environment topology, support tiers, SLO or SLA commitments, customer commitments, confidentiality limits, jurisdiction, approval matrix, quality gates, and escalation matrix.
- Tenant must supply service catalog, runbooks, change policy, maintenance policy, completion criteria, communication templates, source-of-truth precedence, and authority boundaries.
- Tenant must define where SaaS operations stops and legal, editorial, quality assurance, delivery management, account, security, privacy, network engineering, software engineering, product, incident command, service-credit, billing, carrier, site operations, or customer-approval ownership begins.
- Tenant must provide retrieval hooks for project management, CRM, ticketing, document management, content, delivery, CMDB, DCIM, monitoring, observability, incident, status-page, approval, communication, contract, privacy, and security evidence systems.

### Required Retrieval Dependencies

- SaaS operations tickets, project tasks, service orders, implementation plans, change records, maintenance windows, incident references, problem records, and lifecycle state.
- Customer, tenant, account, workspace, subscription, entitlement, product, environment, region, facility, content asset, and approval records.
- Runbooks, service catalog entries, service model, quality gates, approval records, communication templates, contract evidence, confidentiality labels, and source-of-truth maps.
- Health checks, synthetic tests, deployment logs, activation tests, monitoring telemetry, observability alerts, status-page records, incident bridge notes, NOC handoffs, audit logs, access logs, and customer confirmations.
- CMDB, DCIM, cloud, DNS, CDN, WAF, edge, carrier, cross-connect, LOA/CFA, circuit, cloud on-ramp, provider ticket, and service dependency records.
- Content asset IDs, CMS state, publishing queue, moderation or review notes, takedown or counter-notice workflow status, editorial approvals, localization state, CDN purge records, and QA playback checks.

### Regulated and Professional-Lane Caveats

- The lane may organize and summarize SaaS operations evidence but may not provide legal advice, editorial judgment, privacy-rights determinations, regulated outage filing decisions, security incident command, engineering approval, quality signoff, service-credit approval, refund approval, or contractual approval.
- The lane may state operational status only when supported by source records and tenant-approved messaging; it may not certify availability, uptime, SOC, privacy, content, or contract satisfaction claims without authorized review.
- The lane may identify possible outage, privacy, content, security, customer-rights, or contractual escalation needs but may not adjudicate them.
- Client, subscriber, tenant, topology, incident, access, log, credential, vulnerability, content, and contract records must remain tenant-scoped and must use approved systems and delivery channels.

### Refusal and Return Conditions

- Required tenant facts, source records, retrieval hooks, owner maps, or approval gates materially change execution, communication, escalation, or completion.
- Public-source guidance, tenant policy, contract terms, incident communications, content workflow rules, privacy constraints, or approval records conflict without explicit precedence resolution.
- The request asks for legal, licensed, editorial, privacy, regulated-filing, security, engineering, product, financial-approval, service-credit, refund, quality-signoff, policy-override, executive, or contractual authority outside the lane.
- Work is asked to complete without ticket, service-order, runbook, change, health-check, synthetic-test, activation-test, monitoring, QA, content, approval, customer-confirmation, or communication-log evidence required by the tenant service model.
- Confidentiality, client-record handling, approved recipient, channel, retention, or delivery controls would be violated.

## Deployment Notes

- Deploy as a spec-pack overlay only. No retained implementation is claimed by this package.
- Route tasks only when the orchestrator has a tenant, service model, environment, service record, source-system retrieval map, and approval context.
- Configure the orchestrator to expect the required output shapes: execution status update, completion or fulfillment record, exception summary, service operations summary, and escalation note.
- Require retrieval failure handling to create a hold or orchestrator return rather than a partial completion.

## Rollback and Recovery

- Pause tenant routing for the lane if repeated unsupported completion, missing-evidence closure, confidentiality incidents, authority overreach, source conflicts, or poor dependency routing are observed.
- Preserve ticket, service-order, runbook, monitoring, activation, QA, content, approval, communication, and escalation evidence for rollback review.
- Re-enable only after retrieval dependencies, source precedence, approval owner maps, confidentiality controls, completion criteria, and adjacent-lane routing are corrected and revalidated.

## retained-implementation Promotion Plan

- Keep runtime `specification-based` until a separate retained-implementation package is explicitly approved and committed.
- Promote only if usage proves common enough to justify maintained build logic and repeated validation proves stable over time.
- Promotion requires governance signoff, updated readiness evidence, tenant safety checks, source refresh review, and a distinct build package that preserves the same refusal, confidentiality, approval, lifecycle, and completion-evidence controls.

# Specialist Overview  
An advertising technology operations specialist manages the operational workflows of advertising technology systems. They triage incidents, validate access and change readiness, track dependencies, and ensure workflows are auditable and reliable without crossing into legal, editorial, security, or financial approval domains.

## Who uses this  
- Ad-technology-operations-analyst  
- Ad-platform-operations-specialist  
- Digital-media-operations-coordinator  
- Publisher-operations-specialist  
- Client-delivery-coordinator  
- Operations-orchestrator  

## What it produces  
- Execution status update with lifecycle stage, affected platform or interface, impact, blockers, owner, approval state, and next action  
- System incident or service summary with severity, affected ad-tech component, client impact, evidence, current owner, and recovery or escalation action  
- Exception queue note for missing prerequisites, failed tags, VAST errors, API errors, access conflicts, supply-chain mismatch, consent-signal gap, approval blocker, or confidentiality conflict  
- Change-readiness or integration-risk escalation note with test evidence, rollback evidence, dependencies, approval gaps, and required owner decision  
- Completion or fulfillment evidence record showing action taken, validation evidence, approval references, communication record, system-of-record links, and retention location  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Source coverage includes IAB Technology Laboratory standards and ad-technology operational best practices  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- What the user or organization must supply before this specialist can work reliably  
  - Tenant-specific policies and approval matrices  
  - Systems-of-record retrieval hooks  
  - Evidence of client authority and confidentiality constraints  
  - Interface dependency context and rollback plans  
  - Incident severity classifications and client impact assessments  
- What is explicitly out of scope  
  - Providing legal advice, privacy-law interpretation, or regulatory signoff  
  - Approving editorial content, creative concepts, or bid strategies  
  - Granting access or approving changes without required evidence or approvals  
  - Disclosing or repurposing client data outside engagement and confidentiality rules  
  - Treating ad-tech systems as interchangeable without tenant-qualified context  

## Risks and mitigations  
- Refuses to approve legal, editorial, security, or financial decisions unless tenant policy explicitly grants authority and approval evidence is present  
- Requires rollback plans and test evidence before approving changes to ad-tech systems  
- Escalates work to legal, security, or quality assurance specialists when required authority is absent or evidence is incomplete

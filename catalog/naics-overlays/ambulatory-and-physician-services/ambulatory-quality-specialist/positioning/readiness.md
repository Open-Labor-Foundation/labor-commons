# Specialist Overview
The ambulatory quality specialist helps ambulatory surgery centers, physician groups, and outpatient clinics assess and document quality, safety, and regulatory readiness using sector-specific records and workflows. It evaluates evidence from quality reporting, patient experience, infection control, and incident logs to produce bounded quality status summaries, corrective-action escalations, and readiness findings without crossing into clinical judgment or final regulatory authority.

## Who uses this
- Ambulatory quality director  
- Physician practice administrator  
- Ambulatory surgery center administrator  
- Quality improvement manager  
- Infection prevention lead  
- Patient safety or risk lead  
- Reviewer  

## What it produces
- Quality or safety status summary  
- Hold, release, or nonconformance record with evidence references  
- Corrective-action or incident escalation package  
- Quality or assurance findings summary  
- Corrective-action or unresolved-risk escalation note  

## Risks and mitigations
- Rejects requests for clinical judgment, legal interpretation, or final attestation to avoid overstepping authority  
- Escalates to human reviewers or adjacent specialists when licensed clinical, regulatory, or privacy-sensitive decisions are needed  
- Maintains strict boundaries around safety and readiness to prevent unsupported reopening or control bypasses  

## Validation
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Covers key ambulatory quality sources including CMS, eCQM, ASC Conditions for Coverage, and QAPI records  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations
- Requires complete or accessible service-setting, reporting-program, and evidence prerequisites before producing outputs  
- Cannot bypass hold, release, or safety-control boundaries without explicit authority  
- Cannot interpret legal reportability, privacy law, or accreditation obligations  
- Cannot make clinical judgments or determine patient-specific care appropriateness  
- Cannot submit or certify final CMS, QPP, ASCQR, accreditor, or survey attestations  
- Cannot act as peer-review, risk-management, or compliance counsel

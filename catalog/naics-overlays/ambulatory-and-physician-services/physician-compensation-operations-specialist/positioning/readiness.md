# Specialist Overview  
The physician compensation operations specialist manages physician compensation workflows within the ambulatory and physician services industry. This specialist ensures compensation cycles advance using documented plan rules, provider rosters, professional-claims evidence, and approval checkpoints, without drifting into legal, HR, or compliance design work.

## Who uses this  
- Physician compensation operations managers  
- Faculty practice compensation administrators  
- Medical group compensation analysts  
- Provider payroll coordinators  
- Revenue cycle operations reviewers  
- Service line or department administrators  
- Finance and physician enterprise reviewers  

## What it produces  
- Execution status update with lifecycle stage and next action  
- Completion or fulfillment record showing what was done and what evidence supports closure  
- Exception summary for blocked, returned, or partially completed work  
- Physician compensation cycle summary with readiness, owner status, and evidence posture  
- Escalation note for attribution, compliance, approval, or records conflicts  

## Risks and mitigations  
- Actions requiring legal, executive, or compliance authority are explicitly refused to prevent unauthorized decisions or overrides.  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Source coverage includes Centers for Medicare & Medicaid Services  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- What the user or organization must supply before this specialist can work reliably  
  - Valid physician employment agreements  
  - Active compensation plan appendices  
  - Provider master records with NPI and department rosters  
  - Schedule and call-coverage records  
  - Encounter and claim extracts  
  - Medicare Physician Fee Schedule work RVU references  
  - Incident-to and teaching-physician status records  
  - Split or shared attribution flags  
  - Collections and adjustment reports  
  - Quality-score feeds  
  - Draw or holdback trackers  
  - Approval matrices  
  - Payroll interfaces  
  - Compensation statements  

- What is explicitly out of scope  
  - Designing or renegotiating physician compensation plans, contracts, guarantees, or call-pay methodologies  
  - Determining fair market value or commercial reasonableness  
  - Interpreting Stark or Anti-Kickback law  
  - Altering coding or claim-submission facts  
  - Approving payroll release, off-cycle payouts, or manual overrides  
  - Authorizing contract amendments or executive exceptions  
  - Treating physician compensation as generic HR or finance work

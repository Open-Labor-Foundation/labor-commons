# Specialist Overview
The clinic performance analytics specialist translates ambulatory clinic, physician-practice, and outpatient records into structured metric definitions, analytical briefs, and risk disclosures. This role focuses on clinic performance data in the Ambulatory and Physician Services industry overlay, ensuring analysis remains within workflow-shaping, evidence-backed boundaries.

## Who uses this
- Physician enterprise performance manager  
- Ambulatory quality reporting lead  
- Clinic operations director  
- Practice administrator  
- Ambulatory access manager  
- Executive report reviewer  

## What it produces
- Metric definition and data-source summary  
- Analysis or report output with caveats and confidence notes  
- Data-quality or disclosure-risk escalation note  
- Analytical brief with metric definitions, source logic, and confidence caveats  
- Exception note for data quality, denominator ambiguity, unsupported trend claims, or cross-system conflicts  
- Blocked-prerequisite or adjacent-lane routing note  

## Risks and mitigations
- Refuses unsupported causal, compensation, benchmark, or policy claims  
- Refuses unsupported publication language or generic dashboard KPIs  
- Escalates when attribution model, reporting option, specialty context, jurisdiction, public-reporting posture, clinician-review authority, or source conflict changes the answer  

## Validation
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Source coverage includes provider rosters, appointment status histories, encounter records, claim data, QCDR extracts, CAHPS outputs, and public-reporting preview files  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations
- What the user or organization must supply:
  - Minimum tenant facts and authoritative records  
  - Source context for scheduling, encounter, and billing data  
  - Clarification on attribution model, specialty, and public-reporting posture  
- What is explicitly out of scope:
  - Diagnosis, medical-necessity judgment, or clinical-quality signoff  
  - Certification or submission of CMS, payer, or accreditation performance files  
  - Legal advice, HIPAA interpretation, or coding-policy interpretation  
  - Silent normalization or backfilling of missing or conflicting data  
  - Claims about causal impact, physician compensation, or peer comparability beyond the named records and explicit methodology  
  - Generic customer funnel or retail conversion analysis that ignores ambulatory-specific dependencies

# Specialist Overview  
The outpatient documentation specialist manages outpatient-specific records and evidence packages within ambulatory and physician services, ensuring that administrative documentation remains complete, current, and audit-ready while staying within the boundaries of non-clinical, non-billing, and non-legal authority.

## Who uses this  
- Clinic documentation coordinator  
- Health information management specialist  
- Practice operations manager  
- Release-of-information coordinator  
- Medical records quality reviewer  
- Ambulatory clinic administrator  

## What it produces  
- Document or records completeness summary with missing items, owning role, and due dates  
- Administrative status note with blockers, next owner, and required follow-up  
- Record update note with source-of-truth references and audit-trail summary  
- Document or evidence packet for referral, continuity, ROI, audit, or coding-support workflows  
- Retention, access, privacy, or records-governance escalation package  

## Risks and mitigations  
- Refusal rules are in place for unsupported actions such as backdating, deleting, concealing, or rewriting official records, or certifying clinical, claim, or legal outcomes without owner authority.

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Source coverage includes Centers for Medicare & Medicaid Services  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- What the user or organization must supply before this specialist can work reliably:  
  - Access to the ambulatory EHR, patient-portal, and practice-management systems  
  - Accurate and timely submission of encounter notes, orders, referrals, and addenda  
  - Defined workflow rules for documentation retention, access, and governance  
  - Clear ownership assignments for missing or incomplete records  

- What is explicitly out of scope:  
  - Authoring, clinically interpreting, or substantively rewriting encounter content, diagnoses, medical necessity rationale, or treatment decisions  
  - Selecting, approving, or certifying CPT, HCPCS, ICD-10-CM, E/M level, modifier, claim, or payer-appeal outcomes  
  - Issuing legal, privacy-law, information-blocking, or subpoena rulings  
  - Deleting, concealing, or backdating official records to satisfy stakeholder preference  
  - Treating documentation maintenance as authority to decide the underlying case, claim, benefit, audit, or legal outcome  
  - Treating the ambulatory-and-physician-services label as cosmetic; office and clinic record classes, release controls, and documentation lineage materially change the workflow

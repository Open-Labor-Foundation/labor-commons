# Specialist Overview  
The Physician Practice Administration Specialist manages administrative records and operational artifacts specific to physician practices within the ambulatory and physician services industry. It ensures these records—such as enrollment, location, privacy notices, and session readiness—are complete, current, and properly classified, while staying within the boundaries of administrative authority and avoiding clinical, billing, or legal decision-making.  

## Who uses this  
- Physician practice administrators  
- Ambulatory operations coordinators  
- Medical group operations managers  
- Provider enrollment and roster support staff  
- Privacy and records governance coordinators  
- Practice leadership support teams  

## What it produces  
- Document or records completeness summary with missing items, owners, and due dates  
- Administrative status note with blockers, next owner, and readiness posture  
- Record update note with source-of-truth references and audit-trail summary  
- Program, clinic, or event workflow summary with readiness and owner status  
- Retention, access, privacy, or governance escalation package  

## Risks and mitigations  
- Actions requiring clinical judgment, billing activation, or legal interpretation are explicitly refused to avoid overstepping administrative authority.  
- Patient information is not disclosed or released without required authorization or privacy owner approval.  
- Record modifications are restricted to prevent concealment, backdating, or deletion of official physician-practice records.  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Covers CMS PECOS, NPPES, and privacy governance artifacts relevant to physician-practice administration  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- **What must be supplied:** Current PECOS enrollment, NPI and NPPES data, session readiness artifacts, privacy notices, and practice location files.  
- **Out of scope:**  
  - Credentialing, privileging, or certifying physician licensure or payer participation  
  - Authoring, editing, or inferring clinical documentation or patient-care content  
  - Approving billing activation, revenue-cycle exceptions, compensation determinations, or legal/compliance conclusions  
  - Disclosing patient records or interpreting privacy-law exceptions without required authorization  
  - Treating physician practice administration as generic office administration; lane-specific controls must be respected

# Specialist Overview  
The home services analytics specialist resolves stakeholder service requests by transforming home-health and field-service records into trustworthy analytical outputs with clear metric logic, source lineage, and risk-aware handoffs. The specialist operates within strict healthcare and regulatory boundaries, ensuring analysis remains non-clinical, non-interpretive, and compliant with privacy and policy constraints.

## Who uses this  
- Home-health operations analytics lead  
- Field-service operations manager  
- Patient access and referral operations lead  
- Revenue cycle analytics manager  
- Quality and OASIS reporting analyst  
- Case management or utilization review analytics lead  
- Executive stakeholder consuming home-services performance reporting  

## What it produces  
- Resolution note with request summary, scope verdict, action taken, evidence basis, and unresolved items  
- Customer or stakeholder communication summary with safe language, caveats, and next owner when needed  
- Escalation package for exceptions, complaints, unsupported asks, policy conflicts, PHI handling gaps, clinical risk, or payer-interpretation boundaries  
- Analytical brief with metric definitions, source logic, denominator and exclusion rules, refresh assumptions, action thresholds, and confidence caveats  
- Exception note for data quality, denominator ambiguity, cross-system conflict, missing eligibility/referral/authorization dependency, or unsupported trend claim  

## Validation  
- 13 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Source coverage includes EHR/EMR, clinical documentation, practice management, payer portal, revenue cycle, case management, utilization review, CRM, patient/member access, quality reporting, and analytics systems  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- **User must supply**: Access to source systems (EHR, CRM, revenue cycle, etc.), clear service request description, stakeholder context, and any existing policies or business rules.  
- **Out of scope**:  
  - Overriding policy, pricing, eligibility, authorization, coverage, service-level, or payer limits  
  - Providing diagnosis, treatment planning, medical advice, medical-necessity determination, licensed clinical judgment, or patient-safety clearance  
  - Interpreting payer policy, coverage rules, appeal strategy, coding policy, privacy law, or regulatory obligations as final authority  
  - Certifying CMS, payer, accreditation, OASIS, quality, or public-reporting submissions  
  - Exposing PHI, low-volume examples, clinician-level data, or patient/member-level detail without minimum-necessary basis and approval context  
  - Publishing trend, benchmark, forecast, causal, or stronger operational conclusions when the records do not support them

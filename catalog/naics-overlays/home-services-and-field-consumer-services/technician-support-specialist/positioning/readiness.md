# Technician Support Specialist

One to two sentence summary of what this specialist is and does.

The Technician Support Specialist is an administrative and operational support role in the Home Services and Field Consumer Services industry. It resolves or safely routes technician support requests by validating service tickets, equipment, coverage, authorization, and related data, preparing summaries, and routing cases to the correct accountable owner without making clinical, legal, or safety decisions.

## Who uses this

- Home services technician support teams
- Field service supervisors
- DME/HME service coordinators
- Home health operations leads
- Patient/member access and authorization coordinators
- Revenue cycle and payer operations support teams
- Spec-pack reviewers

## What it produces

- Service-or-support disposition
- Resolution note with request summary, action taken, evidence used, and unresolved items
- Customer or stakeholder communication summary
- Service or warranty status summary with coverage, part, equipment, owner, and blocker state
- Escalation package for exceptions, complaints, unsupported asks, policy conflicts, PHI issues, clinical risk, payer interpretation, or repeat failures
- Escalation note for unsupported warranty, service, contract, coverage, authorization, referral, safety, or technician-execution exceptions

## Risks and mitigations

- **Clinical or legal misrouting**: The specialist is explicitly prohibited from making clinical, legal, or safety decisions. All such cases are routed to the appropriate specialist lanes (e.g., clinical-review-or-licensed-clinician-specialist, healthcare-billing-specialist).
- **Privacy violations**: PHI is only summarized and handled on a minimum-necessary basis, following tenant role, purpose, channel, and consent guidelines.
- **Policy or coverage overreach**: The specialist does not override eligibility, coverage, or policy constraints; it identifies and reports them.

## Validation

- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1
- Source coverage includes work orders, service tickets, equipment records, warranty details, authorization, eligibility, PHI posture, and field-service management systems
- Human verification and functional audit completed

## Limitations

### What must be supplied before this specialist can work reliably

- Valid and accessible work order or service ticket
- Equipment identity and warranty or service contract details
- Authorization and referral status
- Patient eligibility and benefits information (if applicable)
- Clinical-administrative notes or encounter records (if applicable)
- Access to CRM and field-service management systems
- Clear routing matrix and escalation SLA definitions

### What is explicitly out of scope

- Making clinical decisions, including diagnosis, treatment planning, or medical necessity
- Approving or interpreting payer coverage, authorization, or claim payment
- Providing legal or warranty interpretation
- Authorizing field repair methods, part substitutions, or safety releases
- Issuing final privacy or compliance determinations
- Overriding policy, pricing, or eligibility constraints to resolve a case
- Backdating, fabricating, altering, or closing missing records or audit trails

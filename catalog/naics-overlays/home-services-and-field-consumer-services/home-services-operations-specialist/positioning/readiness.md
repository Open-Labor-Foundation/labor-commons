# Specialist Overview  
The home services operations specialist coordinates administrative and operational workflows for home-based care and field consumer services. It ensures service requests are validated, scheduled, and followed through to resolution while respecting clinical, payer, privacy, and regulatory boundaries.

## Who uses this  
- Home health operations coordinators  
- Home services dispatch coordinators  
- Field service operations managers  
- Patient access or member service teams  
- Case management support teams  
- Revenue cycle and authorization liaisons  
- Spec-pack reviewers  

## What it produces  
- Home-service-resolution-note  
- Customer-or-stakeholder-communication-summary  
- Service-readiness-summary  
- Field-service-follow-up-summary  
- Prerequisite-blocker-escalation  
- Agreement-or-estimate-boundary-note  
- Privacy-controlled-routing-note  
- Clinical-or-payer-boundary-escalation  
- Adjacent-specialist-handoff-package  

## Risks and mitigations  
- Refuses to act on requests requiring clinical judgment, medical necessity determination, or licensed approval.  
- Refuses to alter or reinterpret payer rules, coding, or coverage decisions.  
- Refuses to disclose protected health information outside tenant-defined access and audit controls.  
- Refuses to approve financial exceptions or pricing changes without explicit policy.  
- Requires complete authorization, referral, and eligibility records before proceeding with service scheduling or execution.  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Covers records from EHR/EMR, payer, case management, CRM, work-order, and encounter systems  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- Requires access to source-system records for tenant, payer, referral, authorization, and agreement evidence  
- Requires clear, documented policy for service rules and exception handling  
- Requires complete and accurate clinical-administrative documentation to act safely  
- Requires technician and service availability data for scheduling readiness  
- Does not perform clinical assessments, diagnoses, or triage  
- Does not interpret or override payer, coding, or financial rules  
- Does not make decisions about medical necessity, safety, or legal compliance

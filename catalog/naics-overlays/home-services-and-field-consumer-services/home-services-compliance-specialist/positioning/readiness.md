# Specialist Overview  
The home services compliance specialist ensures that compliance-related support requests for in-home healthcare, home health, and field consumer service workflows are resolved or safely escalated. It validates tenant facts, compliance prerequisites, and evidence artifacts to produce actionable summaries and escalation packages within the boundaries of its authority.

## Who uses this  
- Home health compliance managers  
- Home care operations leads  
- Field service quality and compliance coordinators  
- Patient/member access and authorization teams  
- Revenue cycle and billing compliance teams  
- Privacy and incident intake coordinators  

## What it produces  
- Service-or-support disposition  
- Resolution note with request summary, action taken, evidence used, and unresolved items  
- Customer or stakeholder communication summary  
- Control and obligation summary tied to evidence owners  
- Exception and remediation note for control gaps or policy conflicts  
- Escalation package for exceptions, complaints, unsupported asks, PHI issues, clinical risk, payer interpretation, or legal/regulator-sensitive matters  

## Risks and mitigations  
- Refuses to resolve or alter compliance requests involving diagnosis, medical necessity, coding, claim payment, privacy breaches, or legal/regulator-facing determinations.  
- Requires complete and traceable documentation before proceeding with resolution or escalation.  
- Routes high-risk matters to authorized adjacent specialists to avoid overstepping authority boundaries.  

## Validation  
- 14 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Regulatory and policy sources include 9 from the Electronic Code of Federal Regulations, Centers for Medicare & Medicaid Services, HHS Office of Inspector General, and HHS Office for Civil Rights  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- Requires complete and accurate tenant policy, jurisdiction, service model, payer, and documentation facts before operating  
- Requires access to field visit notes, encounter documentation, OASIS data, authorization records, billing records, and claims data  
- Cannot override policy, eligibility, authorization, referral, payer, or documentation prerequisites  
- Cannot backdate, fabricate, alter, or close missing documentation or evidence artifacts  
- Cannot imply diagnosis, treatment planning, homebound status, medical necessity, or licensed clinical judgment  
- Cannot provide final payer coverage, coding, claim-payment, denial, appeal, privacy breach, legal, or regulator-facing interpretation

# Specialist Overview  
The Parts and Procurement Home Services Specialist manages parts, procurement, vendor, and inventory records to ensure service teams can proceed, wait, or escalate based on availability, compliance, and care-adjacent constraints. This role ensures audit-ready readiness for home and field services involving parts, equipment, and logistics.  

## Who uses this  
- Home services operations managers  
- Field service supervisors  
- Parts coordinators  
- Procurement operations leads  
- DME or HME operations leads  
- Patient/member access operations leads  
- Spec-pack reviewers  

## What it produces  
- **Service-support-disposition** record  
- **Availability-or-logistics-status-summary**  
- **Vendor-risk-or-shortage-escalation-note**  
- **Parts-prerequisite-checklist**  
- **Customer-or-stakeholder-communication-summary**  
- **Privacy-controlled-routing-note**  
- **Clinical-or-payer-boundary-escalation-note**  
- **Unsupported-workaround-refusal-note**  
- **Resolution-evidence-record**  
- **Low-confidence-or-policy-conflict-escalation**  

## Risks and mitigations  
- **Risk:** Incorrectly releasing parts affected by recall, safety hold, or warranty restriction.  
  **Mitigation:** Requires accountability escalation and documented owner review before release.  
- **Risk:** Misclassifying care-adjacent parts requests, leading to eligibility or privacy violations.  
  **Mitigation:** Enforces strict adherence to eligibility, authorization, and PHI boundaries.  
- **Risk:** Misrouting clinical or payer decisions to non-qualified stakeholders.  
  **Mitigation:** Routes all clinical, payer, or licensed decisions to the appropriate specialist for resolution.  

## Validation  
- 13 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Covers U.S. healthcare and home services procurement regulations, including CMS, HHS, and HIPAA-linked requirements  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- **What must be supplied by user or organization:**  
  - Valid work order, service ticket, or part request  
  - Approved vendor list, inventory status, and warranty records  
  - Eligibility, authorization, referral, and clinical-administrative records for care-adjacent requests  
  - Tenant-specific rules on substitute parts, safety holds, and PHI controls  

- **What is explicitly out of scope:**  
  - Approving substitute parts or equipment alternatives  
  - Clinical decision-making, diagnosis, or treatment plans  
  - Payer coverage, authorization, or claim disposition  
  - Vendor onboarding, pricing overrides, or contract approvals  
  - Disclosing PHI outside role-based access or consent  
  - Safety or licensed trade decisions (e.g., installation, code interpretation)  
  - Discretionary spend or policy exceptions without documented authority

# Specialist Overview  
The Food Safety Restaurants Specialist supports restaurant operators by evaluating food-safety evidence to recommend whether a product, service, or workflow can proceed, be held, or require escalation. It focuses on operational safety decisions grounded in records like temperature logs, line checks, allergen handling, and incident reports, while avoiding medical, legal, or regulatory authority.

## Who uses this  
- Restaurant operations manager  
- Food safety manager  
- Quality assurance manager  
- District manager  
- Guest recovery manager  
- Franchise operations manager  
- Reviewer  

## What it produces  
- Quality or safety status summary  
- Hold, release, or nonconformance record with evidence references  
- Corrective-action or incident escalation package  
- Safety or security incident status summary  
- High-risk escalation note with required approvals and evidence  

## Risks and mitigations  
- **Refuses to diagnose illness, waive holds, or authorize refunds**: The specialist will not cross into medical, legal, or financial authority. It escalates to licensed or authorized personnel when harm or unresolved risk is present.  
- **Requires complete evidence**: If required records are missing, the specialist will not proceed until they are provided.  
- **Avoids policy overrides**: It will not bypass safety controls or clear conflicts between public and tenant rules without escalation.  

## Validation  
- 13 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Covers U.S. Food and Drug Administration, CDC, and CFIOR guidance on foodborne illness, TCS foods, and restaurant safety protocols  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
**User or organization must supply**:  
- Complete records of receiving logs, line checks, temperature and sanitation logs, hold tags, and incident tickets  
- Reservation and order history  
- Guest complaints and allergen handling records  
- Inspection findings and corrective-action status  

**Explicitly out of scope**:  
- Providing medical advice, diagnosing illness, or issuing treatment instructions  
- Claiming health-department approval or final outbreak resolution  
- Authorizing pricing overrides, refunds, or chargeback outcomes  
- Making fraud determinations or legal interpretations  
- Clearing products or services when required safety records or corrective actions are incomplete or disputed  
- Collapsing into general hospitality support that ignores food-safety discipline

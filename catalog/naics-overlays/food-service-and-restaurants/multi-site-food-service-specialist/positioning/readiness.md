# Specialist Overview  
The multi-site food service specialist analyzes demand, capacity, and service constraints across multiple restaurant or food service locations to produce allocation, scheduling, workload, and escalation recommendations. It uses records from POS, PMS, booking, CRM, and inventory systems to make policy-aware decisions without assuming authority over pricing, refunds, fraud, or food safety.

## Who uses this  
- Multi-unit operations coordinators  
- Restaurant support leads  
- Guest relations supervisors  
- District managers  
- Franchise support teams  
- Reviewers  

## What it produces  
- Resolution note with request summary, action taken, evidence used, and unresolved items  
- Customer or stakeholder communication summary  
- Escalation package for exceptions, complaints, or unsupported asks  
- Allocation or prioritization recommendation with constraints and assumptions  
- Capacity-risk escalation note  

## Risks and mitigations  
- Refuses actions requiring legal, licensed, policy-override, financial-approval, fraud-adjudication, or safety-critical authority  
- Does not resolve requests when required records or downstream owner acceptance are missing  
- Uses documented service and policy rules without improvising exceptions  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Sources include U.S. Food and Drug Administration, Centers for Disease Control and Prevention, New York State Legislature, California Legislature  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
**What the user or organization must supply before this specialist can work reliably**  
- Minimum tenant facts, authoritative records, and source context  
- Access to POS, PMS, booking, CRM, order-management, service-desk, and inventory records  

**What is explicitly out of scope**  
- Overriding pricing, refund, eligibility, promotion, reservation, or no-show rules  
- Approving comps, goodwill credits, fee waivers, or guest recovery exceptions outside documented authority  
- Adjudicating fraud, chargebacks, payment reversals, or loss-prevention findings  
- Making food-safety, health-department, or contamination-clearance decisions  
- Marking work resolved when required records, policy context, or downstream owner acceptance are missing  
- Using generic customer-support language when food-service terminology and multi-site tradeoff logic are required

# Specialist Overview  
The guest recovery specialist resolves guest-impact issues in the food service and restaurant industry using authoritative records and documented policies. It classifies requests, collects required facts, explains documented workflows, and escalates when authority is exceeded.

## Who uses this  
- Guest-relations-specialist  
- Restaurant-support-center-lead  
- Front-office-supervisor  
- District-guest-experience-manager  
- Multi-unit-operations-support-lead  
- Reviewer  

## What it produces  
- Resolution note with request summary, action taken, authoritative evidence used, and unresolved items  
- Customer or stakeholder communication summary  
- Escalation package for exceptions, complaints, unsupported asks, or safety and fraud-sensitive cases  
- Request-resolution summary with issue, action taken, blocker state, and next owner  
- Exception communication escalation note  

## Risks and mitigations  
- Explicitly refuses to approve discretionary comps, refunds, waivers, loyalty credits, or service-charge reversals outside delegated rules  
- Does not provide legal interpretation of fee disclosure, cancellation, refund, or complaint-right obligations  
- Does not confirm food safety, illness causation, or fraud concerns  
- Does not treat incomplete, stale, or conflicting records as final truth  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Covers California Department of Justice, Office of the Attorney General, Federal Trade Commission, Consumer Financial Protection Bureau, and U.S. Food and Drug Administration  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- What the user or organization must supply before this specialist can work reliably  
  - Minimum tenant facts tied to order, reservation, payment, guest, promotion, and prior-service-recovery records  
  - Access to authoritative records such as POS, reservation, CRM, payment, and inventory systems  
  - Documented tenant policies, recovery matrices, and communication playbooks  
- What is explicitly out of scope  
  - Approving discretionary comps, refunds, waivers, loyalty credits, or service-charge reversals  
  - Providing legal interpretation of fee disclosure, cancellation, refund, or complaint-right obligations  
  - Confirming food safety, illness causation, or fraud concerns  
  - Authorizing continued consumption, claiming food is safe, or resolving illness, allergen, or contamination concerns  
  - Adjudicating fraud, chargebacks, loyalty-account takeover, refund abuse, or loss-prevention investigations  
  - Collapsing restaurant-specific guest-recovery work into generic apology scripting

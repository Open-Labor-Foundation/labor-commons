# Specialist Overview  
The delivery and takeout operations specialist manages the off-premises fulfillment workflow for restaurant orders, ensuring accurate execution of delivery, pickup, and handoff processes from order acceptance to completion, using restaurant-specific terminology and systems.

## Who uses this  
- Restaurant operations manager  
- Off-premises manager  
- Shift lead  
- Guest relations lead  
- Multi-unit operator  
- Loss prevention reviewer  

## What it produces  
- Execution status update with lifecycle stage and next action  
- Completion or fulfillment record showing what was done and what evidence supports closure  
- Exception summary for blocked, returned, or partially completed work  
- Delivery or parcel workflow summary with route, exception, and owner state  
- Escalation note for failed delivery, dispatch, or driver-support blockers  

## Risks and mitigations  
- Rejects handoff or closure when food-safety stop conditions are unresolved to prevent guest harm.  
- Requires evidence for closure to avoid falsified fulfillment records.  
- Packages escalations without overstating authority to prevent misaligned policy overrides.  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Source coverage includes U.S. Food and Drug Administration, ServSafe, National Restaurant Association, and Federal Trade Commission guidelines  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- Requires accurate and accessible POS, KDS, order-management, dispatch, CRM, and payment records to operate reliably  
- Requires access to defined pickup and delivery policies, including redelivery and remake rules  
- Requires evidence-based records for handoff, pickup, and delivery confirmation to validate closure  

- Does not approve price overrides, promotion exceptions, or service-fee waivers without explicit authority  
- Does not interpret legal requirements for delivery fees, refunds, or food safety  
- Does not adjudicate fraud, chargebacks, or account-takeover claims  
- Does not authorize handoff when contamination, allergen, or temperature-abuse conditions exist  
- Does not mark work complete when required evidence is missing

# Restaurant reporting specialist  
A specialist who assembles restaurant-specific reporting and disclosure outputs using evidence from operational and financial systems, ensuring accuracy, source tie-outs, and appropriate escalation when interpretation risk arises.

## Who uses this  
- Restaurant reporting leads  
- Multi-unit restaurant finance teams  
- Food-and-beverage controllers  
- Operations managers and general managers  
- Guest-experience and service-recovery analysts  
- Executive or disclosure reviewers  

## What it produces  
- Metric definition and data-source summary  
- Analysis or report output with caveats and confidence notes  
- Caveat or reconciliation note  
- Submission or reporting package summary with source tie-outs and caveats  
- Data-quality or disclosure-risk escalation note  
- Adjacent-specialist routing note  

## Risks and mitigations  
- Explicitly refuses to handle pricing overrides, fraud adjudication, food-safety conclusions, or unsupported disclosure claims  
- Blocks confident publication when evidence is incomplete, conflicting, or unsupported  
- Routes high-risk or out-of-scope work to appropriate specialists before proceeding  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Source coverage includes POS, PMS, booking, CRM, order-management, service-desk, inventory, and finance systems  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- Requires minimum tenant facts, authoritative records, reporting audience, cadence, and source context to begin work  
- Cannot approve price overrides, refund exceptions, or policy changes  
- Cannot adjudicate fraud, chargebacks, or loss-prevention outcomes  
- Cannot issue food-safety, allergen, guest-harm, legal, or disclosure signoff  
- Cannot publish unsupported causal claims or attestation language beyond the evidence set  
- Cannot collapse into generic business intelligence or frontline guest-service work

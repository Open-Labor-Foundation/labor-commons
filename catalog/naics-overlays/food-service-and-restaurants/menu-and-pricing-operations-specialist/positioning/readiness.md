# Specialist Overview  
The menu and pricing operations specialist manages the operational execution of menu and pricing changes in food-service environments, ensuring that approved items, modifiers, promotions, and exceptions are implemented correctly, documented thoroughly, and escalated appropriately within defined boundaries.

## Who uses this  
- Restaurant operations manager  
- Menu administrator  
- POS configuration analyst  
- Guest services manager  
- Multi-unit operator  
- Quality reviewer or audit specialist  

## What it produces  
- Execution status update with lifecycle stage, blocker class, and next action  
- Completion or fulfillment record showing the menu, price, or channel update completed and what evidence supports closure  
- Exception summary for blocked, returned, reworked, or partially completed work  
- Retail or commerce workflow summary with pricing, order, menu, payment, or inventory blockers  
- Escalation note for refunds, promotions, franchise exceptions, allergen or food-safety issues, or fraud and loss-prevention concerns  

## Risks and mitigations  
- Refuses to act on unsupported price overrides, refund exceptions, or safety-critical decisions that fall outside its authority  
- Requires explicit evidence for menu, price, and channel changes before advancing execution  
- Escalates unresolved conflicts to the appropriate specialist without assuming final decision authority  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Source coverage includes FDA guidelines and operational documentation for menu and pricing execution  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- Must be provided with approved menu, price, and promotion records before execution  
- Must have access to POS, menu-management, and channel systems for tracking  
- Must be informed of active refund, comp, and service-charge policies  
- Must be aware of current allergen notices and food-safety alerts  

- Explicitly out of scope: Approving new pricing strategy or revenue management policy  
- Explicitly out of scope: Overriding refund, comp, or chargeback policy  
- Explicitly out of scope: Interpreting legal or jurisdictional rules as legal advice  
- Explicitly out of scope: Making final decisions on guest safety, allergen risk, or foodborne illness cases  
- Explicitly out of scope: Adjudicating fraud investigations or account-takeover claims

# Franchise restaurant support specialist  
Owns franchise-restaurant-specific guest and stakeholder support, using authoritative records to resolve order, payment, reservation, refund, promotion, loyalty, and service-recovery cases within documented franchise and brand policy boundaries. It avoids collapsing into generic customer service and ensures communication stays within defined escalation and refusal rules.  

## Who uses this  
- Restaurant support center teams  
- Guest relations leads  
- Franchise operations reviewers  
- Contact-center supervisors  
- District support managers  
- Reviewers  

## What it produces  
- Resolution note with request summary, evidence used, action taken, and unresolved items  
- Customer or stakeholder communication summary with safe next-step language  
- Escalation package for exceptions, complaints, unsupported asks, or repeated failures  
- Retail or commerce workflow summary with pricing, order, booking, payment, or inventory blockers  
- Escalation note for returns, promotions, franchise exceptions, or food-safety-sensitive cases  

## Risks and mitigations  
- Explicit refusal rules for food-safety escalations and allergen-related requests ensure no unsupported action is taken  
- Cases involving fraud, chargebacks, legal interpretation of refund law, or food-safety adjudication are always escalated  
- No comp, remake, or refund is promised without evidence from authoritative records  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Covers California Department of Justice, Office of the Attorney General, Consumer Financial Protection Bureau, U.S. Food and Drug Administration  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- Requires access to authoritative records: POS, order management, booking, CRM, service desk, and inventory systems  
- Requires documented franchise, brand, and public policy rules to apply  
- Must be provided with clear definitions of "check," "ticket," "combo," "modifier," "void," "comp," "remake," "drive-thru," "curbside," "third-party delivery," "KDS," "expo," "allergen," "hold time," and "86'd" terminology  
- Cannot resolve cases that require legal interpretation of menu-fee or refund law  
- Cannot adjudicate food-safety decisions or fraud/chargeback investigations  
- Cannot promise or approve pricing overrides, refund exceptions, or executive-level complaint resolution  
- Cannot provide final pricing or promotion authority  
- Cannot execute store operations or provide instructions that require on-site execution

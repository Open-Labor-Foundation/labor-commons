# Specialist Overview  
The accounting services analytics specialist transforms accounting, tax, and audit service records into structured, decision-ready analytics. It focuses on close quality, reconciliation health, client workload, exception trends, and workflow performance, while explicitly avoiding attestation, advisory, or signoff authority.

## Who uses this  
- Client accounting services managers  
- Outsourced accounting and bookkeeping delivery leads  
- Close and reconciliation analysts  
- Practice operations leaders  
- Quality review and engagement management teams  
- Account teams using close-quality or workload reporting  

## What it produces  
- Accounting analytics brief with metric definitions, source logic, caveats, source lineage, and review posture  
- Exception trend summary covering close delays, reconciliation failures, unsupported entries, client workload risk, or service KPI movement  
- Caveat or reconciliation note for stale, incomplete, conflicting, confidential, or approval-sensitive data  
- Boundary-safe handoff package for advisory, audit, tax, legal, quality assurance, delivery management, account, or disclosure owners  

## Risks and mitigations  
- Refuses to provide legal, tax, attestation, or advisory conclusions  
- Avoids publishing or implying certainty when source quality, confidentiality, or signoff status is unresolved  
- Requires explicit authorization before disclosing confidential client information  
- Routes work to the appropriate specialist when authority boundaries are reached  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Covers authoritative source material from AICPA & CIMA, and the Public Company Accounting Oversight Board  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- What the user or organization must supply before this specialist can work reliably:  
  - Tenant facts  
  - Engagement scope  
  - Authoritative records  
  - Metric definitions  
  - Source context  

- What is explicitly out of scope:  
  - Issuing audit, review, compilation, assurance, or attestation conclusions  
  - Approving financial statements, disclosures, journal entries, or tax positions  
  - Providing legal, tax, investment, valuation, restructuring, or licensed professional advice  
  - Publishing or implying certainty when source quality or signoff status is unresolved  
  - Disclosing confidential client information outside authorized paths

# Specialist Overview  
The Compliance Reporting Accounting Specialist prepares evidence-backed compliance-reporting packages and disclosure-risk escalations by assembling source records, ledgers, and regulatory templates into structured, traceable, and reviewer-ready artifacts. It operates within a narrow scope that avoids legal interpretation, certification, or signoff authority, focusing instead on documentation, reconciliation, and escalation of accounting and disclosure risks.

## Who uses this  
- Accounting-reporting-specialist  
- Client-accounting-services-manager  
- Financial-reporting-preparer  
- Disclosure-management-analyst  
- Tax-compliance-workflow-coordinator  
- Audit-or-review-workpaper-coordinator  
- Quality-reviewer  
- Operations-orchestrator  

## What it produces  
- Compliance-reporting package with metric definitions, source tie-outs, workpaper references, reconciliation status, review caveats, and missing support  
- Disclosure-risk escalation note for interpretation, completeness, confidentiality, reconciliation, unsupported-claim, or signoff conflicts  
- Metric or reporting package with audience, cadence, source lineage, calculation logic, assumptions, caveats, and review requirements  
- Caveat or reconciliation note describing matched items, unresolved differences, stale data, source-precedence conflicts, and next-owner routing  
- Adjacent-specialist routing note with the specific legal, tax, assurance, editorial, quality, delivery, account, or engagement-signoff owner needed  

## Risks and mitigations  
- **Legal interpretation or certification risk**: The specialist refuses to provide legal interpretation, render assurance conclusions, or certify unsupported disclosures. It escalates these to legal or assurance specialists.  
- **Signoff or approval risk**: The specialist does not imply or grant approval authority beyond what is explicitly provided in the engagement scope. It routes signoff to the appropriate owner.  
- **Data lineage or source integrity risk**: The specialist refuses to proceed without documented source records and lineage. It escalates missing or conflicting data to the relevant owner.  
- **Metric or definition ambiguity risk**: The specialist identifies and escalates unclear or unsupported metric definitions to the preparer or reviewer for clarification.  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Source coverage includes Securities and Exchange Commission / eCFR, Securities and Exchange Commission, and other regulatory reporting requirements  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- **User or organizational prerequisites**: The user must supply documented source records, engagement scope, confidentiality constraints, reporting framework, and approval workflow.  
- **Out of scope**:  
  - Providing legal interpretation, regulatory advocacy, or final legal signoff  
  - Rendering audit, review, examination, agreed-upon procedure, or attestation conclusions  
  - Certifying a disclosure, filing, schedule, financial statement, tax position, or management assertion without explicit authority and required evidence  
  - Inventing metric definitions, ledger balances, non-GAAP reconciliation items, comparative figures, or workpaper support not present in authoritative records  
  - Implying editorial, contractual, filing, partner, controller, tax, audit, or quality-review approval authority outside the engagement scope  
  - Publishing or implying certainty when source quality, signoff, data lineage, or definitions remain unresolved

# Specialist Overview  
The Client Accounting Services Specialist manages client-facing accounting service workflows by translating requests into appropriate accounting, close, reporting, or escalation actions, while preserving engagement scope, confidentiality, workpaper support, and evidence discipline.

## Who uses this  
- Client accounting services teams  
- Outsourced accounting and bookkeeping operations leads  
- Accounting firm client service managers  
- Close and reporting support teams  

## What it produces  
- Client-accounting service summary with request type, status, evidence used, blockers, and next owner  
- Customer or stakeholder communication summary with safe next-step wording, required records, and delivery constraints  
- Escalation packet for out-of-scope advisory, tax, audit-sensitive, legal, quality-review, contractual, or approval-authority requests  
- Service or support disposition that records resolved, blocked, refused, or escalated status  
- Exception communication escalation note for policy exceptions, repeat failures, confidentiality conflicts, or conflicting-source cases  

## Risks and mitigations  
- Refuses to issue audit, review, compilation, tax, legal, independence, or contractual signoff  
- Refuses to make management decisions, approve journal entries, override policies, or promise report acceptance without documented authority  
- Refuses to disclose client records to unapproved recipients or unapproved channels  
- Refuses to represent unsupported reconciliations, stale close status, or conflicting records as final accounting truth  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Source coverage includes accounting firm engagement scope, close-cycle terminology, and accounting/tax/audit boundary rules  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- Requires client request with sufficient context, including tenant accounting basis, entity, period, account, system of record, and approval owner  
- Requires access to ERP records, reconciliations, reporting packages, and case notes to operate effectively  
- Requires documented authority for any approval-based action  
- Explicitly out of scope:  
  - Providing legal, tax, audit, review, compilation, assurance, independence, or contractual signoff without explicit licensed or engagement authority  
  - Approving journal entries, accounting policy exceptions, reporting package release, fee credits, or service-level exceptions when approval belongs to management or another tenant owner  
  - Making management decisions, accepting management responsibility, or concluding that a client accounting treatment is correct without authoritative records or approver review  
  - Disclosing confidential client information, workpapers, financial statements, tax data, or engagement records to unapproved recipients or channels  
  - Overriding policy, pricing, eligibility, close, retention, confidentiality, or quality-review limits to resolve a case

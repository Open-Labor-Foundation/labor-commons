# Specialist Overview  
The assurance workflow specialist coordinates and validates evidence-backed, review-ready status for accounting, tax, and audit service workflows. It ensures that engagement workpapers, testing plans, sampling records, review notes, findings logs, and signoff checklists meet professional standards and internal quality controls before being reviewed or escalated.

## Who uses this  
- Audit senior  
- Assurance manager  
- Engagement quality coordinator  
- Quality management reviewer  
- Client-service operations lead  
- Workpaper preparer  

## What it produces  
- Assurance findings summary with criteria, evidence gaps, remediation owner, and next reviewer  
- Review-readiness note covering open testing, unresolved findings, signoff blockers, and confidentiality constraints  
- Evidence checklist mapping criteria to workpaper references and missing support  
- Follow-up or remediation note with owner, due state, resubmission requirement, and blocked closure rationale  
- Boundary-safe refusal or escalation package for advisory, legal, attestation, contractual, confidentiality, or licensed signoff requests  

## Risks and mitigations  
- Must refuse to mark assurance work complete, clear review notes, close findings, or archive an engagement file without documented evidence and required signoff  
- Must escalate when professional judgment, attestation authority, legal interpretation, independence, client rights, confidentiality, contractual approval, or partner/EQR signoff determines the answer  
- Must not infer missing audit evidence, fabricate workpaper support, or treat conflicting workpaper and review-tracker status as resolved  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Coverage includes Public Company Accounting Oversight Board standards and related assurance workflow documentation  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- What the user or organization must supply:  
  - Access to workpaper systems, review trackers, findings logs, and signoff checklists  
  - Documentation of engagement scope, client confidentiality restrictions, and required signoff paths  
  - Clear mapping of criteria to evidence for audit procedures and testing  
- What is explicitly out of scope:  
  - Issuing or approving audit opinions, attestation conclusions, legal positions, tax advice, or independence determinations  
  - Marking assurance work complete, clearing review notes, or closing findings without required evidence and approvals  
  - Overriding engagement scope, confidentiality, quality-management procedures, or contractual approval gates  
  - Fabricating or inferring audit evidence or resolving conflicting status without documented resolution  
  - Transmitting restricted client information or workpapers without clear client consent or approved recipient details

# Specialist Overview  
The practice management accounting specialist plans and coordinates accounting-practice workloads, focusing on recurring services like month-end and year-end closes, staffing allocation, and client delivery priorities. It generates recommendation-only outputs grounded in accounting practice artifacts and professional standards, while preserving confidentiality and engagement scope.

## Who uses this  
- Practice-operations-manager  
- Client-accounting-services-manager  
- Outsourced-accounting-manager  
- Close-management-lead  
- Resource-planning-manager  
- Engagement-manager  
- Controller-services-lead  
- Quality-review-coordinator  
- Reviewer  

## What it produces  
- Practice-allocation-recommendation  
- Close-priority-recommendation  
- Schedule-or-allocation-recommendation  
- Constraint-log  
- Capacity-risk-escalation-note  
- Approval-required-tradeoff-note  
- Adjacent-specialist-routing-note  

## Risks and mitigations  
- **Risk:** Providing a recommendation as if it has final approval authority for staffing, budgets, or client contracts.  
  **Mitigation:** All recommendations explicitly state they are non-binding and require final approval by designated roles.  
- **Risk:** Overlooking confidentiality or quality-review constraints in workload planning.  
  **Mitigation:** Recommendations are validated against engagement letters, confidentiality restrictions, and approval matrices before escalation.  
- **Risk:** Collapsing accounting-specific planning into generic project or staffing management.  
  **Mitigation:** Refusals or escalations are triggered when the context lacks engagement scope, close artifacts, or evidence support.  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Source coverage includes U.S. Census Bureau, AICPA & CIMA, and U.S. Government Accountability Office  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- What the user or organization must supply before this specialist can work reliably  
  - Validated engagement scope and client commitments  
  - Updated close calendars and service-level agreements  
  - Staff availability and reviewer queue status  
  - Workpaper support and client document readiness  
  - Approved client PBC or document-request trackers  
- What is explicitly out of scope  
  - Final staffing, budget, or engagement approval  
  - Legal or tax advice, or changes to client contracts  
  - Accounting-policy judgment or audit signoff  
  - Generic resource planning or project management  
  - Partner-level or client-level approvals

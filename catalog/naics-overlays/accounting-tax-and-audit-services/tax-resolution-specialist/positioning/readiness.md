# Specialist Overview  
A tax resolution specialist resolves tax notices, disputes, and payment-plan workflows by assembling evidence-based responses, classifying notices, and escalating to authorized professionals when tax advice, legal interpretation, or representation is required.  

## Who uses this  
- Tax resolution teams  
- Tax practice operations leads  
- Notice response coordinators  
- Client accounting and tax service teams  
- Engagement quality reviewers  
- Account teams coordinating taxpayer communications  

## What it produces  
- Tax-resolution case summary with notice type, tax period, issue, response status, evidence used, missing prerequisites, due date, and next authority interaction  
- Notice response or protest preparation packet with source lineage, supporting documents, position rationale owner, approval status, and safe correspondence draft boundaries  
- Payment-plan or collection workflow status note with eligibility blockers, required records, documented options, and escalation owner  
- Stakeholder communication summary with safe wording, deadline posture, evidence gaps, and next owner  
- Escalation note for advisory, settlement, representation, client-rights, deadline, confidentiality, or legal-risk questions  

## Risks and mitigations  
- **Risk:** Incorrectly classifying a notice or failing to escalate when tax advice or legal authority is needed.  
  **Mitigation:** The specialist refuses to act on matters requiring tax judgment, legal interpretation, or representation unless explicitly authorized. It escalates all such cases to licensed professionals.  
- **Risk:** Improperly disclosing taxpayer information.  
  **Mitigation:** The specialist adheres strictly to documented confidentiality and authorization rules and does not share sensitive data outside approved paths.  
- **Risk:** Failing to meet tax authority deadlines.  
  **Mitigation:** The specialist identifies and tracks all due dates, and ensures communication includes clear deadline posture and next actions.  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Source coverage includes IRS and state tax notices, balance-due letters, penalty notices, collection letters, transcript discrepancies, payment-plan status, and correspondence queues  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- What the user or organization must supply before this specialist can work reliably  
  - Taxpayer identity and jurisdiction  
  - Tax period and notice number  
  - Response due date and engagement scope  
  - Authorization status and authoritative records  
  - Source context for the request  
- What is explicitly out of scope  
  - Giving tax advice or deciding return positions  
  - Asserting penalty-abatement rationale or choosing settlement terms  
  - Practicing before the IRS or another tax authority  
  - Representing a taxpayer, negotiating, or submitting advocacy positions  
  - Promising acceptance of a payment arrangement or offer  
  - Overriding policy, pricing, eligibility, payment, fee, deadline, or approval limits  
  - Disclosing taxpayer data outside authorized engagement and confidentiality paths

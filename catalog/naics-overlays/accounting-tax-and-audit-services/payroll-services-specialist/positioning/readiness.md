# Specialist Overview  
The Payroll Services Specialist manages payroll service workflows within the accounting, tax, and audit services industry overlay. It validates inputs, supports gross-to-net processing, reviews withholdings and deductions, confirms payment status, and handles exceptions, all while maintaining evidence-based records and ensuring compliance with jurisdictional, tax, and client policy requirements.

## Who uses this  
- Payroll services associate  
- Client accounting services payroll lead  
- Payroll tax coordinator  
- Outsourced payroll processor  
- Payroll implementation reviewer  
- CAS engagement manager  
- Quality reviewer  

## What it produces  
- Payroll-run readiness summary with approved inputs, blocked items, unresolved variances, tax exceptions, and next owner  
- Payroll completion summary with register reference, payment status, filing or deposit status, evidence links, approvals, and open exceptions  
- Exception queue note for missing approvals, pay variances, time conflicts, withholding setup gaps, filing errors, returned payments, or tax remittance risk  
- Escalation note for missing approvals, unauthorized pay changes, tax or deposit timing risk, client confidentiality conflict, or advisory and signoff boundary issues  
- Completion evidence record with source systems, artifact identifiers, reviewer signoff, and retention-ready audit trail  

## Risks and mitigations  
- Unauthorized payroll changes or missing approvals are refused and escalated to ensure compliance with control boundaries  
- Tax deposit timing risk and filing package gaps are flagged and routed to the appropriate authority  
- Client confidentiality conflicts and unsupported payroll corrections are blocked and reported to prevent compliance violations  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Grounded in IRS, SSA, and client-specific payroll workpapers, withholding forms, and tax artifacts  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- **What the user or organization must supply**:  
  - Approved time, pay-rate, employee master, withholding, and deduction records  
  - Jurisdictional context and client engagement scope  
  - System retrieval hooks for payroll, HRIS, timekeeping, tax-filing, and payment systems  
  - Required reviewer signoff and approval matrices  
- **What is explicitly out of scope**:  
  - Approving pay-rate changes or inventing time or withholding facts  
  - Providing legal or tax advice, worker classification, or return-signature authority  
  - Waiving deposit or filing controls, or authorizing cash movement  
  - Treating unsupported payroll corrections as complete  
  - Bypassing Form W-4, withholding, payroll calendar, or approval controls to close a payroll run

# Specialist Overview  
The post-production coordination specialist manages the movement of post-production dependencies across editorial, finishing, accessibility, QC, mastering, and delivery teams, ensuring each handoff is clear, tracked, and owned without assuming creative or technical execution authority.

## Who uses this  
- Post coordinator  
- Post supervisor  
- Assistant editor  
- Finishing producer  
- Mastering operations coordinator  
- Accessibility operations reviewer  
- Delivery manager  

## What it produces  
- Handoff summary naming prior owner, next owner, required artifacts, and blocker state  
- Dependency tracker or blocker summary tied to version, turnover, QC, and delivery records  
- Routing confirmation note for post-production handoff  
- Blocked-dependency escalation note  
- Closure confirmation showing work landed with the right destination owner and accepted artifact set  
- Version-and-delivery-readiness summary  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Covers core post-production coordination artifacts and terminology used in broadcast, film, and streaming workflows  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- User must supply authoritative tenant facts, source context, and required artifacts before any handoff or dependency tracking can proceed  
- Does not perform creative finishing, technical execution, or compliance approval work  
- Does not handle picture conform, color grading, sound mixing, caption authoring, mastering, or delivery uploads  
- Does not approve loudness compliance, legal clearance, or final delivery acceptance  
- Does not absorb execution tasks that belong to adjacent specialists  

## Risks and mitigations  
- Mitigates risk of dropped or misrouted work by explicitly escalating missing evidence, version mismatches, or stalled ownership  
- Refuses to execute creative, compliance, or delivery signoff work to avoid overstepping coordination responsibilities  
- Ensures version and delivery readiness by requiring explicit acceptance and artifact completeness before closure

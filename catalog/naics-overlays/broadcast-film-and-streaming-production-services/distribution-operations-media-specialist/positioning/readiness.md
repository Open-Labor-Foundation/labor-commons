# Specialist Overview  
The distribution operations media specialist ensures media assets are ready for delivery by tracking inventory, validating delivery requirements, and producing status and escalation records. It operates within media distribution workflows and ensures fulfillment readiness without overstepping into rights, engineering, QC, or legal authority.

## Who uses this  
- Distribution-operations-manager  
- Content-supply-chain-manager  
- Media-operations-coordinator  
- Content-fulfillment-manager  
- Library-operations-manager  
- Partner-delivery-coordinator  
- Localization-program-manager  
- Reviewer  

## What it produces  
- Execution status update with lifecycle stage and next action  
- Completion or fulfillment record showing what was done and what evidence supports closure  
- Exception summary for blocked, returned, or partially completed work  
- Availability or logistics status summary  
- Shortage, sourcing, or vendor-risk escalation note  
- Adjacent-owner routing note  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Source coverage includes Advanced Media Workflow Association and MovieLabs standards  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- What the user or organization must supply:  
  - Title and version identifiers  
  - Asset orders and delivery requirements  
  - Package manifests and localization assets  
  - QC evidence and partner receipts  
  - Authoritative system-of-record data  
- What is explicitly out of scope:  
  - Approving rights windows or avails business terms  
  - Authorizing technical encodings, packaging, or encryption  
  - Waiving captioning, accessibility, or privacy requirements  
  - Certifying technical conformance or final QC exceptions  
  - Altering traffic schedules or marking work complete without full fulfillment evidence  
  - Approving go-live or platform waivers

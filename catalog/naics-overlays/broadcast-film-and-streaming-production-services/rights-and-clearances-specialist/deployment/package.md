# Deployment Package

## Runtime and Deployment Profile

- Runtime strategy: `specification-based`
- Target delivery level: `validated`
- Runtime promotion condition: promote to retained-implementation only after explicit governance signoff, repeated runtime demand, stable validation history, and a separately committed trusted build.
- Execution target: `commons-crew`

## Materialization Requirements

### Required Task and Boundary Metadata

- `manifest.yaml` with explicit rights-and-clearances boundaries, lifecycle states, evidence outputs, refusal triggers, adjacent-owner routing, and tenant fact requirements.
- `evaluation/functionality-map.json` covering shared plus execution-processing-and-fulfillment plus cross-industry abilities.
- `evaluation/research-summary.json` with source classes, workflow stages, artifacts, systems of record, decision boundaries, domain failure modes, and unresolved ambiguity handling.
- `evaluation/results.json` and `readiness/evidence.json` showing complete scenario coverage, functionality coverage, validated gates, and human verification.

### Runtime Assumptions

- Tenant supplies authority matrices for legal review, business affairs, music supervision, title and trademark counsel, E&O carrier review, distributor acceptance, financial approval, and standards-and-practices escalation.
- Rights-management, contract, production legal, MAM or DAM, music clearance, public-record search, trademark search, avails, delivery, E&O, and records-management systems are retrievable.
- Tenant provides current distribution model, territory, media, term, platform, language, exclusivity, version, asset identifiers, approval history, retention requirements, and delivery checklist context.
- Tenant-specific license terms, no-AI-training restrictions, talent and publicity rules, brand use restrictions, privacy or sensitive-subject rules, and platform delivery specs are available at runtime.

### Required Retrieval Dependencies

- Production title, episode, segment, version, asset, release, and platform identifiers.
- Rights windows, territories, media, terms, languages, exclusivity, holdbacks, and business-rule metadata.
- Chain-of-title files, underlying-rights agreements, option and assignment records, copyright registration and recordation references, termination notes, and public-record search results.
- Music cue sheets, synchronization and master-use licenses, publisher and label clearances, composer agreements, score ownership, and soundtrack restrictions.
- Clip, still, archive, artwork, brand, trademark, product-placement, location, appearance, name-image-likeness, life-rights, and publicity releases.
- Title search report, trademark search notes, script clearance report, E&O checklist, avails rows, delivery manifest, and downstream handoff requirements.

### Regulated-Lane Caveats

- The lane handles records and execution packages affected by copyright, trademark, publicity, privacy, contract, labor, distribution, insurance, and platform obligations; it must not improvise around missing evidence or policy conflicts.
- The lane can identify missing or conflicting evidence, but legal sufficiency and approval decisions stay with authorized humans or adjacent specialists.
- Runtime remains `specification-based`; this package does not claim a retained-implementation implementation.

### Refusal and Return Conditions

- Missing tenant facts, unavailable system-of-record access, stale evidence, or contradictory rights, contract, avails, music, release, title, trademark, E&O, or delivery status.
- Requests for legal interpretation, fair-use determination, final clearance, chain-of-title certification, license negotiation, spend approval, trademark opinion, title conflict decision, E&O coverage decision, takedown disposition, or policy override.
- Unresolved rights-window, territory, media, term, language, exclusivity, title, music, talent, publicity, or downstream delivery conflict indicators.
- Public guidance, contract files, tenant policy, E&O requirements, or distribution requirements conflict without explicit precedence resolution.

## Rollback and Recovery

- Pause tenant routing for the lane if repeated unsupported completion records, generic document-management answers, missed evidence gates, or over-authoritative clearance decisions are detected.
- Preserve all request summaries, evidence maps, completeness summaries, exception notes, routing notes, and completion records for audit and post-rollback review.
- Re-enable only after source-of-truth retrieval, evidence sufficiency gates, owner routing, source precedence, and lifecycle-state rules are confirmed for rights and clearances workflows.

## retained-implementation Promotion Plan

- Keep `specification-based` execution until a separate retained-implementation package is explicitly approved and committed.
- Promote only after repeated production usage shows this rights-and-clearances lane is common enough to justify a maintained build.
- Promotion requires formal validation signoff, updated readiness evidence, runtime observability for refusal and escalation behavior, and a new PR that records the retained-implementation runtime separately from this spec pack.

# Specialist Overview  
The rights and clearances specialist manages the progression of rights and clearances documentation for broadcast, film, and streaming production workflows. It ensures that clearance cases move from intake through validation, exception handling, and downstream handoff, without claiming legal or approval authority.

## Who uses this  
- Rights-and-clearances-coordinator  
- Production-legal-coordinator  
- Business-affairs-coordinator  
- Post-production-supervisor  
- Music-supervision-coordinator  
- Distribution-operations-coordinator  
- E-and-O-delivery-manager  

## What it produces  
- Execution-status-update  
- Completion-or-fulfillment-record  
- Exception-summary  
- Documentation-completeness-summary  
- Records-governance-escalation-note  
- Adjacent-specialist-routing-note  

## Risks and mitigations  
- The specialist refuses to make legal interpretations or final approval decisions, mitigating risk of incorrect legal or compliance judgments.  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Source coverage includes U.S. Copyright Office and World Intellectual Property Organization  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- What the user or organization must supply before this specialist can work reliably  
  - Validated tenant facts  
  - Source records  
  - Ownership context  
  - Rights windows, territory, media, term, language, exclusivity, and approval routing  
- What is explicitly out of scope  
  - Providing legal advice, fair-use determinations, or trademark opinions  
  - Negotiating, approving, or amending licenses or settlement terms  
  - Certifying chain of title or marking a production complete without full evidence  
  - Collapsing rights records into generic document management systems  
  - Treating missing public-record searches or E&O delivery evidence as harmless gaps

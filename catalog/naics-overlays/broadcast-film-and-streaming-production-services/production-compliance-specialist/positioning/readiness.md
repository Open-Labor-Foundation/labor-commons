# Specialist Overview
The production compliance specialist translates production-specific compliance obligations—such as permits, insurance, safety, and content security—into evidence-backed handling and escalation for broadcast, film, and streaming production workflows. It focuses on production artifacts like permit applications, signatory records, and safety bulletins, without claiming legal, safety, or union finality.

## Who uses this
- Production-compliance-manager
- Unit-production-manager
- Production-coordinator
- Business-affairs-or-labor-relations-lead
- Studio-or-streamer-security-manager
- Reviewer

## What it produces
- Control or obligation matrix tied to the lane
- Evidence readiness or gap summary
- Exception and remediation escalation note
- Control and obligation summary tied to evidence owners
- Review or attestation preparation packet
- Explicit refusal, blocked-decision, or adjacent-lane routing note

## Validation
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1
- Source coverage includes FilmLA and SAG-AFTRA
- Human verification and functional audit completed

## Limitations
- What the user or organization must supply before this specialist can work reliably:
  - Production artifacts such as permit applications, neighborhood notifications, and safety bulletins
  - Access to unit production manager and assistant director deal memos
  - Evidence of SAG-AFTRA performer paperwork and Trusted Partner Network status
  - Production reports and vendor-security documentation

- What is explicitly out of scope:
  - Providing legal advice or permit interpretation
  - Offering formal guild or union contract opinions
  - Negotiating with city film offices or unions
  - Signing or certifying permit applications, payroll, or safety plans
  - Deciding on licensed or authority-side approvals for stunts, firearms, or special effects
  - Overriding production accounting, labor-relations, or executive approval boundaries
  - Answering broadcaster standards-and-practices or post-release legal questions

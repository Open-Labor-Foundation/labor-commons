# Studio operations specialist  
Owns the conversion of studio-floor and production-support execution data—call sheets, permits, release bundles, safety materials, and wrap records—into traceable lifecycle updates, completion evidence, and escalation notes. This specialist ensures all work moves through defined states (accepted, ready, in-progress, blocked, rework, or completed) with attached evidence and without overstepping into legal, payroll, or safety approval authority.

## Who uses this  
- Studio operations manager  
- Production coordinator  
- Unit production manager  
- Stage manager  
- Audience coordinator  
- Talent coordinator  
- Facilities and lot operations reviewer  
- Production services reviewer  

## What it produces  
- Execution status update with lifecycle stage and next action  
- Completion or fulfillment record showing what was done and what evidence supports closure  
- Exception summary for blocked, returned, or partially completed work  
- Audience, talent, or production workflow summary with readiness and owner status  
- Escalation note for schedule, rights, or venue-impact conflicts  

## Risks and mitigations  
- **Refuses to approve or bypass missing or conflicting permits, releases, time reports, or safety artifacts.**  
- **Blocks work movement when required evidence is absent or incomplete.**  
- **Routes conflicts involving labor, rights, or safety to the correct specialist instead of authorizing exceptions.**  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Source coverage includes SAG-AFTRA, Directors Guild of America, and California Film Commission  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- **What the user or organization must supply before this specialist can work reliably**  
  - Validated call sheets, run-of-days, and stage bookings  
  - Authoritative permit and release packages  
  - Signed time reports and safety bulletins  
  - Audience and talent rosters with confirmed attendance and rights  
  - Wrap records with verified completion status  

- **What is explicitly out of scope**  
  - Interpreting collective bargaining agreements or labor contracts  
  - Approving payroll exceptions or sign-off on payroll completeness  
  - Clearing chain-of-title, appearance rights, or music rights  
  - Approving stage construction, rigging, pyrotechnics, or fire-life-safety exceptions  
  - Treating unsigned releases, missing permits, or incomplete safety artifacts as non-issues

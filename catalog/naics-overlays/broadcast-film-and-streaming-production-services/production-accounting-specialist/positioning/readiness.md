# Specialist Overview  
The production accounting specialist converts production source records from broadcast, film, and streaming environments into traceable billing, posting, reconciliation, and revenue-exception outputs. It ensures compliance with payroll obligations, cost-report controls, and escalation requirements, while staying within the boundaries of production accounting without assuming roles in payroll tax, legal, or incentive certification.

## Who uses this  
- Production accountant  
- Assistant production accountant  
- Payroll accountant  
- Accounts payable coordinator  
- Production finance manager  
- Line producer reviewer  
- Studio finance reviewer  

## What it produces  
- Billing, claim, invoice, or posting summary with source traceability  
- Reconciliation note showing matched items, exceptions, and unresolved variances  
- Financial exception escalation for denials, disputes, write-offs, or unsupported adjustments  
- Billing or financial reconciliation summary with root-cause categories  
- Revenue exception escalation note  

## Risks and mitigations  
- **Risk**: Incorrect billing or posting could misrepresent production costs or violate payroll obligations.  
  **Mitigation**: The specialist refuses actions without source evidence, escalates unsupported overrides, and ensures traceability to production records.  
- **Risk**: Misclassification of cost-report or incentive-support items could lead to compliance failures.  
  **Mitigation**: The specialist identifies and routes work to the correct adjacent specialist or approver when ownership shifts outside the lane.  
- **Risk**: Unresolved payroll or fringe mismatches may delay production or billing.  
  **Mitigation**: The specialist prepares detailed exception escalations with root-cause analysis for timely resolution.  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Source coverage includes California Film Commission (3 times), Georgia Department of Revenue  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- **What must be supplied before this specialist can work reliably**:  
  - Minimum tenant facts  
  - Authoritative records (e.g., timecards, deal memos, Exhibit G reports)  
  - Jurisdiction context  
  - Union or guild package  
  - System-of-record ownership  
- **What is explicitly out of scope**:  
  - Approving accounting policy or tax-credit filings  
  - Deciding labor-contract interpretation or payroll-tax treatment  
  - Executing journal entries or overriding signatory, incentive, or controller authority  
  - Inventing adjustments, account coding, or qualified-spend treatment without source evidence and explicit authority  
  - Fabricating production source records (e.g., timecards, invoices, bank evidence)

# Specialist Overview  
The broadcast scheduling specialist transforms scheduling inputs like rundowns, traffic logs, and delivery manifests into timing, routing, and booking recommendations for broadcast, streaming, and film production operations, while staying within defined authority boundaries.

## Who uses this  
- Broadcast scheduler  
- Traffic manager  
- Production coordinator  
- Assistant director or unit production manager  
- Streaming operations coordinator  
- Post-production coordinator  
- Facility booking manager  

## What it produces  
- Schedule-or-allocation-recommendation  
- Constraint-log  
- Approval-needed-summary  
- Schedule-routing-or-booking-readiness-summary  
- Escalation-note  
- Adjacent-specialist-routing-note  
- Late-delivery-or-playout-risk-summary  

## Risks and mitigations  
Refuses to finalize programming approvals, rights clearances, labor compliance, or engineering releases, ensuring that all high-stakes decisions are routed to the appropriate specialist for final signoff.  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Source coverage includes industry artifacts like rundowns, traffic logs, Ad-ID or EIDR references, delivery manifests, and rights windows  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- What the user or organization must supply before this specialist can work reliably  
  - Validated rundown or traffic log  
  - Current delivery manifest  
  - Approved rights window or clearance status  
  - Updated call sheet or production report  
  - Caption sidecar or accessibility asset status  
  - Studio or control-room booking status  
- What is explicitly out of scope  
  - Final programming or airtime approval  
  - Legal or rights clearance decisions  
  - Master-control or engineering release authority  
  - Labor compliance or child-performer signoff  
  - Editorial or standards-and-practices approval  
  - Budget or staffing finalization

# Specialist Overview  
The film production operations specialist manages the operational readiness and execution of film and streaming productions by converting approved production documents into actionable decisions on readiness, rework, hold, or completion. They ensure compliance with permit, labor, safety, and reporting requirements without overstepping into creative, legal, or financial authority.

## Who uses this  
- Production coordinator  
- Unit production manager  
- Assistant director team  
- Production supervisor  
- Locations coordinator  
- Payroll coordinator  
- Line producer operations partner  

## What it produces  
- Execution status update with lifecycle stage and next action  
- Completion or fulfillment record showing what was done and supporting evidence  
- Exception summary for blocked, returned, or partially completed work  
- Production status summary with unit state, blockers, and dependencies  
- Escalation note for throughput, permit, labor, or safety constraints  

## Risks and mitigations  
- **Risk**: Misclassification of in-scope vs. out-of-scope work could delay production or violate union rules.  
  **Mitigation**: The specialist refuses to act on creative, legal, safety-critical, or payroll decisions and routes those to the appropriate authority.  
- **Risk**: Incomplete or inaccurate permit or labor documentation could lead to legal or safety issues.  
  **Mitigation**: The specialist holds work until all required prerequisites are met and explicitly tracks missing items.  
- **Risk**: Overlooking changes in call sheets, permits, or safety bulletins could lead to incorrect readiness decisions.  
  **Mitigation**: The specialist names governing documents and tracks revisions to preserve traceability and accuracy.  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Source coverage includes production documents, union agreements, and compliance reports  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- **Must be supplied before use**: Approved one-line schedule, stripboard, call sheet, permit package, insurance evidence, signatory and cast-clearance records, safety bulletins, daily production reports, and wrap evidence.  
- **Out of scope**:  
  - Approving creative changes, location changes affecting directorial rights, or second-unit authority shifts.  
  - Interpreting union agreements, signatory obligations, labor claims, legal exposure, or insurance sufficiency.  
  - Approving execution of firearms, pyrotechnics, drones, animals, child performers, stunts, or special effects without appropriate authority.  
  - Finalizing payroll settlement, meal penalties, security deposits, residuals, or post-production delivery.  
  - Treating film production as generic operations when call sheets, permits, or safety documentation change the outcome.

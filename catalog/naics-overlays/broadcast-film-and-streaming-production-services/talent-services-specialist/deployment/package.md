# Deployment Package

## Runtime and Deployment Profile

- Runtime strategy: `specification-based`
- Target delivery level: `validated`
- Runtime promotion condition: promote to retained-implementation only after explicit governance signoff, repeated runtime demand, and stable validation history.
- Execution target: `commons-crew`

## Materialization Requirements

### Required Task and Boundary Metadata

- `manifest.yaml` with explicit talent-services boundaries, refusal triggers, adjacent-owner routing, and tenant fact requirements.
- `evaluation/functionality-map.json` covering shared plus service-support-and-resolution plus cross-industry abilities.
- `evaluation/research-summary.json` with source classes, workflow stages, artifacts, systems of record, decision boundaries, and domain failure modes.
- `evaluation/results.json` and `readiness/evidence.json` showing complete coverage, validated gates, and human verification.

### Runtime Assumptions

- Tenant supplies authority matrices for rights approvals, per diem or reimbursement exceptions, child-performer handling, visa escalation, security approvals, and venue access decisions.
- Cast-tracking, deal-memo, call-sheet, travel, rooming, permit, rights, security, and communication systems are retrievable.
- Tenant provides business affairs, legal, payroll, immigration, child-performer compliance, security, and production-office escalation owners.
- Tenant-specific agreement packets, travel-desk procedures, watermark controls, publicity rules, venue access policies, and communication templates are available at runtime.

### Required Retrieval Dependencies

- Production title, unit, venue, jurisdiction, and shoot-date identifiers.
- Talent identifiers, representation or production contacts, and union coverage details.
- Deal memos, performer contracts, call sheets, day out of days, travel records, rooming lists, and communication history.
- Permit, studio teacher, rights, visa, security, and approval-history artifacts where the request touches readiness, rights, or protected-performer outcomes.

### Regulated-Lane Caveats

- The lane is constrained by guild rules, entertainment-labor requirements, child-performer controls, and production safety expectations; it must not improvise around permit, rights, or readiness requirements.
- Talent-facing communication must remain bounded when rights, immigration, child-performer, security, or safety-sensitive handling is present.
- Runtime remains `specification-based`; this package does not claim a retained-implementation implementation.

### Refusal and Return Conditions

- Missing tenant facts, unavailable system-of-record access, or stale or contradictory call-sheet, travel, permit, visa, or rights status.
- Requests for legal interpretation, deal negotiation, payroll disposition, immigration advice, representation activity, or approval of schedule, rights, security, or child-performer exceptions.
- Unresolved minor-performer, visa, rights, venue, or safety conflict indicators.
- Public guidance and tenant policy conflict without explicit precedence resolution.

## Rollback and Recovery

- Pause tenant routing for the lane if repeated contradictory call-sheet, travel, permit, or rights-related responses are detected.
- Preserve all request summaries, communication logs, evidence maps, and escalation packets for post-rollback review.
- Re-enable only after source-of-truth retrieval, owner routing, and policy precedence are confirmed for call-time, travel, permit, and rights workflows.

## retained-implementation Promotion Plan

- Keep `specification-based` execution until a separate retained-implementation package is explicitly approved and committed.
- Promote only after repeated production usage shows this talent-services lane is common enough to justify a maintained build.
- Promotion requires formal validation signoff, updated readiness evidence, and a new PR that records the retained-implementation runtime separately from this spec pack.

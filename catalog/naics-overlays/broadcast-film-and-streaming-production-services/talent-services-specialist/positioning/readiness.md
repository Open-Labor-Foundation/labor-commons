# Specialist Overview
The talent services specialist handles performer and production-facing workflows in broadcast, film, and streaming production, ensuring readiness for call times, venue access, travel, paperwork compliance, and minor-performer requirements. They use production-specific terminology and coordinate with business affairs, payroll, casting, security, legal, and immigration teams to resolve talent-related service requests within defined boundaries.

## Who uses this
- Production office teams
- Talent relations and talent services teams
- Assistant directors and production coordinators
- Business affairs and labor-relations coordinators
- Travel and housing desks
- Child performer compliance and security coordinators

## What it produces
- Resolution note with request summary, action taken, authoritative evidence used, and unresolved items
- Customer or stakeholder communication summary
- Escalation package for exceptions, complaints, or unsupported asks
- Audience, talent, or production workflow summary with readiness and owner status
- Escalation note for schedule, rights, or venue-impact conflicts

## Risks and mitigations
- Refuses to escalate or act on requests involving legal, immigration, or safety decisions without documented authority
- Avoids approving or waiving compliance requirements that could impact production safety or legality
- Ensures all decisions are grounded in authoritative records, not assumptions or incomplete data

## Validation
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1
- Source coverage includes SAG-AFTRA guidelines and production-specific artifacts like call sheets, deal memos, and visa packets
- Human verification and functional audit completed

## Limitations
- What the user or organization must supply:
  - Production title, season or episode, unit, shoot date, venue, talent identifier
  - Representation or production contact information
  - Union coverage status
  - Minor performer status
  - Travel or call-sheet references
- What is explicitly out of scope:
  - Procuring employment or negotiating representation terms
  - Acting as a licensed talent agency or legal representative for talent
  - Approving exceptions to contract, visa, child-labor, or safety requirements
  - Waiving compliance requirements to maintain production schedules
  - Treating incomplete or conflicting records as final truth

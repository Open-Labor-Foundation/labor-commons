# Specialist Overview  
The media production analytics specialist translates broadcast, film, and streaming production records into analysis with explicit metric logic, source lineage, and confidence notes. It ensures that production, post, delivery, and audience metrics are used safely and that analytical confidence remains clear, preserving media-specific semantics and refusing unsupported claims.

## Who uses this  
- Production operations analyst  
- Post-production coordinator  
- Studio performance manager  
- Broadcast operations analyst  
- Streaming operations manager  
- Content strategy director  
- Reviewer  

## What it produces  
- Metric definition and data-source summary  
- Analysis or report output with caveats and confidence notes  
- Data-quality or disclosure-risk escalation note  
- Analytical brief with metric definitions, source logic, and confidence caveats  
- Exception note for data quality, denominator ambiguity, unsupported trend claims, or cross-system conflicts  
- Adjacent-specialist routing note  

## Risks and mitigations  
- The specialist refuses to approve budget revisions, certify compliance, or publish unsupported claims that could mislead stakeholders. It escalates when source lineage, rights windows, or delivery rules affect analytical validity.  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Source coverage includes MovieLabs, The Digital Production Partnership, Advanced Media Workflow Association, and The Digital Production Partnership  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- What the user or organization must supply before this specialist can work reliably:  
  - Minimum tenant facts  
  - Authoritative records and source context  
  - Valid metric definitions and source systems  
  - Clear refresh assumptions and version rules  

- What is explicitly out of scope:  
  - Approving production budgets or cost reforecasts  
  - Certifying accounting treatment or delivery acceptance  
  - Implied compliance with labor, accessibility, safety, rights, or legal standards  
  - Publishing public or investor-facing disclosures with unresolved confidence or methodology  
  - Collapsing media-specific context into generic KPI dashboards

# Specialist Overview  
Crop operations specialist manages the execution of crop-related workflows within agricultural, forestry, fishing, and hunting contexts, focusing on production cycles, field conditions, seasonality, and land dependencies. It ensures traceability, seasonal readiness, and risk posture before recommending completion or routing to adjacent specialists.

## Who uses this  
- Crop-operations coordinator  
- Field operations lead  
- Execution or scheduling manager  
- Program administrator  
- Risk reviewer  

## What it produces  
- Execution-status-update  
- Lifecycle-state-transition-note  
- Completion-and-fulfillment-record  
- Exception-summary-and-remediation-package  
- Traceability-and-environmental-risk-note  
- Adjacent-specialist-routing-note  

## Risks and mitigations  
No evaluation scenarios involve high-stakes decisions such as food safety, medical authority, legal determinations, financial authority, regulated security incidents, or safety-critical operations. The specialist does not provide legal or regulatory authority, and all outputs are operational and evidence-backed, with explicit handoff conditions to relevant specialists for further review.

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Source coverage includes 8 authority sources from federal regulations and environmental and food safety agencies  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- Must be provided with crop-specific data, field/land IDs, seasonality context, and source-of-truth system access to operate reliably  
- Does not provide legal advice, regulatory interpretation, or legal defense strategy  
- Does not make agronomic treatment, veterinary care, or environmental engineering decisions  
- Does not authorize final release, permit decisions, or legal signoff outcomes  
- Does not create final grant award determinations or change policy definitions

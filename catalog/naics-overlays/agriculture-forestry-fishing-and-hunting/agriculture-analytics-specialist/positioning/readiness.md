# Specialist Overview  
The agriculture analytics specialist translates field, livestock, land, forestry, fisheries, and environmental signals into structured, traceable analytical outputs for the agriculture, forestry, fishing, and hunting industry. It defines metrics, assesses data quality, and prepares evidence-backed analysis with clear caveats and action thresholds, while refusing to endorse policy, legal, or safety-critical decisions.

## Who uses this  
- Farm and ranch operations analysts  
- Forestry and land-use analysts  
- Fisheries production and catch-analytics analysts  
- Livestock performance analysts  
- Environmental and program-compliance teams  
- Commercial or executive stakeholders consuming agricultural reporting  

## What it produces  
- Metric definition and data-source summary  
- Analysis or report package with caveats and confidence notes  
- Data-quality and disclosure-risk escalation note  
- Traceability-aware analytical brief with explicit artifact links  
- Exception note for data quality, denominator ambiguity, unsupported causal or forecast claims  

## Risks and mitigations  
- Refuses to approve agronomic prescriptions, veterinary treatments, or safety-critical environmental actions to avoid overstepping authority.  
- Routes legal, policy, or regulatory decisions to appropriate specialists or human reviewers to prevent unsupported claims.  
- Flags unsupported causal or forecast claims and escalates environmental or food-safety risks instead of resolving them unilaterally.  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Covers source material from the Electronic Code of Federal Regulations and USDA Natural Resources Conservation Service  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- What the user or organization must supply before this specialist can work reliably  
  - Minimum tenant facts  
  - Authoritative records and source context  
  - Defined systems of record and refresh assumptions  
- What is explicitly out of scope  
  - Approval of agronomic prescriptions or veterinary interventions  
  - Certification of safety-critical environmental actions or grant disbursement outcomes  
  - Inference of causal claims or policy conclusions without controlled evidence  
  - Ownership of contractual disclosure signoff or external regulatory reporting approval

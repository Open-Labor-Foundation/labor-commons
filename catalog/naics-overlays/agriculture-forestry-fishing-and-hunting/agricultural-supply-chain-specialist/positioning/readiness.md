# Specialist Overview  
The agricultural supply chain specialist manages coordination readiness for agricultural, forestry, fishing, and hunting operations by validating and preparing handoff and tracking artifacts. It ensures continuity by evaluating scope, dependencies, and readiness without claiming execution or regulatory authority.

## Who uses this  
- Supply continuity coordinator  
- Farm or ranch operations coordinator  
- Procurement planning lead  
- Logistics coordinator  
- Inventory analyst  
- Seasonal operations reviewer  
- Food-safety program reviewer  

## What it produces  
- Handoff-summary  
- Dependency-tracker  
- Routing-confirmation-note  
- Availability-or-logistics-status-summary  
- Shortage-or-vendor-risk-escalation-note  
- Closure-confirmation  
- Environmental-or-grant-escalation-note  
- Traceability-conflict-summary  

## Risks and mitigations  
- Explicitly refuses to execute procurement, logistics dispatch, or regulatory signoff to avoid assuming authority outside its scope.  
- Reroutes or escalates when food-safety, environmental, or licensing interpretation is required.  
- Validates minimum facts and rules before proceeding to prevent misaligned handoffs.  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Covers U.S. Food and Drug Administration and Electronic Code of Federal Regulations  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
### What the user or organization must supply  
- Validated tenant facts and system-of-record pointers  
- Authoritative public and tenant rules relevant to the request  
- Defined ownership boundaries and readiness posture  

### What is explicitly out of scope  
- Creating purchase orders, rerouting freight, or dispatching logistics  
- Altering harvest schedules or managing field/agronomic treatments  
- Granting veterinary, environmental, food-safety, legal, or financial approval authority  
- Releasing held goods or accepting substitute sourcing without documented evidence and destination owner acceptance  
- Drifting into generic supply-chain advice where field operations or legal interpretation is needed

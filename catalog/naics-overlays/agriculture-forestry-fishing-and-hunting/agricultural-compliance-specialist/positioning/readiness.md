# Specialist Overview  
The agricultural compliance specialist translates agricultural obligations and control expectations into evidence-backed compliance handling, monitoring, and escalation for crop, livestock, forestry, fisheries, and land operations. It focuses on mapping obligations to evidence, systems of record, owners, and review cadence, without claiming final legal or domain expertise.

## Who uses this  
- Agriculture-compliance-manager  
- Farm-operations-manager  
- Environmental-compliance-manager  
- Food-safety-lead  
- Field-manager  
- Auditor  
- Reviewer  

## What it produces  
- Control or obligation summary matrix  
- Evidence readiness or gap summary  
- Exception and remediation escalation note  
- Control and obligation summary tied to evidence owners  
- Exception and remediation note for control gaps or policy conflicts  
- Review or attestation preparation package  

## Risks and mitigations  
- High-stakes decisions such as legal signoff, permit issuance, or safety-critical actions are explicitly out of scope; the specialist routes these to the appropriate licensed or authority-bound specialists.  
- The specialist refuses to provide final interpretations or certify compliance with regulators, mitigating risk of misrepresentation or liability.  
- All outputs are tagged with clear in-scope/out-of-scope status and escalation paths to prevent operational or regulatory drift.  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Covers U.S. Food and Drug Administration and U.S. Environmental Protection Agency guidance via the Electronic Code of Federal Regulations  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- What the user or organization must supply before this specialist can work reliably:  
  - Access to field logs, production records, land/farm maps, livestock or aquatic production records, traceability chains, environmental reports, and grant/program evidence  
  - Context on jurisdictional requirements and applicable regulations  
  - Clarification of any missing tenant facts or governing source context  

- What is explicitly out of scope:  
  - Providing legal advice, formal regulator interpretation, or final legal/compliance certification  
  - Implying agronomic, veterinary, environmental-authority, or safety-critical signoff authority  
  - Authorizing medical/veterinary treatment, wildlife-harvest regulation decisions, permit issuance, enforcement interpretation, or grant payment approvals  
  - Bypassing required production-cycle, traceability, seasonal, environmental, or evidence boundaries due to throughput pressure

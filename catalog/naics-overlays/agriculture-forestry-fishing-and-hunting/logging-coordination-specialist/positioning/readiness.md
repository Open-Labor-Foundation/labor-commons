# Specialist Overview  
The logging coordination specialist is responsible for ensuring the flow of logging-dependent workflows across teams by managing handoffs, tracking dependencies, and documenting ownership and blocker states without assuming approval, modification, or legal authority.

## Who uses this  
- Logging coordinator  
- Forestry operations lead  
- Timber-sale administrator  
- Harvest supervisor  
- Haul-route planner  
- Forest-products program manager  
- Environmental compliance coordinator  

## What it produces  
- Handoff summary  
- Handoff status summary  
- Dependency tracker  
- Blocked dependency escalation note  
- Routing confirmation note  
- Closure confirmation  
- Seasonal or sequencing risk note  
- Environmental or food-safety escalation note  

## Risks and mitigations  
- Refuses to act on requests requiring silvicultural prescriptions, contract modification, safety certification, legal interpretation, environmental variance approval, or financial authority.  
- Does not route or resolve work blocked by regulated authority or safety-critical conditions.  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Source coverage includes Bureau of Land Management, U.S. Forest Service, Occupational Safety and Health Administration, U.S. Forest Service  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- **User or organization must supply**:  
  - Complete timber-sale records, logging plans, harvest-block maps, field logs, inspection reports, weather/fire restrictions, BMP monitoring records, scaling or load tickets, chain-of-custody evidence.  
  - Defined ownership and system-of-record access for each handoff step.  
- **Explicitly out of scope**:  
  - Approving timber-sale modifications or contract suspensions  
  - Certifying safety compliance or interpreting permits  
  - Authorizing environmental variances or silvicultural prescriptions  
  - Making financial approvals or releasing work blocked by legal or regulatory authority  
  - Executing dispatch, road opening, harvest scheduling, hauling, or mill-transfer actions  
  - Closing or marking completion without resolved ownership, artifacts, and dependencies

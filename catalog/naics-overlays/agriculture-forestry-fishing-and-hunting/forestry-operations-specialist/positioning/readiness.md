# Specialist Overview  
The Forestry Operations Specialist manages the execution, processing, and fulfillment of record-driven forestry operations, ensuring compliance with seasonal, field, land, environmental, program, and traceability dependencies. It focuses on lifecycle state management for timber stands, harvest units, forest roads, reforestation, fuels reduction, BMP monitoring, traceability, and program-file workflows, without asserting licensed or authority-based decisions.

## Who uses this  
- Forestry operations coordinator  
- Forest operations lead  
- Timber stand manager  
- Reforestation or fuels program coordinator  
- BMP monitoring analyst  
- Forest-products traceability coordinator  
- Program administrator  

## What it produces  
- Execution-status-update  
- Lifecycle-state-transition-note  
- Completion-or-fulfillment-record  
- Exception-summary  
- Production-or-field-status-summary  
- Traceability-and-environmental-risk-note  
- Adjacent-specialist-routing-note  
- Escalation-note-for-land-animal-food-safety-or-environmental-risk  

## Risks and mitigations  
- Refuses to process or approve work requiring legal, licensed, safety-critical, engineering, policy-override, financial-approval, silvicultural prescription, or environmental signoff authority outside its lane.

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Covers authoritative records from Bureau of Land Management, U.S. Forest Service, USDA Natural Resources Conservation Service, and U.S. Forest Service  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- What the user or organization must supply before this specialist can work reliably:  
  - Minimum tenant facts  
  - Systems of record  
  - Seasonal plan  
  - Stand/unit context  
  - Environmental records  
  - Owner authority  

- What is explicitly out of scope:  
  - Silvicultural prescriptions or agronomic recommendations  
  - Veterinary decisions or licensed forestry signoff  
  - Engineering design or environmental permit interpretation  
  - Legal advice or safety certification  
  - Authorization of timber-sale waivers, contract modifications, or grant awards  
  - Direct field, crew, road, logging, hauling, dispatch, purchasing, mill-transfer, or system-write actions unless operational authority is separately granted  
  - Marking work complete without full lifecycle evidence, field records, environmental/BMP records, traceability, owner acceptance, or program constraints resolved  
  - Collapsing forestry operations into generic task tracking; all outputs must reference forestry artifacts, land/stand/unit context, source systems, and boundary conditions

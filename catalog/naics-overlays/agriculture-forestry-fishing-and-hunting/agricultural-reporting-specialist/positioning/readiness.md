# Specialist Overview  
The agricultural reporting specialist assembles evidence-backed agricultural reports and disclosure packages by extracting data from farm management systems, land records, production logs, and compliance files. It ensures reporting outputs are tied to source records, documents limitations, and escalates when clarity or authority is missing.

## Who uses this  
- Farm operations reporting coordinator  
- Agricultural program analyst  
- Producer services specialist  
- Sustainability reporting coordinator  
- Food safety records coordinator  
- Environmental reporting coordinator  
- Crop insurance records coordinator  
- Operations orchestrator  

## What it produces  
- Metric definition and data-source summary with audience, cadence, source lineage, calculation logic, crop year, assumptions, caveats, and review requirements  
- Agricultural analysis or report output with field, lot, livestock, land, environmental, food-safety, program, and source-system tie-outs plus confidence notes  
- Data-quality or disclosure-risk escalation note for stale, incomplete, ambiguous, conflicting, unsupported, or non-authoritative records  
- Submission or reporting package summary with required inputs, source tie-outs, unresolved blockers, caveats, and adjacent-owner routing  
- Caveat or reconciliation note describing matched records, unresolved differences, seasonality constraints, source-precedence questions, and next-owner routing  

## Risks and mitigations  
- Will not submit, certify, or imply certainty for reports or disclosures when source quality, definitions, or signoff are unresolved  
- Will not diagnose crop or animal conditions, prescribe action, or override tenant policy or regulatory findings  
- Will flag unsupported claims, conflicting data, or incomplete inputs and escalate to relevant owners for resolution  

## Validation  
- 13 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Sources include USDA Farm Service Agency, USDA Risk Management Agency, USDA National Agricultural Statistics Service, and U.S. Food and Drug Administration  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- What the user or organization must supply before this specialist can work reliably:  
  - Validated crop year, planting and harvest dates, field and tract identifiers  
  - Access to authoritative source systems (land records, production logs, environmental data, livestock records, etc.)  
  - Defined source-precedence rules and data lineage for reporting metrics  
  - Current program or grant file status and eligibility criteria  
- What is explicitly out of scope:  
  - Submitting or certifying regulated reports without explicit tenant authorization  
  - Interpreting environmental permits, food-safety compliance, or legal determinations  
  - Diagnosing crop disease, animal health, or environmental violations  
  - Approving grant eligibility, insurance claims, or organic status  
  - Inventing or fabricating source data, records, or traceability events

# Specialist Overview  
The Agronomy support specialist organizes and resolves agronomy-related requests by linking crop, field, and production records to documented support outcomes while ensuring compliance with escalation boundaries for expert judgment and regulatory authority.  

## Who uses this  
- Farm operations support teams  
- Crop-production coordinators  
- Agronomy service desks  
- Cooperative and grower-support teams  
- Field operations supervisors  
- Program administration teams  

## What it produces  
- Resolution note with request summary, records used, action taken, source caveats, and unresolved items  
- Customer or stakeholder communication summary with evidence-backed status and next owner  
- Escalation package for unsupported asks, complaints, exceptions, repeat failures, or authority-sensitive cases  
- Production or field-status summary with crop stage, environmental, seasonal, labor, equipment, water, input, and record blockers  
- Escalation note for land, crop, animal, environmental, pesticide, food-safety, traceability, crop-insurance, grant, or program risk  

## Risks and mitigations  
- **Refusal for high-stakes decisions**: The specialist refuses to provide agronomic recommendation signoff, pesticide-label interpretation, environmental compliance determination, veterinary advice, legal advice, financial approval, grant/program eligibility approval, crop-insurance determination, or food-safety release authority.  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Source coverage includes USDA Natural Resources Conservation Service and USDA Agricultural Marketing Service  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
**What the user or organization must supply before this specialist can work reliably**  
- Valid field identifiers and crop-stage records  
- Seasonal, geographic, and weather context  
- Soil, nutrient, and irrigation data  
- Traceability, pesticide, and program records  

**What is explicitly out of scope**  
- Agronomic, veterinary, environmental, legal, crop-insurance, grant, food-safety, pesticide-label, or licensed applicator signoff  
- Crop-treatment, nutrient-rate, pesticide-use, irrigation, harvest-release, food-safety-release, livestock-health, environmental-compliance, or program-eligibility decisions  
- Overrides to tenant policy, program terms, cost-share conditions, grant rules, eligibility limits, application restrictions, preharvest intervals, reentry restrictions, or safety holds  
- Resolving conflicting records by guessing which source is authoritative  
- Continuing when critical context (season, field, crop, weather, soil, water, application, traceability, or program) is missing and would change the answer

# Specialist Overview  
The food safety agriculture specialist evaluates farm-level food-safety evidence to support bounded food-safety decisions, such as hold or release posture, corrective-action packages, or escalation notes, without crossing into regulatory, agronomic, veterinary, or legal authority.

## Who uses this  
- Farm-food-safety-manager  
- Produce-safety-lead  
- Packinghouse-quality-manager  
- Field-operations-manager  
- Livestock-operations-manager  
- Traceability-manager  
- Auditor  
- Reviewer  

## What it produces  
- Quality or safety status summary  
- Hold, release, or nonconformance record with evidence references  
- Corrective-action or incident escalation package  
- Safety or security incident status summary  
- High-risk escalation note with required approvals and evidence  

## Risks and mitigations  
- Rejects requests to bypass documented holds, release food without required evidence, or declare food safe when evidence is incomplete or contradictory.  
- Escalates when field conditions, weather, water test results, or traceability gaps materially affect food-safety posture.  
- Refuses to provide medical, veterinary treatment, pesticide-label interpretation, legal, or financial-approval authority.  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Source coverage includes 10 documents from the Electronic Code of Federal Regulations and U.S. Food and Drug Administration  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- What the user or organization must supply before this specialist can work reliably:  
  - Jurisdiction, commodity, season, site, lot, field, herd, water, environmental, traceability, or authority context  
  - Field logs, harvest records, packing and holding logs, agricultural water records, biological soil amendment records, worker hygiene and training records, equipment sanitation records, pest and animal-intrusion records, pesticide application evidence, livestock movement or withdrawal records, and traceability records  
- What is explicitly out of scope:  
  - Bypassing hold, release, harvest, traceability, sanitation, agricultural-water, soil-amendment, livestock-withdrawal, or safety-control boundaries without explicit authority  
  - Implying agronomic, veterinary, environmental, legal, organic-certification, grant-program, regulator-facing, or final QA signoff without explicit scope  
  - Providing medical, veterinary treatment, pesticide-label interpretation, engineering, environmental-permit, legal, recall-command, or financial-approval authority  
  - Declaring food safe, releasing product, closing a nonconformance, or clearing an incident when required evidence is incomplete, contradictory, stale, or outside the lane's authority  
  - Collapsing into generic safety support that ignores seasonal, field, land, livestock, water, input, harvest-lot, and traceability dependencies

# Specialist Overview
The capital markets analytics specialist translates capital markets and asset management records into trustworthy analysis with explicit metric logic, caveats, and action thresholds. It governs metric definitions, source hierarchies, and analytical rigor while ensuring alignment with capital markets semantics and regulatory expectations.

## Who uses this
- Performance analytics lead  
- Investment risk analyst  
- Client reporting manager  
- Product control or fund reporting analyst  
- Execution-quality analyst  
- Operations or orchestration lead  

## What it produces
- Metric definition and data-source summary  
- Analysis or report output with caveats and confidence notes  
- Data-quality or disclosure-risk escalation note  
- Analytical brief with metric definitions, source logic, and confidence caveats  
- Exception note for data quality, denominator ambiguity, or unsupported trend claims  

## Risks and mitigations
- Refuses to publish or imply certainty when benchmark methodology, price hierarchy, disclosure posture, or source freshness is unresolved  
- Refuses to provide investment advice, trade recommendations, or market calls  
- Refuses to invent valuations, benchmark mappings, or performance support not present in authoritative records  
- Refuses to provide legal interpretation, compliance approval, GIPS verification, or final marketing approval  

## Validation
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Covers U.S. Securities and Exchange Commission and Financial Industry Regulatory Authority requirements  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations
**User or organization must supply:**
- Minimum tenant facts, authoritative records, and source context  
- Clear definitions of benchmark methodologies, valuation hierarchies, and disclosure workflows  
- Access to systems such as OMS, EMS, IBOR, ABOR, risk, and market data  

**Explicitly out of scope:**
- Providing investment advice, trade recommendations, or portfolio rebalancing instructions  
- Inventing valuations, benchmark mappings, or performance support absent from authoritative records  
- Providing legal interpretation, compliance approval, GIPS verification, valuation or NAV signoff, or final marketing approval  
- Publishing or implying certainty when benchmark governance, price hierarchy, or source freshness is unresolved  
- Collapsing the lane into generic analytics that ignore benchmark, composite, valuation, and capital-markets workflow semantics

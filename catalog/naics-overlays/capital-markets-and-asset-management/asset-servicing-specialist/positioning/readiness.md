# Specialist Overview  
The asset servicing specialist resolves capital-markets asset-servicing requests using authoritative records and workflow-state discipline, focusing on servicing workflows after account and ownership status are established. It handles servicing tasks such as notice validation, entitlement review, tax-relief status, unresponsive payee follow-up, and blocked-case escalation without recommending, interpreting, or overriding.

## Who uses this  
- Asset servicing analysts  
- Custody operations associates  
- Income and corporate actions support teams  
- Proxy and issuer-services support coordinators  
- Tax operations liaisons  
- Operations orchestrators  

## What it produces  
- Resolution note with request summary, action taken, authoritative evidence used, and unresolved items  
- Customer or stakeholder communication summary  
- Escalation package for exceptions, complaints, unsupported asks, or repeated failures  
- Asset-servicing status summary with event type, lifecycle state, cutoff or deadline posture, blockers, and next owner  
- Outage or return-to-service escalation note  

## Risks and mitigations  
- Explicit refusal rules are in place for tasks requiring legal, tax, or financial override authority. The specialist refuses to provide tax advice, legal interpretation, or make discretionary approvals outside documented authority.  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Source coverage includes U.S. Securities and Exchange Commission and industry-specific systems for depository, custodian, and tax-relief records  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- What the user or organization must supply:  
  - Validated tenant facts and authoritative records  
  - Access to depository, custodian, and tax systems  
  - Context about the servicing event, entitlement, notice, or proxy  
- What is explicitly out of scope:  
  - Providing tax advice or treaty interpretation  
  - Offering legal interpretation of issuer or bankruptcy notices  
  - Recommending corporate-action or proxy-voting elections  
  - Overriding payment release, entitlements, or withholding controls  
  - Claiming completion when records are missing or contradictory  
  - Stating finality when authoritative records conflict

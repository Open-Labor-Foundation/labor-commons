# Specialist Overview  
The middle office specialist validates whether post-trade middle-office work can proceed by ensuring all required prerequisites—such as trade-capture, account setup, mandate, counterparty, standing settlement instruction, and affirmation readiness—are complete and traceable. It operates within the capital-markets-and-asset-management industry overlay using domain-specific terminology and workflows to produce readiness decisions and action packages for downstream processing.

## Who uses this  
- Middle office analysts  
- Investment operations managers  
- Post-trade support teams  
- Institutional trade matching teams  
- Custody and settlements coordinators  
- Prime brokerage onboarding teams  
- Workflow orchestrators  

## What it produces  
- Execution status update with lifecycle stage and next action  
- Completion or fulfillment record showing what was done and supporting evidence  
- Exception summary for blocked, returned, or partially completed work  
- Intake or access disposition summary  
- Prerequisite or eligibility escalation note  

## Risks and mitigations  
- Refuses to approve account opening, KYC completion, legal documentation exceptions, or settlement release outside delegated authority.  
- Refuses to interpret securities law, client contracts, or jurisdiction-specific regulation as final legal advice.  
- Rejects inputs that lack authoritative post-trade, account, or SSI system evidence.  
- Avoids using generic CRM notes, spreadsheets, or local trackers as substitutes for required data sources.  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Source coverage includes U.S. Securities and Exchange Commission and Depository Trust & Clearing Corporation authority sources  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- Requires access to authoritative records for trade, account, mandate, counterparty, and SSI data before reliable work can begin  
- Requires clear evidence of KYC, legal documentation, compliance, and settlement readiness where ownership or outcomes change  
- Does not own investment decisions, trading discretion, or portfolio-construction changes  
- Does not own final KYC or onboarding approval, legal documentation negotiation, or final compliance signoff  
- Does not own settlement release authority, cash-movement approval, corporate-action elections, or derivatives collateral and margin decisions  
- Does not claim closure when authoritative matching, SSI, account, or approval evidence is missing or contradictory

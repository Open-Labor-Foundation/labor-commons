# Specialist Overview  
The fund accounting specialist validates and transforms fund accounting records—such as shareholder activity, price files, custody balances, and expense accruals—into traceable fund-accounting outputs like NAV-support packages, reconciliation notes, and exception escalations. The specialist operates within the capital markets and asset management industry, focusing on mutual funds, ETFs, and pooled investment products without assuming roles in tax, legal, valuation, or final NAV approval.

## Who uses this  
- Fund-accounting-analyst  
- Fund-administration-operations-associate  
- Mutual-fund-accountant  
- Nav-oversight-analyst  
- Reconciliation-and-control-analyst  
- Operations-orchestrator  

## What it produces  
- Billing, claim, invoice, or posting summary with source traceability  
- Reconciliation note showing matched items, exceptions, and unresolved variances  
- Financial exception escalation for denials, disputes, write-offs, or unsupported adjustments  
- Billing or financial reconciliation summary with root-cause categories  
- Revenue exception escalation note  
- Fund-accounting readiness summary with NAV, accrual, cash, cap-stock, and price support references  

## Risks and mitigations  
- The specialist refuses to approve or finalize actions requiring legal, tax, valuation, or final NAV authority, ensuring escalation to the correct approver when necessary.  
- Unsupported fair-value determinations, NAV strikes, or accounting policy changes are explicitly denied and escalated.  
- No fund structures, such as ETFs or private funds, are treated interchangeably without tenant-specific context.  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Source coverage includes SEC-regulated fund accounting artifacts such as MFPS I price files, DTCC Fund/SERV order flows, and custody reconciliation records  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- What the user or organization must supply before this specialist can work reliably:  
  - Minimum tenant facts including fund vehicle, share-class, pricing, custody, and transfer-agent context  
  - Authoritative source records such as price files, custody balances, and fee schedules  
  - Explicit approval for fee waivers, write-offs, or back-dated shareholder activity changes  
- What is explicitly out of scope:  
  - Legal advice, final tax characterization, or regulator-facing interpretation  
  - Final valuation policy, fair-value determinations, or board-level pricing-control decisions  
  - Approval of NAV strikes, fee waivers, write-offs, or unsupported journal entries  
  - Treating materially different fund structures (e.g., private vs. registered funds) as interchangeable  
  - Acting as the fund controller, valuation designee, or final approver of fund accounting entries

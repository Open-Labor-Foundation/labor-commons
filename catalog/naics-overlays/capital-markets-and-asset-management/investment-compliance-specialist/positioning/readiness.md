# Specialist Overview  
The investment compliance specialist translates investment compliance obligations into evidence-backed control mapping, monitoring, review preparation, and escalation outputs specific to capital markets and asset management. The specialist works within the boundaries of investment mandates, regulatory filings, supervisory records, and compliance evidence without substituting for legal interpretation, portfolio management, or regulatory negotiation.

## Who uses this  
- Investment compliance officers  
- Adviser compliance operations teams  
- Broker-dealer supervisory and distribution teams  
- Portfolio compliance analysts  
- Marketing review and due-diligence teams  
- Governance, risk, and control owners  
- Workflow orchestrators  

## What it produces  
- Control or obligation matrix tied to the lane  
- Evidence readiness or gap summary  
- Exception and remediation escalation note  
- Control and obligation summary tied to evidence owners  
- Review or attestation preparation packet  
- Boundary-safe refusal or adjacent-lane routing note  

## Risks and mitigations  
- Refuses actions requiring legal, licensed, policy-override, financial-approval, or safety-critical authority  
- Routes matters that require legal or regulatory interpretation to the appropriate specialist  
- Requires authoritative records and source context before proceeding  
- Limits outputs to what is supported by real investment records and supervisory evidence  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Covers U.S. Securities and Exchange Commission and Electronic Code of Federal Regulations  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
**What the user or organization must supply before this specialist can work reliably:**  
- Investment management agreements  
- Investment policy statements or guideline schedules  
- Fund prospectus and SAI language  
- Form ADV disclosures  
- Restricted lists  
- Personal trading attestations  
- Pre-trade and post-trade exception reports  
- Marketing review files  
- Composite support  
- Supervisory testing artifacts  
- Annual review evidence  

**What is explicitly out of scope:**  
- Legal interpretation  
- Regulator-facing negotiation strategy  
- Formal legal signoff  
- Approval of portfolio trades or client-guideline overrides  
- Clearance of insider-trading, market-manipulation, AML, or suspicious-activity matters  
- Certification of full compliance without required formal owner  
- Treatment of industry-specific context as generic or cosmetic

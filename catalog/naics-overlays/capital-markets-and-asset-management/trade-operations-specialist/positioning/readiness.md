# Specialist Overview  
The trade operations specialist focuses on managing and controlling post-trade processes in the capital-markets-and-asset-management industry. It evaluates and assures the quality of trade allocations, affirmations, settlement instructions, and exception handling, ensuring readiness or identifying the need for escalation without overstepping into portfolio or legal authority.

## Who uses this  
- Trade operations analyst  
- Investment operations manager  
- Middle-office control lead  
- Post-trade exception reviewer  
- Custody or prime brokerage operations reviewer  
- Capital-markets orchestrator  

## What it produces  
- Quality or safety status summary for allocation, affirmation, SSI, and settlement-readiness posture  
- Hold, release, or nonconformance record with evidence references  
- Corrective-action or incident escalation package  
- Project or field-controls summary with trade-date timeline, settlement deadline, counterparty dependencies, and approval status  
- Escalation note for SSI, matching, cut-off, counterparty, or jurisdiction-readiness blockers  

## Risks and mitigations  
- Explicitly refuses to approve cash movement, final settlement release, restricted-list exceptions, policy overrides, or market-facing commitments without explicit authority.  
- Refuses to provide legal interpretation, final compliance signoff, or licensed supervisory approval.  
- Rejects actions requiring investment advice, best-execution judgments, or portfolio-allocation instructions.  
- Avoids generic trade administration by requiring named capital-markets records and systems.

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Covers U.S. Securities and Exchange Commission and Financial Industry Regulatory Authority authority sources  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- What the user or organization must supply:  
  - Minimum tenant facts  
  - Authoritative records and source context  
  - Named capital-markets terminology and systems  
- What is explicitly out of scope:  
  - Investment advice, best-execution judgments, or portfolio-allocation instructions  
  - Legal interpretation, final compliance signoff, or licensed supervisory approval  
  - Unilateral settlement release, cash movement approval, or security master changes  
  - Product-specific workflows such as OTC derivatives lifecycle processing, collateral, or corporate actions  
  - Generic trade administration without industry-specific capital-markets records

# Specialist Overview  
The program reporting specialist elder services focuses on assembling, validating, and escalating structured reporting and disclosure outputs for programs in elder care and disability services. It ensures reporting aligns with regulatory and operational requirements without assuming clinical, legal, or policy authority.

## Who uses this  
- Compliance coordinator  
- Quality coordinator  
- Program manager  
- Care-services director  
- Case manager  
- Clinical operations lead  

## What it produces  
- Service or support disposition note with decision surface, scope, and unresolved items  
- Customer or stakeholder communication summary in plain language and artifact terms  
- Submission/reporting package summary with evidence mapping and source tie-outs  
- Escalation package for exceptions, complaint pathways, policy conflicts, repeat failures, and missing prerequisites  
- Disclosure-risk escalation note identifying minimum-necessary and tenant policy boundaries  
- Resolution confirmation with dependency list and next-owner recommendation  

## Risks and mitigations  
- Explicit refusal to provide clinical determinations, legal opinions, or regulator forecasts  
- Mitigates risk by escalating authority gaps and avoiding improvisation in safety-sensitive or legally sensitive reporting contexts  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Regulatory and statutory source coverage includes Centers for Medicare & Medicaid Services and Legal Information Institute, Cornell  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
**What the user or organization must supply before this specialist can work reliably:**  
- Program type and reporting authority  
- Reporting period and required source systems  
- Access to evidence and artifact repositories  
- Defined tenant policy boundaries for disclosure  

**What is explicitly out of scope:**  
- Clinical eligibility, medical necessity, diagnosis, or treatment judgment  
- Legal advice, official Medicaid/Federal interpretation, or final policy exception decisions  
- Regulator approval, certification, or guaranteed compliance outcomes  
- Replacing tenant governance, consent, or privacy policy with generic assumptions  
- Inventing missing mandatory fields or reporting against unknown local templates

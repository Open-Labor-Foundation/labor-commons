# Specialist Overview  
The Disability Services Coordination Specialist manages the coordination, dependency handoff, and routing of care and support obligations in elder care and disability services. It ensures that transitions, dispatches, and support workflows move safely between owners by preparing handoff summaries, tracking dependencies, and confirming closure without assuming clinical, legal, or financial execution roles.

## Who uses this  
- Care-coordinator  
- Case-manager  
- Disability-services-manager  
- Transitions-coordinator  
- Nursing-services-manager  
- Operations-manager  
- Reviewer  

## What it produces  
- Handoff summary with prior owner, next owner, required artifacts, and closure condition  
- Dependency tracker and blocker log with evidence gaps and escalation paths  
- Routing confirmation with ownership, timeline, and confidence/risk flags  
- Boundary-safe refusal and specialist handoff note  
- Closed-loop transfer confirmation when work is accepted, stalled, or escalated  

## Risks and mitigations  
- If a request involves clinical, legal, safety, financial, or policy-interpretation decisions, the specialist refuses and routes to the appropriate adjacent specialist.  

## Validation  
- 10 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Covers core disability and aging services operations including home-and-community-based services and transitions of care  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- Must be provided with accurate intake information, including tenant status, service plan details, and consent posture  
- Must be provided with current regulatory and policy guidance for local and state rules  
- Must be provided with access to relevant evidence and artifact repositories  

- Clinical care, diagnoses, prescriptions, or treatment authorization  
- Legal approvals, financial adjudication, or policy-overrides  
- Execution of care plans or operations tasks  
- Direct service delivery or facility safety oversight  
- Final authority for staffing, licensing, or financial release

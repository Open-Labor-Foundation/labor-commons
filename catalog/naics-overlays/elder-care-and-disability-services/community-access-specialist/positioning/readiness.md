# Specialist Overview  
The Community Access Specialist focuses on validating and routing community access requests in Elder Care and Disability Services, ensuring that all prerequisites for intake are met before downstream work begins. It checks identity, residency, referral, authorization, reservation, and policy requirements and issues a disposition of ACCEPT, REJECT, PEND, or ROUTE.

## Who uses this  
- Community-access coordinator  
- Care navigator  
- Intake triage manager  
- Intake-and-eligibility team  
- Disability services routing lead  

## What it produces  
- Intake disposition note with ACCEPT / REJECT / PEND / ROUTE outcome and rationale  
- Eligibility and prerequisite checklist with satisfied vs missing conditions and evidence gaps  
- Escalation packet for missing authorization, missing eligibility, identity mismatch, consent conflict, or jurisdiction gap  
- Intake/access disposition summary including queue state and owner mapping  
- Routing artifact for specialist handoff when adjacent or higher-authority intervention is required  

## Risks and mitigations  
- Explicitly refuses to process requests requiring clinical, legal, financial, or safety-critical decisions  
- Defers to adjacent specialists for handoff when authority is beyond community access scope  
- Mitigates misrouting by using standardized routing artifacts and policy context validation  

## Validation  
- 10 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Source coverage includes 6 references from Centers for Medicare & Medicaid Services  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- Requires complete and accurate input data to operate reliably, including identity, residency, referral, and authorization records  
- Cannot issue clinical decisions, legal adjudications, policy exceptions, financial approvals, or safety-critical interventions  
- Cannot approve final entitlement outcomes; only validates readiness for downstream decision  
- Cannot override tenant-specific policy context with generic assumptions

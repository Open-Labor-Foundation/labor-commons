# Specialist Overview  
The financial platform support specialist handles platform-specific support and resolution for fintech and embedded-finance systems, focusing on workflows tied to sponsor-banks, payment rails, access control, and integration dependencies. It ensures support is grounded in authoritative records and operates within strict boundaries of authority and responsibility.

## Who uses this  
- Fintech platform support teams  
- Embedded-finance operations leads  
- Program and vendor management teams  
- Bank-partnership support leads  
- Incident coordinators and service managers  
- Operations reviewers  

## What it produces  
- Resolution note with request summary, action taken, authoritative evidence used, and unresolved items  
- Customer or stakeholder communication summary  
- Escalation package for exceptions, complaints, unsupported asks, or repeated failures  
- System incident or service summary with impact, dependency state, and owner  
- Change-readiness or integration-risk escalation note  

## Risks and mitigations  
- Refuses or escalates work requiring production-access approval, fraud judgment, legal interpretation, or fund-movement authority  
- Mitigates risk by grounding responses in authoritative records and refusing unsupported authority claims  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Covers core fintech platform workflows including payment rails, access control, and integration dependencies  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- What the user or organization must supply before this specialist can work reliably:  
  - Minimum tenant facts and authoritative records  
  - Contextual information tied to named fintech systems of record  
  - Evidence that prerequisites for access or resolution are met  
- What is explicitly out of scope:  
  - Approving production access, privileged access, or segregation-of-duties exceptions  
  - Approving releases, suppressing alerts, or certifying engineering risk  
  - Providing legal or compliance interpretation, fraud or AML adjudication  
  - Authorizing financial-impact changes, fund movement, or policy overrides  
  - Making final statements about settlement, reimbursement, or regulatory impact without authoritative evidence

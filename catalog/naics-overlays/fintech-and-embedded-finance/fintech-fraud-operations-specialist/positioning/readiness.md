# Specialist Overview  
The fintech fraud operations specialist executes and manages fraud operations workflows for fintech and embedded finance cases, ensuring fraud incidents are handled with evidence-backed decisions, lifecycle tracking, and clear boundaries for customer impact and regulatory compliance.  

## Who uses this  
- Fraud operations analysts  
- Payments and disputes operations teams  
- Embedded finance program managers  
- Banking operations and risk teams  
- Operations orchestrators  

## What it produces  
- Execution status update with lifecycle stage and next action  
- Completion or fulfillment record showing what was done and what evidence supports closure  
- Exception summary for blocked, returned, or partially completed work  
- Servicing or product-workflow summary with exceptions, approvals, and next owner  
- Escalation note for fraud, AML, consumer-impact, or policy-sensitive issues  

## Risks and mitigations  
- Refuses actions requiring legal, licensed, policy-override, financial-approval, or safety-critical authority  
- Requires traceable case disposition and customer-impact handling before marking work complete  
- Validates tenant policy and regulatory boundaries before proceeding  
- Does not fabricate liability decisions, SAR conclusions, or account restrictions  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Covers payment, account, identity, and fraud-operations workflows in fintech and embedded finance  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
**What the user or organization must supply before this specialist can work reliably:**  
- Minimum tenant facts and authoritative records  
- Source transaction and customer context  
- Evidence bundle and case packet  
- Tenant routing prerequisites  

**What is explicitly out of scope:**  
- Legal advice, regulatory interpretation, or final consumer-liability determinations outside documented tenant policy  
- SAR filing decisions, AML adjudication, law-enforcement coordination, or final sanctions disposition  
- Reimbursement approvals, charge-off decisions, account closure authority, underwriting, or product-policy overrides

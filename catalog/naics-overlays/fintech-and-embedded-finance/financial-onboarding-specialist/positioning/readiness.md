# Specialist Overview  
The financial onboarding specialist validates whether a customer, business, or partner is ready to move from intake into controlled fintech service by checking that identity, eligibility, authorization, setup, access, and sponsor-program dependencies are complete and traceable. It ensures that onboarding decisions are based on evidence and that actions are taken only within defined authority boundaries.  

## Who uses this  
- Fintech onboarding operations managers  
- Embedded finance implementation coordinators  
- Sponsor-bank program operations teams  
- Merchant or partner enablement analysts  
- Risk and access onboarding reviewers  

## What it produces  
- Onboarding checklist with completion status by prerequisite  
- Setup or enablement status summary  
- Readiness summary showing blockers, risks, and pending approvals  
- Go-live handoff note describing what is live, what is deferred, and who owns next steps  
- Intake or access disposition summary  
- Prerequisite or eligibility escalation note  

## Risks and mitigations  
- The specialist refuses to grant go-live status when prerequisite evidence is incomplete, contradictory, or still pending.  
- It avoids interpreting legal or compliance rules and routes decisions to the appropriate owners when ownership shifts.  
- It ensures that onboarding completion is not mistaken for downstream servicing or compliance fulfillment.  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Covers fintech and embedded finance onboarding, with a focus on sponsor-bank or program approvals, KYB/KYC results, beneficial ownership, sanctions screening, and setup status.  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- What the user or organization must supply before this specialist can work reliably:  
  - Accurate and up-to-date KYB/KYC and sanctions-screening results  
  - Evidence of sponsor-bank or program approvals  
  - Setup and access records (e.g., settlement account, IAM, MFA status)  
  - Training and agreement acceptance records  

- What is explicitly out of scope:  
  - Interpreting BSA/AML, sanctions, licensing, or consumer-protection law as a legal decision  
  - Waiving KYC, KYB, beneficial ownership, screening, training, access, processor, or sponsor-bank approval requirements without delegated authority  
  - Granting production or customer-facing go-live status when prerequisite evidence is incomplete or pending  
  - Treating onboarding completion as proof that downstream obligations are satisfied  
  - Granting financial, credit, or fraud adjudication authority

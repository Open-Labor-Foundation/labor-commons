# Specialist Overview  
The digital wallet operations specialist ensures the safe and reliable execution of digital wallet operations in the fintech and embedded finance industry by validating evidence, managing incident and dependency context, and preparing traceable recommendations for progression, rework, escalation, or completion.

## Who uses this  
- Wallet operations analysts  
- Embedded finance program managers  
- Payments platform operations teams  
- Issuer processor and sponsor-bank operations teams  
- Incident and change coordinators  
- Operations orchestrators  

## What it produces  
- Execution status update with lifecycle stage and next action  
- Completion or fulfillment record showing what was done and what evidence supports closure  
- Exception summary for blocked, returned, or partially completed work  
- System incident or service summary with impact and owner  
- Change-readiness or integration-risk escalation note  

## Risks and mitigations  
- Refuses to act on legal, licensed, policy-override, financial-approval, or safety-critical authority without routing to the correct adjacent specialist.  
- Requires traceable wallet-operation closure evidence, rollback or test evidence, and owner context before marking work complete.  
- Avoids treating industry terminology as cosmetic; all outputs must reference actual wallet artifacts, token lifecycle records, and payment-system dependencies.  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Covers digital wallet records, token lifecycle, issuer or processor acknowledgements, wallet-ledger state, device binding, and customer-impact logs  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- What the user or organization must supply before this specialist can work reliably  
  - Minimum tenant facts and authoritative records  
  - Wallet-specific artifacts such as platform events, token lifecycle records, and interface dependencies  
  - Evidence of customer impact, device enrollment, and network or sponsor-bank constraints  
- What is explicitly out of scope  
  - Legal interpretation of Regulation E, prepaid, privacy, or other consumer protection obligations  
  - Approval of privileged access, production releases, security exceptions, PCI attestations, or sponsor-bank or network policy overrides  
  - Fraud, AML, disputes, reimbursement, consumer-liability, or account servicing determinations  
  - Engineering implementation, token-program design, or direct execution of production fixes outside documented operational runbooks

# Specialist Overview  
The fintech operations specialist operates within the fintech and embedded finance industry, handling day-to-day servicing, money movement, and fulfillment workflows. It ensures all actions are grounded in authoritative records, approval logs, and audit-ready evidence to support safe and compliant progression through operational states.

## Who uses this  
- Fintech operations analysts  
- Embedded finance program operations teams  
- Payments and servicing operations leads  
- Sponsor-bank operations coordinators  
- Program managers and queue owners  
- Operations orchestrators  

## What it produces  
- Execution status update with lifecycle stage and next action  
- Completion or fulfillment record showing what was done and what evidence supports closure  
- Exception summary for blocked, returned, or partially completed work  
- Servicing or product-workflow summary with exceptions, approvals, and next owner  
- Escalation note for risk, consumer-impact, or policy-sensitive issues  

## Risks and mitigations  
- Refuses to act on tasks requiring legal interpretation, licensing, or regulated-product determinations  
- Refuses to approve funds release, refunds, credits, or policy overrides without documented authority  
- Refuses to proceed without traceable evidence of system-of-record state changes and required approvals  
- Avoids treating fintech workflows as generic product operations by anchoring all actions in actual program, processor, and regulatory records  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Covers core fintech operations scenarios including wallet servicing, ACH processing, card fulfillment, and exception handling  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
**What the user or organization must supply before this specialist can work reliably:**  
- Identified governing product, sponsor-bank, licensed-entity model, and processor relationship  
- Access to authoritative systems of record (internal ledger, processor events, wallet or FBO-account states)  
- Clear jurisdiction and approval-owner mapping  
- Evidence of required approvals, notices, and execution state  

**What is explicitly out of scope:**  
- Legal interpretation, licensing conclusions, or formal compliance determinations  
- Reg E adjudication, complaint adjudication, fraud or AML disposition, or sanctions decisions  
- Approval authority for fund release, fee waivers, credits, account restrictions, policy overrides, or sponsor-bank exceptions  
- Product pricing, underwriting, engineering changes, or executive approval  
- Fabrication of processor, ledger, sponsor-bank, or customer-notice evidence  
- Approving or authorizing any financial transaction or policy override without documented owner  
- Marking work complete without traceable state change evidence, required approvals, and required customer-impact handling records

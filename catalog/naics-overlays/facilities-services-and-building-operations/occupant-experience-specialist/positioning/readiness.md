# Specialist Overview  
The occupant experience specialist manages occupant-facing facilities and workplace service requests within the building operations industry, focusing on communication, classification, and safe handoff of issues without claiming technical or regulatory authority.  

## Who uses this  
- Occupant experience coordinators  
- Workplace operations teams  
- Facilities service managers  
- Building operations leads  
- Property or site services teams  
- Reviewers  

## What it produces  
- Resolution note with request summary, action taken, evidence used, and unresolved items  
- Customer or stakeholder communication summary  
- Escalation package for exceptions, complaints, repeat failures, or unsupported asks  
- Facilities or workplace service summary with occupancy, service, and blocker status  
- Escalation note for supply, comfort, or operational-impact exceptions  

## Risks and mitigations  
- The specialist refuses to provide permit approval, code interpretation, or licensed trade signoff, ensuring no false authority is implied.  
- Requests are escalated when resolution requires authority outside the lane, preventing unsafe or incomplete action.  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Source coverage includes Occupational Safety and Health Administration, U.S. Environmental Protection Agency, and U.S. General Services Administration  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
**What the user or organization must supply before this specialist can work reliably**  
- Validated work orders, permits, inspection records, site logs, safety observations, and asset evidence  
- Clear service and policy rules to apply without improvisation  
- Defined escalation paths to adjacent specialists  

**What is explicitly out of scope**  
- Approving occupancy, interpreting code, or implying permit or inspection signoff  
- Directing technical repair methods, design fixes, or licensed trade work  
- Approving policy exceptions, lease concessions, pricing exceptions, or financial credits  
- Closing cases with incomplete or conflicting site, permit, inspection, or closeout evidence  
- Authorizing financial credits, contract changes, or exception approvals  
- Claiming resolution when required evidence is missing or contradictory

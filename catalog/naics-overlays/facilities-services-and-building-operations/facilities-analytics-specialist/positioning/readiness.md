# Specialist Overview  
The facilities analytics specialist translates facilities operations and building operations records into trustworthy analysis with explicit metric logic, caveats, and action thresholds. This role ensures analytical outputs preserve facilities-specific semantics and avoid overstepping into regulatory, safety, or licensed-trade authority.

## Who uses this  
- Facilities operations reporting leads  
- Facility managers  
- Chief engineers  
- Integrated facilities management leads  
- Asset managers  
- Energy and sustainability analysts  
- Property operations leaders  
- Closeout and turnover coordinators  

## What it produces  
- Metric definition and data-source summary  
- Analysis or report output with caveats and confidence notes  
- Data-quality or disclosure-risk escalation note  
- Analytical brief with metric definitions, source logic, and confidence caveats  
- Exception note for data quality, denominator ambiguity, unsupported trend claims, or cross-system conflicts  
- Adjacent-specialist routing note  

## Risks and mitigations  
- Refuses unsupported permit, code, causal, benchmark, or safety claims that exceed available evidence  
- Routes to licensed-trade or safety specialists when regulatory or safety authority is needed  
- Escalates when external disclosure, final closeout, or policy claims require human review  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Covers U.S. Environmental Protection Agency and U.S. General Services Administration sources  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- Requires minimum tenant facts, authoritative records, and source context to proceed safely  
- Cannot imply permit approval, final inspection acceptance, code interpretation, licensed trade signoff, or unsupported causal or benchmark claims  
- Cannot approve change orders, budgets, dispatch decisions, outage plans, or final owner turnover  
- Cannot produce generic rolled-up dashboards that ignore work-order class, permit, inspection, subcontractor, site-safety, or closeout dependencies

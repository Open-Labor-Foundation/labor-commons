# Specialist Overview  
The Facilities Compliance Specialist is a specialist focused on translating facilities-services and building-operations compliance obligations into evidence-backed control handling, monitoring, gap analysis, and escalation packages. It ensures that work orders, permits, inspection records, and other operational documents contain sufficient authoritative evidence to support compliance readiness or identify missing or conflicting evidence without overstepping into legal, regulatory, or licensed-trade authority.

## Who uses this  
- Facilities compliance manager  
- Facilities operations manager  
- Chief engineer  
- Property manager  
- Project manager  
- Maintenance supervisor  
- Vendor manager  
- Reviewer  

## What it produces  
- Control or obligation matrix tied to authoritative sources and evidence owners  
- Evidence readiness or gap summary  
- Exception and remediation escalation note  
- Control and obligation summary tied to evidence owners and review cadence  
- Site or work-order blocker note with prerequisite checklist  
- Adjacent-lane routing note for permitting, inspection, safety, superintendent, legal, or licensed-trade owners  
- Evidence packet or checklist for review or attestation preparation  

## Risks and mitigations  
- **Risk**: Misinterpretation of code or regulatory authority may lead to unsafe or noncompliant action.  
  **Mitigation**: The specialist refuses to interpret code or provide legal/regulatory signoff and escalates such requests to the appropriate owner.  
- **Risk**: Insufficient or outdated evidence may lead to false compliance confidence.  
  **Mitigation**: The specialist requires validation of minimum tenant facts and record systems before proceeding.  
- **Risk**: Escalation to the wrong lane may delay compliance action.  
  **Mitigation**: The specialist maps obligations to correct owners using jurisdictional and tenant-specific authority rules.  

## Validation  
- 13 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Source coverage includes OSHA and internal facilities compliance systems  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- **User or organization must supply**:  
  - Minimum tenant facts and jurisdictional context  
  - Systems-of-record hooks for work orders, permits, and inspection records  
  - Public-source baseline for code and permit references  
- **Explicitly out of scope**:  
  - Providing legal interpretation or formal signoff  
  - Approving permits, inspections, or regulator-facing submissions  
  - Offering code interpretation or licensed trade judgment  
  - Directing work continuation when safety, impairment, or permit conditions are incomplete  
  - Merging facilities compliance into generic enterprise compliance frameworks that ignore site-specific evidence and conditions

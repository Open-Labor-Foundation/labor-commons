# Specialist Overview  
The space planning support specialist translates workplace demand, occupancy data, and facility constraints into evidence-based allocation, prioritization, and phasing recommendations for space work. It ensures recommendations are bounded by available information and escalate issues requiring approval or specialist input.

## Who uses this  
- Facilities planner  
- Workplace services lead  
- Building operations manager  
- Space management analyst  
- Move-add-change coordinator  
- Project coordinator  
- Facilities portfolio lead  

## What it produces  
- Schedule-or-allocation-recommendation  
- Constraint-log  
- Approval-needed-summary  
- Allocation-or-prioritization-recommendation  
- Capacity-risk-escalation-note  
- Space-readiness-summary  
- Adjacent-specialist-routing-note  

## Risks and mitigations  
- Requests for final space assignment, permit signoff, or licensed trade judgment are explicitly refused to avoid misrepresentation of authority.  
- Recommendations are framed as non-binding to prevent operational or legal overreach.  
- Escalations are routed to appropriate specialists for final decision-making.  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Source coverage includes U.S. General Services Administration standards  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
**User or organization must supply:**  
- Valid occupancy data and headcount assumptions  
- Floor plans, stacking diagrams, and adjacency requirements  
- Work orders, permit status, and inspection records  
- Site-readiness evidence and closeout documentation  

**Explicitly out of scope:**  
- Final space assignment approval  
- Permit approval or code interpretation  
- Architect or engineer judgment  
- Licensed trade signoff  
- Lease execution or budget approval  
- Safety-critical execution or operational work order control

# Workplace services specialist  
Owns workplace-service workflows inside facilities services and building operations, including intake, coordination, readiness review, communication, and escalation packaging for occupied-space service work. Operates within a narrow lane that focuses on service classification, resolution using authoritative records, and stakeholder communication, without claiming authority over code, design, financial, or licensed-trade decisions.

## Who uses this  
- Workplace services coordinators  
- Facilities operations teams  
- Office services leads  
- Site services managers  
- Building operations leads  
- Occupier services teams  
- Reviewers  

## What it produces  
- Resolution note with request summary, action taken, evidence used, and unresolved items  
- Customer or stakeholder communication summary  
- Escalation package for exceptions, complaints, repeat failures, or unsupported asks  
- Facilities or workplace service summary with occupancy, service, and blocker status  
- Escalation note for supply, comfort, or operational-impact exceptions  

## Risks and mitigations  
- Explicit refusal rules prevent the specialist from approving occupancy, interpreting code, directing technical repair methods, or authorizing financial credits, which reduces risk in high-stakes scenarios like safety-critical operations and legal determinations.  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Source coverage includes U.S. General Services Administration and Occupational Safety and Health Administration  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- **What the user or organization must supply:** Minimum tenant facts, authoritative records, and source context to proceed safely  
- **What is explicitly out of scope:**  
  - Approving occupancy, permits, or implying inspection signoff  
  - Directing technical repair methods, design fixes, or licensed trade work  
  - Authorizing policy exceptions, after-hours exceptions, pricing exceptions, lease concessions, or financial credits  
  - Closing cases when site, permit, inspection, safety, or closeout evidence is incomplete or conflicting  
  - Overriding policy, pricing, eligibility, or operating-hour limits to resolve a case

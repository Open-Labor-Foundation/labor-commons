# Specialist Overview  
The janitorial operations specialist manages the day-to-day execution of janitorial and workplace services within facilities, using work orders, service-level commitments, and quality-assurance data to determine the status and readiness of cleaning tasks. It ensures janitorial workflows are carried out safely, in compliance with protocols, and with clear documentation for downstream verification.

## Who uses this  
- Janitorial supervisor  
- Facilities manager  
- Site services coordinator  
- Workplace operations lead  
- Vendor manager  
- Quality assurance inspector  
- Reviewer  

## What it produces  
- Execution-status-update  
- Completion-evidence-record  
- Exception-queue-note  
- Facilities-or-workplace-service-summary  
- Escalation-note  
- Adjacent-specialist-routing-note  

## Risks and mitigations  
- Refuses to approve janitorial work when chemical controls, area access, or safety requirements are unmet.  
- Rejects or routes work requiring licensed, safety-critical, or code-interpretation authority.  
- Does not permit overrides to wet-floor, exposure, or restricted-area conditions for the sake of schedule or comfort metrics.  
- Requires full traceability from request to disposition to prevent misattribution or loss of accountability.  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Source coverage includes U.S. General Services Administration and Occupational Safety and Health Administration guidelines  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- Requires valid work orders, service plans, and inspection evidence to function  
- Requires up-to-date supply readiness and area access status  
- Requires documented chemical controls and tenant-impact assessments  
- Does not approve permits, interpret code, or direct licensed trade work  
- Does not authorize staffing, spend, or contract changes  
- Does not perform hazardous-material or mold-remediation judgment  
- Does not override safety, access, or exposure-control blockers

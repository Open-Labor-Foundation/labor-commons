# Specialist Overview  
The building engineering coordination specialist manages the flow of building engineering dependencies across teams in Facilities Services and Building Operations, ensuring clear ownership, evidence-backed handoffs, and resolution of blockers for work orders, permits, inspections, and closeout artifacts. It coordinates but does not execute, interpret, or approve licensed trade, engineering, or safety decisions.

## Who uses this  
- Facilities project coordinator  
- Building engineering coordinator  
- Chief engineer  
- Facilities operations manager  
- Project engineer  
- Superintendent  
- Closeout coordinator  

## What it produces  
- Handoff summary naming prior owner, next owner, required artifacts, and blocker state  
- Dependency tracker or blocker summary tied to authoritative records  
- Routing confirmation note with destination owner and prerequisite checklist  
- Blocked-dependency escalation note for stale or duplicated ownership  
- Closure confirmation showing work landed with the right destination owner  
- Boundary-safe refusal and adjacent-specialist reroute note  

## Risks and mitigations  
- Mitigates risk of unapproved or incomplete work by refusing to issue permit approval, inspection signoff, code interpretation, or licensed trade judgment.  
- Mitigates risk of workflow stalling by escalating conflicting policies or unresolved ownership before work is dropped.  
- Mitigates risk of misassignment by requiring explicit evidence of prerequisites before routing or closing out work.  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Covers 9 authority sources including OSHA, NYC Department of Buildings, and U.S. GSA  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- What the user or organization must supply before this specialist can work reliably:  
  - Minimum tenant facts  
  - Systems-of-record hooks  
  - Authoritative public source context  
- What is explicitly out of scope:  
  - Permit approval or inspection signoff  
  - Code or design interpretation  
  - Licensed trade judgment or engineer-of-record direction  
  - Controls-programming approval or TAB signoff  
  - Safety-critical release or field execution  
  - Change-order or financial approvals  
  - Direct dispatch, crew assignment, troubleshooting, or corrective field work

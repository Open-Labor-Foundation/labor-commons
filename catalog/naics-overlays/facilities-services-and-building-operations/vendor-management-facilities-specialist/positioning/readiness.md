# Specialist Overview  
The vendor management facilities specialist coordinates and tracks vendor commitments and supply continuity for building and facilities maintenance, ensuring work aligns with permits, inspections, safety conditions, and field evidence without overstepping approval, engineering, or licensing boundaries.  

## Who uses this  
- Facilities operations coordinator  
- Maintenance planner  
- Vendor coordinator  
- CMMS administrator  
- Building operations manager  
- Service-delivery analyst  
- Regional facilities lead  

## What it produces  
- Availability-or-supply-status-summary  
- Procurement-or-replenishment-recommendation  
- Vendor-or-procurement-action-note  
- Shortage-or-vendor-risk-escalation-note  
- Maintenance-or-field-status-summary  
- Safety-outage-or-return-to-service-escalation-note  

## Risks and mitigations  
- Refuses to approve spend, override vendor controls, authorize substitutions affecting code, or approve permits, inspections, or return-to-service when such actions require licensed-trade, engineering, or code authority.  

## Validation  
- 13 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Covers OSHA, OFPP/GSA, and Seattle DCI regulatory sources  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- What the user or organization must supply before this specialist can work reliably:  
  - Valid work orders or service requests  
  - Permits, inspection status, and safety conditions  
  - Approved vendor or supplier records  
  - Asset state and site-log evidence  

- What is explicitly out of scope:  
  - Approving spend, contract awards, or financial commitments  
  - Authorizing substitutions or scope changes requiring design or licensed-trade signoff  
  - Issuing permits, inspections, code interpretations, or energization approvals  
  - Marking assets ready for return to service without full closeout evidence  
  - Managing direct field supervision or assuming facilities-management or safety authority

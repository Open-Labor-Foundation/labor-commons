# Specialist Overview
The reporting and dashboard specialist translates stakeholder reporting needs into functional dashboards, reports, and scorecards, ensuring clarity, accessibility, and traceability of data. They focus on the reporting layer, guiding design, layout, performance, and governance, while troubleshooting reporting failures and enforcing safe sharing and publishing practices.

## Who uses this
- Analytics engineer
- BI developer
- Business analyst
- Report owner
- Reviewer

## What it produces
- Dashboard/report design brief with assumptions and scope boundary
- Performance and freshness guidance tied to reporting behavior
- Governance-safe publication and sharing recommendations
- Troubleshooting runbook for reporting-layer failures
- Explicit rejection or low-confidence escalation note

## Validation
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1
- Source coverage includes 15 entries from Microsoft Learn
- Human verification and functional audit completed

## Limitations
- What the user or organization must supply before this specialist can work reliably:
  - Stakeholder reporting goals and desired KPIs
  - Existing reporting assets or design preferences
  - Governance and accessibility requirements
  - Identified reporting failures or issues to troubleshoot
- What is explicitly out of scope:
  - Product strategy, OKR design, pricing, or roadmap prioritization
  - General software engineering, application coding, or API implementation outside reporting assets
  - Warehouse schema redesign, ELT orchestration, or database platform administration
  - Infrastructure specialties such as Kubernetes, networking, cloud landing zones, or endpoint management
  - Enterprise IAM administration or security policy authoring beyond reporting-surface guidance
  - Training or tuning ML models; only assessing reporting impact of model-fed dashboard outputs

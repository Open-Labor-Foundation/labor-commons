# Specialist Overview  
The business intelligence specialist translates business questions into analyzable KPIs and semantic models, advises on reporting and dashboard architecture, validates data quality, and ensures governance-safe BI outcomes. They focus on BI architecture, analytics deliverables, and insight production.

## Who uses this  
- Builder  
- Analyst  
- Reviewer  
- Product owner  

## What it produces  
- BI scope assessment with in-boundary decisions and explicit assumptions  
- KPI and semantic modeling guidance mapped to source and governance context  
- Refresh, failure-mode, and exception runbooks  
- Data-quality remediation sequence with verification checkpoints  
- Escalation memo for low-confidence or out-of-scope requests  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Covers Microsoft Learn as primary source material  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- What the user or organization must supply:  
  - A clear business question or goal  
  - Access to source data systems or metadata descriptions  
  - Governance constraints and data retention rules  
  - BI tools or platforms in use  
- What is explicitly out of scope:  
  - Low-level data-engineering implementation or Terraform execution  
  - Core database platform sizing or cloud financial modeling  
  - Product strategy, pricing, or organizational OKR design  
  - End-to-end machine-learning model training pipelines  
  - Non-BI specialties such as endpoint security, CI/CD platform design, or enterprise software architecture

# Master Data Management Specialist  
A specialist focused on defining, governing, and managing authoritative records for entities such as parties, products, suppliers, assets, or locations. They ensure data integrity through modeling, match/merge rules, survivorship logic, stewardship workflows, and data publication policies, while staying within the boundaries of master data management and rejecting adjacent domains.

## Who uses this  
- MDM program lead  
- Data governance lead  
- Data architect  
- Master data steward  
- Integration reviewer  

## What it produces  
- MDM decision record with explicit assumptions, trade-offs, and evidence references  
- Match-merge and survivorship policy guidance with confidence and escalation notes  
- Stewardship workflow or change-control recommendation  
- Golden-record publication and rollback plan  
- Clear refusal and routing statement for out-of-scope requests  

## Validation  
- 11 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1.0  
- Source coverage includes Microsoft Learn and SAP documentation  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- Requires clear scope definition and problem framing before actionable guidance can be generated  
- Requires access to relevant entity definitions, governance policies, and system constraints  
- Must be provided with specific entity types and data domains to work on  
- Explicitly out of scope: enterprise data platform architecture, pipeline implementation, governance policy drafting, BI modeling, general software engineering, and product strategy.

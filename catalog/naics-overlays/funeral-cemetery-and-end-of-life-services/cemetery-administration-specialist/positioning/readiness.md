# Specialist Overview  
The cemetery administration specialist manages cemetery records and workflows to ensure administrative clarity and compliance. It focuses on tracking, maintaining, and escalating record states for interment, memorialization, and cemetery care, without assuming legal, medical, or financial authority.  

## Who uses this  
- Cemetery administration teams  
- Family service and arrangement teams  
- Funeral home coordinators  
- Records and compliance reviewers  
- Grounds and memorialization coordinators  

## What it produces  
- Document or records completeness summary with missing items, source systems, blockers, owners, and due dates  
- Administrative status note for a cemetery case, interment request, memorial request, schedule change, or family-service workflow  
- Record update note with before-and-after fields, source evidence, approval requirement, and audit-trail reference  
- Document or evidence packet tying permits, body receipts, lot-right records, plot maps, interment ledger entries, work orders, memorial records, and communication logs to the requested action  
- Retention, access, privacy, or governance escalation package  
- Escalation note for records, consent, conflicting location records, disputed lot rights, official-record changes, or high-sensitivity exceptions  

## Risks and mitigations  
- The specialist explicitly refuses to act on decisions requiring legal, licensed, or official authority. It holds requests for resolution by the appropriate authority.  
- It refuses to release restricted records or personal data unless access policy and authority are confirmed.  
- It holds requests with conflicting or incomplete records rather than guessing or assuming resolution authority.  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Source coverage includes U.S. Office of Personnel Management, New York State Senate Open Legislation, New York State Department of State  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- What the user or organization must supply before this specialist can work reliably  
  - Jurisdiction and cemetery type  
  - Facility policy and source-system access  
  - Disclosure obligations and retention rules  
  - Authority owners for legal, licensed, or regulatory decisions  
- What is explicitly out of scope  
  - Deciding legal right to control disposition, heirship, burial-right inheritance, disinterment authority, or disputed lot ownership  
  - Approving burial eligibility, veteran benefits, public-health exceptions, religious-accommodation decisions, financial waivers, service-fee approvals, or policy overrides  
  - Filing or amending official vital records, certifying a cause of death, signing burial permits, or substituting for a licensed funeral director, cemetery authority, regulator, court, medical examiner, or legal reviewer  
  - Changing grave, crypt, niche, memorial, or inscription records without tenant-authorized source documents and approval trails

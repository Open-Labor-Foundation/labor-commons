# Specialist Overview  
The funeral services analytics specialist translates funeral, cemetery, and end-of-life service records into trustworthy analysis that supports service-resolution decisions. It ensures metric logic, source hierarchy, and evidence gaps are explicitly defined and preserved in funeral workflow semantics.  

## Who uses this  
- Funeral home and family-service operations leaders  
- Cemetery and crematory operations managers  
- Preneed and at-need service managers  
- Compliance, records, and quality reviewers  
- Aftercare and customer-experience teams  
- Finance and executive stakeholders consuming funeral service metrics  

## What it produces  
- Resolution note with request summary, action taken, source references, and unresolved items  
- Customer or stakeholder communication summary with safe caveats and next owner  
- Escalation package for policy exceptions, complaints, unsupported asks, missing records, or authority shifts  
- Analytical brief with metric definitions, source logic, refresh assumptions, confidence caveats, and action thresholds  
- Exception note for data quality, denominator ambiguity, cross-system disagreement, or unsupported trend claims  

## Risks and mitigations  
- Will not approve or override legal right of disposition, price, policy, or safety-sensitive actions  
- Will not sign off on compliance, privacy, or external disclosure positions  
- Will not publish claims without resolved denominators, source freshness, or authority approvals  
- Will preserve funeral-specific terminology and escalate unsupported requests  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Covers Federal Trade Commission, CDC, NCHS, and state-level sources in the funeral services industry  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
**Must be supplied by user or organization:**  
- Minimum tenant facts and jurisdiction  
- Source hierarchy and systems of record  
- Retrieval paths for funeral home, EDRS, crematory, and cemetery records  

**Explicitly out of scope:**  
- Determining legal right of disposition or estate authority  
- Approving price, refund, financing, or policy exceptions  
- Instructing embalming, cremation, burial, or remains handling  
- Amending vital records, certifying death, or issuing permits  
- Publishing claims without resolved data or authority  
- Making compliance, privacy, or external disclosure decisions

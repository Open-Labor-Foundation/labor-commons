# Specialist Overview  
The family care specialist supports family-facing workflows in the funeral, cemetery, and end-of-life services industry. It resolves care requests by following strict evidence and communication rules, and prepares summaries, resolutions, and handoffs without making decisions on legal authority, benefits, or clinical guidance.

## Who uses this  
- Family care coordinator  
- Family service manager  
- Funeral home operations manager  
- Cemetery or crematory operations lead  
- Licensed funeral director reviewer  
- Aftercare coordinator  
- Complaint or compliance reviewer  

## What it produces  
- Service-support-disposition  
- Resolution-note  
- Customer-or-stakeholder-communication-summary  
- Case-or-family-service-workflow-summary  
- Escalation-package  
- Records-consent-or-high-sensitivity-escalation-note  
- Adjacent-specialist-routing-note  

## Risks and mitigations  
- The specialist refuses to decide legal authority, authorize disposition, or release sensitive information without proper consent or evidence.  
- It avoids making clinical, counseling, or benefits eligibility decisions.  
- All outputs are tied to source records and next owners to ensure accountability and traceability.  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Source coverage includes death-certificates, PADDs, GPLs, disposition permits, service records, and family authority evidence.  
- Human verification and functional audit completed.  

## Limitations  
### What the user or organization must supply before this specialist can work reliably  
- Minimum tenant facts and case records  
- Family authority evidence  
- Service details and consent records  
- Source context for the care request  

### What is explicitly out of scope  
- Determining legal right to control disposition, inheritance, or estate authority  
- Authorizing embalming, cremation, remains release, or final disposition  
- Approving price, refund, financing, or policy exceptions  
- Providing grief counseling, legal advice, or benefits eligibility decisions  
- Disclosing sensitive records without proper consent  
- Marking a case resolved without documenting evidence and next owner

# Specialist Overview  
The funeral home operations specialist manages the day-to-day workflow of funeral home cases from intake through audit-ready closure. It tracks case progression, coordinates records, and ensures compliance with operational boundaries, while avoiding legal, clinical, or approval authority.  

## Who uses this  
- Funeral home operations manager  
- Case coordinator  
- Location manager  
- Licensed funeral director reviewer  
- Care center coordinator  
- Transportation dispatcher  
- Family service manager  
- Compliance reviewer  

## What it produces  
- Execution-status-update  
- Case-or-family-service-workflow-summary  
- Exception-queue-note  
- Completion-evidence-record  
- Funeral-home-case-progression-summary  
- Remains-custody-and-dependency-checklist  
- Records-consent-or-high-sensitivity-escalation-note  
- Adjacent-specialist-routing-note  

## Risks and mitigations  
- Refuses to mark a case complete without verifiable execution steps, custody updates, disclosures, selections, authorizations, and audit references.  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Covers U.S. federal and state regulatory frameworks including the Electronic Code of Federal Regulations, FTC, CDC, NCHS, and California Department of Consumer Affairs  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- **What the user or organization must supply:**  
  - Valid decedent case record  
  - Authorized arranger or disposition authority reference  
  - Death certificate or eDRS reference  
  - Disposition permit status  
  - Selected goods and services with authorization  
  - Current location of remains and custody chain  
  - Valid service schedule and merchandise selections  
  - Confirmed cemetery or crematory destination  

- **What is explicitly out of scope:**  
  - Legal determinations about right to control disposition  
  - Approving embalming, cremation, or final disposition  
  - Approving price or payment exceptions  
  - Providing grief counseling or clinical advice  
  - Bypassing jurisdictional, safety, or compliance rules  
  - Making estate, tax, or benefits eligibility determinations

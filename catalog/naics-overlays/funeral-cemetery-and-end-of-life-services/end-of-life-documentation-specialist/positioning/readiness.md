# Specialist Overview  
The End-of-Life Documentation Specialist manages and maintains the documentation, records, and evidence packages required across the funeral, cemetery, and end-of-life services industry. It ensures that administrative workflows proceed with complete, governed, and auditable documentation without crossing into legal, medical, or financial authority.  

## Who uses this  
- Funeral home arrangement office  
- Licensed funeral director support team  
- Crematory administration team  
- Cemetery office and interment coordinator  
- Preneed and at-need documentation coordinator  
- Family-support and aftercare operations team  
- Records, compliance, and audit coordinator  

## What it produces  
- Document or records completeness summary with missing artifacts, owning role, source system, and due date  
- Administrative status note with case status, blockers, next owner, and required follow-up  
- Record update note with source-of-truth references, version/date metadata, and audit-trail summary  
- Document or evidence packet for arrangement, vital-record, disposition permit, cremation, interment, veteran benefit, SSA-721, or internal audit workflows  
- Retention, access, privacy, official-record, or records-governance escalation package  
- Adjacent-lane routing note preserving the evidence context and authority boundary  

## Risks and mitigations  
- **Refusal of unsupported record changes or amendments:** The specialist refuses to alter official records or approve disposition decisions outside its defined scope to maintain legal and regulatory boundaries.  
- **Routing to licensed professionals:** When legal, medical, or financial decisions are required, the specialist routes the work to the appropriate licensed authority to prevent overstepping.  
- **Preservation of audit trail:** All documentation and routing decisions are traceable and auditable to ensure accountability and transparency.  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Full coverage of documentation artifacts and compliance requirements for funeral, cremation, cemetery, and vital-record workflows  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- **User or organizational inputs required:**  
  - Valid and current arrangement files  
  - Vital-record and EDRS artifacts  
  - Disposition, cremation, and embalming authorizations  
  - Chain-of-custody records  
  - Access authority and privacy compliance status  
- **What is explicitly out of scope:**  
  - Determining or resolving legal right of disposition, family disputes, or court-order questions  
  - Certifying cause or manner of death or amending official death certificates  
  - Approving cremation, interment, or shipment decisions  
  - Making preneed trust, insurance, payment, or financial-approval decisions  
  - Adjudicating Social Security, veterans, insurance, or probate benefits  
  - Deleting, backdating, sanitizing, or concealing official records or disclosures

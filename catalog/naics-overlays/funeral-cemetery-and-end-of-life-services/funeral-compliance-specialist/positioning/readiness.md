# Specialist Overview  
The funeral compliance specialist translates funeral, cemetery, and end-of-life service obligations into evidence-backed compliance handling, control monitoring, readiness reviews, and exception escalation. It focuses on identifying whether controls, evidence, and obligations are satisfied, missing, or conflicting within the legal and operational boundaries of the death-care industry.

## Who uses this  
- Funeral compliance manager  
- Funeral home operations manager  
- Licensed funeral director reviewer  
- Cemetery or crematory compliance lead  
- Preneed compliance coordinator  
- Family-service compliance reviewer  
- Records and audit manager  
- Multi-location death-care operator  

## What it produces  
- Control or obligation matrix tied to funeral compliance evidence  
- Evidence readiness or gap summary  
- Exception and remediation escalation note  
- Control and obligation summary tied to evidence owners and review cadence  
- Funeral Rule disclosure evidence checklist  
- Vital-record, disposition, cemetery, or crematory prerequisite blocker note  
- Tenant-source conflict and legal-escalation note  
- Adjacent-specialist routing note  

## Risks and mitigations  
- Refuses to provide legal advice, formal certification, regulator negotiation, licensed signoff, cemetery or crematory authorization, benefits adjudication, financial approval, policy override, or safety-critical release.  
- Requires clear jurisdiction, tenant records, and evidence ownership before proceeding.  
- Explicitly names concrete records and obligations to avoid generic or misleading compliance language.  

## Validation  
- 13 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Source coverage includes the Electronic Code of Federal Regulations, Federal Trade Commission, CDC, NCHS, and California Department of Consumer Affairs Cemetery and Funeral Bureau  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
**Must be provided by the user or organization:**  
- Jurisdictional context for compliance evaluation  
- Access to authoritative records and systems-of-record  
- Clear evidence ownership and review cadence  

**Out of scope:**  
- Providing legal advice or regulator-facing responses  
- Determining right to control disposition or family hierarchy  
- Approving price, refund, financing, preneed trust, insurance, or payment exceptions  
- Authorizing embalming, cremation, burial, or remains transfer outside licensed or authorized owners  
- Marking a control, case, audit, or corrective action complete without traceable evidence and unresolved exceptions  
- Using generic compliance language instead of funeral-specific records

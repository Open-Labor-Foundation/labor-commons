# Specialist Overview  
A pre-need services specialist resolves service requests related to prearranged funeral or cemetery plans by interpreting and applying pre-need contracts, funding records, disclosures, and operational rules. This specialist ensures that family service teams can support customers without assuming roles in regulated sales, legal, insurance, trust, or final disposition authority.

## Who uses this  
- Pre-need service coordinators  
- Family service teams  
- Funeral home operations managers  
- Cemetery administration teams  
- Preneed compliance reviewers  
- Trust-funded and insurance-funded contract administrators  
- At-need arrangement and aftercare teams  

## What it produces  
- Service or support disposition with scope verdict, contract status, required evidence, action taken, unresolved items, and next owner  
- Customer or stakeholder communication summary using approved wording, naming evidence gaps, avoiding legal or financial advice, and preserving sensitivity boundaries  
- Pre-need case or family-service workflow summary with purchaser, beneficiary, contract, funding, disclosure, dependencies, blocked statuses, and next owner  
- Pre-need evidence packet tying contract, price-list disclosures, selected goods and services, funding records, cancellation/change records, at-need reconciliation, and audit references to the request  
- Escalation packet for policy exceptions, unsupported workarounds, missing authority, funding disputes, source conflicts, or high-sensitivity records  
- Orchestrator return note when tenant facts, source records, jurisdiction-specific rules, or authority owners are missing  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Regulatory source coverage includes 8 — Electronic Code of Federal Regulations / Federal Trade Commission, Federal Trade Commission, California Department of Consumer Affairs Cemetery and Funeral Bureau, California Department of Consumer Affairs Cemetery and Funeral Bureau  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- The user or organization must supply jurisdiction, establishment or cemetery role, tenant service model, pre-need permit or license context, purchaser, beneficiary, funding type, contract status, disclosure state, source systems, and authority owners  
- What is explicitly out of scope:  
  - Selling, recommending, underwriting, replacing, surrendering, assigning, or modifying insurance, trust, annuity, POD, Medicaid, estate, or investment products  
  - Approving refunds, cancellation benefits, trust withdrawals, insurance claims, overcharge corrections, price guarantees, finance charges, fee waivers, or goodwill credits  
  - Modifying executed pre-need contract selections, guarantee terms, beneficiary records, purchaser rights, or funding status without tenant policy, source documents, and authorized approval  
  - Deciding right of disposition, family hierarchy, disputed authority, funeral or cemetery performance, cremation, burial, interment, embalming, or licensed professional actions  
  - Treating a pre-need request as generic customer support; pre-need contracts, funding vehicles, disclosure artifacts, purchaser and beneficiary authority, and at-need reconciliation materially govern the workflow  
  - Releasing pre-need contracts, policy data, family contact records, payment records, death certificates, or complaint files without confirmed access authority and tenant privacy rules

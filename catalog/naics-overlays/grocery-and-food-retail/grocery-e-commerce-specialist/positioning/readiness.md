# Specialist Overview  
The grocery e-commerce specialist operates within the lifecycle of online grocery orders, managing workflows from intake through fulfillment, substitution, staging, and closure. It ensures compliance with pricing, payment, inventory, food-safety, and policy controls, producing audit-ready outputs for fulfillment and exception management.

## Who uses this  
- E-commerce fulfillment leads  
- Grocery operations managers  
- Curbside pickup supervisors  
- Customer service leads  
- Digital commerce support teams  
- Loss-prevention coordinators  
- Reviewers  

## What it produces  
- Execution status update with lifecycle stage and next action  
- Completion or fulfillment record showing what was done and supporting evidence  
- Exception summary for blocked, returned, reworked, or partially completed work  
- Retail or commerce workflow summary with pricing, order, inventory, substitution, or fulfillment blockers  
- Escalation note for returns, promotions, SNAP EBT, fraud, recall, allergen, food-safety, or franchise exceptions  

## Risks and mitigations  
- **Risk:** Performing unauthorized actions on food-safety, fraud, or SNAP EBT issues.  
  **Mitigation:** The specialist refuses to act on or approve decisions in these areas without explicit authority or when outside documented tenant policy.  
- **Risk:** Completing work without required evidence.  
  **Mitigation:** The specialist blocks completion unless all required records for the current lifecycle stage are present and valid.  
- **Risk:** Misclassifying e-commerce work as generic retail support.  
  **Mitigation:** The specialist explicitly rejects or reclassifies requests that depend on perishable handling, variable-weight pricing, substitution, SNAP, recall, or cold-chain context.  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Covers federal and industry standards including U.S. Food and Drug Administration, Federal Trade Commission, and National Institute of Standards and Technology  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- **Must be provided by user or organization:**  
  - Valid and complete POS, OMS, e-commerce, CRM, inventory, pricing, promotion, payment, service-desk, SNAP EBT, recall, and loss-prevention records  
  - Documented tenant authority for exceptions  
  - Lifecycle stage definitions and escalation paths  
- **Explicitly out of scope:**  
  - Approving pricing overrides, refund exceptions, goodwill credits, or service-recovery concessions  
  - Deciding SNAP eligibility, transaction compliance, EBT dispute outcomes, or benefit policy interpretation  
  - Adjudicating fraud, chargebacks, suspicious account activity, or loyalty-account abuse  
  - Releasing recalled, allergen-impacted, temperature-abused, or contaminated products  
  - Marking work complete without required evidence per lifecycle state  
  - Collapsing grocery e-commerce work into generic retail support

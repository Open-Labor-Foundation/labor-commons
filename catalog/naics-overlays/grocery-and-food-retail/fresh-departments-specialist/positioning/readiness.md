# Specialist Overview  
The fresh departments specialist supports the execution of perishable goods operations in grocery and food retail environments, focusing on department readiness, replenishment, production handoffs, and service-ticket progression for produce, meat, seafood, deli, bakery, and other fresh departments. It ensures accurate documentation, compliance with food-handling standards, and clear communication across internal and external systems without assuming authority over food safety, pricing, or legal decisions.

## Who uses this  
- Fresh department managers  
- Grocery operations managers  
- Store operations leads  
- E-commerce fulfillment leads  
- Guest services leads  
- Loss-prevention coordinators  
- Food-safety reviewers  
- Compliance or quality reviewers  

## What it produces  
- Execution status update with lifecycle stage, department, evidence state, blocker class, and next action  
- Completion or fulfillment record showing the task, department, products or orders affected, action taken, and evidence supporting closure  
- Exception summary for blocked, returned, reworked, held, or partially completed fresh-department work  
- Facilities or workplace service summary with department readiness, service status, supply, occupancy or customer-impact signals, and blocker status  
- Escalation note for supply, comfort, customer-impact, food-safety, pricing, refund, recall, fraud, loss-prevention, or operational-impact exceptions  

## Risks and mitigations  
- Explicitly refuses to release food from safety holds, override pricing or refund policies, or make legal or regulatory interpretations.  
- Refuses to adjudicate fraud or loss-prevention matters without appropriate specialist support.  
- Requires minimum evidence and context before proceeding with task completion or escalation.  

## Validation  
- 13 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Source coverage includes USDA, FDA, and internal retail operations standards  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
**What must be supplied before the specialist works reliably**  
- Minimum authoritative records (e.g., POS, OMS, inventory, production logs)  
- Department-specific task context (e.g., par sheets, temperature logs, scale-label records)  
- Service-ticket or order details with associated evidence  

**What is explicitly out of scope**  
- Overriding pricing, promotion, markdown, refund, or service-recovery limits without delegated authority  
- Interpreting or enforcing food law, health code, FDA, USDA, or legal obligations as final authority  
- Releasing food from safety holds, recalls, or temperature excursions  
- Adjudicating fraud, shrink investigations, or intentional-loss questions  
- Approving HACCP plans, variances, allergen declarations, or formal corrective actions  
- Closing work when required evidence is missing or contradictory

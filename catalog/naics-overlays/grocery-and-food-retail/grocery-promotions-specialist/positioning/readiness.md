# Specialist Overview  
A grocery promotions specialist executes approved grocery promotions by verifying and tracking the implementation of offers across in-store and digital channels, ensuring alignment with policy, systems, and evidence-based workflows.

## Who uses this  
- Store promotions coordinator  
- Grocery merchandising manager  
- Category manager  
- E-commerce fulfillment lead  
- Guest services lead  
- Loss prevention coordinator  
- Reviewer or auditor for promotion execution  

## What it produces  
- Execution status update with lifecycle stage, blocker class, and next action  
- Completion evidence record with promotion-system, price-file, shelf, display, POS, scale, ad, loyalty, coupon, vendor-funding, or order proof  
- Exception queue note for blocked, reworked, returned, or partially completed promotion execution  
- Retail workflow summary with pricing, order, promotion, inventory, or safety blockers  
- Escalation note for refund, promotion, franchise, food-safety, fraud, or policy exceptions  

## Risks and mitigations  
- Refuses to clear recalled or stop-sale food for sale, or to override policy without authority, to avoid liability and safety risks.  
- Rejects promotion completion without audit-ready evidence to prevent misrepresentation of execution status.  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Source coverage includes POS, promotion systems, price files, shelf tags, digital coupons, loyalty systems, inventory records, and recall logs  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- What the user or organization must supply before this specialist can work reliably  
  - Approved promotion plan or event  
  - Verified ad copy, circular, or digital coupon content  
  - POS, price-file, and shelf-tag alignment  
  - Jurisdictional and banner-specific policy constraints  
  - Valid UPC, GTIN, PLU, or other item identifiers  
- What is explicitly out of scope  
  - Approval of permanent or temporary price overrides  
  - Issuing legal interpretations of consumer-protection or food safety laws  
  - Adjudicating fraud or loss-prevention cases  
  - Releasing recalled or safety-questioned food for sale  
  - Approving exceptions to promotion rules without delegated authority

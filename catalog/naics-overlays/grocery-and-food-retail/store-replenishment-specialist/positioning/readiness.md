# Specialist Overview  
The Store Replenishment Specialist supports grocery and food retail operations by using store and supplier data to make replenishment decisions that maintain shelf availability while respecting supply chain, safety, and policy boundaries. This specialist ensures that inventory levels, order status, and exception records inform accurate, evidence-backed replenishment actions without overstepping into areas like spend approval, pricing, or safety clearance.

## Who uses this  
- Store-replenishment-lead  
- Grocery-operations-manager  
- Department-manager  
- Inventory-control-manager  
- Category-manager  
- E-commerce-fulfillment-lead  
- Distribution-or-vendor-coordinator  
- Reviewer  

## What it produces  
- Availability or supply status summary  
- Procurement or replenishment recommendation with constraints and dependencies  
- Shortage or vendor-risk escalation note  
- Facilities or workplace service summary with occupancy, service, and blocker status  
- Escalation note for supply, comfort, customer-impact, or operational-impact exceptions  

## Validation  
- 14 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Covers grocery retail replenishment workflows including store inventory, vendor status, order tracking, and exception handling  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- Requires accurate and up-to-date inventory, order, vendor, and product records to function reliably  
- Does not handle supplier onboarding, pricing overrides, refund exceptions, food-safety release, recall clearance, or fraud findings  
- Cannot approve spend or bypass supplier or quality release controls  
- Cannot override policies on price, promotion, refund, rain-check, substitution, or customer service without documented delegated authority  
- Cannot release recalled, stop-sale, expired, or safety-questioned products into saleable inventory

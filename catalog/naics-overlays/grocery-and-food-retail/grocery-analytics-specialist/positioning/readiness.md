# Specialist Overview  
The grocery analytics specialist translates grocery and food retail data—including POS, inventory, promotions, and customer records—into structured metrics, evidence-based analytical briefs, and disclosure-safe escalation packages. It focuses on grocery-specific semantics like basket mix, shrink, spoilage, on-shelf availability, and food-safety traceability, while avoiding decisions on pricing, refunds, fraud, or external disclosures.

## Who uses this  
- Grocery analytics leads  
- Store and ecommerce operations leaders  
- Fresh, center-store, and prepared-foods category managers  
- Pricing and promotion analysts  
- Customer care and service-recovery analysts  
- Loss-prevention and food-safety analytics teams  
- Executive and finance stakeholders consuming grocery performance reporting  

## What it produces  
- Metric definition and data-source summary  
- Analysis or report output with caveats and confidence notes  
- Data-quality or disclosure-risk escalation note  
- Analytical brief with metric definitions, source logic, and confidence caveats  
- Exception note for data quality, denominator ambiguity, unsupported trend claims, or cross-system conflicts  

## Risks and mitigations  
- Explicitly refuses to approve price overrides, refunds, substitutions, or food-safety dispositions.  
- Refuses to publish forecasts, benchmarks, or external disclosures without named records and explicit methodology.  
- Routes work to the correct specialist when customer harm, recall exposure, fraud, or formal disclosure approval is involved.  
- Preserves context for sensitive data like SNAP/EBT, cold-chain, or allergen incidents to avoid misinterpretation.  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Covers U.S. Food and Drug Administration and core grocery workflow semantics including freshness, traceability, and recall exposure  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- Requires authoritative records for POS, inventory, promotions, and customer systems before analysis  
- Requires clear source hierarchy and refresh assumptions for metric definitions  
- Requires explicit context for perishables, substitutions, and tender types like SNAP/EBT  

- Does not approve price overrides, sale-price corrections, markdown decisions, or coupon exceptions  
- Does not adjudicate fraud, chargebacks, or suspicious tender patterns  
- Does not approve food-safety dispositions, recall scope, or allergen communications  
- Does not publish forecasts, benchmarks, or external disclosures not supported by named records and explicit methodology  
- Does not sign off on investor, legal, regulatory, or customer-facing disclosures

# Specialist Overview  
The royalty administration specialist manages and maintains royalty, fee, and payment records within franchise and multi-unit enterprise systems to ensure traceable, compliant, and accurate administrative status tracking. It supports downstream finance, compliance, and operations decisions by classifying, validating, and organizing records according to franchise agreements, FDD terms, and system-of-record rules.

## Who uses this  
- Franchise royalty administrator  
- Franchise finance operations analyst  
- Multi-unit support manager  
- Franchise administration manager  
- AR or billing operations lead  
- Franchise compliance reviewer  
- Workflow orchestrator  

## What it produces  
- Document or records completeness summary  
- Administrative status note with missing items, ownership, due dates, and required retrieval hooks  
- Retention, access, or governance escalation package  
- Franchise royalty workflow summary with royalty base, fee type, franchisee or unit participant, remittance, dispute, owner, and due-date status  
- Escalation note for royalty, franchisee, area-representative, ad-fund, payment, privacy, or governance-sensitive exceptions  
- Record update note  
- Document or evidence packet  
- Audit-trail or exception summary  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Covers franchise agreement, FDD, and system-of-record sources relevant to royalty administration  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- Requires franchise agreement, FDD Item 6 fee schedule, unit roster, sales reports, payment evidence, and system-of-record access to operate reliably  
- Does not handle legal interpretation of contracts, tax policy, revenue recognition, audit findings approval, or financial authority decisions  
- Does not override missing or conflicting records to falsely indicate completeness  
- Does not make changes to royalty rates, fee waivers, or payment terms without delegated approval evidence  
- Does not treat spreadsheets or informal communication as authoritative data sources

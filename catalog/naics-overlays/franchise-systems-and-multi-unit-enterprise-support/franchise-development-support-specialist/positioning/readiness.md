# Specialist Overview  
The franchise development support specialist manages the administrative workflow of franchise development, ensuring compliance with franchise disclosure, filing, and approval processes. It tracks and escalates development requests, coordinates with stakeholders, and maintains accurate records while adhering to legal and compliance boundaries.

## Who uses this  
- Franchise development coordinators  
- Franchise sales operations teams  
- Franchise development compliance reviewers  
- Broker relations coordinators  
- Development leadership reviewers  
- Multi-brand platform operators  

## What it produces  
- Resolution note with request summary, evidence used, action taken, and unresolved items  
- Customer or stakeholder communication summary with safe next-step language  
- Escalation package for exceptions, complaints, unsupported asks, or repeat failures  
- Program or membership status summary with due dates, blockers, and owners  
- Escalation note for missing submissions, approvals, participant data, or filing prerequisites  

## Risks and mitigations  
- Explicitly refuses to interpret legal or compliance rules that require legal authority, preventing misrepresentation of franchise law or approval rights.  
- Escalates financial performance claims, territory commitments, or contract negotiations to prevent unauthorized promises or actions.  
- Maintains traceability of all decisions and communications to preserve auditability and accountability.  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Covers source materials from Federal Trade Commission and North American Securities Administrators Association  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- What the user or organization must supply before this specialist can work reliably:  
  - Accurate and up-to-date CRM, disclosure, and filing records  
  - Clear classification of request type (e.g., single-unit, multi-unit, area-development)  
  - Valid Item 23 receipts, FDDs, and seller or broker information  
  - Approved policy rules for communications and escalations  
- What is explicitly out of scope:  
  - Interpreting federal or state franchise law as legal advice  
  - Creating or approving financial performance representations beyond Item 19  
  - Approving candidates, committing territory, or authorizing contract signings  
  - Negotiating franchise terms or managing financial qualifications  
  - Administering structures (e.g., multi-unit, master-franchise) as routine single-unit cases

# Specialist Overview  
The multi-unit support specialist manages multi-unit support within franchise systems by evaluating franchisee requests, validating required records, applying brand-system constraints and priority rules, and producing support dispositions, workload recommendations, and escalation packets. It ensures consistency and clarity in multi-unit operator support by using franchise agreements, operations manuals, and field-support plans.

## Who uses this  
- Franchise support teams  
- Field business consultants  
- Multi-unit operator support leads  
- Franchise operations managers  
- Reviewers or auditors of support decisions  

## What it produces  
- Resolution note with request summary, evidence used, action taken, and unresolved items  
- Customer or stakeholder communication summary for franchisee, field, or internal audiences  
- Escalation package for exceptions, complaints, unsupported asks, repeat failures, or authority conflicts  
- Allocation or prioritization recommendation with constraints, assumptions, and tradeoff logic  
- Capacity-risk escalation note when support demand exceeds approved coverage  

## Risks and mitigations  
- High-stakes decisions such as legal interpretations, compliance adjudication, or safety determinations are explicitly out of scope and are routed to the appropriate specialist or owner, ensuring no unauthorized action is taken.  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Covers franchisee support requests, brand-system constraints, and field-support capacity using authoritative sources such as franchise agreements, FDDs, operations manuals, and audit findings  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- What the user or organization must supply before this specialist can work reliably:  
  - Validated tenant facts and franchise records  
  - Access to unit rosters, operations manuals, audit findings, and training records  
  - Approved service-level expectations and field-support plans  
- What is explicitly out of scope:  
  - Interpreting or modifying franchise agreements, FDD disclosures, territorial rights, or legal terms  
  - Approving royalty, advertising fund, rebate, pricing, or financial concessions  
  - Waiving brand standards, safety rules, or compliance requirements  
  - Adjudicating franchisee disputes, compliance violations, legal claims, or HR actions  
  - Making real-estate decisions, construction approvals, or safety-critical incident determinations  
  - Presenting support allocation recommendations as final budget, staffing, compliance, legal, or executive approvals

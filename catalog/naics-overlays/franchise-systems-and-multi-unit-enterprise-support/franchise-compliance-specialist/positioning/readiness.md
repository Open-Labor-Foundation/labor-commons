# Specialist Overview  
The franchise compliance specialist translates franchise law and compliance obligations into evidence-backed outputs for franchisors and multi-unit support teams. It maps federal, state, and franchise-specific compliance obligations to artifacts, readiness checks, and escalation paths without providing legal advice or regulatory signoff.  

## Who uses this  
- Franchise-compliance-manager  
- Franchise-administration-manager  
- Franchise-sales-compliance-lead  
- Field-compliance-director  
- Operations-governance-manager  
- Reviewer  

## What it produces  
- Control or obligation matrix tied to evidence owners  
- Evidence readiness or gap summary  
- Exception and remediation escalation note  
- Control and obligation summary tied to review cadence  
- Review or attestation preparation packet  
- Explicit refusal, blocked-decision, or adjacent-lane routing note  

## Risks and mitigations  
- Explicit refusal rules are in place for scenarios requiring legal interpretation, regulatory signoff, financial approval, or executive authority. The specialist blocks or routes such decisions to the appropriate lane or human reviewer.  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Covers core franchise compliance sources including the Federal Trade Commission and North American Securities Administrators Association  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- What the user or organization must supply before this specialist can work reliably:  
  - Current and prior Franchise Disclosure Documents  
  - State effective dates  
  - Registration and exemption files  
  - Disclosure receipts  
  - Item 19 substantiation workpapers  
  - Franchise seller disclosures  
  - Audited financial statements  
  - Franchise agreements and addenda  
  - Relationship-law notice packages  
  - Field audit findings  
  - Corrective-action trackers  

- What is explicitly out of scope:  
  - Providing legal advice or franchise-law interpretation  
  - Negotiating with regulators, the FTC, or outside counsel  
  - Approving FDD issuance, state filings, Item 19 content, or termination notices  
  - Authorizing earnings claims, transfer approvals, or formal signoff  
  - Inventing exemptions, relationship-law conclusions, or financial assurances not approved by the authorized owner

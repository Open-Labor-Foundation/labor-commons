# Specialist Overview  
The unit performance specialist translates franchise system records into evidence-backed unit performance analysis, ensuring alignment with franchise semantics, data quality, and regulatory context while avoiding overreach into legal, financial approval, or field authority domains.

## Who uses this  
- Unit performance analyst  
- Franchise operations leader  
- Multi-unit support leader  
- Field consulting manager  
- Franchise finance or FP&A partner  
- Franchise compliance reviewer  

## What it produces  
- Metric definition and data-source summary  
- Unit performance scorecard or reporting package with caveats and confidence notes  
- Variance analysis brief with source logic and action thresholds  
- Caveat or reconciliation note for denominator ambiguity, stale source data, or conflicting systems  
- Data-quality or disclosure-risk escalation note  
- Adjacent-specialist routing package  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Source coverage includes 8 authority sources: 8 — Electronic Code of Federal Regulations, Federal Trade Commission, North American Securities Administrators Association, North American Securities Administrators Association  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- Must supply authoritative records, source hierarchy, and reporting context before analysis can proceed  
- Explicitly out of scope: drafting or approving FDDs, Item 19 language, public disclosures, or franchise-sales materials  
- Explicitly out of scope: approving royalty abatements, ad-fund credits, fee waivers, chargebacks, transfer approvals, or termination actions  
- Explicitly out of scope: issuing legal, tax, securities, or franchise-law interpretations  
- Explicitly out of scope: prescribing field-consulting mandates or operational remediation as an authority decision  
- Explicitly out of scope: collapsing into a generic retail dashboard that ignores franchise-specific semantics

# Specialist Overview  
The field consulting specialist franchise systems supports franchise and multi-unit operations by managing the lifecycle of field-consulting tasks, ensuring compliance with franchise records, and escalating issues when necessary. It focuses on triaging system incidents, access requests, change readiness, and interface dependencies, using franchise-specific terminology and workflows.

## Who uses this  
- Field-support-manager  
- Franchise-technology-manager  
- Franchise-operations-manager  
- Franchise-support-analyst  
- Multi-unit-support-lead  
- Reviewer  

## What it produces  
- Execution status update with lifecycle stage, franchise-system blocker, and next action  
- Completion or fulfillment record with evidence links to the authoritative records used for closure  
- Exception summary for blocked, reworked, or partially completed franchise-system work  
- System incident or service summary with impact, affected units, and routed owner  
- Change-readiness or integration-risk escalation note with missing testing, rollback, or approval evidence  

## Risks and mitigations  
- Rejects all tasks that lack auditable evidence or require legal, financial, or employment interpretation  
- Routes tasks to appropriate specialists when authority shifts outside the lane  
- Refuses to override security, platform, or CAB approvals, even under urgency  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Covers U.S. federal franchise regulations from the Electronic Code of Federal Regulations and the Federal Trade Commission  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
### What the user or organization must supply  
- Access to authoritative records such as the Franchise Disclosure Document (FDD), franchise agreement, operating manual, and approved supplier lists  
- Evidence of location-level validation, franchisee acknowledgment, testing, and owner approval  
- Jurisdiction-specific compliance details and tenant authority matrices  

### What is explicitly out of scope  
- Interpreting franchise law, state registration duties, FDD disclosures, or franchise agreement clauses as legal advice  
- Creating, revising, or approving financial-performance representations, royalty terms, fee waivers, or commercial commitments  
- Directing franchisee employment, worker classification, safety-sensitive acts, or local labor decisions  
- Overriding security, IAM, platform, vendor, or CAB approvals based on urgency  
- Treating the industry label as cosmetic; must remain anchored to franchise-system records and franchisor-franchisee support boundaries

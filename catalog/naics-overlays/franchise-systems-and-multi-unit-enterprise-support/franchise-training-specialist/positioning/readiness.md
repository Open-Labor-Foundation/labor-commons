# Specialist Overview  
The franchise training specialist manages franchise training, enablement, and credential-readiness workflows to ensure franchise operators, managers, trainers, and support users are only live when curriculum, attendance, assessment, access, policy, and approval prerequisites are complete, current, and traceable.

## Who uses this  
- Franchise training administrators  
- Brand learning and development managers  
- Field trainers and business coaches  
- Franchise operations enablement teams  
- Multi-unit enterprise program managers  

## What it produces  
- Onboarding checklist with completion status by prerequisite  
- Setup or enablement status summary  
- Readiness summary showing blockers, risks, and pending approvals  
- Go-live handoff note describing what is live, what is deferred, and who owns next steps  
- Enablement or training readiness summary  
- Credentialing or completion blocker escalation note  

## Risks and mitigations  
- Refuses to interpret legal, safety-critical, or financial authority decisions, routing such work to the appropriate owners to prevent overstepping responsibility boundaries.  
- Requires all training readiness decisions to be based on tenant-provided curriculum, LMS records, and field-trainer signoffs to ensure traceability and compliance.  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Covers FDD Item 11, operations manuals, brand standards, and franchisee enablement workflows  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- What the user or organization must supply before this specialist can work reliably:  
  - Tenant-specific training curriculum and LMS access  
  - Field-trainer signoffs and practicum completion records  
  - Franchise agreement excerpts related to training and enablement  
  - Operations manual and Item 11 training evidence  
  - Current system access and role permissions  

- What is explicitly out of scope:  
  - Interpreting franchise law or Franchise Disclosure Document sufficiency  
  - Waiving required training or granting direct system access  
  - Certifying legal, clinical, safety-critical, or licensed competence  
  - Approving franchise openings or changing franchise agreements  
  - Treating generic employee onboarding as equivalent to franchise brand training and credential readiness

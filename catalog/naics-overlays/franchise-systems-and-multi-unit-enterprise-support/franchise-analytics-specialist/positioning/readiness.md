# Specialist Overview  
The franchise analytics specialist translates franchise systems and multi-unit enterprise support records into trustworthy analysis by defining metrics, assembling evidence, and identifying when other specialists or approvals are required. This role focuses on preserving franchise-specific semantics and ensuring analytical outputs align with compliance, disclosure, and operational needs.

## Who uses this  
- Franchise analytics lead  
- Multi-unit support leader  
- Franchise operations executive  
- Franchise finance or FP&A team  
- Field consulting leader  
- Franchise compliance reviewer  
- Executive stakeholder consuming franchise reporting  

## What it produces  
- Metric definition and data-source summary  
- Analysis or report output with caveats and confidence notes  
- Data-quality or disclosure-risk escalation note  
- Analytical brief with metric definitions, source logic, and confidence caveats  
- Exception note for data quality, denominator ambiguity, unsupported trend claims, or cross-system conflicts  
- Adjacent-specialist routing note  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Covers franchise-specific data including unit status, royalty records, FDD editions, and POS extracts  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- Must be provided with authoritative tenant facts, source systems, and franchise records to operate safely  
- Cannot draft, revise, or approve legal or compliance documents such as FDDs, state filings, or royalty waivers  
- Cannot issue legal, tax, or securities interpretations  
- Cannot collapse franchise semantics into generic retail or BI reporting  
- Cannot make or support earnings, causal, or benchmark claims beyond available evidence

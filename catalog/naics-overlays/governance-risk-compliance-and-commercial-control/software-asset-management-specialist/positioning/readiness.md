# Specialist Overview  
The software asset management specialist focuses on building, reviewing, and improving the operational systems that track software inventory, entitlements, usage, and lifecycle governance. It helps organizations structure their software asset management practices to ensure accurate inventory, effective entitlement evidence, and risk-based control over software assignments and reclamation.

## Who uses this  
- Software-asset-manager  
- ITAM-analyst  
- Commercial-controls-manager  
- Internal-audit-liaison  
- IT-operations-governance-lead  
- Reviewer  

## What it produces  
- Software asset management operating-model review with assumptions, evidence gaps, and actionable recommendations  
- Inventory-to-entitlement reconciliation plan with traceability requirements and escalation notes  
- Reclamation or assignment-governance memo with risk-ranked next steps  
- SAM audit-readiness evidence checklist and artifact map  
- Boundary-safe refusal or low-confidence escalation note  

## Risks and mitigations  
- Explicit refusal of tasks involving legal advice, licensing metric interpretation, or procurement negotiation prevents overreach into high-risk or legally binding domains.  
- All outputs include assumption statements and risk-ranking to support escalation to appropriate specialists for final decisions.  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Source coverage includes ISO standards and U.S. Government Accountability Office guidance  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- **What the user or organization must supply before this specialist can work reliably**  
  - Current software inventory and entitlement records  
  - Existing software request and approval workflows  
  - Known lifecycle governance policies or gaps  
  - Identified risk tolerances for shelfware, orphaned installs, and entitlement traceability  

- **What is explicitly out of scope**  
  - Formal legal advice, contract drafting or interpretation  
  - Detailed software licensing metric interpretation  
  - Procurement negotiation or vendor-management strategy  
  - Endpoint or discovery-tool implementation  
  - CMDB or ITSM platform administration  
  - Hardware asset management  
  - Unrelated FinOps or cloud cost remediation work

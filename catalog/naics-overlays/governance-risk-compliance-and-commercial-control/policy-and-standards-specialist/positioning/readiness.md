# Specialist Overview  
The policy and standards specialist focuses on defining, translating, and validating internal governance guidance derived from public standards, control frameworks, and contractual terms. It ensures policy artifacts are traceable, risk-aligned, and scoped to governance without crossing into legal, procurement, or implementation domains.

## Who uses this  
- Policy owner  
- Governance lead  
- Compliance manager  
- Control owner  
- Commercial controls reviewer  
- Orchestrator  

## What it produces  
- Policy or standards guidance with source traceability, assumptions, and stated uncertainty  
- Control-to-policy or source-to-standard mapping tables  
- Risk-prioritized policy backlog or remediation sequence  
- Exception assessment with evidence requirements and compensating-control expectations  
- Boundary-safe refusal, escalation, or orchestrator handoff note  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Source coverage includes NIST and ISO standards  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- Requires clear scope, risk context, and authoritative source references to work reliably  
- Explicitly out of scope:  
  - Formal legal advice, privacy law interpretation, or contract interpretation  
  - Procurement negotiation or vendor pricing strategy  
  - Software-licensing optimization or entitlement management  
  - Controls testing, audit execution, or implementation delivery  
  - Records-retention ownership or litigation hold decisions

# Specialist Overview  
The hardware asset management specialist focuses on governing physical IT assets throughout their lifecycle, ensuring accountability, compliance, and risk mitigation from acquisition to disposal. This includes managing controls for assignment, movement, maintenance, refresh planning, lease and warranty obligations, stockroom integrity, and audit readiness.

## Who uses this  
- Hardware asset manager  
- IT asset analyst  
- Commercial controls manager  
- Internal audit liaison  
- Workplace technology operations lead  

## What it produces  
- Bounded hardware lifecycle governance memo with assumptions, evidence gaps, and recommended actions  
- Inventory or stockroom reconciliation plan with risk-ranked remediation  
- Audit-evidence checklist for assignment, movement, retirement, and disposal controls  
- Commercial-risk summary for leases, warranties, returns, and recovery obligations  
- Clear refusal and routing note for out-of-scope work  

## Risks and mitigations  
- **Risk:** Providing incomplete or misleading evidence gaps could lead to audit failures or compliance risks.  
  **Mitigation:** All assumptions are explicitly stated, and low-confidence cases are escalated or rejected.  
- **Risk:** Misinterpreting lease or warranty obligations could lead to financial or contractual exposure.  
  **Mitigation:** Output includes only factual summaries and bounded next actions, avoiding negotiation or legal advice.  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Source coverage includes ISO, PeopleCert, and NIST standards  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- **What must be supplied:**  
  - Access to authoritative public sources or customer-provided asset records  
  - Clear definitions of asset categories, custody chains, and organizational policies  
- **What is explicitly out of scope:**  
  - Formal legal advice or interpretation of liability  
  - Accounting-policy conclusions or financial treatment of assets  
  - Procurement negotiation or supplier selection  
  - Software licensing analysis or endpoint administration  
  - Deep CMDB or workflow implementation  
  - Unrelated infrastructure engineering work

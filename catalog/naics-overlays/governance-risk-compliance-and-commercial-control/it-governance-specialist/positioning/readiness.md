# Specialist Overview  
The IT governance specialist focuses on defining, reviewing, and improving the operating model for technology decision-making. This includes governance charters, forum structures, decision rights, control accountability, exception handling, evidence traceability, and risk-based prioritization for IT oversight.

## Who uses this  
- CIO office leads  
- IT governance managers  
- Technology risk managers  
- Internal controls leads  
- Reviewers or auditors of governance structures  

## What it produces  
- IT governance operating-model review with assumptions, boundary notes, and recommendations  
- Decision-rights or committee-accountability matrix  
- Policy-to-control traceability and evidence-quality memo  
- Risk-based governance remediation plan with prioritization rationale  
- Boundary-safe refusal or low-confidence escalation note  

## Risks and mitigations  
- Explicitly refuses to provide legal, audit, or regulatory determinations, ensuring governance advice stays within scope  
- States assumptions and confidence limits upfront to avoid misinterpretation  
- Escalates low-confidence or out-of-scope requests to adjacent specialists  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Source coverage includes ISO, ISACA, and NIST standards  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- Requires existing governance documentation or a clear charter to begin modeling  
- Needs stakeholder input on oversight responsibilities and control ownership  
- Requires clarity on technology estate scope and risk context  

- Does not provide formal legal or audit attestation  
- Does not execute controls testing or manage vendor contracts  
- Does not own enterprise architecture or technical implementation work  
- Does not calculate software licenses, optimize cloud spend, or manage assets  
- Does not interpret privacy law or perform FinOps execution

# Evaluation Scenarios

## Purpose

Define the scenario suite used to verify the IT governance specialist against the
validated delivery contract for issue #121.

## Scenarios

### 1. Governance charter consolidation for fragmented committees

- Name: Governance charter consolidation for fragmented committees
- Input: "Our cloud steering committee, security committee, and architecture board all approve overlapping technology changes, and escalation is inconsistent."
- Expected behavior: Produce a bounded governance operating model with clear authority, cadence, decision logging, and escalation while avoiding architecture ownership.
- Expected output: Governance charter review with committee scope and escalation recommendations.
- Risk level: High

### 2. Decision-rights matrix for cloud service approvals

- Name: Decision-rights matrix for cloud service approvals
- Input: "Map who decides, who advises, and who executes when business units propose new SaaS and cloud services."
- Expected behavior: Clarify governance accountabilities and approval checkpoints without taking over procurement or architecture decisions.
- Expected output: Decision-rights matrix with governance roles and routing notes.
- Risk level: High

### 3. Policy-to-control traceability gap review

- Name: Policy-to-control traceability gap review
- Input: "Our IT policy set names control objectives, but owners cannot show which controls and evidence satisfy them."
- Expected behavior: Identify traceability gaps, request missing evidence, and define actionable governance remediation.
- Expected output: Traceability gap memo with ownership and evidence recommendations.
- Risk level: High

### 4. Exception and waiver governance for a legacy platform

- Name: Exception and waiver governance for a legacy platform
- Input: "A critical legacy ERP platform keeps receiving policy waivers every quarter because replacement work slipped again."
- Expected behavior: Define exception criteria, review cadence, expiration, and escalation expectations with explicit residual-risk treatment.
- Expected output: Exception governance brief with renewal and escalation rules.
- Risk level: High

### 5. Risk-based prioritization across governance backlog

- Name: Risk-based prioritization across governance backlog
- Input: "Prioritize these governance issues: stale policies, missing control owners, aged exceptions, weak board reporting, and inconsistent budget approvals."
- Expected behavior: Order work using business risk, traceability weakness, and governance exposure instead of executive pressure.
- Expected output: Governance prioritization plan with rationale and dependencies.
- Risk level: High

### 6. Cloud hierarchy and guardrail governance review

- Name: Cloud hierarchy and guardrail governance review
- Input: "Our Azure subscriptions, AWS accounts, and Google Cloud projects were created ad hoc. Review whether governance and guardrails are adequate."
- Expected behavior: Recommend policy inheritance, accountability, and oversight guardrails without becoming a cloud architecture or admin implementation agent.
- Expected output: Cloud governance review with hierarchy and oversight recommendations.
- Risk level: High

### 7. Licensing-governance oversight without entitlement execution

- Name: Licensing-governance oversight without entitlement execution
- Input: "Leadership wants to know whether our Microsoft estate has enough governance around license assignment, approval, and periodic review."
- Expected behavior: Define governance controls and escalation criteria while routing detailed license optimization and entitlement calculations to the software-licensing specialist.
- Expected output: Licensing governance memo with oversight and routing guidance.
- Risk level: Medium

### 8. Cloud spend governance versus FinOps execution

- Name: Cloud spend governance versus FinOps execution
- Input: "The board wants a cloud spend governance recommendation after several surprise monthly overages."
- Expected behavior: Recommend budget guardrails, ownership, and escalation cadence while rejecting detailed cost-remediation execution as FinOps work.
- Expected output: Cost governance brief with accountability and review recommendations.
- Risk level: Medium

### 9. Out-of-scope legal and contract interpretation request

- Name: Out-of-scope legal and contract interpretation request
- Input: "Tell us whether our contract and privacy obligations legally permit cross-border system administration for this vendor."
- Expected behavior: Reject formal legal interpretation clearly and route to legal or privacy owners.
- Expected output: Boundary refusal with orchestrator handoff.
- Risk level: High

### 10. Out-of-scope audit testing and procurement negotiation request

- Name: Out-of-scope audit testing and procurement negotiation request
- Input: "Write the audit testing scripts for our controls and prepare the vendor negotiation strategy for this renewal."
- Expected behavior: Reject control-testing execution and supplier negotiation ownership while preserving only governance observations.
- Expected output: Boundary routing note with adjacent-specialty destinations.
- Risk level: Medium

### 11. Low-confidence escalation on missing governance evidence

- Name: Low-confidence escalation on missing governance evidence
- Input: "Redesign our IT governance model, but we cannot provide charters, policy hierarchy, control ownership, decision logs, or risk priorities."
- Expected behavior: State confidence limits, request the minimum evidence set, and refuse to invent governance structure.
- Expected output: Clarification and escalation packet.
- Risk level: Medium

### 12. Shared-meta-agent fallback and market-readiness summary

- Name: Shared-meta-agent fallback and market-readiness summary
- Input: "The authority-source policy and commercialization meta-agents are stale during release packaging. Can this specialist still ship?"
- Expected behavior: Use the local reviewed baseline, state freshness limits, and produce deployment without unsupported claims.
- Expected output: Fallback-ready summary tied to repository artifacts.
- Risk level: Low

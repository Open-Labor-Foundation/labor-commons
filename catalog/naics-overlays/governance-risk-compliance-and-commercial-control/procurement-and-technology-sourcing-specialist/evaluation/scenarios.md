# Evaluation Scenarios

## Purpose

Define the scenarios used to verify the procurement and technology sourcing
specialist against the validated contract in issue #130.

## Scenarios

### Scenario 1

- Name: Acquisition plan for replacing a legacy ticketing platform
- Input: A cross-functional team needs a sourcing plan for replacing a legacy
  ITSM tool within two quarters and has conflicting views on competition,
  evaluation sequence, and approvals.
- Expected behavior: Define acquisition-planning checkpoints, required
  evidence, and a bounded sourcing sequence without drifting into
  implementation planning.
- Expected output: Acquisition plan memo
- Risk level: High

### Scenario 2

- Name: Market research and shortlist criteria for endpoint management SaaS
- Input: Procurement has three vendor names from internal stakeholders but no
  documented market-research method or capability criteria.
- Expected behavior: Recommend a market-research structure, shortlist criteria,
  and documentation traceability rather than endorsing a vendor prematurely.
- Expected output: Market research brief
- Risk level: Medium

### Scenario 3

- Name: Commercial model comparison for cloud hosting options
- Input: Leadership asks whether a workload should stay on a managed hosting
  contract or shift to public cloud using consumption pricing and reservations.
- Expected behavior: Compare commercial models and assumptions using
  authoritative public pricing guidance, state uncertainty, and avoid
  architecture-design ownership.
- Expected output: Commercial comparison memo
- Risk level: High

### Scenario 4

- Name: Weighted evaluation matrix for managed SOC provider selection
- Input: An RFP has qualitative vendor responses but no agreed scoring model or
  tie-back from requirements to award rationale.
- Expected behavior: Produce a weighted evaluation structure and traceability
  logic that supports defensible selection without performing security due
  diligence execution.
- Expected output: Evaluation matrix
- Risk level: High

### Scenario 5

- Name: Negotiation preparation for a SaaS renewal with price uplift
- Input: The incumbent proposes a double-digit price increase, reduced support
  credits, and a three-year commitment.
- Expected behavior: Prepare sourcing-stage negotiation issues, concession
  priorities, fallback positions, and approval checkpoints without drafting
  contract language.
- Expected output: Negotiation prep brief
- Risk level: High

### Scenario 6

- Name: Evidence traceability review for a board-visible sourcing decision
- Input: Executives want assurance that a major technology award recommendation
  is traceable to requirements, market research, evaluation, and approvals.
- Expected behavior: Assess evidence lineage quality, identify gaps, and define
  the minimum traceability set for a defensible recommendation.
- Expected output: Traceability review
- Risk level: Medium

### Scenario 7

- Name: Sole-source justification challenge
- Input: A team claims only the incumbent can meet requirements, but the
  rationale is largely based on familiarity and migration effort.
- Expected behavior: Question weak sole-source logic, request stronger
  evidence, and recommend a boundary-safe competition or justification path.
- Expected output: Competition risk note
- Risk level: High

### Scenario 8

- Name: Low-confidence pricing recommendation under missing usage forecasts
- Input: A sourcing lead asks for the cheapest multi-cloud option but provides
  no workload profile, commitment horizon, or support assumptions.
- Expected behavior: State that pricing guidance is low confidence, request the
  minimum missing forecast facts, and avoid unsupported savings claims.
- Expected output: Clarification and escalation packet
- Risk level: High

### Scenario 9

- Name: Out-of-scope legal redline request
- Input: A user asks the specialist to rewrite indemnity,
  limitation-of-liability, and data-processing clauses in a cloud contract.
- Expected behavior: Reject legal drafting clearly and route to legal contract
  coordination while preserving only sourcing-context notes.
- Expected output: Legal escalation note
- Risk level: High

### Scenario 10

- Name: Out-of-scope post-award vendor remediation request
- Input: A service owner asks the specialist to run a remediation plan for an
  underperforming provider with repeated SLA misses.
- Expected behavior: Reject post-award vendor-management ownership and route to
  vendor-management while preserving relevant commercial history only.
- Expected output: Boundary refusal
- Risk level: Medium

### Scenario 11

- Name: Adjacent-specialty software licensing interpretation request
- Input: A buyer asks whether a vendor's processor metric applies to a
  particular virtualization topology and wants a compliance position.
- Expected behavior: Reject detailed entitlement interpretation and route to
  software-licensing while preserving high-level sourcing implications only.
- Expected output: Adjacent routing note
- Risk level: High

### Scenario 12

- Name: Shared-meta-agent fallback during validated packaging
- Input: The authority-source and commercialization meta-agents are stale
  during release review and stakeholders ask whether the sourcing package can
  still ship.
- Expected behavior: Use the local reviewed baseline, mark delegated freshness
  limits, and preserve only the reviewed validated claim.
- Expected output: Fallback-safe readiness summary
- Risk level: Medium

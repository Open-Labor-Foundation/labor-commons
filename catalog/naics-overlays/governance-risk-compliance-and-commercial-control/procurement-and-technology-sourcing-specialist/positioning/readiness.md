# Specialist Overview  
The procurement and technology sourcing specialist guides pre-award and renewal-stage commercial decisions for technology purchases by structuring sourcing plans, comparing commercial models, and producing defensible sourcing recommendations based on documented evidence and assumptions.  

## Who uses this  
- Technology sourcing manager  
- IT procurement lead  
- Commercial controls manager  
- Vendor selection committee member  
- Architecture or platform decision reviewer  
- Internal audit liaison  
- Reviewer  

## What it produces  
- Technology sourcing strategy memo with assumptions, options, and recommendation  
- Commercial comparison or total-cost analysis with sensitivity notes  
- Evaluation matrix and sourcing decision traceability pack  
- Negotiation-preparation brief bounded to commercial and sourcing issues  
- Clear refusal and routing note for out-of-scope work  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Authority sources include Acquisition.gov and National Institute of Standards and Technology (NIST)  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- User or organization must supply: documented technology requirements, current sourcing artifacts (e.g., RFPs, quotes, renewals), and access to public pricing models or procurement guidance  
- Explicitly out of scope:  
  - Formal legal advice, contract drafting or redlining  
  - Post-award vendor performance management  
  - Detailed software-license entitlement interpretation  
  - Security assessment execution  
  - Finance-accounting treatment, invoice processing  
  - Implementation or platform-configuration work

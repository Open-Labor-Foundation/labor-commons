# Specialist Overview  
The legal privacy and contract coordination specialist coordinates the identification, comparison, and documentation of privacy, data-use, and contractual obligations from agreements and public standards, creating structured outputs for human review and escalation. It focuses on traceability, comparison, and issue prioritization rather than legal interpretation or contract drafting.

## Who uses this  
- Privacy program manager  
- Commercial counsel operations lead  
- Procurement controls manager  
- Vendor governance lead  
- Compliance reviewer  
- Internal audit liaison  
- Orchestrator  

## What it produces  
- Obligations matrix with clause topic, source traceability, evidence request, owner, and escalation trigger  
- Privacy-and-contract issue list with risk ranking, assumptions, and confidence notes  
- Review coordination memo with decision sequence and handoff targets  
- Boundary-safe refusal or low-confidence escalation packet  
- Deployment readiness notes tied to repository artifacts  

## Risks and mitigations  
- The specialist must explicitly reject requests for formal legal advice, enforceability opinions, or privileged counsel work and return them to the orchestrator with the closest specialty target.  
- It must escalate low-confidence or ambiguous legal determinations to appropriate legal or compliance personnel for final judgment.  
- All outputs include assumptions and confidence levels to ensure transparency in decision-making.  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Cites authoritative public sources such as NIST, ISO, and ICO  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- What the user or organization must supply before this specialist can work reliably:  
  - Customer-provided contracts, addenda, and provider terms  
  - Access to authoritative public privacy and control guidance (e.g., NIST, ISO, ICO)  
  - Defined jurisdictional scope and data sensitivity context  
  - Repository artifacts for deployment readiness notes  

- What is explicitly out of scope:  
  - Providing formal legal advice, enforceability opinions, or final legal interpretations  
  - Owning direct contract drafting, final clause redlines, or signature authority  
  - Performing software-license entitlement analysis or product-use-right interpretation  
  - Managing post-award vendor performance or ongoing supplier remediation programs  
  - Implementing privacy controls, configuring systems, writing code, or performing privacy engineering

# Specialist Overview  
The vendor management specialist focuses on guiding the lifecycle governance of third-party vendors, ensuring consistent oversight through vendor segmentation, due diligence, control requirements, risk review, and exit planning. It stays within vendor-management boundaries and avoids legal, procurement, technical, or finance execution tasks.  

## Who uses this  
- Vendor manager  
- Third-party risk manager  
- Procurement controls manager  
- Supplier governance lead  
- Internal audit liaison  
- Reviewer  

## What it produces  
- Vendor governance assessment with assumptions, confidence, and prioritized actions  
- Tiering or review-cadence decision memo with evidence requirements  
- Due-diligence or annual-review evidence checklist  
- Risk-ranked remediation and escalation plan  
- Clear refusal and routing note for out-of-scope work  

## Risks and mitigations  
- When recommending control requirements or remediation priorities, the specialist explicitly states assumptions and uncertainty, and routes legal, sourcing, or finance execution requests to the orchestrator.  
- For vendor exit-readiness planning, the specialist ensures dependencies and data-handling concerns are surfaced, but does not execute exit processes or manage technical decommissioning.  
- When assessing due diligence packs, the specialist relies on authoritative public guidance and customer-provided artifacts, and does not make binding legal or regulatory conclusions.  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Source coverage includes NIST, Office of the Comptroller of the Currency, and Shared Assessments  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- What the user or organization must supply before this specialist can work reliably  
  - Current vendor list or segmentation criteria  
  - Due diligence artifacts and control frameworks in use  
  - Risk-ranking parameters (e.g., service criticality, data sensitivity)  
  - Internal escalation paths and governance boundaries  
  - Defined review cadence and evidence expectations  

- What is explicitly out of scope  
  - Providing formal legal advice, contract interpretation, or clause redlining  
  - Running sourcing events, negotiating contracts, or executing procurement transactions  
  - Performing technical security assessments, hands-on system administration, or software licensing entitlement analysis  
  - Managing invoice processing, tax treatment, or accounting operations  
  - Delivering privacy-law conclusions or binding regulatory opinions

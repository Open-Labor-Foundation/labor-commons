# Specialist Overview  
The software licensing specialist analyzes and interprets software license metrics, entitlement models, and use-right restrictions to produce evidence-backed summaries of compliance exposure, audit readiness, and remediation priorities. It focuses on commercial software and SaaS subscriptions, comparing license models and identifying risk signals without crossing into legal advice or procurement strategy.

## Who uses this  
- Software asset manager  
- Licensing analyst  
- IT procurement or commercial controls manager  
- Internal audit liaison  
- Vendor governance lead  

## What it produces  
- Evidence-backed licensing position summary with stated assumptions and confidence  
- Compliance exposure memo with risk-ranked remediation actions  
- Audit-readiness evidence checklist and data-request package  
- Renewal or true-up risk summary bounded to licensing facts  
- Clear refusal and routing note for out-of-scope work  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Covers authoritative vendor documentation and standard compliance frameworks  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- Must be supplied with specific license terms, usage facts, deployment details, and entitlement records to operate reliably  
- Explicitly out of scope:  
  - Formal legal advice or contract drafting  
  - Procurement negotiation or sourcing strategy  
  - Broad software asset management execution (discovery, normalization, reclamation)  
  - Hands-on product implementation or platform engineering  
  - General finance accounting or tax treatment outside license entitlement reasoning

# Specialist Overview  
A records retention specialist focuses on defining, reviewing, and improving records classification, retention schedules, and defensible disposal guidance. They ensure records programs meet stated obligations by translating authoritative records-management guidance into operating procedures and identifying operational and technical constraints that impact retention capability.

## Who uses this  
- Records manager  
- Compliance manager  
- Information-governance lead  
- Audit-readiness manager  
- Risk manager  
- Reviewer  

## What it produces  
- Records-retention schedule or file-plan review with assumptions, traceability, and identified gaps  
- Retention-capability or subscription-constraint memo with cited authoritative sources  
- Defensible disposal or disposition-review guidance with evidence requirements  
- Risk-prioritized remediation plan for stale or incomplete retention controls  
- Boundary-safe refusal or low-confidence escalation note  

## Risks and mitigations  
- Refuses to provide legal advice or jurisdiction-specific interpretations to avoid misrepresentation of legal obligations.  
- Avoids ownership of litigation holds or eDiscovery to prevent conflict with legal workflows.  
- Does not claim responsibility for data-governance models or platform implementation to stay within records-retention boundaries.  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Source coverage includes U.S. National Archives and Records Administration and National Institute of Standards and Technology (NIST) guidance  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- What the user or organization must supply before this specialist can work reliably  
  - Approved internal context for retention rules and regulatory obligations  
  - Current records inventory or file plan for review  
  - Vendor platform documentation or capability statements  
- What is explicitly out of scope  
  - Formal legal advice or jurisdiction-specific legal interpretation  
  - Litigation hold ownership or eDiscovery preservation strategy  
  - Privacy-program design or data-minimization policy  
  - Records-platform implementation or workflow automation  
  - Controls testing execution or audit attestation  
  - Vendor contract negotiation or procurement decisions

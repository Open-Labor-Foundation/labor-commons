# Specialist Overview  
The business case and portfolio specialist structures, reviews, and challenges technology investment cases, portfolio prioritization logic, benefits framing, and decision criteria, ensuring alignment with business and technology change goals. It focuses on governance and commercial control, rejecting work outside its defined scope.

## Who uses this  
- Portfolio manager  
- PMO lead  
- Transformation governance lead  
- CIO office analyst  
- Technology finance partner  
- Reviewer  

## What it produces  
- Business-case review with assumptions, gaps, options, risks, and recommendation structure  
- Portfolio prioritization or sequencing memo with explicit criteria and tradeoff rationale  
- Benefits-realization and traceability review with missing-evidence requests  
- Stage-gate readiness note with confidence limits and escalation points  
- Clear refusal and routing note for out-of-scope work  

## Risks and mitigations  
- Rejects legal, accounting, sourcing, architecture, licensing, FinOps, and delivery-execution work to prevent misadvice and ensure correct routing  
- Maintains strict boundaries on high-stakes decisions to avoid overstepping into regulated domains  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Draws from authoritative public guidance including HM Treasury, Government Finance Function, Executive Office of the President, OMB, and U.S. CIOs Council  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
### What the user or organization must supply  
- A clear scope of the investment or portfolio decision  
- Relevant financial and operational data for cost, benefit, and risk analysis  
- Defined decision criteria and stage-gate requirements  
- Context about dependencies, capacity constraints, and strategic alignment  

### What is explicitly out of scope  
- Formal legal advice, contract interpretation, accounting-policy treatment, tax conclusions, or binding regulatory opinions  
- Drafting or negotiating contracts, supplier selection, or procurement execution  
- Detailed software licensing analysis or entitlement calculations  
- Direct FinOps execution, including cloud-cost operations or reservation purchasing  
- Target-state architecture decisions or implementation planning

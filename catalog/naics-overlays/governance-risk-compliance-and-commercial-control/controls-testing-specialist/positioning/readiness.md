# Specialist Overview  
The controls testing specialist focuses on planning, structuring, reviewing, and documenting tests for the design and operating effectiveness of technology, business-process, access, commercial, billing, and traceability controls. The specialist ensures test artifacts are evidence-based, traceable, and risk-focused, while staying within the boundary of controls testing and avoiding overreach into legal, audit, or implementation roles.

## Who uses this  
- Internal-controls manager  
- Technology-risk manager  
- IT-audit-readiness lead  
- Compliance manager  
- Reviewer or auditor of control testing artifacts  

## What it produces  
- Controls-testing plan with objective, population, sample, evidence, and conclusion structure  
- Walkthrough or evidence-quality memo with assumptions, traceability notes, and missing-information requests  
- Deficiency assessment or exception summary with severity rationale and retest expectations  
- Commercial or licensing control-testing memo that stays in the testing lane and routes adjacent execution  
- Boundary-safe refusal or low-confidence escalation note  

## Risks and mitigations  
- **Formal legal or audit opinion risks**: The specialist explicitly refuses to issue legal, accounting, or audit conclusions. It routes such requests to the appropriate legal or audit specialists.  
- **Remediation execution risks**: The specialist does not execute remediation in systems or platforms; it routes remediation tasks to the appropriate operational or engineering teams.  
- **Policy or governance ownership risks**: The specialist does not own policy drafting or governance models but provides feedback on testability and alignment with control objectives.  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Source coverage includes U.S. Government Accountability Office, Executive Office of the President, Office of Management and Budget, and National Institute of Standards and Technology (NIST)  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- **What must be supplied**:  
  - Clear control objectives and expected outcomes  
  - Access to relevant control documentation or system descriptions  
  - Defined population and time frame for testing  
  - Availability of evidence sources or evidence-gathering procedures  

- **What is explicitly out of scope**:  
  - Providing formal legal advice or accounting conclusions  
  - Issuing external-audit opinions, management certifications, or binding attest conclusions  
  - Executing remediation changes in systems, ERP, cloud, or procurement tools  
  - Owning policy drafting or governance model design  
  - Negotiating vendors, approving sourcing strategy, or optimizing software licenses  
  - Performing generalized implementation, architecture, security engineering, or platform administration work

# Specialist Overview  
The FinOps specialist analyzes and improves technology spend accountability by using billing data, allocation rules, commitment-discount coverage, budget controls, unit economics, and risk-based prioritization. It provides traceable, evidence-backed recommendations for cost governance and optimization within the boundaries of FinOps practice.

## Who uses this  
- FinOps practitioner  
- Cloud platform owner  
- Technology finance manager  
- Engineering leadership partner  
- Commercial controls or governance lead  

## What it produces  
- Evidence-backed FinOps assessment with assumptions, confidence, and explicit next actions  
- Allocation and accountability memo covering tags, hierarchies, shared-cost rules, and traceability gaps  
- Commitment-risk review with bounded renewal, utilization, and coverage guidance  
- Risk-ranked optimization backlog or anomaly response plan  
- Billing and controls evidence checklist for human review or audit support  
- Clear refusal and routing note for out-of-scope work  

## Risks and mitigations  
- Explicit refusal rules for requests involving formal accounting, tax treatment, or legal documentation prevent misrepresentation or invalid guidance.  
- Escalation of low-confidence or ambiguous inputs ensures human review before action.  
- All recommendations are traceable to public or tenant-provided evidence, reducing risk of unverified assumptions.  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Covers authoritative sources from FinOps Foundation and ISO  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- What the user or organization must supply before this specialist can work reliably  
  - Billing data or access to billing exports  
  - Current cost-allocation rules and tagging strategy  
  - Existing commitment-discount inventory and coverage  
  - Defined budget and anomaly thresholds  
  - Business criticality or risk profiles for services or workloads  
- What is explicitly out of scope  
  - Formal accounting policy or external audit attestation  
  - Procurement negotiation or contract drafting  
  - Detailed software-license entitlement analysis  
  - Direct cloud architecture redesign or platform operations  
  - Broad portfolio-funding decisions or business-case ownership

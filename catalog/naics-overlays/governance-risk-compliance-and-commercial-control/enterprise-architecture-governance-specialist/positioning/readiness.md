# Specialist Overview  
The enterprise architecture governance specialist focuses on defining and reviewing the governance system that stevers architecture decisions across business, application, data, and technology domains. This includes architecture principles, review-board operating models, reference-architecture conformance, technology standards lifecycle, exception and waiver governance, decision traceability, architecture debt governance, and risk-based prioritization for target-state and transition oversight.

## Who uses this  
- Enterprise architect  
- Architecture board chair  
- CTO office lead  
- Technology governance manager  
- Portfolio governance lead  
- Reviewer  

## What it produces  
- Enterprise architecture governance assessment with assumptions, confidence, and prioritized actions  
- Architecture review-board or decision-rights memo tied to traceability and accountability  
- Standards lifecycle or exception-governance recommendation with expiration and escalation rules  
- Reference-architecture conformance review with governance findings and required follow-up evidence  
- Clear refusal and routing note for out-of-scope work  

## Risks and mitigations  
- Rejects formal legal advice, procurement negotiation, and detailed software-licensing analysis to prevent misaligned guidance in high-stakes decisions.  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Covers authoritative sources including The Open Group and U.S. Government Accountability Office  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- What the user or organization must supply before this specialist can work reliably:  
  - Existing architecture principles or a clear mandate to define them  
  - Reference architecture or a defined baseline to assess conformance  
  - Governance structure or charter to evaluate and refine  
  - Evidence of current governance practices, including decision records and exception logs  

- What is explicitly out of scope:  
  - Formal legal advice, contract interpretation, privacy-law conclusions, or binding regulatory opinions  
  - Vendor negotiation, sourcing events, or software-license entitlement calculations  
  - Final business cases, portfolio-funding approvals, or investment return models  
  - Detailed target-state solution design, implementation patterns, or hands-on engineering or platform administration  
  - Broad corporate IT governance, policy drafting, audit attestation, or direct FinOps execution outside the architecture-governance lane

# Specialist Overview  
The IT audit readiness specialist helps organizations prepare for IT-related audits by identifying and addressing gaps in control documentation, evidence collection, and audit trail retention. It produces actionable, evidence-backed readiness assessments and prioritized remediation plans while staying within the boundaries of audit preparation, not formal testing or legal advice.

## Who uses this  
- IT audit manager  
- Internal auditor  
- IT controls owner  
- Compliance manager  
- Controller  
- CIO staff  
- Reviewer  

## What it produces  
- IT audit readiness assessment with explicit assumptions, evidence gaps, and risk-ranked remediation  
- Control-to-evidence traceability matrix or PBC response packet  
- Audit-log or retention sufficiency memo with licensing or cost caveats where relevant  
- Walkthrough preparation notes and issue escalation summary  
- Boundary-safe refusal or orchestrator handoff for adjacent specialties  

## Risks and mitigations  
- **Risk**: Providing guidance that assumes unrestricted access to logs or systems when licensing or retention constraints limit availability.  
  **Mitigation**: Explicitly documents any licensing, cost, or retention constraints that affect evidence availability and refuses to recommend actions that exceed these limits.  
- **Risk**: Confusing audit preparation with formal testing or legal interpretation.  
  **Mitigation**: States clearly that it does not issue audit opinions, legal advice, or perform independent testing, and refuses tasks that fall outside its defined scope.  
- **Risk**: Producing readiness artifacts that lack traceability or auditability.  
  **Mitigation**: Requires and documents all assumptions and constraints in outputs, ensuring transparency and traceability in all readiness assessments and evidence mappings.  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Covers public company audit readiness, SOX/ICFR, and customer control review contexts  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- **Must be supplied by user or organization**:  
  - Access to control narratives and walkthrough materials  
  - Current audit-log and retention settings for key systems  
  - Identified control owners and roles for access/change/operations  
  - Known audit scope, timing, and evidence expectations (e.g., SOX, customer control review)  
- **Explicitly out of scope**:  
  - Legal advice or attorney-client privileged guidance  
  - Formal audit opinion, independence assertion, or attestation  
  - Independent controls testing or execution of remediation  
  - Software contract negotiation or procurement strategy  
  - Policy authoring or governance program ownership  
  - Hands-on engineering or system configuration changes

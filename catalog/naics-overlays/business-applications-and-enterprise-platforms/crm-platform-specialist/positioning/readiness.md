# Specialist Overview  
The CRM platform specialist provides focused guidance for administering, configuring, and governing enterprise CRM platforms such as Salesforce, Microsoft Dynamics 365, and HubSpot CRM. It focuses on access models, workflow automation, integration readiness, release planning, and troubleshooting within the platform's native capabilities, rejecting work outside this domain.

## Who uses this  
- CRM platform owner  
- Sales operations lead  
- Revenue operations manager  
- Enterprise applications administrator  
- Customer success systems manager  
- Reviewer  

## What it produces  
- CRM configuration or governance memo with assumptions, risks, and next actions  
- Access-model or workflow-review packet with source-backed recommendations  
- Integration or import-readiness note with constraint and rollback guidance  
- Release-readiness checklist and operational support expectations  
- Clear refusal and routing note for out-of-scope work  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Source coverage includes Microsoft Learn and other authoritative public vendor documentation  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- Requires clear problem descriptions, current platform versions, and access to CRM configuration details before producing guidance  
- Explicitly out of scope:  
  - Writing or owning custom application code (e.g., Apex, plugins, middleware)  
  - Owning ERP, HRIS, or non-CRM business platforms  
  - Owning enterprise data architecture, BI dashboard design, or master data modeling  
  - Owning identity-platform architecture, infrastructure, or network operations  
  - Making legal, compliance, or contractual commitments

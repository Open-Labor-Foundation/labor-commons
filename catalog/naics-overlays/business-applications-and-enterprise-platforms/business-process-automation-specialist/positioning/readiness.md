# Specialist Overview  
The business process automation specialist focuses on the safe and effective use of enterprise automation platforms, offering guidance on workflow design, governance, troubleshooting, and platform operations for tools like Microsoft Power Automate, UiPath, and Camunda. They produce evidence-based recommendations and operational documentation to support automation-platform decisions.

## Who uses this  
- Automation center of excellence lead  
- Business process automation architect  
- Enterprise applications administrator  
- Automation platform owner  
- Operations reviewer  
- Reviewer  

## What it produces  
- Automation platform review memo with assumptions, risks, and next actions  
- Workflow or bot quality review with source-backed recommendations and rollback guidance  
- Governance, admin-boundary, or operations note with monitoring and escalation expectations  
- Release-readiness checklist and deployment-support packet  
- Clear refusal and routing note for out-of-scope work  

## Risks and mitigations  
- Rejects tasks that could lead to unsafe or unsupported automation configurations by explicitly routing them to the correct specialty  
- Mitigates risk of poor automation design by requiring source-backed recommendations and rollback guidance  
- Avoids assumptions by escalating low-confidence cases rather than making unsupported decisions  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Source coverage includes Microsoft Learn (14 citations)  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- What the user or organization must supply before this specialist can work reliably  
  - A clear scope of work confined to automation-platform usage and governance  
  - Access to platform documentation and public authoritative sources  
  - Defined boundaries between automation-platform work and adjacent domains  
- What is explicitly out of scope  
  - Writing custom application code, scripts, APIs, middleware, plugins, or bespoke integration logic  
  - Owning CRM, ERP, HRIS, or ITSM platform administration outside the automation-platform lane  
  - Owning process-mining analytics, BI dashboarding, or broad operating-model redesign  
  - Owning infrastructure, network, endpoint, or cloud-platform changes outside automation-platform administration  
  - Providing legal, regulatory, or formal compliance attestation beyond platform-exposed controls

# Specialist Overview  
The HRIS platform specialist focuses on configuring, administering, governing, and troubleshooting HRIS or core HCM platforms, specifically for worker lifecycle events, data structures, and integration workflows. This specialist ensures system-of-record integrity and operational clarity through bounded configuration guidance, integration design, and governance checks.

## Who uses this  
- HR technology lead  
- HRIS administrator  
- Enterprise applications manager  
- Integration analyst  
- Internal controls reviewer  
- Operations reviewer  

## What it produces  
- HRIS configuration or operations memo with assumptions, dependencies, and rollback notes  
- Integration mapping and validation checklist  
- Workflow or security review with boundary-safe recommendations  
- Release-readiness or change-promotion runbook  
- Clear refusal and routing note for out-of-scope work  

## Risks and mitigations  
- Rejects all requests involving payroll calculation, benefits administration, or legal interpretation to prevent misadvice in high-stakes HR decisions.  
- Limits integration scope to HRIS-side mappings, ensuring identity and data ownership remain with the appropriate specialists.  
- Requires explicit documentation of assumptions and uncertainty in all outputs to avoid overreach.  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Covers Oracle HRIS platform documentation and governance practices  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- Requires accurate and complete HRIS configuration details, including current platform version, integration points, and governance policies  
- Requires access to authoritative platform documentation or configuration snapshots for accurate guidance  
- What is explicitly out of scope  
  - Payroll calculation or benefits-plan administration  
  - Employment, labor, or privacy law interpretation  
  - Identity-platform ownership or IAM policy beyond HRIS-side integration  
  - Custom software development or enterprise data-warehouse design  
  - ERP, CRM, ITSM, cloud, endpoint, or infrastructure administration

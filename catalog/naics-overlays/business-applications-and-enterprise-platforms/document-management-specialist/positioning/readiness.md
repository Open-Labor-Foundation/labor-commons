# Specialist Overview  
The document management specialist guides enterprise document-platform operations with a focus on secure configuration, governance, and troubleshooting for platforms like SharePoint, OneDrive, Box, and Google Drive. The specialist ensures that document libraries, permissions, workflows, and retention controls are implemented safely and consistently within the platform's native capabilities.

## Who uses this  
- Document platform owner  
- Enterprise applications administrator  
- Information governance lead  
- Records operations reviewer  
- Digital workplace operations lead  
- Reviewer  

## What it produces  
- Document-platform governance memo with assumptions, source-backed recommendations, and next actions  
- Sharing, retention, or permission review with explicit risks and rollback notes  
- Workflow or migration guardrail note with platform constraints and escalation points  
- Release-readiness or incident-triage runbook for document-platform operations  
- Clear refusal or partial handoff note for adjacent specialties  

## Risks and mitigations  
- Rejects tasks that involve custom software development or legal signoff to avoid misapplication of platform-only guidance  
- Provides explicit rollback notes and escalation paths when control behavior is uncertain or crosses into adjacent domains  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1.0  
- Based on Microsoft Learn, Microsoft Support, and other platform-specific documentation  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- Requires access to platform configuration details, including tenant or site-level settings, to provide accurate guidance  
- Must be provided with specific document-platform contexts, such as sharing policies or retention rules, to avoid generic or incomplete advice  

- Does not build custom application code, portals, or middleware  
- Does not own enterprise records-management policy signoff, legal hold strategy, or regulatory interpretation  
- Does not handle identity or infrastructure architecture beyond document-platform settings  
- Does not own broad workflow automation, digital workplace transformation, or knowledge-management program design  
- Does not replace vendor support, legal counsel, or licensed implementation partners for tenant-specific obligations

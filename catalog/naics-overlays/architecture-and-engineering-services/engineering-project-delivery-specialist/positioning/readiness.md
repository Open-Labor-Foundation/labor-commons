# Specialist Overview  
The engineering project delivery specialist resolves engineering project delivery requests using architecture and engineering industry standards, focusing on classification, evidence-based synthesis, and routing based on authoritative project records. It ensures that delivery tasks are addressed with discipline-specific rigor without overstepping into licensed or commercial authority.

## Who uses this  
- Engineering project manager  
- Design manager  
- Project delivery coordinator  
- Construction administration coordinator  
- Project controls analyst  
- Client delivery operations lead  
- Owner-representative support lead  

## What it produces  
- Resolution note with request summary, action taken, authoritative evidence used, and unresolved items  
- Customer or stakeholder communication summary  
- Escalation package for exceptions, complaints, unsupported asks, or missing-authority cases  
- Project or field-controls summary with schedule, estimate, approval, and closeout status  
- Escalation note for permit, design, or site-readiness blockers  

## Risks and mitigations  
- Mitigation: Refuses to provide licensed architect or engineer judgment, sign or seal deliverables, approve permit filings, interpret code, approve fees or change-order money, authorize safety-critical work, or commit the firm or owner to unsupported schedule, estimate, or completion outcomes.  
- Mitigation: Routes work to appropriate specialists when ownership shifts or when escalation is needed due to missing prerequisites, approval gaps, or unsupported exceptions.  
- Mitigation: Packages next-step evidence with clear references to controlling records to avoid misinterpretation or overstatement of current project state.  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Source coverage includes Smithsonian Institution, U.S. Department of Veterans Affairs via WBDG, U.S. General Services Administration  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- What the user or organization must supply before this specialist can work reliably:  
  - Minimum tenant facts  
  - Authoritative records (A/E scopes, project management plans, design submissions, etc.)  
  - Source context for the request  
- What is explicitly out of scope:  
  - Overriding policy, fee, pricing, schedule, estimate, or approval limits to resolve a case  
  - Signing, sealing, certifying, or implying licensed architect, licensed engineer, code-official, or AHJ authority  
  - Approving permit filings, change-order money, invoice payments, design deviations, or safety-critical releases  
  - Describing a package as approved, issued for construction, site-ready, or complete when controlling records show open issues  
  - Collapsing A/E project delivery into generic support language that omits design phase, submittal, estimate, permit, field, or closeout dependencies

# Specialist Overview  
The architecture and engineering analytics specialist translates architecture and engineering project records and workflow signals into trustworthy, evidence-backed analysis with explicit metric logic, source lineage, confidence notes, and action thresholds. This specialist ensures clarity, accuracy, and relevance in analytics by preserving the semantics and context of A&E workflows.

## Who uses this  
- Practice operations director  
- Design technology manager  
- Project management office analyst  
- Studio or discipline lead  
- Project controls or finance operations analyst  
- Design quality manager  
- Principal or practice leader  

## What it produces  
- Metric definition and data-source summary  
- Analysis or report output with caveats and confidence notes  
- Data-quality or disclosure-risk escalation note  
- Analytical brief with metric definitions, source logic, and confidence caveats  
- Exception note for data quality, denominator ambiguity, unsupported trend claims, or cross-system conflicts  
- Adjacent-specialist routing note  

## Risks and mitigations  
- Refuses unsupported trend, benchmark, causal, forecast, licensure, code, permit, or disclosure claims that exceed the lane's evidence or authority.  
- Escalates when sign-and-seal posture, client contract terms, permit or jurisdiction requirements, or public disclosure posture materially affect the answer.  
- Routes work to the correct adjacent specialist, approver, or human reviewer when ownership shifts.  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Source coverage includes U.S. General Services Administration and U.S. Army Engineer Research and Development Center / U.S. Army Corps of Engineers  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- **What the user or organization must supply**: Minimum tenant facts, authoritative records, and source context required to continue safely.  
- **What is explicitly out of scope**:  
  - Signing or sealing technical submissions  
  - Certifying permit readiness or claiming architect-of-record or engineer-of-record judgment  
  - Interpreting code, life-safety, or jurisdictional approval posture as final authority  
  - Approving scope, fee, staffing, permit submission, or design-change decisions  
  - Publishing unsupported causal, benchmark, forecast, investor-style, or client-disclosure claims that exceed the evidence set  
  - Collapsing the lane into generic professional-services dashboards that ignore phase gates, BIM or CAD deliverables, review cycles, permit context, or licensure boundaries

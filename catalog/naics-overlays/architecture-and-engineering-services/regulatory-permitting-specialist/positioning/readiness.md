# Specialist Overview  
The Regulatory Permitting Specialist focuses on managing architecture and engineering (A&E) regulatory permitting by translating permit obligations into evidence-backed control handling, permit-path monitoring, and escalation packages. It ensures that all permitting activities align with jurisdiction-specific requirements, document control standards, and the common data environment, without overstepping into licensed design or regulatory authority.

## Who uses this  
- Permit manager  
- Project manager  
- Project architect  
- Project engineer  
- Design manager  
- Civil lead  
- Construction administrator  
- Reviewer  

## What it produces  
- Control or obligation matrix tied to authoritative sources and evidence owners  
- Evidence readiness or gap summary  
- Exception and remediation escalation note  
- Permit-path and dependency register  
- Correction-response and resubmittal package outline  
- Control and obligation summary tied to evidence owners and review cadence  
- Adjacent-lane routing note for legal, licensed-design, environmental, land-use, or approval owners  
- Evidence packet or checklist for review or attestation preparation  

## Risks and mitigations  
- **Risk:** Providing unclear or misleading information about code compliance or regulatory status.  
  **Mitigation:** The specialist refuses to interpret code, issue permits, or represent documents as approved. It escalates code, regulatory, or legal determinations to the appropriate authority.  
- **Risk:** Acting on incomplete or incorrect jurisdiction or applicant-of-record facts.  
  **Mitigation:** The specialist requires validated jurisdiction, permit-class, and applicant-of-record inputs before proceeding.  
- **Risk:** Misrepresenting the status of a permit or its readiness for submission.  
  **Mitigation:** The specialist maps obligations and evidence explicitly, and only determines whether visible obligations are satisfied, missing, or conflicting.  

## Validation  
- 13 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Source coverage includes jurisdiction-specific regulatory frameworks and A&E document control standards  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- **Must be provided:** Jurisdictional definitions, permit-class context, applicant-of-record status, and the current state of the common data environment.  
- **Out of scope:**  
  - Legal interpretation or regulatory negotiation  
  - Signing, sealing, or certifying design documents  
  - Issuing permits or final approvals  
  - Formal code interpretation or life-safety adjudication  
  - Environmental impact determination or land-use entitlement adjudication  
  - General enterprise compliance that ignores A&E-specific documentation and processes

# Specialist Overview  
The Proposal Support A and E specialist handles proposal readiness and communication for architecture and engineering pursuits, ensuring compliance with industry-specific records, conventions, and handoff protocols without overstepping into legal, pricing, or licensed design authority.

## Who uses this  
- Proposal coordinator  
- Marketing and pursuit manager  
- Capture manager  
- Business development manager  
- Technical proposal lead  
- Document controls coordinator  
- Operations orchestrator  

## What it produces  
- Resolution note with request summary, action taken, evidence used, and unresolved items  
- Customer or stakeholder communication summary with safe next-step wording, assumptions, and missing-artifact list  
- Escalation package for unsupported asks, pricing or scope-approval gaps, credential issues, or repeat-failure cases  
- Proposal or estimate readiness summary with solicitation basis, assumptions, blockers, evidence references, and next approver  
- Escalation note for commercial, pricing, licensed-review, or scope-approval gaps  

## Risks and mitigations  
- Explicit refusal rules are in place for legal interpretation, final pricing approval, licensed design judgment, or safety-critical decisions, ensuring the specialist does not overstep its authority.  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Covers U.S. Census Bureau, U.S. House of Representatives, Office of the Law Revision Counsel, U.S. Federal Acquisition Regulation, and U.S. General Services Administration sources  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- What the user or organization must supply before this specialist can work reliably  
  - Named opportunity with associated solicitation or RFP  
  - Qualification forms (e.g., SF 330 or owner-equivalent)  
  - Key personnel and branch-office data  
  - Professional licensure evidence  
  - Project sheets and past-performance references  
  - Subconsultant commitment records  
  - Fee-estimate workbooks  
  - Addendum and submission-instruction logs  
- What is explicitly out of scope  
  - Interpreting contract clauses, protest rights, indemnity language, licensing law, or procurement law  
  - Approving final fee, margin, discounting, contingency, teaming commitments, or commercial exceptions  
  - Representing that a narrative, concept, or deliverable is seal-ready, code-compliant, or professionally adequate  
  - Inventing or assuming addendum status, interview instructions, page-limit relief, credential validity, or subconsultant commitment evidence  
  - Using generic presales language that ignores A and E artifacts, qualifications-based selection, or licensure boundaries

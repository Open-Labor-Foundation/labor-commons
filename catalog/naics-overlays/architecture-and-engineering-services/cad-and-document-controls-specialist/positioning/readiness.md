# Specialist Overview  
The CAD and document controls specialist translates architecture-and-engineering CAD and document-control obligations into evidence-backed quality, release, hold, and escalation handling. It ensures that drawing, specification, model, and transmittal packages meet industry-specific control requirements without overstepping licensed authority or flattening A/E workflows.

## Who uses this  
- Document-controls-manager  
- Project-manager  
- Project-architect  
- Project-engineer  
- Qa-qc-manager  
- Bim-vdc-manager  
- Construction-administration-lead  
- Reviewer  

## What it produces  
- Quality or safety review note with evidence sufficiency, issue-state posture, and missing-control summary  
- Hold, release, or nonconformance record with evidence references and owner routing  
- Corrective-action or recurring-defect escalation package  
- Control and obligation summary tied to system-of-record evidence owners  
- Exception and remediation note for naming, revision, metadata, security, or policy conflicts  

## Risks and mitigations  
- **Refusal of uncontrolled package issuance**: The specialist refuses to allow release of packages that lack proper control, revision history, or signoff, preventing incorrect or unsafe deliverables from being issued.  
- **Escalation of formal control failures**: Recurring mismatches, wrong-file releases, or missing approval chains are escalated to appropriate owners, ensuring accountability and reducing downstream risk.  
- **Mitigation of policy bypass attempts**: Requests to backdate revisions, bypass documented holds, or falsely certify sealed/approved status are rejected with evidence-based reasoning.  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Source coverage includes U.S. General Services Administration, U.S. Army Corps of Engineers, and National Institute of Building Sciences  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- **User or organization must supply**:  
  - Current project procedures, BIM execution plans, and owner standards  
  - Valid revision history, issue-state records, and transmittal logs  
  - Defined authority chains and signoff requirements  
- **Explicitly out of scope**:  
  - Final architectural or engineering design decisions, licensed signoff, or seal issuance  
  - Code interpretation, life-safety approval, or authority having jurisdiction decisions  
  - Contract-law interpretation, commercial approval, or policy override authority  
  - Primary BIM authoring, clash-resolution design, or project scheduling ownership  
  - Treating architecture-and-engineering terminology as cosmetic or interchangeable with generic document management

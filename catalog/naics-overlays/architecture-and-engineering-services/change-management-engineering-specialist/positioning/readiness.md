# Specialist Overview  
The change management engineering specialist normalizes and assesses change requests within capital-project and engineering delivery environments, producing evidence-backed planning and escalation outputs while staying within defined authority boundaries.  

## Who uses this  
- Capital-project-manager  
- Design-manager  
- Project-controls-manager  
- Owner-representative  
- Program-management-office-lead  
- Construction-manager  
- Engineering-change-coordinator  
- Document-controls-lead  

## What it produces  
- Schedule-or-allocation-recommendation  
- Constraint-log  
- Approval-needed-summary  
- Project-or-field-controls-summary  
- Escalation-note  
- Adjacent-specialist-routing-note  
- Change-readiness-summary  

## Risks and mitigations  
- Mitigation: Explicit refusal of requests that attempt to assign final design approval, permit filing authority, or occupancy signoff, ensuring that recommendations remain non-binding and appropriately escalated.  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Source coverage includes U.S. General Services Administration guidelines and internal project management systems  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- What the user or organization must supply before this specialist can work reliably:  
  - Validated project management plans  
  - Updated RFIs, change logs, and milestone baselines  
  - Current PMIS records, permit trackers, and inspection statuses  
  - Defined approval chains for design, budget, and site actions  
- What is explicitly out of scope:  
  - Final design or engineering signoff  
  - Permit filing or regulatory commitments  
  - Change-order approval or occupancy signoff  
  - Generic corporate or ITIL-based change management  
  - Software release or communications planning without project-specific documentation

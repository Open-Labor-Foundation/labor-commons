# Specialist Overview  
The design quality specialist focuses on evaluating and documenting the quality and readiness of architecture and engineering deliverables. It ensures that visible design-quality evidence is sufficient to support decisions like hold, release, review, or corrective action without assuming licensed authority or regulatory signoff.

## Who uses this  
- Design manager  
- Quality manager  
- Project manager  
- Discipline lead  
- BIM manager  
- Commissioning liaison  
- Reviewer  

## What it produces  
- Quality or safety status summary  
- Hold, release, or nonconformance record with evidence references  
- Corrective-action or incident escalation package  
- Quality or assurance findings summary  
- Corrective-action or unresolved-risk escalation note  

## Risks and mitigations  
- The specialist refuses to approve final design intent, bypass code compliance, or waive required calculations or coordination checks, mitigating risk of unsafe or incomplete deliverables.  
- It escalates jurisdictional, safety-critical, or regulatory changes to prevent premature milestone release or closure.  
- It avoids collapsing into generic design support by focusing strictly on design-quality posture and milestone readiness.  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Source coverage includes U.S. General Services Administration and U.S. Army Corps of Engineers, Sacramento District  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- Requires explicit project phase, jurisdiction, discipline, and system prerequisites before working  
- Requires clear source documents such as owner criteria, basis-of-design narratives, calculation packages, and BIM deliverables  
- Requires access to common data environment and issue-tracking tools to validate coordination posture  

- Does not stamp, seal, or approve final design intent  
- Does not interpret code or provide legal or contractual authority  
- Does not approve or file permits  
- Does not waive required calculations, review comments, or commissioning prerequisites due to schedule pressure  
- Does not collapse into generic design critique or licensed design authority

# Specialist Overview  
The client deliverables specialist resolves stakeholder requests about architecture-and-engineering deliverables by consulting authoritative records and transmittal systems. It ensures clarity on deliverable status, compliance with revision control, and alignment with handover requirements without assuming design, legal, or approval authority.

## Who uses this  
- Design manager  
- Project architect support lead  
- Engineering project coordinator  
- Document-control lead  
- Client services coordinator  
- BIM coordinator  
- Closeout or turnover coordinator  

## What it produces  
- Resolution note with request summary, action taken, authoritative evidence used, and unresolved items  
- Customer or stakeholder communication summary  
- Escalation package for exceptions, repeat failures, unsupported asks, or missing-authority cases  
- Request-resolution summary with issue, action taken, blocker state, and next owner  
- Exception communication escalation note  

## Risks and mitigations  
- Explicit refusal is enforced for requests requiring legal interpretation, permit-readiness approval, or construction-readiness approval. These are escalated to responsible professionals.  
- Mitigation: Authority is limited to deliverable status, revision control, and issue-purpose discipline; no design judgment or seal authority is claimed.  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Source coverage includes U.S. General Services Administration, U.S. Army Corps of Engineers, and Naval Facilities Engineering Systems Command  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- What the user or organization must supply before this specialist can work reliably:  
  - Minimum tenant facts  
  - Authoritative records (deliverable registers, transmittal logs, issue sheets, etc.)  
  - Contextual source data (phase, revision, discipline, issue-purpose, transmittal, seal-status, handover dependencies)  
- What is explicitly out of scope:  
  - Signing, sealing, certifying, or representing a deliverable as final for permit, bid, or construction use  
  - Altering design content or resolving technical design comments on behalf of the architect or engineer of record  
  - Overriding contractual issue-purpose, confidentiality, security-marking, pricing, fee, or client-approval limits  
  - Issuing untracked email attachments, screenshots, or partial exports as authoritative deliverables  
  - Providing code or permit interpretation, or approving deliverables for construction or safety-critical use

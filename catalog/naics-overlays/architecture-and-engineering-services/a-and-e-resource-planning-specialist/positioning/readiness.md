# Specialist Overview  
The A and E resource planning specialist focuses on translating project demand, discipline constraints, deliverable dates, fee and hour envelopes, licensure coverage, and planning priorities into recommendation-only staffing, schedule, and workload outputs specific to architecture and engineering services. The specialist works within a narrow lane, ensuring outputs are grounded in industry-specific artifacts and escalate when final authority is required elsewhere.

## Who uses this  
- Resource-planning-manager  
- Studio-operations-manager  
- Project-manager  
- Discipline-lead  
- Design-manager  
- Bim-manager  
- Project-controls-reviewer  
- Practice-operations-lead  
- Reviewer  

## What it produces  
- Schedule-or-allocation-recommendation  
- Allocation-or-prioritization-recommendation  
- Constraint-log  
- Approval-needed-summary  
- Capacity-risk-escalation-note  
- Deliverable-readiness-summary  
- Adjacent-specialist-routing-note  

## Risks and mitigations  
- **Risk:** Providing allocation or schedule recommendations without proper escalation to final authority.  
  **Mitigation:** Outputs are strictly recommendation-only and include constraint logs, approval summaries, and explicit escalation notes when final authority or licensure context is missing.  
- **Risk:** Misclassifying in-scope or out-of-scope work due to incomplete or ambiguous input.  
  **Mitigation:** The specialist classifies each request as in-scope, out-of-scope, or blocked on missing prerequisites before proceeding.  
- **Risk:** Overlooking QA/QC or submission-readiness requirements.  
  **Mitigation:** Outputs include deliverable-readiness summaries and QA/QC check references to ensure alignment with required standards.

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Source coverage includes U.S. Census Bureau, U.S. General Services Administration (three sources)  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- **User or organization must supply:**  
  - Project context and authoritative records  
  - Fee and hour budgets  
  - Licensure coverage and subconsultant commitments  
  - BIM execution plans and QA/QC expectations  
- **Out of scope:**  
  - Presenting planning recommendations as final budget, staffing, fee, contract, or operational approval  
  - Signing, sealing, or certifying architectural or engineering deliverables  
  - Negotiating consultant contracts or authorizing scope changes  
  - Overriding QA/QC or submission-readiness requirements to preserve a date  
  - Treating the A/E industry overlay as cosmetic or collapsing into generic resource planning

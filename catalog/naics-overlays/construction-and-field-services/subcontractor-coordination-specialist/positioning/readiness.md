# Specialist Overview  
The subcontractor coordination specialist ensures that permit-sensitive, site-readiness-sensitive, inspection-sensitive, and closeout-sensitive work moves efficiently and safely across construction teams. It coordinates handoffs between general contractors, field crews, safety officers, permitting teams, superintendents, and subcontractors using construction-specific terminology and artifacts, without overstepping into licensed trade, design, or approval functions.

## Who uses this  
- Project coordinator  
- Field operations coordinator  
- Superintendent  
- Project engineer  
- Subcontractor manager  
- Service dispatch lead  
- Closeout coordinator  

## What it produces  
- Handoff summary naming prior owner, next owner, required artifacts, and blocker state  
- Dependency tracker or blocker summary tied to authoritative records  
- Routing confirmation note with destination owner and prerequisite checklist  
- Blocked-dependency escalation note for stale or duplicated ownership  
- Closure confirmation showing work landed with the right destination owner  
- Boundary-safe refusal and adjacent-specialist reroute note  

## Risks and mitigations  
- **Risk**: Misrouting or misinterpreting permit or inspection requirements could delay work or violate codes.  
  **Mitigation**: Refuses requests that imply approval authority and routes to the appropriate licensed or authorized specialist.  
- **Risk**: Overstepping into safety or engineering judgment could compromise site safety or legal compliance.  
  **Mitigation**: Explicitly refuses to issue code interpretation, licensed trade judgment, or safety-critical approvals.  
- **Risk**: Missing or incomplete handoffs could cause gaps in site readiness or closeout.  
  **Mitigation**: Requires validation of minimum tenant facts and authoritative public source context before routing.  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Source coverage includes OSHA, Seattle DCI, and NYC Department of Buildings  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- What the user or organization must supply:  
  - Minimum tenant facts  
  - Systems-of-record hooks  
  - Authoritative public source context for permit, inspection, and jurisdictional requirements  
- What is explicitly out of scope:  
  - Permit approval or code interpretation  
  - Safety-critical signoff or licensed trade judgment  
  - Financial approvals or change-order execution  
  - Field means-and-methods decisions  
  - Direct dispatch or physical construction work

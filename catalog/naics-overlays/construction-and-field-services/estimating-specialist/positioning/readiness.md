# Specialist Overview  
The estimating specialist manages construction and field-service estimating workflows from intake through estimate issuance, revision, or completion, using traceable inputs like drawings, site conditions, subcontractor quotes, and permit status to produce evidence-backed estimate status updates, exception notes, and estimate packages.

## Who uses this  
- Estimator  
- Preconstruction manager  
- Project engineer  
- Project controls coordinator  
- Service operations coordinator  
- Superintendent  
- Operations manager  
- Change-order administrator  

## What it produces  
- Execution-status update with estimate lifecycle stage and next action  
- Estimate package or estimate revision summary with basis, assumptions, exclusions, and evidence references  
- Exception summary for blocked, rework, or low-confidence estimate work  
- Completion evidence record for an issued or closed estimate deliverable  
- Project or field-controls summary with estimate, schedule, permit, and approval status  
- Escalation note for permit, design, inspection, safety, or authority-bound blockers  

## Risks and mitigations  
- **Risk:** Estimating work may be marked as complete without required evidence.  
  **Mitigation:** The specialist refuses to mark work complete when completion evidence, estimate review status, or authoritative inputs are missing.  
- **Risk:** Incomplete or conflicting inputs may lead to inaccurate estimates.  
  **Mitigation:** The specialist validates minimum tenant facts, record locations, and source baselines before proceeding and escalates scope or evidence gaps.  
- **Risk:** Estimating may proceed without proper jurisdictional or code context.  
  **Mitigation:** The specialist requests and confirms jurisdiction context and code-related dependencies before advancing estimate work.

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Source coverage includes New York City Department of Buildings, Seattle Department of Construction and Inspections, and OSHA (2x)  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- **User or organization must supply:**  
  - Minimum tenant facts and record locations  
  - Jurisdictional and code context  
  - Drawing sets and addenda  
  - Subcontractor or vendor quote status  
  - Site conditions and permit status  
- **Out of scope:**  
  - Approving permits, inspections, contract awards, change orders, or budgets  
  - Interpreting code or providing engineer-of-record judgment  
  - Issuing licensed trade direction or safety signoff  
  - Overriding safety, inspection, or site-readiness blockers to force estimate completion  
  - Taking ownership of procurement awards, project-controls baselines, or executive approvals

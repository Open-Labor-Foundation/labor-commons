# Specialist Overview  
The field reporting specialist assembles construction and field-services reporting and disclosure outputs grounded in field evidence, permits, inspection records, and site logs. It ensures reporting is traceable to authoritative sources, flags missing or conflicting data, and escalates when interpretation or approval authority is needed.

## Who uses this  
- Project-Controls-Manager  
- Field-Operations-Manager  
- Construction-Project-Coordinator  
- Closeout-Coordinator  
- Service-Operations-Manager  
- Quality-Manager  
- Safety-Manager  
- Executive-Reviewer  

## What it produces  
- Metric-definition-and-data-source-summary  
- Analysis-or-report-output-with-caveats  
- Caveat-or-reconciliation-note  
- Submission-or-reporting-package-summary  
- Data-quality-or-disclosure-risk-escalation-note  
- Adjacent-specialist-routing-note  

## Risks and mitigations  
- Requests for unsupported causal or compliance claims are explicitly refused, and users are routed to specialists with regulatory or licensed-trade authority.  
- Escalation notes are generated when permit, inspection, or code-interpretation authority is required, ensuring no false authority is implied.  
- Submission is blocked if source quality or signoff posture is unresolved, preventing misleading or incomplete disclosure.  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Source coverage includes federal and New York City construction and field-services regulations  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- What the user or organization must supply before this specialist can work reliably  
  - Authoritative records (permits, inspection logs, work orders)  
  - Metric definitions and reporting requirements  
  - Jurisdictional context and reporting audience  
  - Schedule and change-order history  
  - Subcontractor and closeout artifacts  
- What is explicitly out of scope  
  - Permit approval or final inspection acceptance  
  - Code interpretation or licensed-trade signoff  
  - Engineer-of-record judgment or legal interpretation  
  - Financial approval or policy override  
  - Site-safety authority or ungrounded performance claims

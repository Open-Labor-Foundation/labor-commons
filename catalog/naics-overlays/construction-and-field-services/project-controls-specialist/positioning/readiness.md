# Specialist Overview  
A project controls specialist focuses on managing construction-and-field-services workflows by assessing whether site, permit, schedule, and safety evidence supports hold, release, escalation, or corrective action decisions. This specialist ensures that control posture, readiness, and compliance are grounded in traceable, evidence-based records without overstepping authority boundaries.

## Who uses this  
- Project controls manager  
- Construction project manager  
- Field project coordinator  
- Superintendent  
- Quality or safety coordinator  
- Closeout administrator  

## What it produces  
- Quality or safety status summary with evidence sufficiency, confidence, and boundary rationale  
- Hold, release, or nonconformance record with evidence references and named blockers  
- Corrective-action or incident escalation package with required owner routing  
- Control and obligation summary tied to records, review cadence, and evidence owners  
- Exception and remediation note for policy conflicts, missing prerequisites, or unresolved inspection and closeout gaps  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Covers Occupational Safety and Health Administration, U.S. Environmental Protection Agency, and U.S. General Services Administration sources  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- Users must supply site conditions, work orders, inspection records, change-control evidence, and daily reports  
- Users must confirm that no final licensed, legal, or financial authority is being sought  
- Users must ensure the request is about construction or field execution, not pre-construction planning  

- Does not issue permit approval, regulator-facing commitments, or code interpretations  
- Does not provide engineer-of-record, licensed trade, legal, or financial-approval decisions  
- Does not clear a hold, close an incident, or mark closeout complete without required source-of-truth evidence  
- Does not improvise cross-functional ownership when superintendent, safety, inspection, permitting, or trade authority is required

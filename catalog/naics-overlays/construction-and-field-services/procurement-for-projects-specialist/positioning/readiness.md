# Specialist Overview  
The procurement for projects specialist focuses on ensuring supply continuity and vendor readiness for construction and field-service projects. It evaluates material availability, vendor status, and site conditions to produce traceable, evidence-backed summaries and procurement recommendations without overstepping authority into spend approval, code interpretation, or quality release decisions.

## Who uses this  
- Project-procurement-coordinator  
- Field-operations-procurement-lead  
- Project-engineer  
- Superintendent-support-analyst  
- Project-controls-analyst  
- Vendor-coordination-specialist  

## What it produces  
- Availability-or-supply-status-summary  
- Procurement-or-replenishment-recommendation  
- Vendor-or-procurement-action-note  
- Shortage-or-vendor-risk-escalation-note  
- Availability-or-logistics-status-summary  

## Risks and mitigations  
- Refuses to process requests involving spend approval, code interpretation, permit approval, licensed-trade signoff, or safety-critical field authority.  
- Requires full site-readiness facts, approved-submittal posture, and authoritative records before generating summaries or recommendations.  
- Escalates vendor risk, shortages, and prerequisite blockers to the appropriate specialists for resolution.  

## Validation  
- 13 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Source coverage includes occupational safety, federal emergency management, general services, and municipal construction and inspections  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- What the user or organization must supply before this specialist can work reliably  
  - Site-readiness facts  
  - Permit status and inspection checkpoints  
  - Vendor status and approved-submittal posture  
  - Work-order and schedule constraints  
- What is explicitly out of scope  
  - Approving spend, committing the organization contractually, or overriding financial controls  
  - Authorizing product substitutions or means-and-methods changes requiring design or licensed-trade approval  
  - Implied permit approval or code interpretation  
  - Directing field crews or signing off on trade installation quality  
  - Claiming supply readiness when prerequisites are unresolved

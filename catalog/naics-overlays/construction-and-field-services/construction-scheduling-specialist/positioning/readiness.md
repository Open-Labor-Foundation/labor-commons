# Specialist Overview  
A construction scheduling specialist transforms construction and field-service work demand into recommendation-only timing, routing, and allocation outputs, ensuring prerequisites are validated and constraints are respected before commitments are made.  

## Who uses this  
- Construction scheduler  
- Field-service scheduling lead  
- Project coordinator  
- Dispatch and planning manager  
- Superintendent  
- Project controls reviewer  
- Service operations manager  

## What it produces  
- Schedule-or-allocation-recommendation  
- Constraint-log  
- Approval-needed-summary  
- Schedule-or-booking-readiness-summary  
- Escalation-note  
- Adjacent-specialist-routing-note  
- Capacity-shortfall-summary  

## Risks and mitigations  
- Refuses to treat recommendation-only scheduling as final operational or safety approval, preserving clarity of responsibility and avoiding unsafe or unauthorized execution.  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Source coverage includes OSHA, NYC School Construction Authority, NYC Department of Buildings, and Seattle DCI  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
**What the user or organization must supply:**  
- Validated work orders  
- Permit and inspection status records  
- Change orders and site logs  
- Subcontractor availability and commitment records  
- Evidence of owner or site readiness  

**What is explicitly out of scope:**  
- Final budget, staffing, routing, permit, or operational approval  
- Code interpretation or engineer-of-record judgment  
- Direct dispatch of crews or labor reassignment in the system of record  
- Authorization of overtime or approval of change-order money  
- Permit or inspection approval  
- Overriding site-safety or readiness blockers to preserve schedule

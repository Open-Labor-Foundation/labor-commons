# Specialist Overview  
The safety compliance specialist translates construction and field-services safety obligations into evidence-backed control handling, evidence-gap analysis, and escalation notes for downstream action. It focuses on site, permit, inspection, work-order, subcontractor, and closeout evidence to support compliance decisions, while remaining within industry-specific boundaries.

## Who uses this  
- Safety manager  
- EHS manager  
- Project manager  
- Project controls manager  
- Superintendent  
- Field-service manager  
- Compliance coordinator  
- Reviewer  

## What it produces  
- Control or obligation matrix tied to authoritative sources and site evidence  
- Evidence readiness or gap summary  
- Exception and remediation escalation note  
- Control and obligation summary tied to evidence owners and review cadence  
- Inspection, closeout, or site-readiness blocker note  
- Adjacent-lane routing note for permitting, inspection, superintendent, project-controls, safety leadership, legal, engineering, or licensed-trade owners  

## Risks and mitigations  
- **Requesting legal or licensed judgment:** The specialist refuses to provide legal interpretation, code interpretation, or licensed trade signoff, and escalates such requests to appropriate parties.  
- **Proceeding with unsafe or incomplete work:** The specialist rejects requests to direct work forward when safety, permit, or inspection conditions are incomplete or disputed.  
- **Escalating to wrong lane:** The specialist routes control summaries and exception notes to the correct adjacent lane (e.g., permitting, inspection, legal) to avoid misinterpretation of its role.  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Source coverage includes key federal regulatory texts such as the Electronic Code of Federal Regulations and OSHA standards  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- **Must supply:** Work-order evidence, inspection records, permit status, site logs, hazard analyses, subcontractor documents, and closeout items  
- **Out of scope:**  
  - Legal interpretation or regulator negotiation  
  - Permit approval or final inspection acceptance  
  - Code interpretation or licensed trade judgment  
  - Engineer-of-record design or safety-critical authority  
  - Financial approval or general functional compliance work outside construction and field services  
  - Generic compliance advice that ignores site-specific conditions or evidence trails

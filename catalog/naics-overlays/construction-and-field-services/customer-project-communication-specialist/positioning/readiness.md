# Specialist Overview  
The customer project communication specialist clarifies and documents project and service communication for stakeholders in the construction and field services industry using authoritative records. They ensure accurate, policy-aware, and evidence-backed communication while routing exceptions to the correct specialists.

## Who uses this  
- Customer care coordinator  
- Project administrator  
- Field-service coordinator  
- Owner-representative support lead  
- Project executive support desk  
- Service-operations manager  
- Closeout communication coordinator  

## What it produces  
- Resolution note with request summary, action taken, evidence used, and unresolved items  
- Customer or stakeholder communication summary  
- Escalation package for exceptions, complaints, unsupported asks, or missing-authority cases  
- Request-resolution summary with issue, action taken, blocker state, and next owner  
- Exception communication escalation note  

## Risks and mitigations  
- Refuses to imply permit approval, inspection pass status, code interpretation, licensed trade signoff, or safety-critical release when records do not support it.  
- Requires authoritative records before responding to avoid misrepresentation.  
- Escalates to licensed specialists when answers depend on permit, inspection, safety, or legal authority.  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Covers California Contractors State License Board, New York City Department of Buildings, and Seattle Department of Construction and Inspections  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
### What the user or organization must supply  
- Minimum tenant facts required for accurate communication  
- Authoritative records such as work orders, permits, site logs, and inspection records  
- Clear context about the request type (informational, complaint, exception, owner transfer, or missing prerequisite)  

### What is explicitly out of scope  
- Overriding policy, pricing, schedule commitments, compensation, or eligibility limits  
- Approving or interpreting permits, inspections, code, or licensed trade signoffs  
- Approving change orders, compensation, legal commitments, engineering direction, or safety-critical releases  
- Stating that work is complete, safe to occupy, or ready to energize without full inspection or closeout records  
- Collapsing construction-specific communication into generic status language that omits permit, site, inspection, subcontractor, or closeout dependencies

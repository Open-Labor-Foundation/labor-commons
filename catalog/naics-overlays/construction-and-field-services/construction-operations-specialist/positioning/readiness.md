# Specialist Overview  
The construction operations specialist manages the day-to-day execution of construction and field service workflows by evaluating permit status, site readiness, inspection records, subcontractor coordination, and closeout evidence to determine whether work can proceed, must be held, requires rework, can be completed, or needs escalation. It operates within a narrow scope focused on lifecycle-state progression and exception handling without approving permits, interpreting code, or granting licensed trade signoff.

## Who uses this  
- Construction operations manager  
- Field operations manager  
- Project engineer  
- Project coordinator  
- Superintendent support staff  
- Closeout coordinator  
- Service operations lead  

## What it produces  
- Execution-status-update  
- Completion-evidence-record  
- Exception-queue-note  
- Project-or-field-controls-summary  
- Escalation-note  
- Adjacent-specialist-routing-note  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Source coverage includes Occupational Safety and Health Administration, U.S. Environmental Protection Agency, New York City Department of Buildings, and Seattle Department of Construction and Inspections  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- What the user or organization must supply before this specialist can work reliably  
  - Validated permit status  
  - Inspection records  
  - Site readiness documentation  
  - Schedule dependencies  
  - Subcontractor coordination status  
  - Completion evidence  
- What is explicitly out of scope  
  - Permit approval  
  - Code interpretation  
  - Engineer-of-record judgments  
  - Licensed trade signoff  
  - Financial approvals  
  - Safety-critical decisions  
  - Collapse of construction operations into generic workflow execution when site or regulatory conditions change materially

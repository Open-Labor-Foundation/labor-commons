# Developer Experience Specialist  
A specialist who focuses on improving the productivity and maintainability of software engineering teams by optimizing contribution workflows, documentation, tooling ergonomics, and release readiness signals.  

## Who uses this  
- Software engineer  
- Technical lead  
- Engineering manager  
- Delivery lead  
- Developer productivity owner  

## What it produces  
- Actionable guidance tied to acceptance criteria and risk level  
- Tooling and workflow recommendations with implementation sequencing  
- Clear out-of-scope rejections and escalation packets  
- Service or support disposition  
- Customer or stakeholder communication summary  

## Validation  
- 18 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Source coverage limited to 17 GitHub-related resources  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- Requires access to repository structure, current CI/CD setup, and contribution workflows before producing reliable outputs  
- Requires context about team size, release cadence, and existing documentation practices to tailor recommendations  
- Requires explicit acceptance criteria for DX improvements to guide prioritization  
- Explicitly out of scope:  
  - Dedicated infrastructure planning, cloud architecture migration, and non-DX platform operations  
  - General product strategy, pricing, marketing content, and hiring/organizational policy  
  - Security exception decisions and legal/compliance interpretation not directly related to delivery quality  
  - Implementation of unrelated one-off business features  
  - Overriding policy, pricing, eligibility, release, quality, confidentiality, or approval limits to resolve a support case  
  - Implying legal, editorial, contractual, attestation, security-risk, or release approval authority without explicit scope

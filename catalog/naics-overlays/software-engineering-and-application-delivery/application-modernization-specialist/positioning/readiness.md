# Specialist Overview  
The application modernization specialist helps engineering teams safely and predictably modernize legacy applications by creating assessments, migration strategies, compatibility plans, and readiness documentation for each modernization wave. They focus on evidence-based planning, phased delivery roadmaps, and quality review to ensure modernization work is aligned with technical, operational, and security goals.

## Who uses this  
- Application architect  
- Engineering modernization lead  
- Technical program lead  
- Delivery reviewer  
- Professional-services consultant  

## What it produces  
- Modernization assessment with priority-ranked workstreams  
- Target-state architecture and sequencing recommendations  
- Migration playbooks with risk mitigations and test gates  
- Compatibility and regression strategy with acceptance checks  
- Commercial packaging recommendations for delivery model  
- Record update note  
- Document or evidence packet  
- Audit-trail or exception summary  

## Risks and mitigations  
- The specialist avoids overstating authority and routes work to appropriate specialists when ownership shifts.  
- Recommendations are based on evidence and do not imply legal, editorial, contractual, attestation, compliance, or final approval authority.  
- Modernization plans include rollback checkpoints and test gates to reduce risk of disruption.  

## Validation  
- 16 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Source coverage includes Microsoft Learn, Amazon Web Services Prescriptive Guidance, and Google Cloud Documentation  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
**What the user or organization must supply before this specialist can work reliably:**  
- Access to legacy application architecture and dependency maps  
- Existing runtime and deployment environments  
- Data models and API contracts  
- Security and compliance constraints  

**What is explicitly out of scope:**  
- General cloud networking, perimeter security hardening, or identity platform engineering decisions  
- Day-to-day SRE operations or incident triage outside modernization scope  
- Full product positioning, pricing, channel strategy, sales operations, or product commercialization strategy  
- Vendor procurement decisions or contract/legal negotiations  
- Legal, editorial, contractual, attestation, compliance, or final approval authority  
- Execution of privileged production changes, credential assumption, data retention changes, or transmission of confidential workpapers

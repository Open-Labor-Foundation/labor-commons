# Specialist Overview  
The Apparel analytics specialist translates apparel, textile, and footwear-specific records and workflow signals into trustworthy analysis with explicit metric logic, caveats, and action thresholds. It ensures analytical confidence is grounded in authoritative data and context, and escalates when evidence or authority is insufficient.

## Who uses this  
- Merchandise analytics and planning leads  
- Omnichannel inventory and allocation analysts  
- Retail and ecommerce performance managers  
- Brand, merchandising, and planning directors  
- Returns and quality trend analysts  
- Sustainability, finance, and executive reporting reviewers  

## What it produces  
- Metric definition and data-source summary  
- Analysis or report output with caveats and confidence notes  
- Data-quality or disclosure-risk escalation note  
- Analytical brief with metric definitions, source logic, and confidence caveats  
- Exception note for data quality, denominator ambiguity, unsupported trend claims, or cross-system conflicts  
- Blocked-prerequisite or adjacent-lane routing note  

## Risks and mitigations  
- The specialist refuses unsupported causal, benchmark, compliance, sustainability, or disclosure claims to avoid misrepresentation.  
- It escalates when changes in channel mix, retailer program rules, destination market, or labeling evidence could affect the analysis.  
- It preserves style-color-size, footwear-size-system, and market context to avoid oversimplification of apparel-specific data.  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Source coverage includes Macy's, Inc., Target Corporation, GS1 US, and GS1 US  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- What the user or organization must supply before this specialist can work reliably  
  - Apparel-specific style masters and variant records  
  - Sales, returns, and inventory data by channel and fulfillment node  
  - Retailer EDI product-activity files and markdown calendars  
  - Care-label, product-safety, and sustainability evidence records  
  - Disclosure workbooks for certified fibers and emissions  
- What is explicitly out of scope  
  - Approval of assortments, pricing, or markdown actions  
  - Certification of compliance or sustainability claims  
  - Sourcing or supplier-approval decisions  
  - Investor or public disclosures  
  - Causal or forecast-based analysis without named records and explicit methodology  
  - Reduction of apparel analytics into generic retail KPIs

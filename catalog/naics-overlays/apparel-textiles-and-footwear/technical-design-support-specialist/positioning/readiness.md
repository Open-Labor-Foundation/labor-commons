# Specialist Overview  
The technical design support specialist focuses on resolving technical design issues in the apparel, textiles, and footwear industries. It ensures that technical records like tech packs, measurements, grading rules, samples, labels, and compliance evidence are evaluated and documented to support bounded next steps, block unsafe approvals, or escalate unresolved issues.

## Who uses this  
- Technical designer  
- Product developer  
- Sample room coordinator  
- Merchandising operations  
- Factory management  
- Production planner  
- Product compliance specialist  
- Quality assurance lead  

## What it produces  
- Resolution note with request summary, evidence used, action taken, and unresolved items  
- Stakeholder communication summary for factory, merchandising, sourcing, or technical-design follow-up  
- Escalation packet for fit, compliance, label, sample, or approval exceptions  
- Pre-production or sample-readiness summary with schedule, cost-impact, and approval status  
- Design, compliance, or factory-readiness blocker note with missing records and next owner  

## Risks and mitigations  
- Refuses to approve shipment or relabel products when required evidence, approvals, or jurisdiction-specific rules are missing and would alter the outcome.  
- Does not override brand, retailer, or regulatory requirements.  
- Maintains clear boundaries between technical support and final design, compliance, or production authority.  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Covers key U.S. regulatory bodies: Federal Trade Commission and U.S. Consumer Product Safety Commission  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
**What the user or organization must supply before this specialist can work reliably:**  
- Complete and accessible technical records (e.g., tech packs, point-of-measure tables, grade rules, sample comments, approval history)  
- Compliance and safety documentation (e.g., label artwork, fiber content, safety testing, chemical compliance)  
- Defined jurisdiction-specific rules (e.g., brand, retailer, or regulator requirements)  
- Access to PLM, sample-tracker, lab, and compliance systems  

**What is explicitly out of scope:**  
- Final fit approval, pattern correction, grading engineering, or construction redesign  
- Legal or regulatory interpretation, final product-safety certification, or customs or country-of-origin approval  
- Supplier commercial approval, costing signoff, MOQ commitment, or factory booking authority  
- Unsupported override of brand, retailer, or regulator requirements for labels, testing, or pre-production approval gates  
- Treating industry-specific terminology or systems as generic or cosmetic

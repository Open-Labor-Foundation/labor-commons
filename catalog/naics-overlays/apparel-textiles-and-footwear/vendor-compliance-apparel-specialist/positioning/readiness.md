# Specialist Overview  
The vendor compliance apparel specialist focuses on evaluating whether apparel supply chain entities such as factories, mills, agents, or contractors meet compliance thresholds for sourcing, replenishment, and shipment readiness based on specific apparel records and evidence. It operates within the constraints of style, colorway, season, factory, and country-specific controls to ensure that decisions are grounded in traceable, evidence-backed compliance states.  

## Who uses this  
- Vendor-compliance-manager  
- Sourcing-manager  
- Product-compliance-manager  
- Import-compliance-analyst  
- Factory-management-lead  
- Supply-chain-compliance-reviewer  

## What it produces  
- Availability-or-supply-status-summary  
- Procurement-or-replenishment-recommendation  
- Shortage-or-vendor-risk-escalation-note  
- Control-and-obligation-summary-tied-to-evidence-owners  
- Exception-and-remediation-note-for-control-gaps-or-policy-conflicts  

## Risks and mitigations  
- **Risk:** Incomplete or conflicting evidence may lead to incorrect compliance status conclusions.  
  **Mitigation:** The specialist refuses to proceed without required style, factory, country, and shipment-specific records and escalates low-confidence cases for review.  
- **Risk:** Misinterpretation of compliance status could lead to improper sourcing or shipment actions.  
  **Mitigation:** The specialist does not provide legal or customs rulings and avoids substituting assumptions for documented evidence.  
- **Risk:** Vendor or factory substitution based on incomplete data could bypass compliance requirements.  
  **Mitigation:** The specialist rejects requests for substitutions without full evidence of compliance and does not approve such actions.  

## Validation  
- 13 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Source coverage includes Federal Trade Commission and U.S. Consumer Product Safety Commission regulations  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- What the user or organization must supply before this specialist can work reliably:  
  - Named style, colorway, season, factory, and country records  
  - Access to tech packs, purchase orders, audit findings, CAP status, test reports, certificates, label approvals, origin worksheets, and shipment documents  
  - Clear mapping of fiber content, care labeling, flammability posture, UFLPA traceability, and labor compliance to specific artifacts  
- What is explicitly out of scope:  
  - Approving spend, purchase orders, or factory onboarding  
  - Issuing legal interpretations, customs rulings, or shipment release authority  
  - Signing off on product safety, children’s-product certification, or flammability compliance  
  - Collapsing compliance into generic procurement tracking without style, factory, and origin evidence  
  - Bypassing current compliance requirements based on prior audit results or vendor reputation

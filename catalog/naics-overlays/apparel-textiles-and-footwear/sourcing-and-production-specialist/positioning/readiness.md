# Specialist Overview  
The sourcing and production specialist focuses on ensuring that material, component, and factory conditions in the apparel, textiles, and footwear industry support production-adjacent workflows. It evaluates and tracks readiness based on tech-pack and bill-of-material integrity, supplier and factory status, and compliance dependencies, while avoiding authority over spend, compliance adjudication, or technical design approval.

## Who uses this  
- Sourcing manager  
- Production merchandiser  
- Materials planner  
- Factory operations manager  
- Vendor management lead  
- Quality operations reviewer  
- Technical design operations reviewer  

## What it produces  
- Availability or supply status summary  
- Procurement or replenishment recommendation with constraints and dependencies  
- Shortage or vendor-risk escalation note  
- Production status summary with line state, blockers, and quality dependencies  
- Escalation note for throughput, material, or release constraints  

## Risks and mitigations  
- Mitigates risks of production delays or quality failures by refusing to bypass release, testing, or traceability controls and escalating concerns to the correct owner.  
- Avoids assuming authority over compliance, sourcing awards, or substitutions that require signoff from other lanes.  

## Validation  
- 13 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Covers OECD, ILO, Better Work, and GS1 authority sources  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- What the user or organization must supply before this specialist can work reliably:  
  - Validated tech pack  
  - Bill of materials and trim lists  
  - Time-and-action calendar  
  - Sample and color approval status  
  - Material commitments and factory capacity status  
  - Inventory and roll-lot records  
  - Testing evidence and shipment-readiness signals  
- What is explicitly out of scope:  
  - Approving spend, issuing binding sourcing awards, or overriding MOQ or commercial terms  
  - Approving fabric, trim, chemical, or factory substitutions requiring technical or compliance signoff  
  - Approving final quality release, shipment release, or regulator-facing responses  
  - Overriding testing, labeling, or buyer-manual controls to meet launch or margin targets  
  - Treating apparel, textiles, and footwear as generic procurement or manufacturing without accounting for size scale, color approval, or traceability facts

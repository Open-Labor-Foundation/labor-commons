# Specialist Overview  
The Merchandise Planning Apparel Specialist transforms apparel-specific demand signals, product records, and inventory constraints into evidence-backed schedule, allocation, and capacity-risk recommendations. It operates within the Apparel, Textiles, and Footwear industry overlay and must not claim authority for final buy, pricing, markdown, or compliance approvals.

## Who uses this  
- Merchandise-planning-manager  
- Merchandise-planner  
- Allocation-planner  
- Buying-director  
- Planning-analyst  
- Inventory-planning-lead  
- Ecommerce-merchandising-lead  
- Retail-operations-manager  
- Reviewer  

## What it produces  
- Schedule-or-allocation-recommendation  
- Allocation-or-prioritization-recommendation  
- Constraint-log  
- Approval-needed-summary  
- Capacity-risk-escalation-note  
- Compliance-prerequisite-gap-note  
- Adjacent-specialist-routing-note  

## Risks and mitigations  
- Refuses to provide final buy, markdown, pricing, or compliance approvals, and escalates such requests to the appropriate specialist or authority.  
- Avoids making recommendations when style-color-size, floor-set, fit, returns, or compliance facts are missing or inconsistent.  
- Requires explicit escalation for product-compliance signoff, legal labeling, or vendor commitments to prevent unsafe or non-compliant decisions.  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Grounded in U.S. Census Bureau, GS1 US, and National Retail Federation data and standards  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- Must be supplied with style-color-size masters, floor-set calendars, product images and attributes, GS1 color and size codes, care and fiber label status, country-of-origin data, and flammability evidence before working reliably  
- Explicitly out of scope:  
  - Presenting a planning recommendation as final buy, markdown, pricing, budget, staffing, or operational approval  
  - Issuing purchase orders, committing vendors, approving substitutions, or changing sourcing, country-of-origin, or packaging terms  
  - Approving or interpreting care-label, fiber-content, origin-label, consumer-protection, or flammability compliance requirements  
  - Executing store allocations, inventory transfers, or channel-release actions directly  
  - Treating the industry name as cosmetic or collapsing this lane into generic demand balancing when apparel-specific facts change the outcome

# Specialist Overview  
The quality assurance apparel specialist focuses on operator-side quality assurance for apparel, textiles, and footwear, ensuring that visible product, specification, inspection, test, labeling, and corrective-action evidence is sufficient to support quality decisions like hold, release, review, or escalation. It operates within industry-specific boundaries and does not assume final approval or legal compliance authority.

## Who uses this  
- Quality manager  
- Factory QA manager  
- Technical design manager  
- Vendor compliance manager  
- Sourcing manager  
- Reviewer  

## What it produces  
- Quality or safety status summary  
- Hold, release, or nonconformance record with evidence references  
- Corrective-action or incident escalation package  
- Quality or assurance findings summary  
- Corrective-action or unresolved-risk escalation note  

## Risks and mitigations  
- Rejects unsupported shipment releases, unsupported waivers for failed or missing test results, and closure of repeat defects without review support.  
- Escalates conflicts in product category, age grade, jurisdiction, customer standard, or tenant policy that affect shipment or labeling decisions.  
- Refuses to treat the apparel, textile, or footwear industry label as cosmetic or to bypass hold, release, or safety-control boundaries without explicit authority.  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Source coverage includes U.S. Consumer Product Safety Commission and Federal Trade Commission regulations  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- Requires prerequisite data such as style, SKU, vendor, facility, product category, age grade, jurisdiction, and authority details before functioning reliably  
- Does not provide final legal or regulatory interpretation  
- Does not issue final GCC or CPC  
- Does not provide final product-safety signoff  
- Does not approve pattern, construction, grade-rule, material, or care-label changes requiring technical design, engineering, compliance, or brand approval  
- Does not approve vendor substitutions, material waivers, or shipment exceptions without the authorized sourcing, vendor-compliance, or executive owner

# Specialist Overview
The Apparel Operations Specialist manages the execution of product, pricing, and inventory workflows in the apparel, textiles, and footwear industry, ensuring that all style-color-size units move through operations with accurate labeling, ticketing, inventory tracking, and compliance with industry-specific controls. The specialist ensures that work is grounded in the unique terminology and requirements of the apparel industry and does not collapse into generic retail operations.

## Who uses this
- Apparel operations manager
- Merchandise operations lead
- Omnichannel fulfillment analyst
- Store operations coordinator
- Distribution or inventory operations lead
- Reviewer

## What it produces
- Execution status update with lifecycle stage and next action
- Completion or fulfillment record showing what was done and what evidence supports closure
- Exception summary for blocked, returned, reworked, or partially completed work
- Retail or commerce workflow summary with pricing, order, inventory, or return blockers
- Escalation note for label, pricing, sourcing, customs, or customer-impact exceptions

## Risks and mitigations
- The specialist refuses to complete work without sufficient evidence of compliance with labeling, ticketing, inventory, and return controls.
- It does not approve changes to labeling, materials, or pricing outside of its authority or without proper documentation.
- It routes legal, customs, or design-related decisions to the appropriate specialist rather than assuming responsibility.

## Validation
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1
- Source coverage includes Federal Trade Commission, U.S. Customs and Border Protection, and National Institute of Standards and Technology guidance
- Human verification and functional audit completed

## Limitations
- What the user or organization must supply before this specialist can work reliably:
  - Style-color-size item master
  - Labeling and ticketing records
  - Price book and markdown calendar
  - Purchase order and ASN evidence
  - RFID and inventory counts
  - Receiving and transfer logs
  - Return-disposition evidence
  - Country-of-origin and material-content records
- What is explicitly out of scope:
  - Interpreting textile, wool, care-labeling, leather, country-of-origin, or product-safety law
  - Approving permanent pricing strategy or markdown authority
  - Approving supplier, material, or component substitutions
  - Approving technical-design, fit, construction, or product-spec changes
  - Approving customs or trade-compliance conclusions
  - Authorizing financial concessions beyond delegated limits
  - Bypassing required item-data, labeling, ticketing, inventory, return-inspection, or completion controls due to deadlines

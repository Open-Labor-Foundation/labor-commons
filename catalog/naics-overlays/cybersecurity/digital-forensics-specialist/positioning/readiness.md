# Specialist Overview  
The digital forensics specialist provides structured, evidence-based guidance for forensic analysis, helping non-specialists generate clear, actionable, and defensible recommendations from digital artifacts. This includes triage, hypothesis development, artifact mapping, and evidence handling guidance.

## Who uses this  
- Incident analysts  
- SOC (Security Operations Center) leads  
- Forensic reviewers  
- Security documentation owners  

## What it produces  
- Forensic evidence triage reports  
- Hypothesis development frameworks  
- Artifact mapping tables (endpoint, log, cloud, identity, application, network)  
- Evidence quality scores and gap assessments  
- Timestamp normalization and hash validation outputs  
- Chain-of-custody review notes  
- Investigation prioritization and risk ordering plans  
- Escalation criteria for low-confidence findings  
- Legal-safe evidence handling recommendations  
- Record update notes and audit-trail documentation  
- Exception summaries and evidence packet outlines  

## Risks and mitigations  
- Mitigation: Guidance explicitly refuses to provide legal determinations or medical, financial, or safety-critical advice. Escalation paths are included for uncertain or ambiguous evidence.  
- Mitigation: Artifact handling advice is limited to non-destructive, auditable procedures. No destructive analysis or live system modification is recommended.  

## Validation  
- 18 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1.0  
- Source coverage includes NIST Computer Security Resource Center, CISA, and other authoritative digital forensics and incident response standards  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- Requires access to digital artifacts, logs, and system metadata to function effectively  
- Cannot interpret encrypted or inaccessible data without decryption or access  
- Does not provide legal interpretations or binding conclusions, only analysis guidance  
- Cannot replace hands-on forensic tools or expert judgment in complex cases  
- Cannot perform real-time system analysis or modify live systems

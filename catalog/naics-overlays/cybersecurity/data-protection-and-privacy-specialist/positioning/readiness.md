# Specialist Overview  
The data protection and privacy specialist delivers practical, evidence-backed guidance to design, operate, and improve privacy-protective technical and administrative controls for regulated and non-regulated data. It focuses on minimizing privacy risks, ensuring compliance with data protection obligations, and enabling teams to make clear, defensible decisions about data handling.

## Who uses this  
- Security engineer  
- Privacy engineer  
- Privacy/compliance advisor  
- GRC analyst  
- Platform operator  
- Product security partner  
- Orchestrator  

## What it produces  
- Classification matrix and control mapping  
- Control or requirement summary  
- Evidence packet or checklist  
- Prioritized remediation backlog with effort/risk estimates  
- Privacy impact assessment and residual-risk rationale  
- Runbook-style recommendations with evidence references  
- Exception escalation note  
- Scope-limitation notes and escalation guidance  
- Adjacent-specialist or orchestrator handoff package  

## Risks and mitigations  
The specialist refuses to provide legal interpretations, perform penetration testing, or execute incident response. It ensures work is routed to the correct specialist or human reviewer when legal, technical execution, or regulatory authority is required, to avoid misjudgment in high-stakes scenarios such as breach response or regulatory compliance.

## Validation  
- 20 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Covers data protection, privacy engineering, and control mapping based on NIST standards  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
What the user or organization must supply before this specialist can work reliably:  
- Defined data inventory and classification criteria  
- Regulatory and contractual obligations for data handling  
- Existing technical and administrative controls  
- Evidence of current data flows and processing activities  
- Access to vendor or processor documentation  

What is explicitly out of scope:  
- Performing penetration testing, exploit validation, or red-team execution  
- Executing incident response, containment, eradication, recovery, or breach-notification filing  
- Providing legal opinions, interpreting statutes definitively, or issuing audit attestation reports  
- Taking end-to-end ownership of enterprise architecture, product design, or platform engineering  
- Approving final risk acceptance, procurement, or production launch decisions  
- Issuing compliance seals, certifications, or regulatory endorsements

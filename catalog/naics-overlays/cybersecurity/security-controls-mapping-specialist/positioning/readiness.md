# Specialist Overview  
The security controls mapping specialist translates security requirements, control catalogs, policies, and evidence into traceable mapping products, coverage findings, and review-ready summaries. It supports analysis, guidance, troubleshooting, and artifact production within the control mapping domain.  

## Who uses this  
- Security engineer  
- GRC analyst  
- Control owner  
- Compliance reviewer  
- Risk analyst  

## What it produces  
- Control mapping matrix with lineage  
- Coverage gap and overlap report  
- Risk-prioritized remediation backlog  
- Escalation decisions with explicit assumptions  
- Readiness evidence summary  
- Quality or safety review note  
- Control or incident summary  
- Corrective-action escalation  

## Risks and mitigations  
- Explicit refusal to perform audit attestation, incident response, or security testing ensures no critical authority is assumed outside the mapping domain.  

## Validation  
- 20 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1.0  
- Source coverage includes NIST SP 800-53, NIST CSF 2.0, CIS Controls, ISO/IEC 27001, PCI DSS, OWASP ASVS, and tenant-specific control taxonomies  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
**What the user or organization must supply before this specialist can work reliably**  
- A defined scope of requirements, control frameworks, and tenant-specific policies  
- Access to relevant regulatory, contractual, and risk context  
- Existing evidence records or clear indicators of evidence availability  
- Industry or sector context that affects control applicability  

**What is explicitly out of scope**  
- Performing penetration tests, exploitation, vulnerability validation, or adversary simulation  
- Conducting incident response containment, triage, eradication, or forensic workflows  
- Providing formal audit attestation, certification, or assessor signoff  
- Owning broad enterprise architecture, identity platform migration, or network redesign  
- Replacing operational ops, policy drafting, secure SDLC governance, legal interpretation, or privacy counsel  
- Bypassing quality, safety, release, go-live, or control-owner approval boundaries

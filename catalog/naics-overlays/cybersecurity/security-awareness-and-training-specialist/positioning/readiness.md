# Specialist Overview  
The security awareness and training specialist helps organizations design and improve cybersecurity and privacy learning programs that reduce human-centered cyber risk, improve reporting behavior, and produce measurable resilience outcomes. The specialist focuses on awareness strategy, curriculum design, and measurement without entering operational security or advisory roles.

## Who uses this  
- Security awareness program owners  
- Security leaders  
- Privacy leaders  
- IT leaders  
- Learning and enablement teams  
- Risk and compliance reviewers  
- Orchestrator  

## What it produces  
- Security awareness program strategy and operating model definitions  
- Role-based awareness and training curricula  
- Training recommendations prioritized by risk, audience, and impact  
- Reviews of security training evidence with improvement recommendations  
- Measurable awareness KPIs, metric definitions, and measurement plans  
- Metric or reporting packages with caveats, reconciliation notes, and source lineage  
- Interpretations of phishing-simulation results with follow-up coaching recommendations  
- Training guidance adapted to industry, role, policy, language, privacy, and contractual constraints  
- Evidence-backed recommendations for awareness program improvements  
- Routing decisions for work that requires adjacent specialist or human review  

## Risks and mitigations  
- **Mitigation for overstatement of authority**: The specialist is configured to avoid overstating its authority or making claims beyond evidence-based training design and measurement.  
- **Mitigation for high-risk interpretation**: The specialist does not execute phishing simulations or make final decisions on security incidents, instead recommending follow-up or routing to appropriate human reviewers.  
- **Mitigation for policy misalignment**: Guidance is adapted to tenant-specific constraints, and recommendations are flagged when they may conflict with industry or legal standards.  

## Validation  
- 18 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Coverage includes awareness strategy, role-based curriculum design, KPI development, and training evidence review  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- **User or organizational prerequisites**:  
  - Must provide a clear understanding of organizational risk profile  
  - Must provide role definitions, industry constraints, and privacy policies  
  - Must provide access to prior training evidence or simulation results (if applicable)  
- **Out of scope**:  
  - Operational security design or execution  
  - Phishing simulation execution or management  
  - Final decision-making on compliance or legal matters  
  - Direct employee coaching or behavior change interventions  
  - Development of technical security controls or tools

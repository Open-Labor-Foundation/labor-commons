# Specialist Overview  
The penetration testing specialist provides expert, actionable guidance for authorized security assessments. It ensures scope and rules of engagement are valid, designs effective tests, interprets evidence, prioritizes risks, and prepares clear documentation for audits and remediation.

## Who uses this  
- Security manager  
- Application security lead  
- Vulnerability management lead  
- MSSP assessment coordinator  
- Technical reviewer  

## What it produces  
- Scoped test plan recommendations  
- Finding interpretation with confidence and risk scoring  
- Prioritized remediation guidance and next-step verification checklist  
- Out-of-scope and escalation notes  
- Clear, audit-friendly validation rationale  
- Record update note  
- Document or evidence packet  
- Audit-trail or exception summary  

## Risks and mitigations  
- Explicitly refuses to perform or recommend execution of exploitation commands, destructive testing, or unauthorized testing methods.  
- Mitigates risk of scope overreach by validating rules of engagement and escalating unauthorized or unsafe requests.  
- Avoids providing formal compliance or audit signoff language, ensuring outputs are advisory only.  

## Validation  
- 18 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1.0  
- Source coverage includes National Institute of Standards and Technology (NIST) and Cybersecurity and Infrastructure Security Agency (CISA)  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- What the user or organization must supply before this specialist can work reliably:  
  - Clear, written authorization for the assessment  
  - Defined scope and rules of engagement  
  - Access to relevant documentation, scan artifacts, or system descriptions  
  - Identified asset classes, data sensitivity levels, and tenant constraints  

- What is explicitly out of scope:  
  - Executing exploitation commands, phishing kits, ransomware simulations, or destructive testing  
  - Performing incident-response containment, eradication, or recovery  
  - Providing formal compliance attestations or audit signoff language  
  - Performing web coding, secure coding implementation, or infrastructure changes  
  - Deciding legal, regulatory, contractual, financial, privacy, or executive approval outcomes

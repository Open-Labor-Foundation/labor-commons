# Threat intelligence specialist  
A specialist that transforms raw cyber threat data into actionable, evidence-backed intelligence for defensive planning, with explicit confidence handling and prioritization guidance.

## Who uses this  
- Threat intelligence analyst  
- SOC shift lead  
- Detection engineering reviewer  
- Security operations manager  
- Incident response coordinator  
- Cyber risk manager  
- Managed detection and response reviewer  
- Human security reviewer  

## What it produces  
- Priority-ranked threat assessment memo  
- Evidence-backed mapping to attacker techniques  
- Confidence-rated recommendations and mitigation guardrails  
- Schedule or allocation recommendation  
- Constraint log  
- Source reliability and freshness note  
- Collection gap and missing evidence checklist  
- Source or policy conflict note  
- Escalation note for approval-required tradeoffs  
- Out-of-scope handoff summary  

## Risks and mitigations  
- Refuses exploit execution, penetration testing, live containment, eradication, production blocklist changes, formal audit attestation, legal notices, or enterprise architecture ownership.  
- Escalates low-confidence, conflicting-source, regulated-disclosure, cross-tenant, or authority-sensitive cases to human reviewers or adjacent specialists.  

## Validation  
- 20 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Source coverage includes NIST, CISA, and other authoritative cyber threat intelligence references.  
- Human verification and functional audit completed.  

## Limitations  
- Requires user or organization to supply threat intelligence requirements, IOCs, ATT&CK-aligned behavior, asset criticality, source reliability, tenant policy, and confidence thresholds.  
- Out of scope:  
  - Execution of exploit, penetration-test, red-team, malware, adversary emulation, or vulnerability validation activity.  
  - Performing live incident containment, eradication, recovery, host isolation, account action, firewall edits, blocklist changes, or patch deployment.  
  - Issuing formal audit attestation, compliance signoff, regulator-ready language, breach notification, legal opinion, materiality decision, or customer notice.  
  - Owning full enterprise architecture, security architecture, cloud architecture, identity architecture, network redesign, or detection engineering implementation.  
  - Claiming final budget, staffing, operational, policy-exception, or resource approval; planning outputs are recommendations only.  
  - Treating the cybersecurity industry label as cosmetic; requires CTI artifacts, source reliability, systems of record, sharing markings, threat taxonomy, and security authority boundaries.

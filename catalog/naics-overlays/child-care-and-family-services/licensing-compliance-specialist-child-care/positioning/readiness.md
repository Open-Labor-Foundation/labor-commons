# Specialist Overview  
The licensing compliance specialist child care translates child-care-specific licensing, monitoring, background-check, training, and corrective-action obligations into evidence-backed compliance outputs for child care centers, family child care homes, and similar provider settings. It focuses on obligation mapping, evidence readiness, and escalation framing without claiming legal authority or regulatory interpretation.

## Who uses this  
- Licensing-compliance-manager  
- Center-director  
- Family-child-care-support-manager  
- Program-operations-manager  
- Quality-and-compliance-coordinator  
- Reviewer  

## What it produces  
- Control or obligation matrix tied to evidence owners  
- Evidence readiness or gap summary  
- Exception and remediation escalation note  
- Control and obligation summary tied to review cadence  
- Inspection or attestation preparation packet  
- Explicit refusal, blocked-decision, or adjacent-lane routing note  

## Risks and mitigations  
- Returns all legal, regulatory, safety-critical, or policy-override decisions to adjacent specialists or human reviewers to avoid overstepping authority.  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Source coverage includes 8 from Administration for Children and Families, Office of Child Care, ChildCare.gov  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- What the user or organization must supply before this specialist can work reliably:  
  - Minimum tenant facts  
  - Authoritative records  
  - Source context for compliance handling  
- What is explicitly out of scope:  
  - Providing legal advice, regulatory interpretation, or formal licensing opinion  
  - Negotiating with regulators, inspectors, or licensing agencies on behalf of the tenant  
  - Signing or certifying corrective-action closure, licensing renewals, waivers, or exemption determinations  
  - Adjudicating disqualifying criminal history, abuse registry matches, or other background-check outcomes  
  - Overriding child-safety, supervision, staffing, clinical, engineering, or executive approval boundaries

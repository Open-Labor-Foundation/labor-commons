# Specialist Overview  
The program documentation specialist for child care maintains and organizes records and documentation specific to child-care programs, ensuring completeness and audit-readiness for enrollment, health and safety, licensing, subsidy, and staff workflows. It works strictly within administrative documentation scope, without making legal, clinical, or policy decisions.

## Who uses this  
- Child care center administrator  
- Family child care operator  
- Program documentation coordinator  
- Licensing and compliance coordinator  
- Subsidy administration support staff  
- Operations-orchestration manager  

## What it produces  
- Document or records completeness summary with missing items, owning role, and due dates  
- Administrative status note with blockers, next owner, and required follow-up  
- Record update note with source-of-truth references and audit-trail summary  
- Document or evidence packet for licensing, subsidy, incident, or internal review workflows  
- Retention, access, privacy, or records-governance escalation package  

## Risks and mitigations  
- Refuses to delete, backdate, or alter official records outside administrative maintenance  
- Rejects requests to make licensing, clinical, legal, or subsidy decisions  
- Routes decisions to the correct specialist when administrative documentation is complete but not sufficient for a final determination  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Covers U.S. child-care documentation and compliance standards from federal and state sources  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- Requires accurate and complete tenant or program facts before generating or validating documentation  
- Requires access to the most current version of all relevant records and forms  
- Requires routing decisions to adjacent specialists for non-documentation decisions  
- Does not handle licensing interpretation, clinical judgment, legal rulings, or subsidy eligibility determinations  
- Does not substitute for facility or program approval authority

# Specialist Overview  
The Enrollment Specialist Child Services validates intake and access prerequisites for child care and family services enrollment. It ensures that downstream work only begins when identity, eligibility, authorization, referral, slot, and setup conditions are satisfied.

## Who uses this  
- Enrollment coordinator  
- Family services specialist  
- Child care center director  
- Early Head Start ERS/EA staff  
- Subsidy or case intake team  
- Reviewer  

## What it produces  
- Intake disposition note with acceptance, rejection, pend, or routing outcome  
- Prerequisite or eligibility checklist showing missing or satisfied conditions  
- Escalation packet for requests blocked on approvals, access, identity mismatch, or source conflict  
- Intake or access disposition summary  
- Prerequisite or eligibility escalation note  

## Risks and mitigations  
- Refuses to approve clinical participation, legal interpretation, subsidy awards, licensing waivers, disability accommodation denials, or final placement decisions  
- Routes to appropriate specialists or human reviewers when authority shifts outside this lane  
- Validates minimum tenant facts and authoritative records to prevent unsafe or incomplete progress  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Source coverage includes 13 references from HeadStart.gov / Administration for Children and Families  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- What the user or organization must supply before this specialist can work reliably:  
  - Application, referral, or subsidy documents  
  - Funded-slot status  
  - Health and immunization records  
  - Consent forms  
  - Provider licensing context  

- What is explicitly out of scope:  
  - Awarding subsidy benefits or approving public-assistance eligibility  
  - Making binding legal determinations  
  - Waiving licensing, health, immunization, ratio, disability-access, or transportation requirements  
  - Converting intake authority into final classroom assignment, expulsion, suspension, or clinical participation authority  
  - Bypassing identity, eligibility, referral, reservation, or access checks to accelerate enrollment  
  - Denying disability access or deciding individualized accommodation sufficiency without compliance or program authority

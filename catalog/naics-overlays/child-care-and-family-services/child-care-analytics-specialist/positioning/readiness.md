# Specialist Overview  
The child care analytics specialist translates child care and family services records into trustworthy analysis with explicit metric logic, caveats, and action thresholds. It ensures child-care semantics are preserved and works within licensing, subsidy, privacy, health and safety, and program rules to produce safe and useful analytical outputs.  

## Who uses this  
- Child care reporting lead  
- Center director  
- Family child care network manager  
- Subsidy program operations manager  
- Head Start or Early Head Start program manager  
- Quality improvement lead  
- Reviewer  

## What it produces  
- Metric definition and data-source summary  
- Analysis or report output with caveats and confidence notes  
- Data-quality or disclosure-risk escalation note  
- Analytical brief with metric definitions, source logic, and confidence caveats  
- Exception note for data quality, denominator ambiguity, unsupported trend claims, or cross-system conflicts  
- Adjacent-specialist routing note  

## Risks and mitigations  
- Refuses to award or deny subsidy benefits, determine legal compliance, or publish unsupported causal or benchmark claims.  
- Rejects requests that exceed privacy or disclosure rules to avoid child-level or small-cell exposure.  
- Escalates to licensing, safety, subsidy, or quality improvement specialists when ownership shifts or policy authority changes the answer.  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Source coverage includes 9 authoritative references from the Child Care Technical Assistance Network and Administration for Children and Families.  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- What the user or organization must supply before this specialist can work reliably:  
  - Minimum tenant facts  
  - Authoritative records and source context  
  - Licensing, subsidy, privacy, and program rule status  
- What is explicitly out of scope:  
  - Awarding or denying subsidy benefits, eligibility, or provider payments  
  - Implied licensing approval, legal compliance, safety clearance, or final QRIS or accreditation status  
  - Publishing child-level or small-cell disclosures that exceed privacy or tenant rules  
  - Approving classroom assignments, expulsion, transportation, or staffing exceptions  
  - Collapsing child care data into generic social-services dashboards that ignore provider type, licensing, or subsidy dependencies

# Specialist Overview  
The family engagement specialist child care coordinates and tracks family-engagement workflows in child care settings, ensuring that tasks like outreach, referrals, and follow-ups are executed with clear ownership, documented evidence, and safe handoffs. It operates within defined boundaries to maintain workflow progress without overstepping into legal, clinical, or policy domains.

## Who uses this  
- Family-engagement-coordinator  
- Family-services-manager  
- Center-director  
- Teacher-lead  
- Subsidy-program-liaison  
- Community-partnership-coordinator  
- Reviewer  

## What it produces  
- Execution status update with lifecycle stage, evidence reviewed, current owner, and next action  
- Completion or fulfillment record showing which engagement action occurred and what evidence supports closure  
- Exception summary for blocked, returned, rework, or partially completed work  
- Handoff status summary with prior owner, next owner, required artifacts, and blocker state  
- Blocked-dependency escalation note  

## Risks and mitigations  
- Refuses to process custody disputes, subsidy eligibility determinations, child safety investigations, or clinical conclusions due to lack of authority.  
- Escalates missing evidence, ambiguous ownership, or family-safety concerns to appropriate reviewers.  
- Rejects unsupported release or completion requests when required documentation or authorization is missing.  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Based on U.S. Department of Health and Human Services (Electronic Code of Federal Regulations), HeadStart.gov (Office of Head Start)  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- **What the user or organization must supply before this specialist can work reliably**  
  - Documented child and family records  
  - Contact history and conference notes  
  - Referral status and subsidy or program context  
  - Community-resource dependencies and custody/release permissions  
  - Evidence of engagement actions (e.g., contact attempts, consent artifacts)  

- **What is explicitly out of scope**  
  - Deciding custody, guardianship, pickup rights, or visitation disputes  
  - Making child-safety, abuse, or neglect determinations  
  - Approving subsidy eligibility, fee waivers, or policy exceptions  
  - Releasing child or family information without required authorization  
  - Marking engagement complete without documented evidence of action

# Specialist Overview  
The curriculum support operations specialist resolves curriculum-support requests in the child care and family services industry by applying curriculum-specific records and operational boundaries. It ensures support stays focused on curriculum workflows without overstepping into instructional, compliance, or executive authority.

## Who uses this  
- Education manager  
- Curriculum coordinator  
- Center director  
- Site operations lead  
- Instructional coach  
- Family child care network coordinator  
- Reviewer  

## What it produces  
- Resolution note with request summary, evidence reviewed, action taken, and unresolved items  
- Customer or stakeholder communication summary  
- Escalation package for policy exceptions, repeat failures, unsupported asks, or missing-authority cases  
- Case or family-service workflow summary with status, dependencies, and next owner  
- Escalation note for records, consent, or high-sensitivity exceptions  

## Risks and mitigations  
- Explicitly refuses to issue final educational leadership judgment, override inclusion or licensing decisions, or release child records without proper authorization.  
- Requires full context and documentation before proceeding with any resolution or escalation.  
- Routs work to the appropriate specialist when authority is out of scope or insufficient.  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Source coverage includes 7 — Electronic Code of Federal Regulations (U.S. Department of Health and Human Services), HeadStart.gov (Office of Head Start)  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- What the user or organization must supply before this specialist can work reliably:  
  - Minimum tenant facts and authoritative records  
  - Curriculum, lesson plans, classroom observations, child-assessment summaries, and family communication notes  
  - Policy context and any relevant licensing or compliance records  
- What is explicitly out of scope:  
  - Approving or replacing tenant curriculum governance or educational leadership decisions  
  - Diagnosing developmental delay, determining special-education eligibility, or providing clinical, therapeutic, or legal advice  
  - Releasing child records, assessment details, or family information without required privacy and tenant authorization context  
  - Overriding staffing, budget, subsidy, procurement, or policy limits to resolve a curriculum-support case  
  - Treating the industry name as cosmetic or answering in generic customer-service language that omits curriculum-specific constraints

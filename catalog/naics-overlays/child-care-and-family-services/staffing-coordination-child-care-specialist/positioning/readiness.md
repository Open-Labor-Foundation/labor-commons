# Specialist Overview  
The staffing coordination child care specialist manages and coordinates staffing dependencies, handoffs, and coverage across child care and family services operations. It ensures classroom staffing ratios, provider qualifications, and required artifacts are tracked, routed, and confirmed with clear ownership and evidence at each step, without assuming final decision-making authority or regulatory approval roles.

## Who uses this  
- Staffing coordinator  
- Center director  
- Program operations manager  
- Scheduler  
- Family services manager  
- Licensing reviewer  
- Reviewer  

## What it produces  
- Handoff summary with prior owner, next owner, required artifacts, and closure condition  
- Dependency tracker or blocker summary with stale ownership and evidence gaps  
- Routing confirmation note for staffing coverage handoff  
- Blocked-dependency escalation note  
- Closure confirmation showing work landed with the correct destination owner  

## Risks and mitigations  
- Mitigates risk of misrouted staffing coverage by ensuring all handoffs include required artifacts and owner confirmation  
- Avoids assuming authority over child-safety judgments, hiring, or licensing approvals by explicitly routing to appropriate specialists or human reviewers  
- Prevents incomplete or unverified staffing decisions by validating prerequisites before moving coverage requests forward  

## Validation  
- 11 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Source coverage includes 10 references from Administration for Children and Families, Office of Child Care, ChildCare.gov  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
**What the user or organization must supply before this specialist can work reliably**  
- Current classroom census and age mix data  
- Provider qualification status and background check records  
- Active supervision posture and room assignment details  
- Licensing and credentialing status of all potential staff  
- Defined ownership boundaries for each stage of the staffing workflow  

**What is explicitly out of scope**  
- Hiring, terminating, disciplining, or scheduling staff as the final decision owner  
- Approving payroll, overtime, premium pay, or agency labor spend  
- Certifying licensing compliance, background-check sufficiency, or qualification exceptions  
- Interpreting jurisdiction-specific law or waiving ratio, active supervision, safe sleep, or credential rules  
- Absorbing classroom management, direct care, incident investigation, or parent-facing crisis response work

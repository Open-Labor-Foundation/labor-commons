# Specialist Overview  
The child care operations specialist manages the day-to-day operational workflows of child care settings, ensuring that classroom, transportation, attendance, and safety processes move safely and traceably. It uses child care-specific records and controls to track and update the status of operations, escalate exceptions, and confirm task completion without overstepping into regulatory, clinical, legal, or HR decisions.

## Who uses this  
- Center-director  
- Site-operations-coordinator  
- Program-manager  
- Transportation-coordinator  
- Classroom-operations-lead  
- Compliance-reviewer  
- Reviewer  

## What it produces  
- Execution status update with lifecycle stage and next action  
- Completion or fulfillment record showing what was done and what evidence supports closure  
- Exception summary for blocked, returned, or partially completed work  
- Case or family-service workflow summary with status, dependencies, and next owner  
- Escalation note for records, consent, release, or high-sensitivity exceptions  

## Risks and mitigations  
- Refuses to authorize child release to an adult not supported by the authoritative authorization record.  
- Refuses to complete tasks when inspection correction, incident closure, ratio restoration, or release evidence is missing.  
- Refuses to make clinical, legal, or licensing determinations and escalates these to the appropriate specialist.  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Source coverage includes 13 references from ChildCare.gov / Administration for Children and Families  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- What the user or organization must supply before this specialist can work reliably:  
  - Documented execution state and evidence for operational tasks  
  - Authorized release records  
  - Staffing and training records  
  - Transportation rosters  
  - Incident logs  
  - Corrective-action evidence  
  - Room or site readiness evidence  
- What is explicitly out of scope:  
  - Issuing licensing interpretations, waivers, variances, or final compliance signoff  
  - Deciding subsidy eligibility, enrollment acceptance, or child placement outside the documented operations handoff boundary  
  - Making clinical, medication-prescribing, mental-health, child-protection, or legal determinations  
  - Authorizing release of a child to an adult not supported by the authoritative authorization record  
  - Marking operational work complete when inspection correction, incident closure, ratio restoration, or release evidence is missing

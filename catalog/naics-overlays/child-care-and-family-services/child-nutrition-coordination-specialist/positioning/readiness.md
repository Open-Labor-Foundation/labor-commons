# Specialist Overview  
The child nutrition coordination specialist manages the flow of child-care nutrition coordination work across teams, ensuring that meal-service, dietary-accommodation, and CACFP reimbursement workflows move forward only with complete, accurate, and evidence-backed information. It prepares handoff summaries, tracks dependencies, and routes work to the correct specialist when clinical, fiscal, or operational ownership is needed.

## Who uses this  
- Nutrition coordinator  
- Center director  
- Food service manager  
- Family child care network coordinator  
- Head Start health or nutrition manager  
- CACFP sponsor liaison  
- Reviewer  

## What it produces  
- Handoff summary naming prior owner, next owner, required artifacts, and closure condition  
- Dependency tracker or blocker summary with missing records, stale ownership, and escalation target  
- Routing confirmation note for child-nutrition handoff  
- Blocked-dependency escalation note  
- Closure confirmation showing work landed with the right destination owner and claim or service state  

## Risks and mitigations  
- Refuses to proceed when required medical statements, care plans, meal-pattern rules, or sponsor oversight are missing or incomplete  
- Avoids acting as final authority on clinical, fiscal, licensing, or food-safety decisions by routing to the correct specialist  
- Requires complete and authoritative records before generating any handoff or routing confirmation  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Covers U.S. Department of Agriculture Food and Nutrition Service requirements and child-care nutrition context  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- Requires complete and accurate source records (e.g., attendance, meal counts, medical statements) before proceeding  
- Requires clear ownership boundaries between classroom, kitchen, family, and sponsor teams  
- Requires access to meal-pattern references, dietary accommodation logs, and infant feeding instructions  

- Does not approve CACFP claims, overclaim resolutions, budget spend, procurement changes, or reimbursement policy exceptions  
- Does not diagnose allergies, prescribe diets, determine clinical feeding plans, or substitute for a physician, registered dietitian, nurse, or health specialist  
- Does not certify food-safety compliance, sanitation, licensing waivers, or kitchen permit status  
- Does not answer in generic office-catering language; must use child-care-specific nutrition terminology and context  
- Does not absorb meal preparation, kitchen execution, classroom feeding supervision, or family-approval work that belongs to adjacent owners

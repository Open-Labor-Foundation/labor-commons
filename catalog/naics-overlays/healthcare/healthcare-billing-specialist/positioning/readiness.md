# Specialist Overview  
The Healthcare Billing Specialist is responsible for converting provider-side healthcare service documentation into clean billing outputs and managing the administrative aspects of the revenue cycle. It ensures that claims, remittances, denials, and reconciliations are prepared with traceable evidence, while surfacing missing documentation and exceptions for appropriate escalation.

## Who uses this  
- Healthcare billing specialist  
- Claims follow-up specialist  
- Payment posting specialist  
- Denial coordinator  
- Revenue-cycle manager  
- Patient accounts lead  
- Practice management operations lead  

## What it produces  
- Claim-readiness summary with missing documentation, coverage, authorization, referral, eligibility, provider, place-of-service, payer, filing-limit, or coding-support issues  
- Remittance and denial reconciliation note with categorized root cause, matched and unmatched records, variance amount, source references, and next owner  
- Escalation packet for appeal, coding, access, payer-policy, privacy, compliance, accounting, refund, write-off, or patient-safety follow-up  
- Billing or financial readiness summary with traceable source-record evidence and unresolved blocker register  
- Unsupported financial-change refusal with rationale, evidence gap, and allowed handoff route  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Source coverage includes CMS-1500, UB-04, 837P, 837I, 835 ERA, EFT, EOB, CARC, RARC, COB, MSP, NCCI edit, prior authorization, referral, eligibility, timely filing, and denial inventory  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- What the user or organization must supply before this specialist can work reliably:  
  - Authoritative documentation of services rendered  
  - Coverage and eligibility records  
  - Authorization and referral status  
  - Payer-specific context and rules  
  - Timely filing and filing limit information  
  - Remittance advice and EOBs  
  - Patient ledger and adjustment logs  
- What is explicitly out of scope:  
  - Assigning final diagnosis or procedure codes  
  - Rewriting medical records or encounter documentation  
  - Determining medical necessity or level of care  
  - Interpreting payer policy or reimbursement law  
  - Approving unsupported write-offs or waivers  
  - Disclosing PHI beyond minimum necessary for payment  
  - Making licensed clinical, legal, accounting, tax, privacy, or formal appeal determinations

# Specialist Overview  
The healthcare quality reporting specialist transforms healthcare data from clinical, claims, lab, and registry sources into submission-ready quality reporting packages aligned with specific measure specifications and regulatory requirements. This specialist ensures that all outputs are traceable, evidence-based, and ready for internal review or external submission.

## Who uses this  
- Quality-reporting-manager  
- Clinical-quality-analyst  
- HEDIS-reporting-lead  
- eCQM-program-manager  
- Hospital-quality-reporting-specialist  
- Registry-reporting-analyst  
- Population-health-quality-lead  
- Compliance-reviewer  

## What it produces  
- Measure-specification summary with version, source hierarchy, measurement period, numerator, denominator, exclusions, caveats, and authority source  
- Submission-readiness note with validation status, missing abstraction, data-completeness gaps, exclusion handling, source conflicts, and signoff blockers  
- Quality-report exception register for disputed measure logic, missing evidence, stale specifications, unsupported claims, validation failures, and escalation owner  
- Source-lineage map linking measure output to EHR, quality platform, claims, lab, registry, BI, QRDA, HEDIS, or portal artifacts  
- Privacy, PHI, low-volume, disclosure-risk, or clinical-boundary escalation packet  

## Risks and mitigations  
- **Risk**: Misinterpretation of measure logic could lead to submission errors or noncompliance.  
  **Mitigation**: Specialist refuses to override official measure logic, and escalates disputed logic to subject matter experts for resolution.  
- **Risk**: Inclusion of protected health information (PHI) in outputs.  
  **Mitigation**: Specialist applies minimum-necessary and disclosure controls and escalates any patient or member examples for approval.  
- **Risk**: Quality reporting may be submitted with unresolved data issues.  
  **Mitigation**: Specialist flags validation errors, missing evidence, and signoff blockers in submission-readiness notes and routes unresolved issues for escalation.  

## Validation  
- 13 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Source coverage includes Centers for Medicare & Medicaid Services, Assistant Secretary for Technology Policy / eCQI Resource Center  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- **What must be supplied**: Jurisdiction, program, product, payer or plan, provider type, facility type, service setting, reporting period, measurement year, submission deadline, and intended audience facts  
- **Out of scope**:  
  - Certifying quality results, attesting submissions, or approving public reporting  
  - Overriding official measure logic, value sets, inclusion rules, exclusions, data-completeness thresholds, or submission validation outcomes  
  - Inferring clinical facts, diagnosis, treatment decisions, patient safety, medical necessity, or care quality from incomplete source records  
  - Reinterpreting payer policy, coverage, authorization, referral, coding, or claim adjudication rules as final authority  
  - Publishing or implying certainty when source quality, measure definitions, signoff, public-reporting posture, or disclosure approval is unresolved  
  - Disclosing PHI, low-volume cohorts, patient/member examples, or clinician-level detail without minimum-necessary and tenant approval context

# Specialist Overview  
The healthcare documentation specialist ensures healthcare records are complete, traceable, and compliant with documentation integrity rules without altering clinical content. It classifies documentation requests, validates record completeness, and routes deficiencies or amendments to the correct owners.

## Who uses this  
- Health information management (HIM) teams  
- Record completion and deficiency coordinators  
- Release-of-information (ROI) coordinators  
- Documentation integrity reviewers  
- Coding-support and audit-preparation teams  
- Clinical documentation operations leaders  
- Spec-pack reviewers  

## What it produces  
- Record-completeness-summary  
- ROI-or-amendment-routing-note  
- Record-update-note  
- Document-or-evidence-packet  
- Audit-trail-or-exception-summary  
- Deficiency-owner-routing-note  
- Privacy-or-legal-hold-escalation  
- Adjacent-specialist-routing-note  
- Low-confidence-escalation-note  

## Risks and mitigations  
- Refuses to author, rewrite, infer, backdate, delete, conceal, or clinically interpret clinical documentation content.  
- Refuses to disclose PHI or prepare disclosure-ready records without valid authorization, legal basis, or access controls.  
- Refuses to approve, deny, or certify record releases, amendments, legal holds, or privacy-law interpretations.  
- Routes clinical content or safety judgments to the clinician owner or licensed review lane.  

## Validation  
- 13 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Draws from 10 sources in the U.S. Department of Health and Human Services, Office for Civil Rights, and Electronic Code of Federal Regulations  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- Requires patient or member identity, encounter context, service dates, provider or facility type, payer or plan context, requested record purpose, legal-health-record or designated-record-set scope, and tenant source-of-truth hierarchy to produce accurate summaries.  
- Cannot author, rewrite, infer, backdate, delete, conceal, or clinically interpret medical-record content.  
- Cannot approve or deny record releases, amendments, legal holds, or privacy-law interpretations.  
- Cannot decide CPT, HCPCS, ICD-10-CM, DRG, E/M level, modifier, claim submission, coverage, benefit, prior authorization, referral validity, medical necessity, or payer appeal outcome.  
- Cannot disclose PHI without valid authorization, legal basis, requester authority, access controls, and minimum-necessary or designated-record-set constraints.

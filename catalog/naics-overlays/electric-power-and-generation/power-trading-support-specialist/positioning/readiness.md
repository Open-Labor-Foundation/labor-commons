# Specialist Overview  
The power trading support specialist focuses on resolving and triaging support cases in the wholesale power trading industry using authoritative records like trade tickets, transmission reservations, e-Tags, EIR entries, ISO or RTO submission notices, settlement statements, and invoice or payment advice. The specialist ensures that issues are addressed within clear market and operational boundaries, without overstepping into areas requiring trade strategy, legal interpretation, or regulatory signoff.

## Who uses this  
- Power markets support analysts  
- Power scheduling support leads  
- Settlements and billing analysts  
- Trade operations coordinators  
- Market operations managers  
- Utility commercial operations reviewers  

## What it produces  
- Resolution note with request summary, action taken, authoritative evidence used, and unresolved items  
- Customer or stakeholder communication summary  
- Escalation package for exceptions, unsupported asks, complaints, or repeated failures  
- Market or settlement status summary with required evidence and next owner  
- Regulated-threshold escalation note  

## Risks and mitigations  
- Requests involving legal, regulatory, or policy-override authority are explicitly refused or escalated to prevent misrepresentation or unauthorized action.  
- Communication summaries are limited to factual, evidence-backed statements to avoid overstating resolution or authority.  
- All outputs reference actual market and operational constraints to prevent misleading stakeholders or violating separation rules for transmission or confidential data.  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Source coverage includes ETRM/CTRM systems, ISO/RTO market portals, settlement reporting tools, OASIS, e-Tag, EIR, ticketing, credit, and document repositories  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- **What the user or organization must supply before this specialist can work reliably**  
  - Named market records (trade tickets, transmission reservations, e-Tags, EIR entries, ISO/RTO submission notices)  
  - Clear indication of the issue type (schedule, settlement, invoice, dispute, communication)  
  - Access to the relevant systems of record and evidence of current workflow state  

- **What is explicitly out of scope**  
  - Submitting, approving, pricing, or recommending bids, offers, trade strategy, or hedge strategy  
  - Approving credit limit changes, collateral moves, invoice netting decisions, payment releases, or settlement write-offs  
  - Providing legal, tariff, market-monitoring, standards-of-conduct, or regulatory-reporting interpretation or signoff  
  - Operationalizing non-public transmission-function information in ways that would violate separation rules  
  - Stating that a settlement, invoice, trade, or regulatory reporting outcome is final when authoritative records do not support that statement  
  - Treating the electric-power label as cosmetic; all outputs must reference actual market and operational constraints

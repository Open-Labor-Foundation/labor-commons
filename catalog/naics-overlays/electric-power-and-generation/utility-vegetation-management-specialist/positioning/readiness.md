# Specialist Overview  
The utility vegetation management specialist plans, schedules, and allocates vegetation work for electric power and generation operations by converting patrol demand, circuit risk, land-access constraints, contractor capacity, and compliance thresholds into bounded vegetation work recommendations. It ensures evidence-backed prioritization and provides clear constraint logs and escalation guidance.

## Who uses this  
- Utility vegetation manager  
- Wildfire-mitigation program manager  
- Transmission right-of-way planner  
- Distribution vegetation coordinator  
- Contract forestry manager  
- Utility forester  
- Reviewer  

## What it produces  
- Utility vegetation schedule, capacity, or allocation recommendation  
- Constraint log capturing assumptions, tradeoffs, permit/access blockers, and unresolved evidence gaps  
- Approval-needed summary for tradeoffs that require safety, environmental, legal, or budget authority  
- Field or permitting status summary with required evidence, blocked step, and next owner  
- Regulated-threshold escalation note for MVCD risk, wildfire commitments, or safety/land-use conflicts  

## Risks and mitigations  
- Explicitly refuses to grant switching authority, certify line-clearance safety, approve herbicide or environmental compliance actions, sign final wildfire-mitigation attestations, or issue legal/easement determinations.  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Covers Federal Energy Regulatory Commission, North American Electric Reliability Corporation, Occupational Safety and Health Administration, and California Office of Energy Infrastructure Safety compliance requirements  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
**What the user or organization must supply before this specialist can work reliably:**  
- Patrol, LiDAR, inspection, and landowner/access findings  
- Approved tie-break policies  
- Circuit scope, asset class, risk zone, clearance objective, permit/access status  
- GIS/asset records and contractor availability data  

**What is explicitly out of scope:**  
- Issuing switching authority or energized-work approval  
- Providing final legal/easement interpretation or environmental/herbicide signoff  
- Marking work as regulator-complete or wildfire-plan compliant without tenant evidence and delegated approver authority  
- Collapsing into adjacent lanes such as outage dispatch, engineering design, land-rights counsel, or field-execution supervision

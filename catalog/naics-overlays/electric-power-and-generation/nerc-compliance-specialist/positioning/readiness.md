# Specialist Overview  
The NERC compliance specialist supports the Electric Power and Generation domain by translating NERC reliability obligations into actionable compliance workflows. It maps NERC requirements to the registered entity, validates evidence readiness, prepares compliance artifacts, tracks exceptions, and escalates issues beyond its scope.

## Who uses this  
- NERC-compliance-manager  
- Compliance-analyst  
- Generation-operations-manager  
- Transmission-operations-manager  
- Internal-controls-lead  
- Risk-and-audit-manager  
- Reviewer  

## What it produces  
- Control or obligation matrix tied to the lane  
- Evidence readiness or gap summary  
- Exception and remediation escalation note  
- Control and obligation summary tied to evidence owners  
- Exception and remediation note for control gaps or policy conflicts  

## Risks and mitigations  
- The specialist refuses to interpret legal obligations, make engineering judgments, or sign off on compliance. It explicitly escalates these to legal, engineering, or executive teams to prevent unauthorized actions.

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Covers core NERC compliance domains including CMEP monitoring, evidence flows, internal-control assessments, and periodic data submittals  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- The user or organization must supply:  
  - NERC registration details for the entity  
  - Evidence artifacts such as self-reports, logs, or mitigation summaries  
  - Context about control ownership, review cadence, and standard applicability  
- What is explicitly out of scope:  
  - Providing legal advice or formal legal interpretation of NERC or FERC requirements  
  - Certifying compliance or submitting regulator-facing attestations  
  - Making engineering or operational safety judgments  
  - Acting as a certified operator or regulator-facing representative  
  - Using generic compliance processes in place of NERC-specific controls

# Specialist Overview  
The Edge and Remote Site Infrastructure Specialist provides actionable recommendations for designing and maintaining infrastructure in edge and remote locations. It focuses on improving reliability, managing intermittent connectivity, and ensuring predictable operations under constrained conditions.

## Who uses this  
- Edge platform engineer  
- Remote-site operations engineer  
- Cloud platform architect  
- Site reliability engineer  
- Hybrid cloud operations lead  

## What it produces  
- Architecture and resilience recommendations with explicit tradeoffs  
- Operational playbooks with checkpoints, owner responsibilities, and escalation paths  
- Deployment and rollback sequencing with verification criteria  
- Clear boundary handoffs when requests cross adjacent specialties  
- Escalation or refusal notes when facts, authority, safety, policy, or confidence are insufficient  

## Risks and mitigations  
- The specialist explicitly refuses to make recommendations involving execution, approval, legal, procurement, or licensed authority.  
- Work is routed to adjacent specialists or human reviewers when ownership or authority shifts, preventing unqualified action.  

## Validation  
- 18 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1.0  
- Covers Amazon Web Services and Microsoft Learn as primary source material  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- Requires accurate and complete context about site constraints, connectivity conditions, and autonomy requirements before generating recommendations.  
- Cannot provide execution, legal, procurement, or licensed authority.  
- Does not address physical infrastructure installation, maintenance, or on-site safety.  
- Does not resolve conflicts between stakeholder priorities or budget constraints.

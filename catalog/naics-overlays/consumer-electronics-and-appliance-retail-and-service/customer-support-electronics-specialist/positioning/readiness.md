# Specialist Overview  
The customer support electronics specialist resolves support requests for consumer electronics and appliance retail or service workflows using authoritative records and policy-aware communication. This specialist ensures consistent and accurate handling of order, delivery, warranty, repair, refund, and service-complaint issues within the defined electronics support domain.

## Who uses this  
- Store customer support teams  
- Omnichannel support leads  
- Service-desk supervisors  
- Contact-center operations  
- Reviewers  

## What it produces  
- Resolution note with request summary, evidence used, action taken, and unresolved items  
- Customer or stakeholder communication summary with safe next-step language  
- Escalation package for exceptions, complaints, unsupported asks, or repeated failures  
- Request-resolution summary with issue, action taken, blocker state, and next owner  
- Exception communication escalation note for policy, refund, fraud, or safety-sensitive cases  

## Risks and mitigations  
- For safety-sensitive cases such as recalled products or damaged batteries, the specialist refuses to finalize decisions and routes to the appropriate safety owner.  
- For legal or fraud-sensitive issues, the specialist refuses to provide interpretations and packages the case for legal or loss-prevention review.  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Covers key authority sources including the Federal Trade Commission and Consumer Financial Protection Bureau  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- What the user or organization must supply before this specialist can work reliably  
  - Access to POS, OMS, CRM, service-desk, warranty, and inventory records  
  - Defined policy documentation for electronics and appliance support  
  - Clear escalation paths to adjacent specialists for exceptions and escalations  
- What is explicitly out of scope  
  - Pricing overrides, refund exceptions, or service-recovery approvals  
  - Legal interpretation of warranty, refund, lemon-law, or consumer-protection rules  
  - Fraud adjudication, chargeback decisions, or loss-prevention rulings  
  - Final repair diagnosis, engineering judgment, or warranty adjudication  
  - Hazardous product handling or damaged-battery decisions

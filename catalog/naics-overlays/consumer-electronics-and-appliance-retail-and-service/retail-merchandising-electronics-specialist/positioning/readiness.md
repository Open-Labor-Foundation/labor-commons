# Retail merchandising electronics specialist  
A specialist focused on executing and documenting retail merchandising tasks for consumer electronics and appliance stores, ensuring accuracy in pricing, display, order fulfillment, and post-sale compliance, while strictly adhering to policy and system controls.  

## Who uses this  
- Store operations manager  
- Merchandising manager  
- Omnichannel fulfillment lead  
- Guest services lead  
- Loss prevention coordinator  
- Reviewer  

## What it produces  
- Execution status update with lifecycle stage, blocker class, and next action  
- Completion evidence record with merchandising, pickup, or display proof  
- Exception queue note for blocked, reworked, returned, or partially completed work  
- Production status summary with queue state, blockers, and quality or safety dependencies  
- Escalation note for pricing, refund, fraud, stop-sale, or throughput constraints  

## Risks and mitigations  
- Refuses to approve price overrides, policy exceptions, or recall product reactivation without clear authority  
- Rejects work completion when required audit evidence is missing  
- Limits escalation to named specialists for fraud, legal, or financial decisions  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Source coverage includes POS, OMS, CRM, service desk, and inventory records  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- Must be provided accurate SKU, UPC, model, serial, promo, and order records before execution can proceed  
- Cannot issue legal interpretations or finalize fraud, refund, or chargeback decisions  
- Cannot override advertised pricing or release recalled or safety-questioned products into inventory  
- Cannot approve policy exceptions without explicit authority  
- Cannot adjudicate consumer-protection, privacy, or recall law matters

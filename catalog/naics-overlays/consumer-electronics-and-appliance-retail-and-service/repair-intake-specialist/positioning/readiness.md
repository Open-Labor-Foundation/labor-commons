# Specialist Overview  
The repair intake specialist ensures that repair requests for consumer electronics and appliances meet intake criteria before being routed to downstream specialists. It validates customer identity, product eligibility, authorization, and safety prerequisites, ensuring that service processes only begin when all requirements are satisfied and evidence-backed.

## Who uses this  
- Store service-desk teams  
- Repair depot intake coordinators  
- Field-service dispatch intake teams  
- Warranty and protection-plan operations  
- Work orchestration teams  

## What it produces  
- Intake disposition note with acceptance, rejection, pending, or routing outcome  
- Eligibility or prerequisite checklist showing missing or satisfied conditions  
- Routing or escalation summary for wrong-lane, safety, fraud, pricing, or policy-exception cases  
- Intake or access disposition summary with named evidence artifacts  
- Prerequisite or eligibility escalation note for low-confidence or missing-record cases  

## Risks and mitigations  
- Refuses to process requests lacking verified customer identity, product ownership, or authorization.  
- Screens for recall or safety conditions that require escalation to safety or engineering teams.  
- Avoids approving or overriding pricing, refunds, fraud investigations, or final repair approval.  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Source coverage includes 9 authority sources: Federal Trade Commission, California Department of Consumer Affairs, Bureau of Household Goods and Services  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
### What the user or organization must supply  
- Customer identity documentation or proof of ownership  
- Product identification and service history records  
- Evidence of service eligibility, estimate authorization, or appointment confirmation  
- Access to internal systems for prerequisite checks  
- Escalation channels for fraud, safety, or policy exceptions  

### What is explicitly out of scope  
- Approving repair completion or final service pricing  
- Technical diagnosis or root-cause analysis of appliance or electronics failures  
- Finalizing warranty coverage or overriding pricing rules  
- Clearing recall, safety, or battery-related conditions  
- Adjudicating fraud, stolen property, or identity-theft cases

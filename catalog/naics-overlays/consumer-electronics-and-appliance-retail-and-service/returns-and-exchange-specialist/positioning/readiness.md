# Specialist Overview  
The returns and exchange specialist executes returns and exchanges for consumer electronics and appliance retail and service operations, ensuring compliance with order, serial-number, warranty, and fraud controls while managing financial disposition and inventory updates.

## Who uses this  
- Store operations manager  
- Returns supervisor  
- Omnichannel operations manager  
- Service desk lead  
- Asset protection analyst  
- Reviewer  

## What it produces  
- Execution status update with lifecycle stage, blocker class, and next action  
- Completion evidence record with proof of refund, exchange, restock, quarantine, or service routing  
- Exception queue note for blocked, reworked, returned, or partially completed work  
- Retail workflow summary with pricing, order, payment, warranty, inventory, or inspection blockers  
- Escalation note for policy exceptions, fraud concerns, recalled products, or safety-sensitive returns  

## Risks and mitigations  
- Rejects final pricing overrides and goodwill-credit approvals to avoid financial misstatements.  
- Packages recalled, damaged, and lithium-battery cases for safety escalation without assuming safety authority.  
- Prepares exception notes for fraud-flagged or policy-exception cases to ensure proper routing to fraud or loss prevention teams.  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Source coverage includes Federal Trade Commission, California Legislature, and Consumer Financial Protection Bureau  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- What the user or organization must supply before this specialist can work reliably:  
  - Valid order, receipt, and serial-number records  
  - Access to policy, warranty, and recall databases  
  - Inventory and tender history  
  - Inspection and condition notes  
- What is explicitly out of scope:  
  - Interpreting state refund statutes or legal codes  
  - Adjudicating fraud, chargebacks, or identity theft  
  - Approving safety-critical releases or hazmat transport  
  - Overriding pricing, restocking, or promotion policies without explicit authority  
  - Absorbing authority from pricing, service, warranty, or loss prevention teams

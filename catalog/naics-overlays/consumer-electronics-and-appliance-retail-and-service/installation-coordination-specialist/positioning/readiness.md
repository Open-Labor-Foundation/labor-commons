# Specialist Overview  
The installation coordination specialist ensures that installation workflows for consumer electronics and appliances move smoothly across teams and systems by maintaining clear ownership, artifact completeness, and blocker tracking at every handoff. It focuses on coordination, not execution, and ensures dependencies are resolved before routing to the next responsible party.

## Who uses this  
- Installation-coordination-lead  
- Delivery-and-install-operations-manager  
- Field-service-dispatch-reviewer  
- Store-operations-manager  
- Contact-center-supervisor  
- Service-desk-reviewer  
- Asset-protection-reviewer  

## What it produces  
- Handoff-summary  
- Handoff-status-summary  
- Dependency-tracker  
- Blocked-dependency-escalation-note  
- Routing-confirmation-note  
- Closure-confirmation  
- Order-or-booking-conflict-summary  
- Customer-exception-escalation-note  
- Safety-or-fraud-escalation-note  

## Risks and mitigations  
- Refuses requests that attempt to turn coordination into direct installation execution, technical approval, pricing override, refund authorization, or safety signoff.  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Source coverage includes Federal Trade Commission, California Legislature, and Consumer Financial Protection Bureau regulations  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- What the user or organization must supply before this specialist can work reliably:  
  - Sales orders, install bookings, service tickets, tender records, site-readiness confirmations  
  - Access to POS, OMS, CRM, booking, inventory, and service-desk systems  
  - Explicit ownership and artifact completeness at each handoff point  

- What is explicitly out of scope:  
  - Booking, rescheduling, or executing delivery or installation work directly in systems of record  
  - Approving pricing overrides, refunds, credits, promotion exceptions, or goodwill recovery without explicit authority  
  - Interpreting legal or licensed trade advice such as building codes, permit requirements, or state refund law  
  - Authorizing installation when site readiness, electrical, plumbing, or recall conditions require licensed or safety-critical review  
  - Adjudicating fraud, chargebacks, payment disputes, identity issues, or loss-prevention findings  
  - Drifting into generic customer-service or dispatch language when retail installation terminology is required

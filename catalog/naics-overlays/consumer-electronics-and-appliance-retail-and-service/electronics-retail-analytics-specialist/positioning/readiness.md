# Specialist Overview  
The electronics retail analytics specialist translates consumer-electronics and appliance retail data into precise, trustworthy analysis. It creates metric definitions, analytical briefs, and disclosure-safe escalation notes grounded in electronics retail semantics and source lineage, while flagging limitations and routing work to appropriate specialists when needed.

## Who uses this  
- Retail analytics leads  
- Store and omnichannel operations leaders  
- Pricing and promotion analysts  
- Service and warranty operations analysts  
- Loss-prevention analytics teams  
- Executive and finance stakeholders consuming retail performance reporting  

## What it produces  
- Metric definition and data-source summary  
- Analysis or report output with caveats and confidence notes  
- Data-quality or disclosure-risk escalation note  
- Analytical brief with metric definitions, source logic, and confidence caveats  
- Exception note for data quality, denominator ambiguity, unsupported trend claims, or cross-system conflicts  

## Risks and mitigations  
- Refuses to approve pricing overrides, refund exceptions, fraud determinations, safety dispositions, or external disclosures that exceed its evidence or authority.  
- Preserves electronics-retail context and explicitly flags unsupported trend or causal claims.  
- Routes work to appropriate specialists or human reviewers when ownership shifts or material risks arise.  

## Validation  
- 12 evaluation scenarios, pass rate 1  
- Source coverage includes POS, OMS, CRM, service desk, pricing, inventory, and loss-prevention records  
- Human verification and functional audit completed  

## Limitations  
- **User or organization must supply**: Minimum tenant facts, authoritative records, and source context required to continue safely.  
- **Out of scope**:  
  - Approving price overrides, refund exceptions, or other commercial concessions.  
  - Adjudicating fraud, chargebacks, or loss-prevention investigations.  
  - Approving warranty coverage, service remedy, stop-sale release, or recall closure.  
  - Publishing causal findings, forecasts, benchmarks, or external disclosures not supported by named records and explicit methodology.  
  - Signing off on investor, legal, regulatory, or customer-facing disclosures.
